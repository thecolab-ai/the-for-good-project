Why this matters

A "$4.99 → SPECIAL $2.99" sticker implies you're saving against a price that's rarely actually charged. Both chains share an almost identical floor, ceiling and time-weighted average across a supposed duopoly — they're just caught at different points in the same cycle. Multiply this one bottle across a whole grocery basket and it quietly shapes what every household pays.

This is one worked example. The project is now gathering more — worst same-day cross-store gaps, promo-cycling across a staples basket, and the "store-location tax" — to build a cited, evidence-backed picture.
