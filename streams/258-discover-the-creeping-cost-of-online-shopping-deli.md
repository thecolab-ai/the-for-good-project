# Online shopping fees are getting harder to see and compare

## The problem, in plain language

Buying online can look cheap until delivery, rural surcharges, return postage,
or "free shipping" conditions appear late in the process. These small costs matter
most to people who compare every dollar, live outside easy store access, or need
confidence that returning a faulty item will not cost them more.

This synthesis draws on eight completed pieces of research. **The stream is now
waiting on its human steward to decide direction** (see "Where this is heading").

## What we've learned so far

- **There is no single, simple NZ online delivery fee.** Some retailers publish
  flat small-parcel prices, some calculate freight only after item or address
  details, and "free shipping" can depend on spend thresholds, loyalty accounts,
  subscriptions, promo codes, or selected products. (confidence: Medium) -
  [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
- **Several retailers have raised the spend needed to earn free shipping since
  2023 — and this no longer rests on one vendor's numbers.** Dated snapshots of
  retailers' own pages show Chemist Warehouse's free-shipping threshold rose from
  $50 to $99, Hallensteins' from $60 to $90, and Glassons' from $60 to $80, on
  top of the earlier Woolworths case ($80 to $100). Standard delivery fees moved
  unevenly, though: Mitre 10's small-parcel fee rose $1, while New World's
  comparable South Island fees looked unchanged. (confidence: Medium) -
  [threshold changes](../research/findings/other/retailer-shipping-threshold-changes.md),
  [delivery fee trend](../research/findings/other/online-delivery-fee-trend-nz.md)
- **The delivery price a shopper sees before paying can differ from the same
  retailer's published policy.** A live test with identical small baskets across
  metro, rural, and remote addresses found only two of five retailers gave
  comparable quotes before checkout. One product page showed "from $8" for all
  three locations while the retailer's own table lists $13.50 for rural parcels;
  another quoted $8 for the Chatham Islands while its FAQ warns outer islands
  cost extra. (confidence: Medium) -
  [checkout basket audit](../research/findings/other/checkout-basket-delivery-costs-nz.md)
- **Rural and remote shoppers face extra cost and uncertainty.** Where retailers
  publish fixed small-parcel metro/rural tables, the rural add-on in the sample
  was $2 to $6, but bulky or remote delivery can add much more, and several
  retailers still require checkout or address entry before the final price is
  clear. (confidence: Medium) -
  [rural delivery surcharge](../research/findings/other/rural-delivery-surcharge-nz.md)
- **The law is clearer for faulty goods than for change-of-mind returns.** The
  Consumer Guarantees Act covers faulty, unsuitable, or misdescribed goods, not
  ordinary buyer's remorse. Retailers can set change-of-mind terms, but they
  cannot use those terms to charge a customer for returning a genuine CGA fault.
  (confidence: High) -
  [returns legal backdrop](../research/findings/other/online-returns-cga-legal-backdrop.md)
- **Return costs are usually findable before you pay — but not at the buying
  moment.** Across twelve retailers, change-of-mind return charges (customer-paid
  postage, non-refundable delivery fees, restocking conditions) were generally
  published somewhere public before checkout, but almost always in a help or
  policy page rather than beside the price or the checkout button. Only one
  sampled retailer (PB Tech) published a specific restocking percentage (20%).
  (confidence: Medium) -
  [return-fee disclosure audit](../research/findings/other/return-fee-disclosure-audit.md)
- **The original news concern is plausible, but some headline figures were thin
  starting points rather than settled evidence.** The framing found single-origin
  Shippit/RNZ numbers on delivery fees, free returns, and bad delivery experience,
  plus a clear need to verify retailer-specific fees against current pages.
  (confidence: Medium) -
  [stream framing](../research/findings/other/online-shopping-delivery-costs-framing.md)

## What we're not sure about yet

- **The final checkout price is still unproven for most retailers.** The live
  basket test reached comparable pre-payment quotes at only two of five
  retailers — the others deferred the price to a later step or blocked automated
  access — and where quotes did appear, they sometimes disagreed with the same
  retailer's policy table. No test went all the way to a final payment screen.
- **The threshold-rise trend is now multi-source, but still a selected sample.**
  The dated snapshots cover a handful of retailers whose pages could be archived
  and read, not a market-weighted survey, and one retailer's current page even
  carries two conflicting thresholds ($80 and $70). Whether standard delivery
  fees are broadly rising — as opposed to thresholds — remains unsettled: the
  one-year fee averages trace to a single delivery-software vendor, and the
  independent snapshots show uneven movement.
- **The returns law is high-confidence, but market practice is not.** We do not
  know how common restocking fees are beyond the one verified example (the other
  reported figures trace to a single Consumer NZ article), how often retailers
  mislabel CGA faults as change-of-mind, or whether policy-page-only disclosure
  would satisfy the "upfront about the fee" standard in a real dispute — that is
  a legal judgement no finding here can settle.
- **Rural small-parcel add-ons do not describe the whole rural burden.** Published
  add-ons of $2 to $6 cover only retailers with clear tables and mostly small
  parcels. Bulky goods, remote islands, marketplace sellers, and address-specific
  checkout rules remain under-measured — and the basket test showed even a
  remote-address quote on a product page may not be the final charge.
- **Nobody has yet watched the actual cart and payment screens.** Both new audits
  stopped short of full checkout: we know where fees are *published* and what
  early quote surfaces *show*, but not what a real shopper sees at the moment of
  payment, on mobile, or when logged in.

## What we could do about it

- **Create a public shopper fee tracker for major NZ retailers** - helps:
  shoppers, consumer advocates, budgeting advisers, and journalists. Effort:
  Medium. Supported by [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
  (confidence: Medium), [threshold changes](../research/findings/other/retailer-shipping-threshold-changes.md)
  (confidence: Medium), [rural delivery surcharge](../research/findings/other/rural-delivery-surcharge-nz.md)
  (confidence: Medium), and [returns legal backdrop](../research/findings/other/online-returns-cga-legal-backdrop.md)
  (confidence: High). Would need: dated snapshots, clear caveats for
  checkout-calculated prices (the basket audit showed page estimates can
  understate the real rural fee), and a plan for keeping fast-changing pages
  current.
- **Run a controlled checkout audit across metro, rural, and remote addresses** -
  helps: rural shoppers, council or community workers, and anyone trying to
  compare the real landed cost before buying. Effort: Large. Supported by
  [checkout basket audit](../research/findings/other/checkout-basket-delivery-costs-nz.md)
  (confidence: Medium), [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
  (confidence: Medium), and [rural delivery surcharge](../research/findings/other/rural-delivery-surcharge-nz.md)
  (confidence: Medium). Would need: identical test baskets, screenshots or saved
  pages, no real purchases, and a way past the login walls and bot checks that
  stopped the pilot at three of five retailers — likely a human doing the
  clicking.
- **Publish a plain-language returns rights guide** - helps: shoppers and support
  workers distinguish "faulty item" from "change of mind" before paying an
  avoidable fee. Effort: Small. Supported by [returns legal backdrop](../research/findings/other/online-returns-cga-legal-backdrop.md)
  (confidence: High), [return-fee disclosure audit](../research/findings/other/return-fee-disclosure-audit.md)
  (confidence: Medium), and [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
  (confidence: Medium). Would need: review by a human with consumer-law authority
  before being used as advice.
- **Build a dated threshold and fee trend watch** - helps: funders, researchers,
  and consumer advocates see whether the problem is getting worse or just feels
  worse. Effort: Medium. Supported by [threshold changes](../research/findings/other/retailer-shipping-threshold-changes.md)
  (confidence: Medium), [delivery fee trend](../research/findings/other/online-delivery-fee-trend-nz.md)
  (confidence: Medium), and [stream framing](../research/findings/other/online-shopping-delivery-costs-framing.md)
  (confidence: Medium). Would need: the archive-snapshot method the threshold
  audit already proved out (including its known blind spots — some retailers'
  old pages are unrecoverable behind bot walls), and discipline not to overstate
  one-vendor averages.
