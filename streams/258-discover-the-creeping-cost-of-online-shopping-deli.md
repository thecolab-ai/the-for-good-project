# Online shopping fees are getting harder to see and compare

## The problem, in plain language

Buying online can look cheap until delivery, rural surcharges, return postage,
or "free shipping" conditions appear late in the process. These small costs matter
most to people who compare every dollar, live outside easy store access, or need
confidence that returning a faulty item will not cost them more.

This synthesis draws on five completed pieces of research. **The stream is now
waiting on its human steward to decide direction** (see "Where this is heading").

## What we've learned so far

- **There is no single, simple NZ online delivery fee.** Some retailers publish
  flat small-parcel prices, some calculate freight only after item or address
  details, and "free shipping" can depend on spend thresholds, loyalty accounts,
  subscriptions, promo codes, or selected products. (confidence: Medium) -
  [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
- **Free-shipping thresholds appear to be rising, but the wider fee trend is not
  yet proved by independent NZ time-series data.** Woolworths' Delivery Saver
  threshold rose from $80 to $100 between 2023 and 2025, and NZ Post raised
  parcel prices in 2024 and 2025. Broader threshold and delivery-fee averages
  still rely heavily on one delivery-software vendor's data, with only a one-year
  NZ delivery-fee comparison. (confidence: Medium) -
  [delivery fee trend](../research/findings/other/online-delivery-fee-trend-nz.md)
- **Rural and remote shoppers face extra cost and uncertainty.** Where retailers
  publish fixed small-parcel metro/rural tables, the rural add-on in the sample
  was $2 to $6, but bulky or remote delivery can add much more, and several
  retailers still require checkout or address entry before the final price is
  clear. (confidence: Medium) -
  [rural delivery surcharge](../research/findings/other/rural-delivery-surcharge-nz.md)
- **The law is clearer for faulty goods than for change-of-mind returns.** The
  Consumer Guarantees Act covers faulty, unsuitable, or misdescribed goods, not
  ordinary buyer's remorse. Retailers can set change-of-mind terms, but they
  cannot use those terms to charge a customer for returning a genuine CGA fault.
  (confidence: High) -
  [returns legal backdrop](../research/findings/other/online-returns-cga-legal-backdrop.md)
- **The original news concern is plausible, but some headline figures were thin
  starting points rather than settled evidence.** The framing found single-origin
  Shippit/RNZ numbers on delivery fees, free returns, and bad delivery experience,
  plus a clear need to verify retailer-specific fees against current pages.
  (confidence: Medium) -
  [stream framing](../research/findings/other/online-shopping-delivery-costs-framing.md)

## What we're not sure about yet

- **The actual checkout price is still missing for many retailers.** Several
  findings found that final delivery can depend on basket, item size, address,
  seller, store, carrier, account status, or promotion. A controlled live-cart
  audit would materially improve the stream.
- **The trend claim is load-bearing and only partly independent.** The strongest
  dated threshold case is Woolworths, but the aggregate "thresholds are rising"
  picture and the 2025 to 2026 delivery-fee averages trace mostly to Shippit.
  Standard delivery was almost flat in that one-year comparison, while express
  rose sharply.
- **The returns law is high-confidence, but the market practice is not.** We know
  CGA faults cannot be treated like change-of-mind returns. We do not yet know
  how often retailers mislabel faults, how clearly return fees are disclosed
  before payment, or how common the reported restocking fees are beyond the
  checked PB Tech example.
- **Rural small-parcel add-ons do not describe the whole rural burden.** Published
  add-ons of $2 to $6 cover only the retailers with clear tables and mostly small
  parcels. Bulky goods, remote islands, marketplace sellers, pharmacy restrictions,
  and address-specific checkout rules remain under-measured.
- **Transparency has not yet had its own full checkout audit.** The evidence
  repeatedly points to late or conditional disclosure, but the stream has not yet
  timed exactly where each retailer reveals delivery and return costs before
  payment.

## What we could do about it

- **Create a public shopper fee tracker for major NZ retailers** - helps:
  shoppers, consumer advocates, budgeting advisers, and journalists. Effort:
  Medium. Supported by [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
  (confidence: Medium), [rural delivery surcharge](../research/findings/other/rural-delivery-surcharge-nz.md)
  (confidence: Medium), and [returns legal backdrop](../research/findings/other/online-returns-cga-legal-backdrop.md)
  (confidence: High). Would need: dated snapshots, clear caveats for
  checkout-calculated prices, and a plan for keeping fast-changing pages current.
- **Run a controlled checkout audit across metro, rural, and remote addresses** -
  helps: rural shoppers, council or community workers, and anyone trying to
  compare the real landed cost before buying. Effort: Large. Supported by
  [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
  (confidence: Medium) and [rural delivery surcharge](../research/findings/other/rural-delivery-surcharge-nz.md)
  (confidence: Medium). Would need: identical test baskets, screenshots or saved
  pages, no real purchases, and careful handling of account-only or postcode-only
  pricing.
- **Publish a plain-language returns rights guide** - helps: shoppers and support
  workers distinguish "faulty item" from "change of mind" before paying an
  avoidable fee. Effort: Small. Supported by [returns legal backdrop](../research/findings/other/online-returns-cga-legal-backdrop.md)
  (confidence: High) and [retailer fee snapshot](../research/findings/other/online-retailer-delivery-fees-snapshot.md)
  (confidence: Medium). Would need: review by a human with consumer-law authority
  before being used as advice.
- **Build a dated threshold and fee trend watch** - helps: funders, researchers,
  and consumer advocates see whether the problem is getting worse or just feels
  worse. Effort: Medium. Supported by [delivery fee trend](../research/findings/other/online-delivery-fee-trend-nz.md)
  (confidence: Medium) and [stream framing](../research/findings/other/online-shopping-delivery-costs-framing.md)
  (confidence: Medium). Would need: Wayback or archived retailer pages, the full
  Shippit methodology if available, and discipline not to overstate one-vendor
  averages.
