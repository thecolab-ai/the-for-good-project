# What iwi settlement capital has and has not shown so far

## The problem, in plain language

Treaty settlements returned financial and commercial assets to iwi, and many iwi have used that base to build commercial portfolios, fund grants, and support whānau programmes. The hard question is not whether money moved, but what the public record can fairly say about long-term wealth, risk, and community benefit without confusing settlement top-ups, market valuations, Crown-funded services, or iwi-defined goals. That matters for councils, funders and charities who may want to partner with iwi without turning partial public data into a league table.

## What we've learned so far

- **Major settled iwi have generally grown settlement capital into much larger commercial portfolios, but the scale and risk profile vary sharply.** (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-commercial-baseline.md)
- **Starting conditions matter before investment skill is judged: early large settlements, relativity clauses, asset location, and capital access structurally widen outcomes.** (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-structural-inequality.md)
- **The public record supports venture case studies, not a clean success-rate figure; durable assets and strong partners look more resilient than concentrated exposure to shock-prone operating sectors.** (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-venture-outcomes.md)
- **The common governance pattern is a representative iwi authority setting mandate and distributions, with a separate commercial arm managing investments under explicit rules, but public evidence does not prove one best model.** (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-commercial-governance-models.md)
- **Iwi annual reports show real social spending and programme outputs, but reviewed public NZ evidence rarely proves that settlement capital caused measured social outcomes.** The one candidate peer-reviewed study on procurement outcomes, previously unreadable behind a publisher block, has now been read in full via a public copy — it covers social procurement for Māori and Pasifika construction workers generally, not any iwi's own programmes, so the gap stands. (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-social-outcomes.md)
- **A responsible public comparison could cover stewardship metrics, risk, distributions and data limits, while leaving outcome data under iwi governance rather than forcing a universal return or wellbeing ranking.** (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-reporting-standard.md)

## What we're not sure about yet

- How many ventures really succeeded or failed is still unclear because comparable venture-level registers, lifetime returns, write-downs and exits are not public across iwi.
- The strongest commercial evidence and the weakest social-impact evidence sit side by side: balance sheets, distributions and programme outputs are visible, but public sources rarely connect those to measured whānau wellbeing changes. This gap is now confirmed rather than provisional — the last unread academic lead on procurement outcomes has been checked and does not fill it.
- Some comparisons are structurally loaded. Relativity payments, new redress, valuation methods, debt, asset location, member-count denominators and Crown delivery delays can make iwi look more or less successful than a simple investment-performance reading would suggest.
- Smaller or later-settled iwi are harder to compare from public reports, so the evidence is strongest for the larger iwi and weaker for a full sector-wide picture.
- The proposed reporting-standard work is only a desk-research proposal. It would need iwi governors, iwi data specialists, Māori commercial lawyers and members to test what is appropriate before anyone treats it as a standard.

## What we could do about it

- **A plain-language guide to reading iwi commercial success without mistaking redress, valuation gains, distributions, or Crown-funded programmes for investment performance.** — helps: funders, councils, charities and non-specialist partners. Effort: Small. Supported by [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-commercial-baseline.md) (confidence: Medium), [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-structural-inequality.md) (confidence: Medium), [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-social-outcomes.md) (confidence: Medium), and [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-reporting-standard.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: careful wording, Māori/iwi review, and a clear promise that it is not an iwi ranking.
- **A prototype public stewardship table for a small sample of iwi, showing assets, debt, asset mix, distributions, reporting basis and data caveats.** — helps: researchers, council teams, funders and charity partners trying to understand public reports consistently. Effort: Medium. Supported by [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-reporting-standard.md) (confidence: Medium), [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-commercial-baseline.md) (confidence: Medium), and [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-commercial-governance-models.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: public-only data, agreed definitions, and guardrails against league-table use.
- **A focused case-study pack on venture risk patterns, comparing a few publicly documented successes, recoveries and failures.** — helps: economic-development partners and funders understand what kinds of risk are visible before backing similar projects. Effort: Medium. Supported by [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-venture-outcomes.md) (confidence: Medium), [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-commercial-governance-models.md) (confidence: Medium), and [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-structural-inequality.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: enough public records for each case, and iwi-approved input for anything beyond public sources.
- **An iwi-mandated outcomes-evaluation design for jobs, procurement, housing, education, marae support and wellbeing.** — helps: iwi, funders and evaluators who need stronger outcome evidence without taking control of Māori data. Effort: Large. Supported by [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-settlement-social-outcomes.md) (confidence: Medium) and [finding](../research/findings/other/iwi-reporting-standard.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: iwi mandate, Māori data-governance review, and agreement that public desk research is not consent to use member-level data.
