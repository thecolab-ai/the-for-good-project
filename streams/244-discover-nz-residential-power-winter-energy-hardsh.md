# NZ winter power hardship: who is cold, who is missed, and what is still hidden

## The problem, in plain language

Every winter, some New Zealand households cannot afford enough power to keep their homes warm. The harm is not just a high bill: cold, damp homes are linked to illness, missed school, and avoidable public costs. This research round found that help exists, but it is uneven, conditional, and often weakest for renters, low-income households, and prepay customers. The stream is now **awaiting a direction decision from its human steward**.

## What we've learned so far

- **The best current headline is 132,000 households, but it is not a complete map.** In 2024, 6.7% of households could not afford to keep their home adequately warm. MBIE says the 2019 to 2024 change was not statistically significant, while the rise from 2021 to 2024 was statistically significant. The highest published rates for this exact measure are among households reporting income inadequacy or DEP-17 material hardship. (confidence: Medium) — [who is most affected finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-disaggregates-2024-vs-2019.md)
- **Energy hardship has several interacting causes, not one clean percentage split.** The evidence supports income, poor housing quality, electricity prices and tariffs, and the landlord/tenant split incentive as real drivers, but no public NZ evidence found here divides hardship into shares such as "housing explains X% and prices explain Y%". (confidence: Medium) — [drivers finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-drivers.md)
- **Renters show up repeatedly as a high-need group, while major help does not cleanly fit them.** Warmer Kiwi Homes is aimed at eligible owner-occupiers and excludes rentals; the Winter Energy Payment is tied to benefit or pension status rather than cold homes; and retailer, community, MSD, and budgeting-service help forms a patchwork rather than one clear entitlement. (confidence: Medium) — [intervention gaps finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-intervention-gaps.md), [drivers finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-drivers.md)
- **Prepay power is the clearest connection-risk gap.** Mandatory Consumer Care Obligations have raised the floor for postpay customers, but prepay supply can still stop when credit runs out. The finding reports about 27,000 prepay auto-disconnections a month in early dashboard data, compared with about 810 postpay disconnections a month, and says outcome evidence is still missing. (confidence: Medium) — [consumer care finding](../research/findings/other/consumer-care-mandatory-effectiveness.md)
- **Cold and damp homes have real health and cost consequences, but the death numbers need care.** The strongest causal burden estimate found is still a 2010-2017 study: about 162 deaths a year attributed to crowding, cold, damp or mould, before adding fall hazards. A much larger excess-winter-mortality figure should not be described as deaths caused by cold housing. Current programme evaluations support the case that warm, dry housing interventions reduce illness, but they do not replace the national burden model. (confidence: Medium) — [cold and damp housing costs finding](../research/findings/other/cold-damp-housing-costs.md)
- **The published data is better for national and subgroup decisions than local targeting.** MBIE does not publish region or climate-zone breakdowns for the exact "could not afford adequate warmth" measure, and the finding could not verify published disabled-household or older-alone cross-tabs. Damp and mould data can help as a housing-condition proxy, but it is not the same measure. (confidence: Medium) — [who is most affected finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-disaggregates-2024-vs-2019.md)

## What we're not sure about yet

- **This round did not answer the future supply-pressure question.** The stream root included large new electricity loads, including data centres, but none of the merged findings listed for this synthesis tests how those loads would affect household bills or energy hardship.
- **No public evidence found here can separate the causes cleanly.** Income, housing condition, tariffs, tenure, household type, and climate interact, so a single-cause solution would be weakly supported.
- **The intervention map is missing coverage data.** The findings show eligibility gaps, but not a national, cross-tabbed picture of who actually receives Warmer Kiwi Homes, Winter Energy Payment, EnergyMate, retailer hardship support, urgent MSD help, or community help by tenure, income source, ethnicity, region, and household type.
- **The renter story is strong but incomplete.** Renters are more exposed, and some owner-occupier schemes exclude them, but the evidence does not yet show whether Healthy Homes compliance, tenancy enforcement, or rental-facing supports are closing the gap in practice.
- **Consumer care is visible, but outcomes are not.** We know more about reported compliance and prepay disconnection counts than about whether households stayed warm, avoided self-rationing, avoided health harm, or trusted the process.
- **The health-cost case rests on older national modelling.** Programme evaluations are current and positive, but there is no public post-2017 national re-run of the cold/damp housing burden model in the evidence reviewed here.

## What we could do about it

- **A plain-language winter power help map** — show who is likely covered, who may be missed, and where to start across Warmer Kiwi Homes, Winter Energy Payment, retailer hardship support, MSD urgent help, MoneyTalks, CAB, and community routes. Helps: charity workers, budgeting services, councils, and households trying to navigate support. Effort: Small. Supported by the [intervention gaps finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-intervention-gaps.md) (confidence: Medium) and [who is most affected finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-disaggregates-2024-vs-2019.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: current scheme rules, frontline review, and clear caveats that support is conditional.
- **A renter winter home evidence pack** — a simple tool or guide for renters and support workers to record cold, damp, mould, heating, and Healthy Homes issues and choose the right next support route. Helps: renters in cold homes and the people supporting them. Effort: Medium. Supported by the [drivers finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-drivers.md) (confidence: Medium) and [intervention gaps finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-intervention-gaps.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: tenancy-domain review, careful wording so it is not mistaken for legal advice, and input from renters or tenant advocates.
- **A prepay and consumer-care evidence brief or tracker** — keep the new public disconnection and compliance evidence readable, with the difference between postpay disconnection and prepay self-disconnection made clear. Helps: financial mentors, consumer advocates, and services supporting prepay households. Effort: Medium. Supported by the [consumer care finding](../research/findings/other/consumer-care-mandatory-effectiveness.md) (confidence: Medium) and [drivers finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-drivers.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: the Electricity Authority data to stay public, a refresh rhythm, and caution not to infer harm from short disconnections without lived-experience or outcome data.
- **A health-and-cost explainer for warm, dry homes** — summarise the defensible health, school, and public-cost evidence without overstating excess winter deaths. Helps: funders, councils, and community groups making the case for warm-home work. Effort: Small. Supported by the [cold and damp housing costs finding](../research/findings/other/cold-damp-housing-costs.md) (confidence: Medium) and [intervention gaps finding](../research/findings/other/nz-energy-hardship-intervention-gaps.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: strict wording around what is causal, what is inflation-adjusted, and what still needs a newer national model.
