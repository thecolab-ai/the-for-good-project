# Rural internet gaps are about usable service, not just coverage

## The problem, in plain language

Many rural people in New Zealand can now buy some kind of internet connection, but that does not mean the service is fast, affordable, reliable, orderable, or useful in an emergency. The remaining harm falls on rural households, farms, schools, health users, small businesses, and people travelling or working outside mobile coverage.

## What we've learned so far

- **New Zealand cannot publicly say which rural addresses fail a usable broadband standard.** Public data can show coverage, prices, speeds, and programme outputs, but not one live address-level answer for orderability, speed, latency, data allowance, price, and reliability together. (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-orderability-gap.md)
- **The household baseline is useful, but it is not a service-quality count.** The evidence estimates about 285,000 rural households, with about 29,700 stated-response households without internet access and about 62,900 either without internet or without a stated response, but this does not separate no coverage, poor service, affordability, choice, or missing data. (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-underserved-baseline.md)
- **Coverage can exist while service is still not orderable or usable.** Rural 4G fixed wireless covers about 95-96% of rural addresses, but public maps are provider-supplied and not live order tools; stop-sells, local terrain, old copper, loaded latency, satellite line-of-sight, and rural prices still matter. (confidence: Medium) — [service outcomes](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-service-outcomes.md), [orderability gap](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-orderability-gap.md)
- **Government programmes improved rural connectivity, but public reporting is stronger on outputs than outcomes.** UFB is the clearest high-quality service success where it reaches a place; RBI, mobile black spot, capacity upgrades, and remote-user grants are harder to judge because public reporting often counts premises, towers, roads, sites, or grants rather than before-and-after user experience. (confidence: Medium; remote-user grant outcomes are Low) — [finding](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-service-outcomes.md)
- **Voice, mobile coverage, 111 access, and power resilience are a separate problem from home broadband.** Copper withdrawal, the 3G shutdown, mobile landmass gaps, road black spots, and power outages mean a household can have broadband at home but still lack dependable emergency or on-the-move connectivity. (confidence: Medium) — [voice and resilience](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-voice-mobile-resilience.md), [post-transition gaps](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-resilience-gaps-after-transitions.md)
- **The clearest observed harms are access constraints, not national loss totals.** Public evidence links poor rural connectivity to telehealth limits, learning-continuity risks, farm operations, and reported safety concerns, but it does not give national counts of missed consults, learning loss, farm incidents, compliance failures, lost revenue, or business closures. (confidence: Medium; farm operational harms are Low-Medium) — [observed outcomes](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-observed-outcomes.md), [digital exclusion cost](../research/findings/other/rural-digital-exclusion-cost.md)
- **The cost story is real, but the headline dollar value is not settled.** The NZIER $16.5 billion ten-year figure is best treated as a modelled upper-bound scenario, not an observed national loss. (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/rural-digital-exclusion-cost.md)
- **Overseas comparisons support a usable-service benchmark, not a simple coverage race.** Canada is useful for a clear 50/10 unlimited broadband floor, Australia for mobile-map and satellite treatment, and Nordic countries for separating legal safety nets, strategy targets, and measured coverage achievement. (confidence: Medium) — [finding](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-comparators.md)

## What we're not sure about yet

- The biggest gap is still the current public count of rural addresses that cannot order a service meeting a defined standard for speed, upload, latency, data, price, and reliability.
- The findings use different denominators: Census households, Commerce Commission rural addresses, connections, copper-area premises, programme outputs, towers, roads, tourism sites, and registered vulnerable landline users. They cannot be added into one clean percentage.
- There is a tension between high nominal rural 4G fixed-wireless coverage and evidence of households without internet, stop-sells, poor ADSL performance, rural price premiums, satellite limits, and residual remote-user support. The findings show why these can all be true at once: they measure different things.
- Emergency resilience is only partly visible. Public data gives some lower-bound counts for copper connections, 3G-only address exposure, landmass mobile coverage, and 111-vulnerable landline consumers, but not a joined view of weak mobile signal, road risk, tower backup power, outage duration, vulnerable people, and local emergency access.
- The observed-outcomes evidence is strongest for access barriers and reported harms. It is weaker for causal national totals, especially farm safety, compliance, and small-business losses.
- New Zealand does not appear to have a public rural broadband service floor comparable to Canada's 50/10 unlimited target, so any local "usable service" benchmark still needs a human policy choice.

## What we could do about it

- **Create a plain-language rural connectivity scorecard for usable service.** — helps: councils, funders, community advocates, and policy staff. Effort: Medium. Supported by [underserved baseline](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-underserved-baseline.md) (confidence: Medium), [orderability gap](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-orderability-gap.md) (confidence: Medium), [service outcomes](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-service-outcomes.md) (confidence: Medium), and [comparators](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-comparators.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: agreement on a simple standard for orderability, speed, upload, latency, data, price, reliability, and emergency resilience.
- **Build a community evidence kit for checking whether mapped coverage works in real life.** — helps: rural communities, schools, health providers, marae, farms, and local advocates. Effort: Medium. Supported by [orderability gap](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-orderability-gap.md) (confidence: Medium), [underserved baseline](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-underserved-baseline.md) (confidence: Medium), [service outcomes](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-service-outcomes.md) (confidence: Medium), and [observed outcomes](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-observed-outcomes.md) (confidence: Medium, with farm harms Low-Medium). Would need: a privacy-safe way to collect failed orders, stop-sells, black spots, prices, outage experiences, and local service impacts without publishing personal household details.
- **Prepare a rural emergency communications risk brief.** — helps: emergency managers, councils, rural health and disability advocates, marae, farming groups, and policy staff. Effort: Medium. Supported by [voice and resilience](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-voice-mobile-resilience.md) (confidence: Medium) and [post-transition gaps](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-resilience-gaps-after-transitions.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: careful handling of security-sensitive infrastructure information and a way to talk about vulnerable users without identifying people.
- **Publish a short policy benchmark brief comparing New Zealand with overseas usable-service approaches.** — helps: funders, councils, policy teams, and advocates deciding what "good enough" should mean. Effort: Small. Supported by [comparators](../research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-comparators.md) (confidence: Medium), [orderability gap](../research/findings/civic-transparency/rural-broadband-orderability-gap.md) (confidence: Medium), and [digital exclusion cost](../research/findings/other/rural-digital-exclusion-cost.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: wording that uses overseas targets as comparators, not ready-made answers for New Zealand.
