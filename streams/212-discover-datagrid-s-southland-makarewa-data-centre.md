# DataGrid's Southland data centre: the verifiable facts in one place

## The problem, in plain language

DataGrid plans to build a very large data centre at Makarewa, just north of Invercargill. The public debate has mixed confirmed consent facts, company claims, campaign claims, and different ways of counting the project's size and impacts. This stream assembled a factual baseline so Southland residents, councils, funders, journalists, and advocates can see what is confirmed, what is contested, and what is still unknown.

## What we've learned so far

- **The project has real council approvals, and the main public consent record is Environment Southland's ordinary council decision, not a completed fast-track approval.** Environment Southland granted nine regional consents on 11 March 2026 for groundwater, wastewater, generator air discharges, wetland works, construction dewatering, and coastal cable activities; the attempted fast-track listing did not produce the March 2026 approvals. (confidence: Medium overall; High for the Environment Southland and fast-track points) - [consent record finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-consents.md)
- **The Southland District Council and Invercargill City Council approvals are better supported than the first synthesis said, but their full decision documents are still missing.** The Southland District Council consent is cross-referenced in the Environment Southland decision, and the Invercargill City Council cable-related consent is corroborated by public council comments; the exact conditions still need the council records. (confidence: Medium) - [SDC/ICC consent reconciliation finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-sdc-icc-consents-mw-reconciliation.md)
- **The 240 MW and 280 MW numbers can both describe the same consented build.** The consent record uses "up to 240 MW of IT capacity" for computing load, while DataGrid's 280 MW figure is total campus power draw; the implied efficiency ratio is plausible, but DataGrid's exact design efficiency figure is not published. (confidence: Medium) - [SDC/ICC consent reconciliation finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-sdc-icc-consents-mw-reconciliation.md)
- **The water numbers are now exact from the consent instruments.** The operating consent allows up to 7 litres per second, 604,800 litres a day, and 220,752,000 litres a year of Waihopai-zone groundwater for cooling water and potable supply; wastewater discharge is capped at 5,000 litres a day and is amenities wastewater, not cooling-water discharge. (confidence: High) - [water consents finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-water-consents.md)
- **The wetland issue is real but narrower than some public wording suggests.** The consent allows removal of a 0.24 ha low-value wetland, while the nearby higher-value Taylor Road Wetland is meant to be protected by monitoring and recharge; whether that protection will work over time is a technical question still outside this desk review. (confidence: High for what the documents say; Medium for long-term ecological sufficiency) - [water consents finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-water-consents.md)
- **The confirmed power story is large, staged, and often overstated.** The announced campus is 280 MW, about half of Tiwai's 572 MW load, not twice Tiwai. The Mercury deal is a 15-year, 140 MW power purchase option, not proof that the whole 280 MW already has a firm power contract; grid documents show staged 100-120 MW near-term planning, while "up to 1 GW" remains an ambition without public consent, supply, or grid arrangements. (confidence: Medium overall; High for the 280 MW, 140 MW, and Tiwai arithmetic) - [energy finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-energy-phase-load.md)
- **Ownership, cost, and jobs are only partly visible.** The New Zealand companies sit in a New Zealand/Singapore corporate chain, but current ultimate beneficial ownership is not visible in New Zealand registers. Public cost figures appear to mix currencies and scopes, and construction jobs figures use different measures that cannot be reconciled without the economic assessment. (confidence: Medium) - [economics and ownership finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-economics-ownership.md)
- **The public debate contains confirmed facts, contested interpretations, and unsupported assertions on both sides.** The opposition petition is real and points to real consented impacts, but its water-price, power-bill, broad wildlife, and government-spending claims were not supported by the checked evidence. DataGrid's consent and Mercury claims are better evidenced than its broader climate, jobs, emissions, and latency benefit claims. (confidence: Medium) - [claims catalogue finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-claims-status.md)

## What we're not sure about yet

- The full Southland District Council and Invercargill City Council decision documents and conditions are still not in the evidence file, so the baseline cannot yet state every consent condition across all three councils.
- The exact design efficiency figure is not published; the 240 MW computing-load / 280 MW total-power reconciliation is well supported but still partly inferred.
- Public energy records do not yet show a final phase-by-phase load schedule, firm power supply above the 140 MW Mercury option, or binding arrangements for any build beyond the announced 280 MW campus.
- The current ultimate owners, financing stack, economic assessment, job definitions, and GDP claim are not fully public.
- Taylor Road Wetland protection depends on future pump testing, monitoring, trigger levels, and recharge performance; a hydrologist or wetland ecologist would be needed to judge adequacy.
- Several public claims remain unverified or single-sourced: the Press "quite different projects" quote, Wellington electricity comparisons, consumer bill impacts, low-carbon/emissions savings claims, latency savings, and the current status of the University of Otago tenant arrangement.

## What we could do about it

- **Publish a plain-language fact sheet separating approved facts, company claims, campaign claims, and unknowns** - helps: Southland residents, journalists, councillors, funders, and advocates who need a shared baseline. Effort: Small. Supported by the [claims catalogue](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-claims-status.md) (confidence: Medium), [factual baseline](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-factual-baseline.md) (confidence: Medium), and [consent record](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-consents.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: editorial sign-off and clear caveats on the missing SDC/ICC conditions.
- **Request and summarise the remaining council and economic documents before any public-facing output** - helps: anyone relying on the baseline for accuracy. Effort: Small-Medium. Supported by the [consent record](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-consents.md) (confidence: Medium), [SDC/ICC reconciliation](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-sdc-icc-consents-mw-reconciliation.md) (confidence: Medium), and [economics and ownership finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-economics-ownership.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: someone to lodge and track council information requests, then update the overview from the returned records.
- **Create a simple numbers explainer for power and water** - helps: people trying to compare the project with Tiwai, town water use, farm water use, and the company's 1 GW ambition without mixing unlike figures. Effort: Small. Supported by the [water consents finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-water-consents.md) (confidence: High), [energy finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-energy-phase-load.md) (confidence: Medium), and [MW reconciliation finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-sdc-icc-consents-mw-reconciliation.md) (confidence: Medium). Would need: careful wording that keeps consented capacity, staged grid planning, and long-term ambition separate.
- **Prepare a short technical brief for a hydrologist or wetland ecologist** - helps: the steward or local stakeholders ask a qualified person the one environmental question the desk research cannot settle. Effort: Small for the brief; dependent on finding the expert. Supported by the [water consents finding](../research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-water-consents.md) (confidence: High for the consented framework; Medium for ecological sufficiency). Would need: the expert to review the monitoring, pump-test, trigger, and recharge conditions.
