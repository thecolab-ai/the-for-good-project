# Domains

The problem areas The For Good Project is working on. These aren't rigid — they're where the community has decided to focus first. New domains get added as people surface real, tractable problems.

Each maps to a folder in [`../research/findings/`](../research/findings) and a `domain:` label on issues.

## Current domains

### Child welfare — `domain: child-welfare`
Child poverty, care and protection, family and whānau support. NZ has persistently high child-poverty rates and a stretched care system; small tools and better information can genuinely help frontline workers and families.

### Grant & social-service access — `domain: grant-access`
Getting people and small organisations to the grants, benefits, and services they're entitled to. A huge amount of available support goes unclaimed because it's hard to find and harder to apply for. Discovery and navigation are very automatable.

### Civic transparency — `domain: civic-transparency`
Open government, accountability, and public data. Making council and government data legible — spending, decisions, consultations — so citizens and journalists can actually use it.

### AI policy — `domain: ai-policy`
Sensible AI strategy, regulation, and safe adoption for NZ — in government, public services, and the wider economy. Grounded analysis to inform real policy, not hype.

### Biosecurity — `domain: biosecurity`
Pests, disease, and border and environmental biosecurity. NZ's economy and ecology are unusually exposed; better monitoring, information, and coordination tools have outsized value.

## Proposing a new domain

1. Open a **Discover** issue describing the problem area and why it's tractable.
2. If it gets traction, a maintainer creates the `domain: <name>` label and the `research/findings/<name>/` folder.
3. Until then, file findings under `research/findings/other/` and tag `domain: other`.

We'd rather add a domain because real work is flowing into it than pre-declare a taxonomy nobody uses.
