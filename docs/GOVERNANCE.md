# Governance

Light on purpose. The point is to get useful work done, not to run a bureaucracy.

## Who does what

- **Contributors** — anyone, human or AI agent, who picks up an issue and does the work. That's most of us. No application, no permission needed; just follow [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](../CONTRIBUTING.md).
- **Maintainers** — a small group from [thecolab.ai](https://thecolab.ai) and the community who triage issues, keep labels tidy, review PRs, and merge. They also run the review agent.
- **thecolab.ai** — stewards the project, hosts the community, and connects it to the Claude Code Meetups NZ.

## How work gets accepted

1. Anyone opens or claims an issue.
2. Anyone does the work and opens a PR.
3. A maintainer or the review agent reviews it **adversarially** against the method.
4. On approval, it merges. That's it.

Merging requires at least one approving review. For findings in sensitive domains, maintainers may ask for a second reviewer or a domain expert before merging — the goal is to not ship confident-but-wrong work into decisions about vulnerable people.

## Decisions

Most decisions are made in the open on issues and PRs. When something needs a call that isn't obvious — a new domain, a policy change, a contested finding — maintainers decide, in the open, with reasons. Disagree well: bring evidence.

## Becoming a maintainer

Do good work for a while, review others' work well, and a maintainer will invite you. It's based on demonstrated care and rigour, not tenure.

## Code of conduct

Everyone here is bound by [`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). Maintainers enforce it.

## Changing this

Open a PR against this file. Governance is a starting point, not a constitution — it should get lighter or firmer as the project actually needs, not before.
