# Public child and family support pre-check tools should automate only low-sensitivity screening variables and route judgement-heavy variables to agencies

## Executive answer

- A public pre-check tool can safely ask low-sensitivity screening questions that public agency pages already use in their own eligibility guidance: caregiver age, child age band, broad care arrangement, benefit status, family or household income band, paid-work/study status, childcare service type and hours, housing-cost type, public-housing status, and broad urgent-cost categories. [Inland Revenue, Working for Families eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it); [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost); [Work and Income, Childcare Subsidy](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html); [Work and Income, Accommodation Supplement](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/accommodation-supplement.html); [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/)
- The tool should not present final eligibility for shared care, disputed care, residence or visa edge cases, fluctuating income, hardship, disability or health costs, non-parent care after family breakdown, child support liability, or exceptional grants; those variables should be framed as "ask Inland Revenue, Work and Income, or the provider" because public pages require evidence, formal assessment, legal definitions, or agency discussion. [Inland Revenue, Shared care](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/shared-care); [Work and Income, Special Needs Grant](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/special-needs-grant.html); [Work and Income, Disability Allowance](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/disability-allowance.html); [Work and Income, Unsupported Child's Benefit](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/unsupported-childs-benefit.html)
- The safest output wording is "you may be able to get..." or "worth checking with..." rather than "you qualify"; MSD's own public checker says it is a guide only and that benefit decisions are made under detailed legislation, while Inland Revenue's Working for Families estimator says calculator/chart results are estimates and actual entitlement can differ when income, family situation, or shared care changes. [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/); [Inland Revenue, Estimate your entitlement](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/estimate-your-entitlement)
- Privacy minimisation points in the same direction: a navigation tool should collect only what is necessary for the pre-check purpose, avoid names, IRD numbers, client numbers, addresses, medical diagnoses, parent contact details, and documents unless the user is inside an official application channel, and state whether answers are kept or used to identify the person. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 1](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/1/); [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 3](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/3/); [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/)
- This finding covers public national rules for child/family financial supports in the sibling entitlement inventory and does not validate exact question wording with families, advocates, MSD, Inland Revenue, the Ministry of Education, or legal advisers. [Family entitlements inventory](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/child-welfare/family-entitlements-nz.md)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the classification is grounded in current official public pages and agency checker disclaimers, but it is a product-safety interpretation rather than agency-approved eligibility-tool design.

## Evidence

### Boundary for a public pre-check

A public pre-check should behave like a routing guide because MSD's own "Check what you might get" says it tells users payments they "might be able to get", does not keep answers or use them to identify the user, directs users to MyMSD or phone channels to apply, and says benefit decisions are made under detailed legislation after contacting or applying to the Ministry. [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/)

Inland Revenue also treats its Working for Families calculator as an estimate: the page says Inland Revenue works out entitlement for eligible families and issues a notice of entitlement, and it warns that the calculator/chart are estimates only, that the calculator does not account for shared-care arrangements for the Minimum Family Tax Credit, and that actual entitlement can differ if income, family situation, or shared care changes during the year. [Inland Revenue, Estimate your entitlement](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/estimate-your-entitlement)

Privacy law supports the same minimal-question design because Privacy Principle 1 says personal information should be collected only for a lawful purpose connected with the agency's activities and only when necessary for that purpose, while Privacy Principle 3 says people should be told why information is collected, who will receive it, whether providing it is compulsory or voluntary, and what happens if it is not provided. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 1](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/1/); [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 3](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/3/)

[Inland Revenue, Shared care](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/shared-care); [Inland Revenue, Child support eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/child-support/can-you-get-child-support) | "Shared care can change the result. [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost); [Work and Income, Special Needs Grant](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/special-needs-grant.html); [Work and Income, Sole Parent Support](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/sole-parent-support.html); [Inland Revenue, Child support eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/child-support/can-you-get-child-support) | "If you are not sure about your residence, visa, ordinary residence, or tax-residence status, ask the agency." | High |

[Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost amount](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/how-much-familyboost-can-you-claim); [Inland Revenue, Minimum family tax credit](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/minimum-family-tax-credit); [Work and Income, Temporary Additional Support](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/temporary-additional-support.html) | "Use a broad income band for screening; if income varies or includes self-employment, ask the agency before making financial decisions." | High |
| Hardship judgements: "no other way to pay", whether a cost was foreseeable, and whether the person could have covered it | Special Needs Grant requires an immediate/essential or emergency cost with no other way to pay, and MSD says it also looks at whether the emergency cost was known, whether the person could have covered it, and how the grant will help. | Temporary Additional Support essential-cost assessment | TAS requires essential costs with no other way to pay and depends on income/assets, other help available, cost types, and what the person is doing to reduce costs or increase income; MSD also says unlisted costs require talking to them. [Work and Income, Temporary Additional Support](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/temporary-additional-support.html) | "A pre-check can flag TAS as worth asking about, but Work and Income decides which costs count." | High |
| Disability, health, and extra-care assessments | Child Disability Allowance requires assessment that the child needs constant care and attention for 12 months or more, and the application needs a health practitioner to complete a medical certificate; Disability Allowance costs must be additional and ongoing because of disability, with health-practitioner confirmation and proof of costs. [Work and Income, Child Disability Allowance](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/child-disability-allowance.html); [Work and Income, Disability Allowance](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/disability-allowance.html) | "You may be able to get disability-related help; a health practitioner and Work and Income will need to confirm the details." | High |

[Work and Income, Unsupported Child's Benefit](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/unsupported-childs-benefit.html); [Work and Income, Orphan's Benefit](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/orphans-benefit.html) | "If you care for someone else's child, Work and Income can assess which carer payment fits and what evidence is needed." | High |

[Work and Income, Extraordinary Care Fund](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/extraordinary-care-fund.html) | "If you receive Orphan's Benefit or Unsupported Child's Benefit, ask about the Extraordinary Care Fund for specific documented needs." | Medium |

### Product rule

The most defensible public tool design is a two-tier output: "likely worth checking/applying" when all low-sensitivity public variables line up, and "ask the agency" when any judgement-heavy variable is present; that follows the wording of MSD's own checker, which tells users what they "might be able to get" and says agency contact or application is needed for detailed qualification. [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/)

The pre-check should not collect identifiers or documents because the public routing purpose can usually be met with banded, self-reported answers, and Privacy Principle 1 says identifying information should not be required when the lawful purpose does not require it. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 1](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/1/)

## What would change this conclusion

- Agency approval or published implementation guidance from Inland Revenue, MSD, or the Ministry of Education could move some variables from "ask the agency" into "safe to automate", especially if an agency publishes a maintained machine-readable eligibility ruleset or API. [Inland Revenue, Estimate your entitlement](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/estimate-your-entitlement); [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/)
- A legal review could change the privacy boundary, especially if the tool stores answers, profiles users, transfers data to agencies, supports applications, or handles children's, health, family-violence, custody, immigration, or benefit-debt information. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Privacy Act 2020](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/)
- Lived-experience and frontline-worker testing could show that even some "safe" variables are harmful or confusing in practice, such as asking care-percentage, visa/residence, relationship, disability, income, or family-breakdown questions before trust is established. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 4](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/4/)
- Policy changes after 3 July 2026 could alter thresholds, payment interactions, or agency calculators; a live product should refresh official pages and rates before release. [Family entitlements inventory](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/child-welfare/family-entitlements-nz.md)
- I could not verify an official cross-agency data dictionary for eligibility pre-check variables, nor an official statement that a third-party public navigation tool may reuse agency calculator logic; this finding therefore recommends conservative routing language rather than a deterministic eligibility engine. [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families eligibility tool](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/eligibility-tool)

## Open follow-up questions

- What exact question wording do families and advocates find safe, understandable, and non-stigmatising for income, care arrangements, residence, disability, and hardship?
- Which variables should be hidden behind optional "check more supports" steps so a public tool does not ask sensitive questions before they are necessary?
- Can Inland Revenue, MSD, and the Ministry of Education provide a maintained public ruleset or approval pathway for third-party support-navigation tools?
- What consent, retention, and deletion pattern should a public pre-check use if a future version lets users save answers?
