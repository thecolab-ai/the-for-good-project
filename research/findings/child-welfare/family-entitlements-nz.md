# New Zealand child and family financial entitlements are cross-agency

## Executive answer

- The main national family-support map is cross-agency: Inland Revenue administers Working for Families tax credits, Best Start, FamilyBoost, paid parental leave payments, and child support; Work and Income/MSD administers childcare payments, housing and hardship assistance, main benefits for sole/young parents, disability-related allowances, and non-parent carer benefits; the Ministry of Education funds 20 Hours ECE through early learning services. [Inland Revenue, Working for Families overview](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/all-about-working-for-families/overview); [Work and Income, Working for Families](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/working-for-families.html); [Inland Revenue, Paid parental leave](https://www.ird.govt.nz/paid-parental-leave); [Inland Revenue, Child support](https://www.ird.govt.nz/child-support); [Work and Income, A-Z benefits and payments](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/index.html); [Ministry of Education, 20 Hours ECE](https://www.education.govt.nz/early-childhood/funding-and-data/20-hours-ece-for-ece-services)
- A support-finder tool should ask first about: who has principal or day-to-day care; child age; household income; benefit status; tax residence/residence status; work/study/training; childcare provider and hours; housing costs and area; disability or health costs; urgent essential costs; and whether the applicant is a parent, non-parent carer, sole parent, or young parent. [Inland Revenue, Can you get Working for Families?](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it); [Work and Income, Childcare Subsidy](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html); [Work and Income, Accommodation Supplement](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/accommodation-supplement.html); [Work and Income, Child Disability Allowance](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/child-disability-allowance.html); [Work and Income, Special Needs Grant](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/special-needs-grant.html)
- Working for Families is not a single payment: it includes Family Tax Credit, In-work Tax Credit, Minimum Family Tax Credit, and Best Start, and families may qualify for some credits but not others. [Inland Revenue, Types of payment](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types)
- Childcare help is split across Ministry of Education 20 Hours ECE, Work and Income childcare payments, and Inland Revenue FamilyBoost, so a support finder should not model childcare as one agency or one payment. [Ministry of Education, 20 Hours ECE](https://www.education.govt.nz/early-childhood/funding-and-data/20-hours-ece-for-ece-services); [Work and Income, Childcare](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/childcare.html); [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost)
- This inventory is accurate for public national rules as at 2 July 2026, but payment rates, income thresholds, and policy settings change; any live product should call or scrape current agency sources rather than hard-code thresholds. [Inland Revenue, Best Start, last updated 25 February 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/best-start); [Work and Income, Changes announced in Budget 2026](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/about-work-and-income/news/2026/changes-announced-in-budget-2026.html)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the inventory is based on current official Inland Revenue, Work and Income/MSD, Employment New Zealand, and Ministry of Education pages, but several MSD-only rows remain single-source and the boundary around smaller or programme-specific payments is still a judgement call.

## Evidence

### Scope and rule shape

This finding covers nationwide financial supports that a New Zealand child/family support-finder would likely screen before local, discretionary, non-cash, or narrow programme-specific supports. [Work and Income, A-Z benefits and payments](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/index.html); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families); [Work and Income, Childcare](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/childcare.html)

It includes tax credits, cash benefits, subsidies, allowances, child support administered by Inland Revenue, hardship assistance, and major provider-paid childcare subsidies where the effect is a direct reduction in family costs. [Ministry of Education, 20 Hours ECE](https://www.education.govt.nz/early-childhood/funding-and-data/20-hours-ece-for-ece-services); [Work and Income, Working for Families](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/working-for-families.html); [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost)

Eligibility is defined across several dimensions rather than one universal family-support test: Inland Revenue's Working for Families base test is age 16 or over, care of a dependent child, principal caregiver status, and residency requirements; MSD payments add separate income, asset, residence, housing, childcare, disability, work, or hardship tests depending on the payment. [Inland Revenue, Can you get Working for Families?](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it); [Work and Income, A-Z benefits and payments](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/index.html)

### Eligibility logic a support finder should model first

A first-pass support finder should treat "who has care of the child" as a gateway variable because Working for Families uses "principal caregiver" and dependent-child tests, child support uses care percentages for formula assessments, and non-parent carer benefits turn on day-to-day care and whether the carer is a parent. [Inland Revenue, Principal caregiver](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/principal-caregiver); [Inland Revenue, Can you get child support?](https://www.ird.govt.nz/child-support/can-you-get-child-support); [Work and Income, Unsupported Child's Benefit](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/unsupported-childs-benefit.html)

The child's age is another gateway variable because Best Start applies during the first three years, FamilyBoost applies to children aged 5 and under, 20 Hours ECE applies to children aged 3 to 5, Childcare Subsidy is generally for pre-school children with specific cohort-entry and Child Disability Allowance exceptions, OSCAR is generally for school children under 14 or under 18 with Child Disability Allowance, Child Disability Allowance applies under 18, and Sole Parent Support ends when the youngest child turns 14. [Inland Revenue, Best Start](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/best-start); [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost); [Ministry of Education, 20 Hours ECE](https://www.education.govt.nz/early-childhood/funding-and-data/20-hours-ece-for-ece-services); [Work and Income, Childcare Subsidy](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html); [Work and Income, OSCAR Subsidy](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/oscar-subsidy.html); [Work and Income, Child Disability Allowance](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/child-disability-allowance.html); [Work and Income, Sole Parent Support](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/sole-parent-support.html)

Income needs to be represented with care because different schemes use different income concepts: Working for Families uses family income for the tax year, FamilyBoost uses quarterly household income, Disability Allowance uses weekly income limits, Recoverable Assistance uses annual income limits, and MSD extra help often assesses income and assets together. [Inland Revenue, Family tax credit](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/family-tax-credit); [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost); [Work and Income, Disability Allowance](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/disability-allowance.html); [Work and Income, Recoverable Assistance Payment](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/recoverable-assistance-payment-grant.html); [Work and Income, Temporary Additional Support](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/temporary-additional-support.html)

Benefit status changes which agency pays some support and which credits are available: MSD says benefit clients can get Family Tax Credit paid with other Work and Income payments while non-benefit clients are paid by Inland Revenue, and Inland Revenue says main-benefit families may qualify for Best Start and Family Tax Credit but not In-work Tax Credit or Minimum Family Tax Credit. [Work and Income, Working for Families](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/working-for-families.html); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families and MSD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/all-about-working-for-families/msd)

Residency or tax-residence questions cannot be collapsed into one field because Working for Families uses its own residency requirements, FamilyBoost requires New Zealand tax residence, MSD benefits commonly require citizenship/permanent residence/residence-class status plus normal residence or ordinary residence, and paid parental leave is tied to primary-carer and work-history tests rather than the MSD residence formulations. [Inland Revenue, Working for Families residency requirements](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/residency-requirements); [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost); [Work and Income, Sole Parent Support](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/sole-parent-support.html); [Inland Revenue, Who can apply for paid parental leave](https://www.ird.govt.nz/paid-parental-leave/qualifying/who-can-apply)

## What would change this conclusion

- A formal cross-agency product catalogue from Inland Revenue, MSD, Employment New Zealand, Oranga Tamariki, and the Ministry of Education could change the "main support" boundary used here, especially for smaller grants and non-cash supports. [Work and Income, A-Z benefits and payments](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/index.html); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families); [Ministry of Education, 20 Hours ECE](https://www.education.govt.nz/early-childhood/funding-and-data/20-hours-ece-for-ece-services)
- Legislative or policy changes after 2 July 2026 could change thresholds, rates, qualifying ages, abatement rules, or agency ownership; this is already plausible because Work and Income has announced Budget 2026 housing-support changes for 2027 and Inland Revenue pages show 2026 Working for Families threshold/rate changes. [Work and Income, Changes announced in Budget 2026](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/about-work-and-income/news/2026/changes-announced-in-budget-2026.html); [Inland Revenue, Best Start](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/best-start)
- I could not verify a single official machine-readable eligibility ruleset covering Inland Revenue, MSD, and Ministry of Education supports; a production support-finder would need agency confirmation before presenting eligibility as more than a guided pre-check. [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families eligibility tool](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/eligibility-tool); [Work and Income, Childcare](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/childcare.html)
- This finding uses public sources only. I did not verify where families misunderstand eligibility, abandon applications, or experience cultural/language barriers, so those should be treated as research questions for families, advocates, and frontline workers rather than as findings from this inventory.
- I did not include local council grants, NGO hardship funds, school-level support, Oranga Tamariki board payments, Home Help, International Custody Dispute Payment, or education-sector operational funding beyond 20 Hours ECE because those areas need separate scoped findings before they can be treated as part of the main entitlement inventory. [Work and Income, A-Z benefits and payments](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/index.html); [Oranga Tamariki, Caregiver support](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/caring-for-someone/caregiver-support/)
