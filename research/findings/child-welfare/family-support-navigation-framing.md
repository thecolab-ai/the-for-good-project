# Families missing child and family support is a real NZ navigation problem

## Executive answer

- This is a valid discover stream: New Zealand has child poverty and hardship at a scale where missed support can matter, with Stats NZ reporting that in the year ended June 2025, 12.6% of children lived in households below the before-housing-costs low-income threshold, 17.8% lived below the after-housing-costs fixed-line threshold, and 14.3% lived in material hardship. [Stats NZ, 2026](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/child-poverty-statistics-year-ended-june-2025/)
- The support map is split across agencies: Inland Revenue administers Working for Families tax credits, Best Start, FamilyBoost, paid parental leave payments, and child support; Work and Income/MSD administers childcare, housing, hardship, disability, sole-parent, young-parent, and non-parent-carer payments; the Ministry of Education funds 20 Hours ECE through early learning services. [Finding #24](family-entitlements-nz.md); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families overview](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/all-about-working-for-families/overview); [Work and Income, A-Z benefits](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/index.html); [Ministry of Education, 20 Hours ECE](https://www.education.govt.nz/early-childhood/funding-and-data/20-hours-ece-for-ece-services)
- Non-take-up is measurable, but not uniformly measured across entitlements: MSD estimated 87% Working for Families take-up among eligible families in tax year 2020, with overall annual take-up ranging from 83% to 90% across the 2007-2020 series; the same source estimated close-to-100% take-up for eligible families with main-benefit income and 75-85% for eligible families without main-benefit income. [MSD via SIA Hub, 2022](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates-2007-2020); [Finding #93](take-up-gap-child-family-entitlements.md)
- Existing official entry points already include separate agency tools and pages, including MSD's "Check what you might get", Inland Revenue's Working for Families eligibility tool, SmartStart, govt.nz family financial-help pages, and Work and Income's children pages; the unresolved question is whether families can move across these entry points when their situation spans agencies. [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families eligibility tool](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/eligibility-tool); [SmartStart](https://smartstart.services.govt.nz/); [govt.nz, Financial help for your family](https://www.govt.nz/browse/family-and-whanau/financial-help-for-your-family/); [Work and Income, Children](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/index.html)
- The stream should stay in research until the navigation-breakdown question is answered: #24 has already mapped the main entitlement inventory, #93 has quantified part of the take-up gap, and #25 is the active follow-up on where families go and where the journey breaks down. [Issue #24](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/24); [Issue #93](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/93); [Issue #25](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/25)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the problem, agency split, and some non-take-up patterns are supported by current official pages and MSD administrative studies, but the strongest take-up statistics are dated or single-origin and the stream still lacks direct evidence on family navigation journeys.

## Evidence

### Problem statement and affected families

The problem to research is not simply that some families are poor; it is that eligible families may miss public support because eligibility depends on care arrangements, income, benefit status, residence, work or study, childcare use, housing costs, disability, and hardship circumstances across several agencies. [Finding #24](family-entitlements-nz.md); [Inland Revenue, Can you get Working for Families?](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it); [Work and Income, Childcare Subsidy](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html); [Work and Income, Accommodation Supplement](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/accommodation-supplement.html); [Work and Income, Child Disability Allowance](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/child-disability-allowance.html)

The affected population is large enough to justify research: Stats NZ's year ended June 2025 child-poverty release reports 12.6% of children below the before-housing-costs low-income measure, 17.8% below the after-housing-costs fixed-line measure, and 14.3% in material hardship, with no statistically significant annual change from the year ended June 2024 for those primary measures. [Stats NZ, 2026](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/child-poverty-statistics-year-ended-june-2025/)

Stats NZ also reports higher material-hardship rates for some groups in the year ended June 2025: 25.1% for tamariki Maori, 31.0% for Pacific children, 26.9% for disabled children, and 27.5% for children in disabled households; these figures make cultural, disability, language, and access design relevant to later research, but they do not by themselves prove why support is missed. [Stats NZ, 2026](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/child-poverty-statistics-year-ended-june-2025/)

### What is already known in stream #4

Finding #24 identifies a cross-agency entitlement landscape rather than a single "family support" scheme: Working for Families includes Family Tax Credit, In-work Tax Credit, Minimum Family Tax Credit, and Best Start; childcare support can involve Ministry of Education 20 Hours ECE, MSD childcare payments, and Inland Revenue FamilyBoost; hardship, housing, disability, and non-parent-carer supports sit mainly with Work and Income/MSD. [Finding #24](family-entitlements-nz.md); [Inland Revenue, Types of Working for Families payments](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types); [Work and Income, Childcare](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/childcare.html); [Inland Revenue, FamilyBoost eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost)

Finding #93 shows that non-take-up is not just hypothetical for Working for Families and Best Start: MSD estimated that Working for Families take-up was 87% in tax year 2020, that annual overall take-up ranged from 83% to 90% across the study series, and that take-up was close to 100% for eligible families with main-benefit income but 75-85% for eligible families without main-benefit income. [MSD via SIA Hub, 2022](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates-2007-2020); [Finding #93](take-up-gap-child-family-entitlements.md)

Finding #93 also cites a separate MSD Best Start study: among children born in New Zealand between 1 July 2018 and 30 September 2019, an estimated 96.5% had families eligible for Best Start and take-up was estimated at 96.9% among eligible children, with 99% take-up for children in main-benefit families, 97% for paid-parental-leave/no-benefit families, and 94% for other eligible families. [MSD via SIA Hub, Best Start, 2022](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit); [Finding #93](take-up-gap-child-family-entitlements.md)

For a broader income-support analogue, MSD's Income Support Survey Findings Pack 7 reports that among survey respondents who appeared potentially eligible for the Accommodation Supplement, 43.9% self-reported receiving it; among potentially eligible respondents not receiving it, 32.3% said they were not aware of the payment, and among aware non-recipients, 35.9% thought they were not eligible. [MSD Income Support Survey Findings Pack 7, 2024](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf); [Finding #93](take-up-gap-child-family-entitlements.md)

### What is already being done

Official entry points already exist, so a future solution should not start by assuming there is no information online. MSD runs "Check what you might get"; Inland Revenue has a Working for Families eligibility tool; SmartStart connects pregnancy and early-childhood services; govt.nz publishes a family financial-help page; and Work and Income has children, childcare, urgent-costs, housing, and benefit pages. [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families eligibility tool](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/eligibility-tool); [SmartStart](https://smartstart.services.govt.nz/); [govt.nz, Financial help for your family](https://www.govt.nz/browse/family-and-whanau/financial-help-for-your-family/); [Work and Income, Children](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/index.html)

The research gap is therefore not "do pages exist?", but "which families do not reach the right page, do not recognise themselves as eligible, cannot complete the application path, or fall between Inland Revenue, MSD, Ministry of Education, and community-service entry points?" [Issue #25](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/25); [MSD Income Support Survey Findings Pack 7, 2024](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A newer official Working for Families take-up estimate after tax year 2020 could change the scale or concentration of the non-take-up problem, because the current WFF take-up series cited here ends at 2020. [MSD via SIA Hub, 2022](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates-2007-2020)
- A completed #25 finding could overturn the implicit tool hypothesis if it shows that the main breakdown is not finding or understanding support, but distrust, documentation burden, agency discretion, digital exclusion, debt/overpayment risk, or benefit adequacy. [Issue #25](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/25)
- A human with lived experience, frontline benefit-navigation experience, disability-access expertise, Maori service-design expertise, Pacific service-design expertise, or legal/welfare-rights authority is needed before ideation, because public sources cannot verify what families find confusing, unsafe, stigmatising, or culturally inappropriate in the application journey. [Stats NZ, 2026](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/child-poverty-statistics-year-ended-june-2025/); [docs/STREAMS.md](../../../docs/STREAMS.md)
- I could not verify a single official cross-agency machine-readable ruleset for all child/family supports; #24 found official pages and eligibility tools, not one shared eligibility API across Inland Revenue, MSD, and the Ministry of Education. [Finding #24](family-entitlements-nz.md); [Work and Income, Check what you might get](https://check.msd.govt.nz/); [Inland Revenue, Working for Families eligibility tool](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/can-you-get-it/eligibility-tool)
- I did not open duplicate research issues for the two original #4 questions because #24 already covers the entitlement inventory and #25 is already the active navigation-breakdown issue in stream #4; I opened #151 and #152 for non-duplicate follow-up questions raised by the current evidence. [Issue #24](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/24); [Issue #25](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/25); [Issue #151](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/151); [Issue #152](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/152)
