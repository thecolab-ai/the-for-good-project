# NZ's care-and-protection pathway is designed around an open report-of-concern front door, Oranga Tamariki intake triage, statutory response timeframes, assessment/investigation, and Family Group Conferences where the statutory threshold is met

## Executive answer

- Oranga Tamariki is the Crown agency that leads New Zealand's child-protection effort and describes its own role as supporting children whose wellbeing is at significant risk of harm, while the Oranga Tamariki Act 1989 gives the chief executive duties to take prompt action, promote awareness/reporting of child abuse, develop reporting protocols, provide training and standards, and monitor services under the Act. [New Zealand Government, Oranga Tamariki organisation page](https://www.govt.nz/organisations/oranga-tamariki-ministry-for-children/); [Oranga Tamariki, About us](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/); [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, s 7](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)
- The front door is deliberately open: Oranga Tamariki says anyone worried about a child or young person can make a report of concern to Oranga Tamariki or Police; section 15 of the Act similarly permits "any person" who believes a child or young person has been, or is likely to be, harmed, ill-treated, abused, neglected, or deprived to report the matter to the chief executive or a constable. [Oranga Tamariki, "Report of concern"](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/our-work/how-we-keep-children-safe/report-of-concern/); [Oranga Tamariki, "Worried about a child? Tell us"](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/worried-about-a-child-tell-us/); [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, s 15](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)
- Intake is designed to sort each report into either no further statutory action, referral to services, or further action; where Oranga Tamariki decides a statutory response is required, its Practice Centre sets three response timeframes for completing Tiaki Oranga: 24 hours for critical, 48 hours for very urgent, and 10 working days for urgent. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Considerations when developing a decision response"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/considerations-when-developing-a-decision-response); [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Report of concern response timeframe"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-timeframe)
- Further action means the core assessment phase: either a child and family assessment or an investigation; that phase is meant to understand safety, harm and needs, test whether the section 14 care-or-protection threshold is met, and decide between no further action, referral to services, or a Family Group Conference. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Report of concern response pathway - further action required"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-pathway); [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Core assessment phase"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/our-work/assessment-and-planning/assessments/intake-and-early-assessment/core-assessment-phase); [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, ss 14, 18](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)
- Family Group Conferences are the designed statutory decision-making forum once the threshold is reached or when a conference would help formulate a support plan; referrals can come from a social worker or Police constable under section 18, or from courts and other bodies concerned with child wellbeing under section 19. [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, ss 18-20](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/); [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "About family group conferencing"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/our-work/interventions/family-group-conferencing/about-family-group-conferencing); [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Family group conferences for care or protection concerns"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/policy/family-group-conferences-for-care-andor-protection-concerns)
- Poutasi recommendations 8 and 9 would change the front door by moving from an open voluntary reporting model to a legislated mandatory-reporting layer for defined professionals, backed by a guide, a high-risk report category, and regular training; as of the official February 2026 update, Government had accepted all 14 Poutasi recommendations, begun staged design of mandatory reporting, and started testing mandatory child-protection training for designated/core children's workforces, but the Monitor still recorded recommendations 8 and 9 as not achieved in its implementation review. [Dame Karen Poutasi final report, recommendations 8-9](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/About-us/Performance-and-monitoring/Reviews-and-Inquiries/System-review-Dame-Karen-Poutasi/CAB-22-MIN-0540-Final-report-by-Dame-Karen-Poutasi-on-the-death-of-Malachi-Subecz.pdf); [MSD, Integrated Government Response to the Poutasi Review](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/child-wellbeing-and-poverty-reduction/integrated-government-response-to-the-poutasi-review.html); [Aroturuki Tamariki, response to the Poutasi review](https://aroturuki.govt.nz/reports/poutasi-review/response)

**Overall confidence:** High — the designed pathway is described consistently by the Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, Oranga Tamariki public/practice guidance, the 2024/25 annual report, and official Poutasi implementation material. Confidence is Medium only on current implementation status for mandatory reporting/training because that work is moving through policy design and staged rollout.

## Evidence

### Statutory role and operating frame

Oranga Tamariki is listed by the New Zealand Government as the agency that leads national efforts to protect children and young people, including working with children, young people and families, funding community organisations, providing residential and care services, and supporting youth justice responses. [New Zealand Government, Oranga Tamariki organisation page](https://www.govt.nz/organisations/oranga-tamariki-ministry-for-children/)

Oranga Tamariki's own "About us" page says it supports any child in Aotearoa whose wellbeing is at significant risk of harm now or in future, and also works with young people who have offended or are likely to offend. [Oranga Tamariki, About us](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/)

The Oranga Tamariki Act 1989 gives the chief executive duties to take positive and prompt action to attain the Act's purposes, promote public and professional awareness of child abuse and how to report it, develop child-abuse reporting protocols with agencies and professional groups, ensure service providers are trained and meet appropriate standards, monitor services under the Act, and seek strategic partnerships with iwi and Maori organisations. [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, s 7](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)

The 2024/25 annual report frames Oranga Tamariki's core agency role as delivering a statutory care-and-protection and youth-justice agency while also leading and driving the wider children's system; in 2024/25 it reported 108,032 reports of concern for 63,103 children, more than 44,000 investigations, more than 10,000 Family Group Conferences, and more than 4,000 children in care. [Oranga Tamariki, Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/corporate-reports/annual-report/)

**Confidence: High** — these are official agency, government directory, statute, and annual-report sources.

### The report-of-concern front door

Oranga Tamariki's public reporting pages say anyone worried about a child, young person, or unborn baby can make a report of concern to Oranga Tamariki or Police, and that immediate danger should go to Police on 111. [Oranga Tamariki, "Report of concern"](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/our-work/how-we-keep-children-safe/report-of-concern/); [Oranga Tamariki, "Worried about a child? Tell us"](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/worried-about-a-child-tell-us/)

Section 15 of the Oranga Tamariki Act 1989 is the statutory basis for that open front door: it allows any person who believes a child or young person has been, or is likely to be, harmed, ill-treated, abused, neglected, or deprived to report the matter to the chief executive or a constable. [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, s 15](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)

Oranga Tamariki's public guidance says reports can be made by free 24/7 phone line or online form; it asks referrers to give what they have seen or heard, details of incidents, and details about the child or young person and whānau. [Oranga Tamariki, contact/report guidance](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/contact-us/); [Oranga Tamariki, "Worried about a child? Tell us"](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/worried-about-a-child-tell-us/)

**Confidence: High** — public guidance and the Act align.

### Intake triage and statutory response timeframes

Oranga Tamariki's Practice Centre says that when it determines a report of concern requires a statutory response, it must decide the timeframe to complete Tiaki Oranga. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Report of concern response timeframe"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-timeframe)

The three Practice Centre response-timeframe categories are: 24 hours, labelled critical, where the child or young person has been seriously harmed or is at immediate risk of serious harm and immediate Oranga Tamariki involvement is required to establish safety; 48 hours, labelled very urgent, where serious harm or risk exists but some protective factors are present for the next 48 hours; and 10 working days, labelled urgent, where there is risk of harm or neglect, cumulative or developmental concerns remain unresolved, and vulnerability/patterns limit protective factors. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Report of concern response timeframe"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-timeframe)

If the initial assessment indicates that further assessment or investigation is not necessary, Oranga Tamariki's decision response can be no further action; the Practice Centre lists reasons such as no disclosed harm, safety or wellbeing needs, whānau or family actively responding, other services already meeting needs, an initial assessment finding no need for core assessment, the same matters already being dealt with, appropriate alternative referrals being available, or a Family Violence Inter Agency Response System outcome of no further Oranga Tamariki action. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Considerations when developing a decision response"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/considerations-when-developing-a-decision-response)

If the concern meets the Child Protection Protocol, the Practice Centre says it cannot be deemed no further action unless consultation has occurred with New Zealand Police. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Considerations when developing a decision response"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/considerations-when-developing-a-decision-response)

The same guidance describes referral to services as a voluntary pathway when another agency, iwi or cultural social service is likely to achieve positive outcomes and no further Oranga Tamariki statutory action is required; Oranga Tamariki is responsible for making the referral once it has established the service can deliver in a timeframe that meets the child's and caregiver's needs. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Considerations when developing a decision response"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/considerations-when-developing-a-decision-response)

The 2024/25 annual report reports only the public performance measure for critical or very urgent reports of concern addressed within operational timeframes; the target was 95% and actual result was 92%, with Oranga Tamariki attributing pressure partly to increased report volume and social-worker shortages at some sites. [Oranga Tamariki, Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/corporate-reports/annual-report/)

**Confidence: High** on the designed triage categories and NFA/referral pathways; **Medium** on public timeliness visibility because the annual report measure found in this pass covers critical/very urgent reports, not a public compliance rate for all three response-timeframe categories.

### Assessment, investigation, and decisions after further action

Where further action is required, Oranga Tamariki says it undertakes the core assessment phase as either a child and family assessment or an investigation. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Report of concern response pathway - further action required"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-pathway)

The Practice Centre describes a child and family assessment as appropriate where statutory assessment may be necessary or desirable because serious harm is being experienced or is likely, there is significant impact on development/safety/health/wellbeing, cumulative effects are harmful and unresolved, and there has been no criminal offending against the child. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Report of concern response pathway - further action required"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-pathway)

The core assessment phase is designed to understand current and future oranga, including safety and harm or likelihood of harm, decide whether needs require addressing and who should address them, and inform the decision about future Oranga Tamariki involvement such as a Family Group Conference, no further action, or referral to other services. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Core assessment phase"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/our-work/assessment-and-planning/assessments/intake-and-early-assessment/core-assessment-phase)

The Practice Centre says assessment must apply Oranga Tamariki practice models, standards, tools and resources to decide whether the threshold for section 14 of the Oranga Tamariki Act 1989 is met; if that threshold is met, Oranga Tamariki must refer to a Family Group Conference. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Core assessment phase"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/our-work/assessment-and-planning/assessments/intake-and-early-assessment/core-assessment-phase); [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, ss 14, 18](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)

**Confidence: High** — the Practice Centre and Act describe the same threshold-to-conference pathway.

### Family Group Conferences

The Family Group Conference is a legislated process under the Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, and Oranga Tamariki's Practice Centre lists care-and-protection routes into it, including section 18 referral where the chief executive, a constable, or an enforcement officer believes there are care-or-protection concerns; section 18AAA referral where the chief executive is not satisfied the child needs care or protection but believes a conference would help formulate a support plan; and section 19 referral where a court or certain other people refer care-or-protection concerns to a care-and-protection coordinator and that coordinator decides to convene a conference. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "About family group conferencing"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/our-work/interventions/family-group-conferencing/about-family-group-conferencing); [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, ss 18AAA-20](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)

Oranga Tamariki's policy page says a Family Group Conference referral may be made by a social worker or Police constable under section 18(1), or by a court or another body/organisation concerned with the wellbeing of a child or young person under section 19(1)(a) or (b). [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Family group conferences for care or protection concerns"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/policy/family-group-conferences-for-care-andor-protection-concerns)

For section 19 referrals, the Practice Centre says the referral is not itself a report of concern and the intake decision-response guidelines do not apply; the care-and-protection coordinator must decide whether a Family Group Conference is necessary, may require a social-worker investigation under section 19(3), and if a social worker then believes the child is in need of care or protection, the social worker must refer to a coordinator for a Family Group Conference under section 18(1). [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "Section 19 referrals to a care and protection coordinator"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/our-work/interventions/family-group-conferencing/care-and-protection-family-group-conference/section-19-referrals-to-a-care-and-protection-coordinator); [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, ss 18-19](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)

After a care-and-protection Family Group Conference, Oranga Tamariki says it supports the child or young person, whānau/family and others involved to implement the conference decisions, recommendations and plan; the Practice Centre also notes statutory requirements after a conference, including seeking agreement from people needed for implementation and reporting back if agreement cannot be reached. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, "After the care and protection family group conference"](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/our-work/interventions/family-group-conferencing/care-and-protection-family-group-conference/after-the-care-and-protection-family-group-conference); [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, ss 29-31](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/)

**Confidence: High** — the conference routes and post-conference plan implementation are statutory and reflected in Practice Centre policy.

### How mandatory reporting and training would change the front door

Dame Karen Poutasi's final report recommended mandatory reporting and initiatives to support mandatory reporting so children subject to serious abuse, ill-treatment or harm are made safe earlier; the report describes a defined group of children-sector staff as mandatory reporters who would be required to report concerns about "high risk abuse" to Oranga Tamariki for investigation. [Dame Karen Poutasi final report, recommendations 8-9](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/About-us/Performance-and-monitoring/Reviews-and-Inquiries/System-review-Dame-Karen-Poutasi/CAB-22-MIN-0540-Final-report-by-Dame-Karen-Poutasi-on-the-death-of-Malachi-Subecz.pdf)

Recommendation 8 was to legislate mandatory reporting by defining the professionals and service providers classed as mandatory reporters, while recommendation 9 was to support mandatory reporting through a package that includes a reporting guide, a definition of red flags for a high-risk report of concern, a "High Report of Concern" category, regular training for mandatory reporters, undergraduate professional education on abuse risks/signs, and practising-certificate conditions tied to training and refreshers. [Dame Karen Poutasi final report, recommendations 8-9](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/About-us/Performance-and-monitoring/Reviews-and-Inquiries/System-review-Dame-Karen-Poutasi/CAB-22-MIN-0540-Final-report-by-Dame-Karen-Poutasi-on-the-death-of-Malachi-Subecz.pdf); [Aroturuki Tamariki, response to the Poutasi review](https://aroturuki.govt.nz/reports/poutasi-review/response)

MSD says the Government accepted all 14 Poutasi recommendations in September 2025, committed to staged implementation, and would begin mandatory-reporting design with mandatory training for designated workforces so people working in the system know how to identify and report child abuse while further action is sequenced to build system capacity. [MSD, Integrated Government Response to the Poutasi Review](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/child-wellbeing-and-poverty-reduction/integrated-government-response-to-the-poutasi-review.html)

The Beehive announcement likewise says work would start on mandatory-reporting design, beginning with mandatory training for designated workforces and staged to maximise child safety while mitigating risk. [Beehive, "Strengthened system protects children from harm", 9 October 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/strengthened-system-protects-children-harm)

The January 2026 official update says the mandatory child-protection training module was entering a testing phase for core children's workers from Health NZ, NZ Police, MSD, Ministry of Education, Department of Corrections, and Oranga Tamariki; the Ministry of Education update says existing child-safety responsibilities and reporting processes for schools, kura and early learning services did not change at that point. [MSD, Integrated Government Response to the Poutasi Review](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/child-wellbeing-and-poverty-reduction/integrated-government-response-to-the-poutasi-review.html); [Ministry of Education, "New inter-agency hub and child protection training", 29 January 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/new-inter-agency-hub-and-child-protection-training)

The Monitor's implementation review says the lead agency for recommendations 8 and 9 is Oranga Tamariki, supported by justice, police, social-development, health, education and other agencies; it records recommendations 8 and 9 as "Not Achieved" and notes mandatory reporting would require legislative change. [Aroturuki Tamariki, response to the Poutasi review](https://aroturuki.govt.nz/reports/poutasi-review/response)

**Confidence: High** on what recommendations 8 and 9 would add to the designed front door; **Medium** on current implementation status because official sources show design/testing activity but not completed legislation or a fully operational mandatory-reporting regime as of the cited February 2026 update.

## What would change this conclusion

- A legislative amendment creating mandatory reporting, defining mandatory reporters, or creating a high-risk report category would change the "current front door" description from voluntary/open reporting to open reporting plus a mandatory professional reporting duty. [MSD, Integrated Government Response to the Poutasi Review](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/child-wellbeing-and-poverty-reduction/integrated-government-response-to-the-poutasi-review.html); [Beehive, "Strengthened system protects children from harm", 9 October 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/strengthened-system-protects-children-harm)
- A newer Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre update could change the pathway detail, especially because the Practice Centre pages themselves warn printed pages may be out of date and several relevant pages were modified or reviewed in 2025-2026. [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, response timeframe](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-timeframe); [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, response pathway](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-pathway)
- A later Aroturuki Tamariki or Government implementation update could change the status of Poutasi recommendations 8 and 9 from not achieved/design/testing to implemented, partially implemented, or replaced by an alternative regime. [Aroturuki Tamariki, response to the Poutasi review](https://aroturuki.govt.nz/reports/poutasi-review/response); [MSD, Integrated Government Response to the Poutasi Review](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/child-wellbeing-and-poverty-reduction/integrated-government-response-to-the-poutasi-review.html)
- I could not verify, from public sources in this pass, whether Oranga Tamariki publishes a current aggregate compliance rate for the 10-working-day urgent timeframe alongside the critical/very urgent annual-report measure, or whether any internal triage tools differ materially from the public Practice Centre text. [Oranga Tamariki, Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.orangatamariki.govt.nz/about-us/corporate-reports/annual-report/); [Oranga Tamariki Practice Centre, response timeframe](https://practice.orangatamariki.govt.nz/core-practice/practice-tools/intake-decision-response-tool/report-of-concern-response-timeframe)
- This finding maps the designed pathway only; whether the pathway is experienced as accessible, timely, culturally safe, or effective requires lived experience, frontline practitioner input, iwi/Māori authority, and aggregate operational data beyond this baseline design map. [Oranga Tamariki Act 1989, principles and chief executive duties](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1989/24/en/latest/); [Aroturuki Tamariki reports](https://aroturuki.govt.nz/reports)
