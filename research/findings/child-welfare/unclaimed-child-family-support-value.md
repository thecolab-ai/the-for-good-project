# Public NZ data can estimate main Working for Families non-take-up at roughly $400m a year, but most other child/family supports lack a public unclaimed-value denominator

## Executive answer

- **The best public aggregate estimate is for the main Working for Families credits (Family Tax Credit and In-Work Tax Credit): about $407 million unclaimed in the 2020 tax year under an equal-average-entitlement assumption.** MSD estimated 278,200 eligible families and 243,000 recipient families in the 2020 tax year, while Inland Revenue reported $2.810 billion of 2019/20 Family Tax Credit and In-Work Tax Credit expenditure; multiplying paid expenditure by the non-recipient/recipient ratio gives $407 million, but the estimate is sensitive to whether non-recipients had smaller average entitlements than recipients. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD Annual Report 2020](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2020.pdf?modified=20251019232054)
- **The same WFF method gives a current-scale check of about $432 million using 2024/25 expenditure, not a refreshed take-up estimate.** Inland Revenue reported $2.984 billion of 2024/25 Family Tax Credit and In-Work Tax Credit expenditure, but the latest public WFF take-up estimate still stops at the 2020 tax year. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)
- **Best Start likely has much smaller non-take-up value, roughly $11 million a year on 2024/25 scale under the same equal-average-entitlement assumption.** MSD estimated 96.9% take-up for children in eligible families, and Budget 2026 reports $346 million of 2024/25 Best Start spending; applying the 3.1% non-take-up share to the paid amount gives about $11 million, but the take-up cohort was children born from July 2018 to September 2019, not the 2024/25 population. [SIA Hub/MSD Best Start take-up study](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit); [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf)
- **For Accommodation Supplement, MSD childcare assistance, and hardship assistance, public data gives spend and recipient/grant scale, but not a defensible child/family unclaimed-dollar estimate.** Budget 2026 reports 2024/25 spending of $2.232 billion for Accommodation Assistance, $167 million for Childcare Assistance, and $755 million for Hardship Assistance, while MSD's March 2026 Benefit Fact Sheets report 377,922 Accommodation Supplement recipients and $699.2 million hardship assistance granted across the four quarters to March 2026; none of those public tables identify eligible non-recipients or their average foregone entitlement. [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf); [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets March 2026](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html)
- **FamilyBoost is the childcare-support exception with a public under-claim signal, but it is still not a clean unclaimed-entitlement estimate.** Inland Revenue says FamilyBoost paid $50.5 million to 60,300 households in 2024/25 and that households paid/claimed were lower than projected, while the 2025 regulatory impact statement says current-settings uptake was expected to be about 71%; those sources show material under-claiming, but also say the original eligible-family model included families with low or no fees who may receive little value from claiming. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [IRD/Tax Policy RIS, FamilyBoost](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/tp/publications/2025/ris-familyboost-tax-bill.pdf?modified=20251216030428)

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the WFF estimate combines two official public sources and uses a transparent calculation, but the key assumption about average entitlement among non-recipients is not publicly verified. Confidence is lower for all other supports because public sources do not publish both eligible non-recipient counts and foregone entitlement amounts.

## Evidence

### A defensible public method exists for main Working for Families, but it is still a model

MSD's WFF take-up study estimates that 278,200 families were eligible for the main WFF payments in the 2020 tax year and that 243,000 of those families received payments, leaving about 35,200 eligible families not observed receiving the main WFF credits. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

The same study defines the main WFF payments as Family Tax Credit and In-Work Tax Credit, says those credits accounted for 97-99% of WFF payments during the study period, and estimates potential entitlement by applying payment levels and abatement rules to linked survey and administrative data in the Stats NZ Integrated Data Infrastructure. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [SIA Hub WFF summary](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates-2007-2020)

Inland Revenue's 2019/20 annual report records $2.188954 billion of Family Tax Credit expenditure and $620.964 million of In-Work Tax Credit expenditure, or $2.809918 billion combined. [IRD Annual Report 2020](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2020.pdf?modified=20251019232054)

Using the official recipient/non-recipient counts and the official paid amount, the equal-average-entitlement estimate is:

`$2.809918b paid x (35,200 non-recipient eligible families / 243,000 recipient families) = $407m`.

That $407 million is not an official MSD or IRD estimate; it is a transparent public-data approximation that assumes the average foregone entitlement of eligible non-recipients equalled the average paid entitlement of recipients. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD Annual Report 2020](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2020.pdf?modified=20251019232054)

The same calculation on 2024/25 Inland Revenue expenditure gives a current-scale proxy, not a current take-up estimate: Inland Revenue records $2.434255 billion of Family Tax Credit and $550.040 million of In-Work Tax Credit expenditure in 2024/25, and applying the 2020 non-recipient/recipient ratio gives about $432 million. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

This estimate could be an overstatement if eligible non-recipients had smaller entitlements than recipients, because the WFF study notes take-up patterns differ by income, benefit status, family type, area deprivation, and age of youngest child, and it explicitly warns that estimates have limitations and sources of error. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

### Best Start has enough data for an order-of-magnitude estimate, but the likely value is much smaller

MSD estimated that Best Start was taken up for 96.9% of children in eligible families in its study cohort, with take-up of 99% for children in eligible families supported by a main benefit, 97% for those receiving paid parental leave and no benefit, and 94% for other eligible families. [SIA Hub/MSD Best Start take-up study](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit)

Budget 2026 reports $346 million of 2024/25 Best Start spending and forecasts $327 million in 2025/26, so applying the 3.1% non-take-up share to the paid 2024/25 amount gives an equal-average-entitlement estimate of about $11 million. [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf); [SIA Hub/MSD Best Start take-up study](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit)

The Best Start dollar estimate is weaker than the WFF estimate because the take-up study followed children born between 1 July 2018 and 30 September 2019, while the $346 million spending line is a later 2024/25 aggregate. [SIA Hub/MSD Best Start take-up study](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/sitemap/estimates-of-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit); [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf)

### Accommodation Supplement has a take-up-gap signal, but not a credible public unclaimed-dollar estimate

Budget 2026 reports $2.232 billion of 2024/25 Accommodation Assistance spending and forecasts $2.308 billion in 2025/26, while MSD's March 2026 Benefit Fact Sheets report 377,922 Accommodation Supplement recipients at March 2026. [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf); [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets March 2026](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html)

MSD's Income Support Survey found that 52.9% of working-age low-to-middle-income respondents appeared potentially eligible for Accommodation Supplement and that 43.9% of those potentially eligible respondents self-reported receiving it, but the survey is not child/family-specific and does not publish the average Accommodation Supplement entitlement of eligible non-recipients. [MSD Income Support Survey, Accommodation Supplement findings](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf)

Multiplying Accommodation Assistance spend by the survey's non-recipient/recipient ratio would produce a large number, but that would be a low-confidence scenario rather than a finding because non-recipients may have lower housing costs, lower maximum rates, different family composition, or survey-classification error that changes their average entitlement. [MSD Income Support Survey, Accommodation Supplement findings](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf); [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf)

### Childcare assistance and FamilyBoost should be treated separately

Budget 2026 reports $167 million of 2024/25 Childcare Assistance spending and forecasts $159 million in 2025/26, but the public Budget table does not identify eligible non-recipient families or foregone entitlement amounts for Childcare Subsidy, OSCAR Subsidy, Guaranteed Childcare Assistance Payment, Early Learning Payment, or Flexible Childcare Assistance. [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf); [Work and Income childcare support pages](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/childcare.html)

FamilyBoost has a stronger public under-claim signal because Inland Revenue's 2024/25 annual report says $50.5 million was paid to 60,300 households in the first year, that households paid and amounts claimed were lower than projected, and that outreach aimed to alert households still missing out. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138)

The FamilyBoost regulatory impact statement says Budget 2024 originally provided about $174 million annually for up to 100,000 eligible families, that about $50 million had been paid to 59,747 families across the three available quarterly claims, and that a more realistic eligible population was 113,000 with expected current-settings uptake around 71%. [IRD/Tax Policy RIS, FamilyBoost](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/tp/publications/2025/ris-familyboost-tax-bill.pdf?modified=20251216030428)

FamilyBoost should not be converted mechanically into a single unclaimed-dollar estimate because the RIS says the original model included families with low or no unsubsidised fees who may receive little or no rebate, and because Inland Revenue says families can claim credits up to four years after the quarter. [IRD/Tax Policy RIS, FamilyBoost](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/tp/publications/2025/ris-familyboost-tax-bill.pdf?modified=20251216030428); [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138)

### Hardship assistance has spend and grant counts, but "unclaimed entitlement" is not well-defined

Budget 2026 reports $755 million of 2024/25 Hardship Assistance spending and forecasts $816 million in 2025/26. [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf)

MSD's March 2026 Benefit Fact Sheets report $699.2 million of hardship assistance granted across the four quarters ending June 2025, September 2025, December 2025, and March 2026, including $416.2 million of Special Needs Grants, $265.0 million of Advances, and $18.0 million of Recoverable Assistance Payments. [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets March 2026](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html)

Those hardship tables measure grants and amounts paid, not all families who had an immediate or emergency need and did not apply, so they cannot estimate aggregate unclaimed hardship assistance without a separate public need/eligibility denominator. [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets March 2026](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html); [Work and Income Special Needs Grant](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/special-needs-grant.html)

## What would change this conclusion

- A refreshed MSD/IRD WFF take-up study with estimated foregone dollars, not only eligible and recipient counts, would replace the $407m/$432m approximation. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)
- A public administrative take-up model for Accommodation Supplement, including child/family households and average eligible non-recipient entitlement, would change the conclusion that AS cannot currently support a credible public unclaimed-dollar estimate. [MSD Income Support Survey, Accommodation Supplement findings](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf)
- A public MSD childcare-assistance eligibility denominator, by subsidy type and family circumstances, would change the conclusion for Childcare Assistance. [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf); [Work and Income childcare support pages](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/eligibility/children/childcare.html)
- A FamilyBoost annual take-up release after the four-year back-claim window, or a current IRD estimate of eligible households with positive claimable fees, would make FamilyBoost's unclaimed value much more defensible. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [IRD/Tax Policy RIS, FamilyBoost](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/tp/publications/2025/ris-familyboost-tax-bill.pdf?modified=20251216030428)
- I could not verify a public source that reports total aggregate "unclaimed child/family support" across IRD and MSD products, and I did not use personal data or request non-public administrative data. [Budget 2026 BEFU](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/befu2026/befu26.pdf); [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets March 2026](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html)
- A human with frontline MSD/IRD, Citizens Advice Bureau, budgeting-service, or ECE-provider experience should test whether the largest public-data gap is application awareness, eligibility misunderstanding, document burden, or churn; the public aggregate datasets do not resolve mechanism. [MSD Income Support Survey, Accommodation Supplement findings](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf); [IRD/Tax Policy RIS, FamilyBoost](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/tp/publications/2025/ris-familyboost-tax-bill.pdf?modified=20251216030428)

## Open follow-up questions

- Can MSD or IRD publish a WFF non-take-up value table by entitlement size, child age, benefit status, and ethnicity without compromising privacy?
- What is the aggregate unclaimed value of FamilyBoost among households with positive eligible ECE fees after allowing for the four-year back-claim period?
- Can the Income Support Survey or IDI support a child/family-specific Accommodation Supplement take-up and foregone-entitlement estimate?
- Which childcare-assistance products have enough administrative data for a public take-up estimate: Childcare Subsidy, OSCAR Subsidy, Guaranteed Childcare Assistance Payment, Early Learning Payment, or Flexible Childcare Assistance?
