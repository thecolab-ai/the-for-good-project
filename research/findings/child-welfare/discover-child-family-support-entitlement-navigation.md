# NZ families measurably miss child & family support they qualify for, and the system's split ownership is a documented cause

> **Stage note:** this is a **Discover-stage** report for issue #4. It frames the problem with verified NZ figures and proposes researchable follow-up questions. It is not a single-question Research finding, but it follows the research template so claims and confidence are auditable. Method: multi-angle web research; 24 sources fetched, 119 claims extracted, the 25 highest-value claims adversarially verified with 3 independent votes each (23 confirmed, 2 refuted and excluded).

## Executive answer

- NZ child/family support is split mainly between **IRD** (Working for Families incl. Best Start, FamilyBoost) and **MSD/Work and Income** (Childcare Subsidy, OSCAR, Accommodation Supplement, Temporary Additional Support) — and Working for Families is uniquely **paid by both agencies**, with benefit-only families forced to choose a payer and switch agencies when moving between benefit and work.
- Non-take-up is real and measurable: **83–90%** of eligible families took up Working for Families (2010–2020), but only **75–85% among working (non-beneficiary) families**; Treasury/WEAG modelling suggested up to **~100,000** additional people may have been eligible for the Accommodation Supplement (likely an over-estimate) and **~30,000–53,000** for Temporary Additional Support.
- **Awareness is a documented cause**, not a guess: ~41% of families not using the Childcare Subsidy didn't know it existed or didn't know if they were eligible (Stats NZ 2017 Childcare Survey, via WEAG); MSD's 2022 Income Support Survey independently confirmed awareness gaps for childcare assistance and the Accommodation Supplement.
- The counter-example that proves the mechanism: **Best Start achieved ~97% take-up** — near-universal even in the most deprived areas — because entitlement is broad and enrolment is tied to an existing touchpoint (birth registration / paid parental leave).
- Evidence on navigation channels (SmartStart, CAB, community orgs) and on current remediation initiatives survived verification only thinly — those are the highest-value follow-up Research issues.

**Overall confidence:** High — headline claims are drawn from primary government sources (IRD, MSD/Work and Income, WEAG/Treasury) and each survived a 3-vote adversarial verification; dated figures are flagged below.

## Evidence

### The entitlement landscape and agency ownership

| Entitlement | Owning agency | Eligibility in brief |
|---|---|---|
| Working for Families (incl. Best Start) | IRD (MSD pays it for beneficiary families) | Principal caregiver of a dependent child, aged 16+, residency; abates with family income [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families) |
| Best Start | IRD | Up to $77/wk from end of paid parental leave (or birth) until child turns 3; income-tested from birth for children born on/after 1 Apr 2026 (abates above ~$79,000) [govt.nz](https://www.govt.nz/browse/family-and-whanau/financial-help-for-your-family/apply-for-best-start-payments/), [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/best-start) |
| FamilyBoost (from 1 Jul 2024) | IRD | Up to 40% of licensed ECE fees, capped at $1,560/quarter; household income < $180k; child 5 or under; claimed quarterly via myIR [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost) |
| Childcare Subsidy / OSCAR | MSD / Work and Income | Income-tested; child generally under 5 (with cohort-entry and Child Disability Allowance exceptions); ≥3 hrs/wk at an approved ECE service; 9 hrs/wk default, up to 50 hrs when working/studying/training [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html) |
| Accommodation Supplement | MSD / Work and Income | Conjunction of tests: cash assets < $8,100 (single) / $16,200 (couple or sole parent), income cutoff, accommodation-cost floor, not in social housing; **no main benefit required** [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/accommodation-supplement.html), [check.msd.govt.nz](https://check.msd.govt.nz/services/accommodation-supplement) |
| Temporary Additional Support | MSD / Work and Income | Last-resort weekly hardship top-up [WEAG](http://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf) |

Two structural oddities verified at 3–0:

- **Working for Families is paid by two separate agencies.** IRD: "Both Inland Revenue and MSD (the Ministry of Social Development) pay Working for Families payments"; families whose only income is a main benefit **choose** between MSD and IRD, and IRD must pay once monthly family income exceeds $3,741 before tax or care is shared [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/all-about-working-for-families/msd), independently confirmed by [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/working-for-families.html). Families moving between benefit and work must switch payer.
- **Childcare help is split across agencies**: FamilyBoost at IRD (quarterly myIR claim), the income-tested Childcare Subsidy at MSD (Social Security (Childcare Assistance) Regulations 2004), plus 20 Hours ECE at the Ministry of Education. Even within MSD, the Accommodation Supplement application route forks by client status: people receiving no MSD payments apply online via MyMSD, while existing beneficiaries must complete a separate dedicated application form [check.msd.govt.nz](https://check.msd.govt.nz/services/accommodation-supplement) (verified live 2026-07-02).

### Scale of non-take-up

- **Working for Families: 83–90% take-up, 2010–2020** — "the estimated proportion has remained relatively stable from 2010 to 2020, with 83–90 percent taking up payments"; near-100% for beneficiary families vs **75–85% for families not supported by benefits** [MSD Families Package evaluation](https://msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/reports-on-the-income-support-system-payments-and-incomes.html) (underlying report: MSD, *Estimates of Working for Families eligibility and take-up rates 2007–2020*, Dec 2022, Stats NZ IDI-linked data).
- **Accommodation Supplement:** WEAG/Treasury TAWA microsimulation (2017 analysis): "Currently around 285,000 people receive AS… It is estimated that there may be up to another 100,000 people who may be eligible for AS who are not receiving it. This is likely to be a significant over-estimate…" (it ignores the cash-asset test) [WEAG background paper, p.10](http://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf). Independently, MSD's 2022 Income Support Survey (n=1,852) found **only ~44% of potentially eligible respondents were receiving it**, lowest among non-beneficiaries [MSD Income Support Survey](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/income-support-survey-main-findings.html).
- **Temporary Additional Support:** ~61,000 recipients (Jun 2018) vs an indicative **33,000–53,000 further people** possibly eligible but not receiving; "more recent analysis suggests… closer to around 30,000" [WEAG background paper, p.13](http://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).
- **Best Start: ~97% take-up** in its first 15 months (births Jul 2018–Sep 2019): 97% of newborns eligible, payment taken up for 97% of eligible children; 96–97% across all NZDep deprivation quintiles; 99% for benefit-supported families, 97% for PPL families, 94% for other eligible families [MSD](https://msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/reports-on-the-income-support-system-payments-and-incomes.html), [MSD Best Start take-up report (PDF)](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit.pdf).

### Awareness as a documented failure point

- Stats NZ 2017 Childcare Survey (via WEAG, Table 10): "of those who did not access the Childcare Subsidy, almost 41% either didn't know about the Childcare Subsidy or didn't know if they were eligible" [WEAG background paper](http://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).
- Children on the Childcare Subsidy fell from ~42,600 (Jun 2007) to ~25,200 (Jun 2018) — **causation caveat:** partly policy-driven (20 Hours ECE introduction 2007; 2010 income-threshold cuts/non-indexation), not pure non-take-up [WEAG background paper](http://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).
- MSD 2022 Income Support Survey: "Lack of awareness, and difficulty in finding appropriate childcare contributed to non-receipt of Childcare/Out of School Care and Recreation (OSCAR) Subsidies" [MSD](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/income-support-survey-main-findings.html).

### Navigation channels (partially answered — see gaps)

- MSD operates ["Check what you might get"](https://check.msd.govt.nz/), a ~5-minute guided eligibility checker covering entitlements including the Accommodation Supplement; verified live 2026-07-02. The application routes it points to differ by benefit status (see above), illustrating navigation forks even within one agency.
- Sources on SmartStart, Citizens Advice Bureau (2025 welfare spotlight report; digital-exclusion reports), Whānau Ora navigators, FamilyBoost uptake shortfalls, and IRD–MSD information sharing were fetched but their specific claims did not make the verified cut — **parts (3) and (4) of issue #4 are only partially answered here** and are the priority follow-ups.

## What would change this conclusion

- **Fresher take-up data.** The WFF series ends at tax year 2020 (no confidence intervals); AS/TAS gap estimates rest on 2017 Treasury TAWA modelling and June 2018 recipient counts (AS receipt has since risen to roughly 364,000 by 2024). A post-2020 MSD/IRD update could materially shrink or grow the headline gaps.
- **The AS ~100,000 figure is self-flagged as an over-estimate** (ignores the cash-asset test). If corrected modelling put the true gap far lower, the problem framing shifts from "scale" to "targeting".
- **Policy contamination in the Childcare Subsidy decline** — the 2007–2018 drop is partly 20 Hours ECE substitution and threshold freezes; treating it as pure non-take-up would overstate the problem.
- **Needs a human:** lived-experience input on why eligible working families don't claim (shame, debt fear — MSD's own WFF consultation raises overpayment-debt anxiety — churn, language, digital exclusion) cannot be answered from public statistics alone; CAB and community-org practitioners are the obvious interviewees.

## Open follow-up questions

Candidates for Research issues under #4 (not opened — repo owner to triage):

1. What is Working for Families take-up **post-2020**, and is an update to the 2007–2020 IDI-based estimates published or planned — especially after FamilyBoost (2024) and Best Start means-testing from birth (2026)?
2. What is **actual FamilyBoost take-up since July 2024**? Its quarterly opt-in myIR claim process looks structurally prone to the same 75–85% non-beneficiary gap; late-2024 reporting suggested fewer than half of eligible families were claiming (unverified — [1News, Dec 2024](https://www.1news.co.nz/2024/12/16/fewer-than-half-of-families-eligible-for-familyboost-get-payments/)).
3. Where do families **actually go for navigation help** (SmartStart, govt.nz, Citizens Advice Bureau, Whānau Ora navigators, community orgs), and is there evaluation evidence on which channels convert awareness into successful claims?
4. What **concrete initiatives exist to close the take-up gap** — IRD–MSD data sharing (AISA PAYE information-sharing amendments phased for Oct 2026 and Jul 2028), proactive/automatic enrolment pilots, NGO screening tools — and have any been evaluated for impact?
5. Why did **Best Start reach ~97% while AS/TAS languish** — which elements of tying enrolment to birth registration/PPL are transferable to other entitlements?
