# NZ families measurably miss child & family support they qualify for; awareness gaps are a documented cause, and split agency ownership may contribute to navigation breakdowns

> **Stage note:** this is a **Discover-stage** report for issue #4. It frames the problem with verified NZ figures and proposes researchable follow-up questions. It is not a single-question Research finding, but it follows the research template so claims and confidence are auditable. Method: multi-angle web research; 24 sources fetched, 119 claims extracted, the 25 highest-value claims adversarially verified with 3 independent votes each (23 confirmed, 2 refuted — one later narrowed to a partial refutation; see the refuted-claims note in the load-bearing section).
>
> **Revised 2026-07-02** after adversarial review on PR #27 (two rounds). Round 1: causal framing softened (split ownership is documented as a fact, not as a proven cause; Best Start is corroborating, not proof), single-origin sources flagged explicitly in the load-bearing table, and previously uncited claims either cited or removed. Round 2: WEAG citations repointed to an archived copy after the original URL began redirecting (all quoted figures re-verified against the archived PDF); FamilyBoost settings corrected to post-July-2025 values; the Best Start annual-confirmation "refutation" narrowed to its causal half — the annual-confirmation step itself is real.

## Executive answer

- NZ child/family support is split mainly between **IRD** (Working for Families incl. Best Start [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families), FamilyBoost [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost)) and **MSD/Work and Income** (Childcare Subsidy [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html), OSCAR, Accommodation Supplement [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/accommodation-supplement.html), Temporary Additional Support [WEAG](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf)) — and Working for Families is uniquely **paid by both agencies**, with benefit-only families choosing a payer and switching agencies when moving between benefit and work [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/all-about-working-for-families/msd), [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/working-for-families.html).
- Non-take-up is real and measurable: **83–90%** of eligible families took up Working for Families (2010–2020), but only **75–85% among working (non-beneficiary) families** [MSD Families Package evaluation](https://msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/reports-on-the-income-support-system-payments-and-incomes.html); Treasury/WEAG modelling suggested up to **~100,000** additional people may have been eligible for the Accommodation Supplement (self-flagged as likely an over-estimate) and **~30,000–53,000** for Temporary Additional Support [WEAG](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).
- **Awareness is a documented cause of non-take-up**: ~41% of families not using the Childcare Subsidy didn't know it existed or didn't know if they were eligible (Stats NZ 2017 Childcare Survey, via [WEAG](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf)); MSD's 2022 Income Support Survey independently found awareness gaps for childcare assistance and the Accommodation Supplement [MSD](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/income-support-survey-main-findings.html).
- A contrasting case is **consistent with — but does not prove — the navigation/awareness thesis**: Best Start reached ~97% take-up, near-universal even in the most deprived areas [MSD Best Start take-up report](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit.pdf). MSD's own language is cautious: high take-up "may be due to" near-universal eligibility and application ease, and was "probably enabled" by near-universality and SmartStart birth-registration prompts (same report).
- **What is documented vs. inferred:** split agency ownership is a documented *fact*, and awareness gaps are a documented *cause*; that split ownership itself causes missed entitlements is an *inference* this report does not claim as established — testing it is a priority follow-up. Evidence on navigation channels (SmartStart, CAB, community orgs) and current remediation initiatives survived verification only thinly — those are the highest-value follow-up Research issues.

**Overall confidence:** High for the headline that eligible families measurably miss support — it rests on several independent origins (MSD IDI-based take-up analysis, Treasury TAWA modelling via WEAG, the Stats NZ 2017 Childcare Survey, and MSD's 2022 Income Support Survey). Several *specific statistics* are single-origin and are flagged as such in the load-bearing table below; causal claims are limited to awareness.

## Evidence

### The entitlement landscape and agency ownership

| Entitlement | Owning agency | Eligibility in brief |
|---|---|---|
| Working for Families (incl. Best Start) | IRD (MSD pays it for beneficiary families) | Principal caregiver of a dependent child, aged 16+, residency; abates with family income [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families) |
| Best Start | IRD | Up to $77/wk from end of paid parental leave (or birth) until child turns 3; income-tested from birth for children born on/after 1 Apr 2026 (abates above ~$79,000) [govt.nz](https://www.govt.nz/browse/family-and-whanau/financial-help-for-your-family/apply-for-best-start-payments/), [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/best-start) |
| FamilyBoost (from 1 Jul 2024) | IRD | For quarters ending after 1 Jul 2025: up to 40% of licensed ECE fees, capped at $1,560/quarter, household income < $57,286/quarter (≈$229k/yr); before 1 Jul 2025 the settings were 25% / $975/quarter / income < $45,000/quarter (≈$180k/yr); child 5 or under; claimed quarterly via myIR [IRD eligibility](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/can-you-get-familyboost), [IRD payments](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost/how-much-familyboost-can-you-claim) |
| Childcare Subsidy / OSCAR | MSD / Work and Income | Income-tested; child generally under 5 (with cohort-entry and Child Disability Allowance exceptions); ≥3 hrs/wk at an approved ECE service; 9 hrs/wk default, up to 50 hrs when working/studying/training [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html) |
| Accommodation Supplement | MSD / Work and Income | Conjunction of tests: cash assets < $8,100 (single) / $16,200 (couple or sole parent), income cutoff, accommodation-cost floor, not in social housing; **no main benefit required** [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/accommodation-supplement.html), [check.msd.govt.nz](https://check.msd.govt.nz/services/accommodation-supplement) |
| Temporary Additional Support | MSD / Work and Income | Last-resort weekly hardship top-up [WEAG](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf) |

Two structural oddities verified at 3–0:

- **Working for Families is paid by two separate agencies.** IRD: "Both Inland Revenue and MSD (the Ministry of Social Development) pay Working for Families payments"; families whose only income is a main benefit **choose** between MSD and IRD, and IRD must pay once monthly family income exceeds $3,741 before tax or care is shared [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/all-about-working-for-families/msd), independently confirmed by [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/working-for-families.html). Families moving between benefit and work must switch payer.
- **Childcare help is split across agencies**: FamilyBoost at IRD (quarterly myIR claim) [IRD](https://www.ird.govt.nz/familyboost); the income-tested Childcare Subsidy at MSD [Work and Income](https://www.workandincome.govt.nz/products/a-z-benefits/childcare-subsidy.html) — its former legal basis, the Social Security (Childcare Assistance) Regulations 2004, was revoked on 26 November 2018 by the Social Security Regulations 2018 [legislation.govt.nz](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/regulation/public/2004/0268/latest/whole.html), which carried the childcare-assistance provisions forward [MSD mapping document](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/newsroom/mapping-the-social-security-regulations-2018.pdf); plus 20 Hours ECE at the Ministry of Education [Ministry of Education](https://www.education.govt.nz/early-childhood/running-an-ece-service/funding/20-hours-ece-for-ece-services). Even within MSD, the Accommodation Supplement application route forks by client status: people receiving no MSD payments apply online via MyMSD, while existing beneficiaries must complete a separate dedicated application form [check.msd.govt.nz](https://check.msd.govt.nz/services/accommodation-supplement) (verified live 2026-07-02).

### Scale of non-take-up

- **Working for Families: 83–90% take-up, 2010–2020** — "the estimated proportion has remained relatively stable from 2010 to 2020, with 83–90 percent taking up payments"; near-100% for beneficiary families vs **75–85% for families not supported by benefits** [MSD Families Package evaluation](https://msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/reports-on-the-income-support-system-payments-and-incomes.html) (underlying report: McLeod & Wilson, *Estimates of Working for Families eligibility and take-up rates 2007–2020*, MSD, Dec 2022, Stats NZ IDI-linked data [PDF](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf) — same origin as the summary page; see load-bearing table).
- **Accommodation Supplement:** WEAG/Treasury TAWA microsimulation (2017 analysis): "Currently around 285,000 people receive AS… It is estimated that there may be up to another 100,000 people who may be eligible for AS who are not receiving it. This is likely to be a significant over-estimate…" (it ignores the cash-asset test) [WEAG background paper, p.10](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf). Separately, MSD's 2022 Income Support Survey (n=1,852) found **only ~44% of potentially eligible respondents were receiving it**, lowest among non-beneficiaries [MSD Income Support Survey](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/income-support-survey-main-findings.html) — this corroborates the *direction* (substantial non-take-up) but not the ~100,000 estimate itself.
- **Temporary Additional Support:** ~61,000 recipients (Jun 2018) vs an indicative **33,000–53,000 further people** possibly eligible but not receiving; "more recent analysis suggests… closer to around 30,000" [WEAG background paper, p.13](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).
- **Best Start: ~97% take-up** in its first 15 months (births Jul 2018–Sep 2019): 97% of newborns eligible, payment taken up for 97% of eligible children; 96–97% across all NZDep deprivation quintiles; 99% for benefit-supported families, 97% for PPL families, 94% for other eligible families [MSD](https://msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/reports-on-the-income-support-system-payments-and-incomes.html), [MSD Best Start take-up report (PDF)](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit.pdf). The report's own causal language is cautious: high take-up "may be due to" near-universal eligibility and ease of application, and was "probably enabled" by near-universality and SmartStart birth-registration prompts — a plausible mechanism, not proof.

### Awareness as a documented failure point

- Stats NZ 2017 Childcare Survey (via WEAG, Table 10): "of those who did not access the Childcare Subsidy, almost 41% either didn't know about the Childcare Subsidy or didn't know if they were eligible" [WEAG background paper](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).
- Children on the Childcare Subsidy fell from ~42,600 (Jun 2007) to ~25,200 (Jun 2018) — **causation caveat:** partly policy-driven (20 Hours ECE introduction 2007; 2010 income-threshold cuts/non-indexation), not pure non-take-up [WEAG background paper](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).
- MSD 2022 Income Support Survey: "Lack of awareness, and difficulty in finding appropriate childcare contributed to non-receipt of Childcare/Out of School Care and Recreation (OSCAR) Subsidies" [MSD](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/income-support-survey-main-findings.html).

### Navigation channels (partially answered — see gaps)

- MSD operates ["Check what you might get"](https://check.msd.govt.nz/), a ~5-minute guided eligibility checker covering entitlements including the Accommodation Supplement; verified live 2026-07-02. The application routes it points to differ by benefit status (see above), illustrating navigation forks even within one agency.
- Sources on SmartStart, Citizens Advice Bureau (2025 welfare spotlight report; digital-exclusion reports), Whānau Ora navigators, FamilyBoost uptake shortfalls, and IRD–MSD information sharing were fetched but their specific claims did not make the verified cut — **parts (3) and (4) of issue #4 are only partially answered here** and are the priority follow-ups.

**Claims refuted or corrected during verification** (read carefully before reuse):

- **Partially refuted — the fact is true, the causal interpretation is not established.** Best Start *does* require annual confirmation: "You need to confirm your details for Best Start each year — it does not automatically keep going" [govt.nz](https://www.govt.nz/browse/family-and-whanau/financial-help-for-your-family/apply-for-best-start-payments/). That administrative step is real and safe to cite. What was struck (1–2 in verification) and remains **unverified** is the claim that this creates a *recurring drop-off point* — no take-up data by year of entitlement was found, and the ~97% figure above covers only the first 15 months. Do not reuse the drop-off claim without new evidence; do not mistake this note for the confirmation step being fictional.
- ~~IRD-administered WFF take-up is ~95–97%, attributable to IRD automation~~ — refuted 0–3 against the [WEAG paper](https://web.archive.org/web/20250123194938/https://www.weag.govt.nz/assets/documents/WEAG-report/background-documents/38f35441ff/Take-up-of-Income-Support-010419.pdf).

## What would change this conclusion

- **Fresher take-up data.** The WFF series ends at tax year 2020 (no confidence intervals); AS/TAS gap estimates rest on 2017 Treasury TAWA modelling and June 2018 recipient counts, while AS receipt has kept rising — up a further 18,600 people (5.1%) in the year to December 2024 alone [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets, December 2024 snapshot](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/2024/benefit-fact-sheets-snapshot-december-2024.pdf). A post-2020 MSD/IRD update could materially shrink or grow the headline gaps.
- **The AS ~100,000 figure is self-flagged as an over-estimate** (ignores the cash-asset test). If corrected modelling put the true gap far lower, the problem framing shifts from "scale" to "targeting".
- **Direct evidence on the split-ownership inference.** This report documents split ownership and documents awareness-driven non-take-up, but found no source establishing split ownership *itself* as a cause of missed entitlements. Evidence either way (e.g. take-up differences at the IRD–MSD hand-off points, or evaluation of the dual-payer arrangement) would firm up or kill the navigation thesis.
- **Policy contamination in the Childcare Subsidy decline** — the 2007–2018 drop is partly 20 Hours ECE substitution and threshold freezes; treating it as pure non-take-up would overstate the problem.
- **Needs a human:** lived-experience input on why eligible working families don't claim (shame; debt fear — officially acknowledged: MSD's 2026 information-sharing consultation is explicitly motivated by creating "less overpayment debt for clients" [MSD consultation](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/consultations/enabling-msd-to-more-quickly-use-paye-income-information-provided-by-inland-revenue.html); churn; language; digital exclusion) cannot be answered from public statistics alone; CAB and community-org practitioners are the obvious interviewees.

## Open follow-up questions

Candidates for Research issues under #4 (not opened — repo owner to triage):

1. What is Working for Families take-up **post-2020**, and is an update to the 2007–2020 IDI-based estimates published or planned — especially after FamilyBoost (2024) and Best Start means-testing from birth (2026)?
2. What is **actual FamilyBoost take-up since July 2024**? Its quarterly opt-in myIR claim process looks structurally prone to the same 75–85% non-beneficiary gap; late-2024 reporting suggested fewer than half of eligible families were claiming (unverified — [1News, Dec 2024](https://www.1news.co.nz/2024/12/16/fewer-than-half-of-families-eligible-for-familyboost-get-payments/)).
3. Where do families **actually go for navigation help** (SmartStart, govt.nz, Citizens Advice Bureau, Whānau Ora navigators, community orgs), and is there evaluation evidence on which channels convert awareness into successful claims?
4. What **concrete initiatives exist to close the take-up gap** — IRD–MSD data sharing (the proposed AISA amendment consulted on April–May 2026, with changes phased for October 2026 and July 2028 [MSD consultation](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/consultations/enabling-msd-to-more-quickly-use-paye-income-information-provided-by-inland-revenue.html), [IRD Tax Policy](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/news/2026/aisa-ir-msd-consultation)), proactive/automatic enrolment pilots, NGO screening tools — and have any been evaluated for impact?
5. **Does split agency ownership measurably cause missed entitlements?** Best Start reached ~97% while AS/TAS languish — which elements of tying enrolment to birth registration/PPL are transferable, and is there direct evidence (not inference) that agency hand-offs drive drop-off? Relatedly: the ~97% figure covers only the first 15 months, and Best Start requires annual re-confirmation ("it does not automatically keep going" [govt.nz](https://www.govt.nz/browse/family-and-whanau/financial-help-for-your-family/apply-for-best-start-payments/)) — does take-up decay in entitlement years 2–3?
