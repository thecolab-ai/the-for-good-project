# Public IRD/MSD data puts unclaimed main Working for Families entitlement at roughly a few hundred million dollars a year

## Executive answer

- **Headline estimate:** the public-data order of magnitude for eligible families' unclaimed main Working for Families (WFF) entitlement is **about $200m-$400m a year**, not a precise official number. The upper scale check is about **$407m in the 2020 tax year** if eligible non-recipient families had the same average Family Tax Credit (FTC) and In-Work Tax Credit (IWTC) entitlement as recipient families; a more cautious planning range is about **$200m-$300m** if non-recipient families averaged 50%-75% of recipient entitlement. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD Annual Report 2020](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2020.pdf?modified=20251019232054)
- **Current-scale check:** applying the same 2020 non-recipient/recipient ratio to later IRD expenditure gives about **$393m using 2024 tax-year FTC/IWTC WFF statistics**, or about **$432m using IRD's 2024/25 annual-report FTC/IWTC expenses**; these are scale checks, not refreshed take-up estimates. [IRD WFF statistics dataset page](https://www.ird.govt.nz/about-us/tax-statistics/working-for-families-statistics/working-for-families-statistics-datasets); [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)
- **Best Start is smaller on public data:** MSD estimated 96.9% Best Start take-up for the studied birth cohort, so applying an equal-average non-recipient/recipient ratio to IRD's $425.867m 2024/25 Best Start expense gives about **$14m**. [MSD Best Start take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit.pdf); [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138)
- **Why not "missed families x average" as the answer:** that calculation assumes non-recipient eligible families look like recipient families, but MSD says take-up is close to 100% for eligible families with main-benefit income and 75%-85% for eligible families without main-benefit income, while IRD says WFF entitlement abates as family income rises. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD Family Tax Credit](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/family-tax-credit)
- **Other child/family payments remain outside the defensible public dollar estimate:** public MSD/IRD data shows spending and recipient scale for products such as accommodation, childcare, hardship assistance, and FamilyBoost, but I did not find an official public table with both eligible non-recipient counts and average foregone entitlement for those products. [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html); [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138)

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the WFF anchor uses two official sources and simple arithmetic, but the decisive lower-bound adjustment depends on an explicit judgement about non-recipient average entitlement that no public official table verifies.

## Evidence

### Method and arithmetic

This finding answers a narrow question: what annual dollar value is suggested by public aggregate official data for eligible NZ families who do not take up child/family entitlements, especially WFF; it does not claim to be an official IRD/MSD estimate. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD WFF statistics dataset page](https://www.ird.govt.nz/about-us/tax-statistics/working-for-families-statistics/working-for-families-statistics-datasets)

MSD's WFF take-up study estimated 278,200 eligible families in the 2020 tax year and 243,000 recipient families, so the public aggregate non-recipient count is about 35,200 eligible families and the non-recipient/recipient ratio is 35,200 / 243,000 = 0.1449. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

The same MSD study says it focuses on the main WFF tax credits, FTC and IWTC, and says Best Start is covered by a separate study, so the like-for-like dollar base should not use the all-WFF total that includes Best Start. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD WFF technical information](https://www.ird.govt.nz/about-us/tax-statistics/working-for-families-statistics/working-for-families-statistics-technical-information)

Inland Revenue's 2019/20 annual report records $2.188954b of FTC expense and $620.964m of IWTC expense, or $2.809918b combined, so the equal-average estimate is `$2.809918b x 0.1449 = $407m`. [IRD Annual Report 2020](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2020.pdf?modified=20251019232054); [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

The IRD WFF statistics workbook downloaded from the official dataset page records 2024 tax-year aggregate entitlements of $2.273b for FTC and $437m for IWTC, or $2.710b combined, so applying the 2020 non-recipient/recipient ratio gives `$2.710b x 0.1449 = $393m`. [IRD WFF statistics dataset page](https://www.ird.govt.nz/about-us/tax-statistics/working-for-families-statistics/working-for-families-statistics-datasets); [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

Inland Revenue's 2024/25 annual report records $2.434255b of FTC expense and $550.040m of IWTC expense, or $2.984295b combined, so applying the same 2020 ratio gives `$2.984295b x 0.1449 = $432m`. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

These calculations describe entitlement-period non-take-up, not an in-year cash delay, because MSD's methodology assigns WFF income to the period in which the entitlement fell regardless of when the payment was made. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

### Why the equal-average calculation probably overstates the foregone dollars

The equal-average calculation is a useful upper scale check, but it is not a best estimate because MSD says take-up was close to 100% for eligible families with main-benefit income and 75%-85% for eligible families without main-benefit income. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

The likely direction of bias is upward because MSD says eligibility and take-up differences reflect differences in potential entitlement size and parental income, while IRD says FTC entitlement depends on family income and is reduced by abatement when income is over $44,900. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD Family Tax Credit](https://www.ird.govt.nz/working-for-families/types/family-tax-credit)

I therefore treat 50%-75% of recipient average entitlement as a transparent planning assumption, not an official finding: under that assumption the 2020 FTC/IWTC estimate is about $204m-$305m, the 2024 tax-year proxy is about $196m-$294m, and the 2024/25 expenditure proxy is about $216m-$324m. [IRD Annual Report 2020](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2020.pdf?modified=20251019232054); [IRD WFF statistics dataset page](https://www.ird.govt.nz/about-us/tax-statistics/working-for-families-statistics/working-for-families-statistics-datasets); [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138)

### Best Start and other child/family entitlements

MSD's Best Start study estimated 96.9% take-up for children in eligible families born in the first 15 months of Best Start, with 99% take-up for children in eligible families supported by a main benefit and 94% for children in other eligible families. [MSD Best Start take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit.pdf)

Inland Revenue's 2024/25 annual report records $425.867m of Best Start expense, so the same equal-average non-recipient/recipient method gives about `$425.867m x (3.1 / 96.9) = $14m`; this estimate is weaker than the main WFF calculation because the take-up cohort is July 2018 to September 2019 births, not a 2024/25 population. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [MSD Best Start take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-take-up-of-the-best-start-tax-credit.pdf)

FamilyBoost has public under-claim signals, but I did not include it in the headline because IRD's 2024/25 annual report says paid households and amounts claimed were lower than projected, while the official public sources I checked do not provide a clean eligible-positive-entitlement denominator after fees, subsidies, and back-claim timing. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [Tax Policy RIS: FamilyBoost](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/tp/publications/2025/ris-familyboost-tax-bill.pdf?modified=20251216030428)

Accommodation Supplement, MSD childcare assistance, and hardship assistance are not dollarised here because the public official data I found reports spending, recipients, grants, or survey potential eligibility, not the combination needed here: eligible child/family non-recipient counts and their average foregone entitlement. [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html); [MSD Income Support Survey: Accommodation Supplement](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A refreshed official MSD/IRD take-up release for WFF after the 2020 tax year, especially one that reports foregone dollars by entitlement size, would replace the 2020-ratio scale checks. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)
- A public table of average potential entitlement among eligible WFF non-recipients would replace the 50%-75% planning assumption and could move the answer below $200m or above $400m. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)
- A public official FamilyBoost take-up estimate for households with positive eligible fees, after allowing for the four-year claim window, could add another entitlement line to the estimate. [IRD Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf?modified=20251029230138); [Tax Policy RIS: FamilyBoost](https://www.taxpolicy.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/tp/publications/2025/ris-familyboost-tax-bill.pdf?modified=20251216030428)
- A child/family-specific Accommodation Supplement, childcare-assistance, or hardship-assistance take-up model with average foregone entitlement would change the conclusion that WFF is the only publicly dollariseable main line. [MSD Benefit Fact Sheets](https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/index.html); [MSD Income Support Survey: Accommodation Supplement](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/work-programmes/income-support-survey/findings-pack-7-accommodation-supplement-self-reported-take-up-and-other-findings.pdf)
- I could not verify an official public aggregate estimate of total unclaimed child/family entitlement across IRD and MSD products, and I did not use private IDI or personal administrative data. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf); [IRD WFF statistics dataset page](https://www.ird.govt.nz/about-us/tax-statistics/working-for-families-statistics/working-for-families-statistics-datasets)
- A human with IRD/MSD policy or service-delivery authority should test whether the useful intervention target is awareness, eligibility calculation, re-application prompts, documentation burden, language/accessibility, or trust; public aggregate dollar data does not identify the mechanism. [MSD WFF take-up study](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/evaluation/families-package-reports/estimating-changing-working-for-families-eligibility-and-take-up-rates.pdf)

## Open follow-up questions

- Can IRD/MSD publish a privacy-preserving WFF non-take-up value table by potential entitlement band, benefit status, family type, and child age?
- What is FamilyBoost's unclaimed value among households with positive eligible ECE fees after the back-claim window is mature?
- Can MSD's Income Support Survey or IDI analysis produce a child/family-specific Accommodation Supplement foregone-entitlement estimate?
