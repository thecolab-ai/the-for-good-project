# Wakari Ward 10A: claims vs confirmed — what the Ombudsman found and recommended, what Health NZ and the Ministry of Health have committed to versus done, and the 2014 → 2026 warning trail

This is research child **#234** of Stream **#224**. The discover baseline ([`mh-inpatient-inspections-baseline.md`](mh-inpatient-inspections-baseline.md)) verified the starting facts; this finding does the deep dive the issue asks for: a dated **claims-vs-confirmed** table for each 2026 finding, recommendation, and agency commitment, plus the full warning trail. It deliberately treats a party's statement about itself as a *claim*, not confirmation (docs/METHOD.md).

**Sensitive-case note.** Ward 10A holds a very small number of intellectually disabled adults under compulsory care; several sources describe individual patients' circumstances. Nothing in this finding identifies any patient or staff member, and none should be added. A human with clinical and disability-rights authority should review before any of this is treated as settled (Constitution Art. III).

## Executive answer

- **The findings are real and primary-sourced, but the evidence base is a media release, not the report.** The Chief Ombudsman, John Allen, inspected Ward 10A at Wakari Hospital, Dunedin — a secure forensic inpatient unit for adults with intellectual disabilities — under the Crimes of Torture Act 1989 (NZ's OPCAT mechanism), **17–20 March 2026**, and reported findings on **3 July 2026** [Ombudsman media release, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [Scoop mirror of full release](https://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/GE2607/S00008/failures-at-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-highlight-need-for-strengthened-commitment-from-health-agencies.htm). **The full inspection report is not published:** an Ombudsman resources search for "Wakari" as of 3 July 2026 returns only the 2022 Ward 10A report and 2024 follow-ups for Wards 9A/9B — no 2026 report [Ombudsman resources search "Wakari"](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?search=Wakari). Findings beyond the release wording cannot yet be checked against the primary document.
- **There is essentially one recommendation, and its terms of reference do not yet exist.** The Ombudsman "called on agencies to act immediately" and recommended **an independent investigation into the treatment and conditions of people at Ward 10A**; the Ministry of Health accepted it [Ombudsman release](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need). No terms of reference are published; the Ministry and district inspector are still "considering legal options for further investigation," which means the investigation is accepted in principle but **not yet scoped** [1News, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.1news.co.nz/2026/07/03/wakari-hospital-ward-closed-after-ombudsman-disturbed-by-findings/); [RNZ, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman).
- **Almost every agency response is a self-reported claim, and most are days old.** Health NZ says it ceased admissions on **24 April 2026**, began relocating patients in **May 2026**, and its **board decided on 3 July 2026** to close Ward 10A as a forensic intellectual-disability unit (future use undecided) [ODT "HNZ confirms 10A", 3 Jul 2026](https://odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/health/hnz-confirms-10a-allegations); [1News](https://www.1news.co.nz/2026/07/03/wakari-hospital-ward-closed-after-ombudsman-disturbed-by-findings/). These are Health NZ's statements about its own conduct, reported the same day as the findings — **claims, not independently confirmed outcomes.** No patient has been publicly confirmed relocated; no closure completion date exists.
- **The warning trail is long and documented.** A 2014 predecessor report already flagged the ward; the **May 2021** inspection (published **2 February 2022**) found it "not fit for purpose and in critical need of upgrade and redevelopment," made **15 recommendations**, and recorded staff calling their own facility "a disgrace" [Ombudsman 2021 report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); [1News, 2 Feb 2022](https://www.1news.co.nz/2022/02/02/chief-ombudsman-scathing-of-environment-at-wakari-hospital-ward/). In 2026 Allen recorded "next to no improvement to the physical structure of Ward 10A despite ongoing concerns" [ODT, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/horror-conditions-wakari-ward-some-worst-we-have-observed). A **district inspector is investigating two complaints** about care in the ward [1News](https://www.1news.co.nz/2026/07/03/wakari-hospital-ward-closed-after-ombudsman-disturbed-by-findings/); [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman). **No public HDC or coroner reference to Ward 10A was found** — see "what would change this."
- **Net:** the *findings* are trustworthy (a National Preventive Mechanism, primary-sourced, echoed by RNZ/ODT/1News). The *remedies* are, as of 3 July 2026, a set of fresh commitments — a closure decision made that day, an accepted-but-unscoped investigation, an unpublished report, and an unstarted national review. Nothing on the remedy side is yet confirmed by observation. **This is the honest state of the file.**

**Overall confidence:** High — not that the ward is fixed (it plainly is not), but that the claims-vs-confirmed split below is accurate: findings are primary-sourced and load-bearing agency actions are double-sourced but self-reported and unverified as outcomes. The one-day-old timeline is the reason so much lands in the "claim" column.

### B. The recommendation, and why "accepted" ≠ "underway"

The Ombudsman's operative recommendation is singular and clear: **an independent investigation into the treatment and conditions of people at Ward 10A**, with agencies to "act immediately" [Ombudsman](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need). The Ministry of Health **accepted** it [Ombudsman](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman).

But acceptance is not commencement. As of 3 July 2026, **"the district inspector and the Ministry are considering legal options for further investigation to ensure the concerns are fully examined"** [1News](https://www.1news.co.nz/2026/07/03/wakari-hospital-ward-closed-after-ombudsman-disturbed-by-findings/) — i.e. the mechanism, powers, and scope are still being worked out. **No terms of reference for the independent investigation are published**, and none was found in any source. The Ombudsman says he "will monitor" both agencies' progress [Ombudsman](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need) — which is itself an acknowledgement that the remedy is prospective, not delivered.

## What would change this conclusion

- **Publication of the full 2026 inspection report** would let F1–F7 be checked against the primary document (numbers affected, specific recommendations, the Ombudsman's own confidence). Until then, everything beyond the release wording is release-level detail.
- **Published terms of reference for the independent investigation**, naming who conducts it, its powers, scope and reporting line, would move that row from "accepted, unscoped" toward "underway" — and would let us judge its independence.
- **Independent confirmation of the operational claims** — e.g. an Ombudsman monitoring update, an OIA response, or Health NZ board-minute release confirming admissions ceased 24 April, patients relocated (and to where), and the ward physically closed — would move those rows from "claim" to "confirmed." A follow-up finding after the promised monitoring is the natural next step.
- **Any HDC or coronial material.** I found **no public HDC decision or coronial finding referencing Ward 10A**. That is an absence-of-evidence result, not evidence of absence: HDC complaints and any coronial matters are often not public, and a targeted HDC-decisions and Coronial-findings search (and possibly an OIA) is needed before stating either way. A human with the relevant access should check.
- **The "may have extended to unlawful administration of medication" finding (F6)** is hedged even by the Ombudsman; the full report or the district-inspector investigation could either firm it up or narrow it.
- **Lived-experience and rights authority.** Whether these practices meet the legal threshold of torture, or breach the Intellectual Disability (Compulsory Care and Rehabilitation) Act 2003 / NZ Bill of Rights, is a legal judgement this finding does not make. A person with disability-rights and clinical authority should weigh in (Constitution Art. III).
