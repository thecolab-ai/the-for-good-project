# NZ digital-service duplication is clearest in shared building blocks, but the current GDDA model still needs enforceable funding and procurement levers

## Executive answer

- The best-supported duplication zones are not every agency's specialist systems; they are repeatable "building blocks" such as identity and credentials, payments, notifications, appointment/case tracking, data exchange, AI tooling, cloud/infrastructure sourcing, forms, and back-office processes [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf); [Sir Brian Roche keynote, 2026](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/strategy/insights-and-commentary/sir-brian-roche-keynote-2026); [digital.govt.nz Products and services A-Z](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z).
- The PSC review gives concrete but anonymised examples: one large agency pursued its own payments system rather than reusing a mature system; multiple agencies pursued digital-identity initiatives with limited coordination; two related agencies tendered independently for service-delivery modules before the process was stopped; and a single-agency AI/cloud demand was too small to justify NZ-based vendor investment [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).
- New Zealand already has common-capability machinery that should reduce duplication, including RealMe, Confirmation/Retrieval identity services, FormBuilder.govt, IaaS/TaaS, Marketplace, cloud framework agreements, data.govt.nz, and the AoG Common Process Model [digital.govt.nz Products and services A-Z](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z); [Buying products and services](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/buying-products-and-services); [RealMe developers](https://developers.realme.govt.nz/how-realme-works).
- A coordinated model would consolidate strategy, architecture, assurance, procurement panels/contracts, reference architectures, shared data-exchange and identity patterns, and a small number of platform products; it should not centralise every agency delivery project or every domain-specific case-management workflow [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf); [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf).
- The GDDA is structurally closer to the problem than the old GCDO because it is now inside the PSC and paired with a Ministerial/GCDO mandate, but the PSC review says the inherited function had only "paper mandates" and limited influence over funding, design, and procurement; the model is therefore plausible only if Treasury/GDDA concurrence, ministerial vetoes, cluster decisions, and procurement panels are actually enforced [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf); [GDDA page](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/leadership/government-digital-delivery-agency); [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf).

**Overall confidence:** Medium -- high for the existence of the PSC diagnosis, common-capability catalogue, and GDDA structure; medium for the map of duplication categories because the strongest examples are anonymised and public sources do not expose agency-by-agency reuse decisions [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf); [digital.govt.nz Products and services A-Z](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z); [GDDA page](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/leadership/government-digital-delivery-agency).

## Evidence

### What is duplicated, and what is only potentially duplicated

The PSC Digital Reset Plan says Cabinet acted after concern about waste, duplication, and poor technology-project delivery, and the review found system issues including poor prioritisation, duplication, and projects falling between unclear agency and system accountabilities [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf). Its most specific examples are anonymised rather than named: a large agency chose to build a payments system instead of reusing mature systems operated by another large agency; multiple agencies pursued digital-identity initiatives with limited coordination; two related agencies tendered independently for service-delivery modules without integration or wider all-of-government planning; and one agency's AI tooling need was too small to get NZ-based data-centre investment from the vendor [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).

The official GDDA keynote published in June 2026 describes the same target surface as common building blocks: identity and credentials, payments, messaging and notifications, appointment and case tracking, and automatic information sharing [Sir Brian Roche keynote, 2026](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/strategy/insights-and-commentary/sir-brian-roche-keynote-2026). I treat that speech as an official statement of the reform thesis, not as independent proof that each building block is currently duplicated at material scale [Sir Brian Roche keynote, 2026](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/strategy/insights-and-commentary/sir-brian-roche-keynote-2026).

Identity is a high-confidence overlap zone because RealMe is already described as a government common capability for central government, local government, and private-sector organisations, while the PSC review separately says multiple agencies were pursuing digital-identity initiatives with limited coordination [RealMe developers](https://developers.realme.govt.nz/how-realme-works); [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf). The Products and Services catalogue also lists Confirmation Service, which checks identity information against official databases, and Retrieval Service, which lets authorised agencies submit identity information electronically for matching against official databases [digital.govt.nz Products and services A-Z](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z).

Payments are a medium-confidence duplication zone because the PSC review gives a clear payments example, but does not name the agencies or quantify the duplicate spend [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf). The June 2026 GDDA keynote includes payments in the common-building-block list, which corroborates that payments are in reform scope, but it does not prove the size of the overlap [Sir Brian Roche keynote, 2026](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/strategy/insights-and-commentary/sir-brian-roche-keynote-2026).

Case management and service-delivery modules are a medium-confidence overlap zone because the PSC review says two related agencies tendered separately for new service-delivery modules before that process was stopped, and FormBuilder.govt offers a fully funded cloud-based case-management portal for reviewing submissions and approving or declining them [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf); [FormBuilder.govt](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/formbuilder-govt). This does not mean a single case-management product should replace specialist systems such as courts, immigration, tax, or welfare administration; it means reusable intake, forms, status, notification, workflow, and approval patterns should be tested before agencies procure another bespoke service module [FormBuilder.govt](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/formbuilder-govt); [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).

Cloud hosting and procurement are high-confidence consolidation zones because New Zealand already has IaaS, public-cloud framework agreements, Marketplace, and the C3 replacement programme for infrastructure, telecommunications, and managed security services [Infrastructure as a Service](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/infrastructure-as-a-service); [Optional public-cloud agreements](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/cloud-services/adopt-public-cloud-services/ways-to-buy/optional-agreements-and-contracts); [Marketplace](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/marketplace); [C3 Programme](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/key-areas-of-work/c3-common-capabilities-and-cloud-programme). The key distinction is that common cloud buying consolidates commercial terms, assurance artefacts, catalogues, and data-residency/security patterns, while agencies still need local risk assessments for their own information systems [Optional public-cloud agreements](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/cloud-services/adopt-public-cloud-services/ways-to-buy/optional-agreements-and-contracts).

Data platforms are a medium-confidence overlap zone because the PSC review identifies trusted data exchange as one of a few foundational common-capability projects, and the April 2026 PSC BIM says a draft data-exchange architecture was due for consultation by the end of June 2026 with a data access policy later in 2026 [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf); [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf). Open-data discovery is already centralised through data.govt.nz, but that is not the same as operational consented data exchange between agencies [digital.govt.nz Products and services A-Z](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z); [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf).

Back-office processes are a high-confidence consolidation zone because the AoG Common Process Model provides reusable cloud-based business processes for finance, HR, payroll, enterprise asset management, IT service management, procurement, and work health and safety [NZ AoG Common Process Model](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/common-process-model). The April 2026 PSC BIM also says an all-of-government procurement panel for HR and payroll systems was being launched to reduce duplicated legal, procurement, configuration, and related costs over the next 10 years [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf).

### What a coordinated model would consolidate

The common-platform model should consolidate repeatable rails and decision rights, not every delivery team, because the PSC review recommends narrowing GDDA to strategy, standards, architecture, assurance, strategic procurement, and system capability while devolving project delivery and ongoing operations to lead agencies [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf). The same plan recommends identifying a small number of high-impact common projects such as digital identity, data exchange, and AI capability, nominating a single accountable official, and enforcing compliance through funding/resource consequences if projects are not submitted to the right governance committee [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).

The April 2026 PSC BIM describes the Target State as a more centralised approach to digital investment, procurement, infrastructure, and delivery, with agencies required to align to an architecture in which systems and platforms are shared when that makes sense [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf). It says agency digital clusters are core to the Target State, that clusters let related agencies use shared platforms rather than each having their own, and that secure identity, data flows, AI-first delivery, a common payment platform, and data exchange underpin the model [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf).

The overseas comparators support the shape but also warn against overclaiming: the UK has common platforms such as GOV.UK Forms, GOV.UK Notify, and GOV.UK One Login, but GOV.UK One Login still says it works with only some services and will replace other sign-in routes over time [GOV.UK Service Manual](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/technology/using-common-components); [GOV.UK One Login](https://www.gov.uk/using-your-gov-uk-one-login). Australia separates myGov as a service access account from Digital ID/myID as identity proofing, and myGov says myID can be connected as a sign-in option rather than being identical to myGov [myGov Digital ID help](https://my.gov.au/en/about/help/digital-id); [Digital ID System](https://www.digitalidsystem.gov.au/). The lesson for New Zealand is that shared platforms can consolidate common capabilities, but identity, service access, delivery workflow, and domain systems remain distinct layers [GOV.UK One Login](https://www.gov.uk/using-your-gov-uk-one-login); [myGov Digital ID help](https://my.gov.au/en/about/help/digital-id).

### Is the GDDA structured to fix duplication?

The GDDA is now formally closer to centre-of-government power than the previous GCDO arrangement because digital.govt.nz says the GDDA was established on 1 April 2026, is part of the PSC, and takes on GCDO functions [GDDA page](https://www.digital.govt.nz/digital-government/leadership/government-digital-delivery-agency). The April 2026 PSC BIM says the Commissioner/GDDA will support the Minister for Digitising Government and that the Minister, with the Minister of Finance, has power to veto government ICT investment proposals that are inconsistent with the Target State and digital government investment and procurement advice [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf).

The problem is that the PSC review describes the inherited centre as underpowered: it says GDDA had fuzzy boundaries, "paper mandates", and very limited influence or control over funding, design, and procurement decisions [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf). The same review says large agencies often routed around GDDA, current funding approval processes could drive agencies toward larger business cases, and confusion remained about what qualifies as core or common technology capability and who should lead delivery [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).

My assessment is therefore conditional: the GDDA structure can fix duplication where duplication sits in procurement, standards, architecture, platform selection, and foundational rails, but only if GDDA/Treasury concurrence and ministerial vetoes are used before agencies commit to separate builds [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf); [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf). It is less likely to fix duplication through the mere existence of a central agency, because New Zealand already had common capabilities and a GCDO mandate while the PSC still found fragmentation, duplication, bypassing, and unclear accountability [Buying products and services](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/buying-products-and-services); [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).

## What would change this conclusion

- A published list of the PSC review's anonymised examples, or a later GDDA/Treasury investment pipeline showing which payments, identity, case-management, data-exchange, AI, and cloud projects were stopped, merged, or exempted, would raise confidence and allow agency-level attribution.
- Evidence that agencies are routinely granted exemptions from common capabilities, or that common capabilities are unsuitable because of performance, privacy, sovereignty, Treaty, accessibility, or domain-law constraints, would shift more cases from "duplication" to "appropriate federation".
- A public benefits-realisation report showing actual savings, decommissioned systems, shared contracts, reduced procurement effort, or lower run costs would strengthen the reform case; without that, the $3.9b/five-year and almost-$6b/ten-year savings remain forecasts or reported government claims [PSC BIM, Apr 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Briefings-to-the-Incoming-Minister-for-Public-Service-and-Digitising-Government-April-2026.pdf); [Issue #255 framing](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/analysis/govt-digital-spend-effectiveness-framing.md).
- I could not verify the exact agencies behind the PSC review's payments, identity, service-module, or AI examples because the review anonymises them [PSC Digital Reset Plan, 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).
- I did not verify agency-by-agency adoption rates for RealMe, FormBuilder.govt, IaaS/TaaS, Marketplace, data-exchange patterns, or the Common Process Model; that would require procurement, investment-intention, and service-usage data that is not in the public catalogues cited here [RealMe developers](https://developers.realme.govt.nz/how-realme-works); [FormBuilder.govt](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/formbuilder-govt); [Infrastructure as a Service](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/infrastructure-as-a-service); [Marketplace](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/marketplace); [NZ AoG Common Process Model](https://www.digital.govt.nz/products-and-services/products-and-services-a-z/common-process-model).
- I did not contact agencies, vendors, Māori data-governance experts, disabled users, privacy specialists, or frontline service teams; human/domain review is needed before treating any specific shared-platform mandate as operationally or ethically appropriate.

## Open follow-up questions

- Which agencies have active or planned digital-identity, credential, biometric, or wallet projects, and how many are required to use RealMe, the Digital Identity Services Trust Framework, or the Government App wallet?
- Which public-sector payment systems are mature enough to be reused, and what legal, accounting, privacy, or operating-model barriers prevent reuse by other agencies?
- What agency digital clusters have been approved since April 2026, and what systems have they actually consolidated or stopped?
- What share of cloud, managed security, telecommunications, SaaS, HR/payroll, and back-office spend has moved through AoG agreements or Marketplace versus direct agency procurement?
- Can GDDA/Treasury publish a de-duplication register showing avoided procurements, merged platforms, exemptions, and realised savings without exposing sensitive security or commercial information?
