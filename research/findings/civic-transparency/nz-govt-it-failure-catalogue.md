# Major NZ government IT failures show recurring governance, assurance, and optimism-bias patterns

## Executive answer

- I found four central-government IT failures with public, source-pinned costs: Police INCIS, Education Novopay, DIA Te Ara Manaaki's Civil Registration replacement, and MBIE/Immigration New Zealand's Biometric Capability Update (BCU). The anchored public costs are about $82.5m net after the IBM settlement for INCIS, $56.8m total cost to date for Novopay at the 2013 inquiry point, $22.915m impaired for Te Ara Manaaki/Civil Registration, and about $35m for BCU plus $8.5m for the related IPE project [Beehive, 1999](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/settlement-cuts-incis-cost-825-million); [Beehive, 2013](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/ministerial-inquiry-report-novopay-released); [DIA annual report 2023/24](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2023-24-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2023-2024-Internal-Affairs-Annual-Report.pdf); [MBIE independent review, 2026](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/32155-review-of-the-biometric-projects-biometric-capability-update-bccu-and-ipe-projects).
- The recurring causes are not "bad software" alone. Independent/OAG reviews repeatedly point to weak governance, unrealistic or insufficiently evidenced business cases, weak or ignored quality assurance, unclear requirements, excessive customisation or solution misfit, poor ministerial reporting, and late failure to stop [OAG, 2000](https://oag.parliament.nz/2000/it-oversight/docs/part3.pdf); [Beehive, 2013](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/ministerial-inquiry-report-novopay-released); [MBIE independent review, 2026](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/32155-review-of-the-biometric-projects-biometric-capability-update-bccu-and-ipe-projects).
- The public cost picture is inconsistent across cases. INCIS has settlement-adjusted and gross cost figures, Novopay has a "total cost to date" at inquiry release and later remediation/settlement costs, DIA has an impairment/write-off rather than total programme spend, and BCU has current estimates plus a reviewer warning that earlier records suggest BCU alone may have approached $40m [Beehive, 1999](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/settlement-cuts-incis-cost-825-million); [Beehive, 2013](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/ministerial-inquiry-report-novopay-released); [Beehive, 2014](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/government-owned-company-take-over-school-payroll); [DIA annual report 2023/24](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2023-24-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2023-2024-Internal-Affairs-Annual-Report.pdf); [MBIE independent review, 2026](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/32155-review-of-the-biometric-projects-biometric-capability-update-bccu-and-ipe-projects).
- I did not include other likely leads, such as the National Library NDIS project or Oranga Tamariki's recent IT reset, because I could not pin a comparable public cost from an official or clearly attributable source within this pass; OAG names the National Library and INCIS as examples of public-sector IT difficulty, and RNZ reports the Oranga Tamariki reset but says costs were treated as commercially sensitive [OAG, 2000](https://oag.parliament.nz/2000/1st-report-2000/docs/part4.pdf); [RNZ, 2024](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/513360/internal-affairs-terminates-birth-death-and-marriage-registrations-it-project).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the included cases and broad causes are strongly supported, but this is a catalogue with public-source limits rather than a full forensic cost reconciliation across departmental ledgers.

### Cross-case pattern

OAG's 2000 governance report was written because public-sector IT projects, including the National Library and Police INCIS projects, had seen failures to deliver what was required and major time and cost overruns [OAG, 2000](https://oag.parliament.nz/2000/1st-report-2000/docs/part4.pdf). OAG said the same mistakes recur when lessons are not shared, and it framed the problem around governance and oversight rather than only project management [OAG, 2000](https://oag.parliament.nz/2000/1st-report-2000/docs/part4.pdf).

The repeated failure modes across the included cases align closely with OAG's generic risk model for public-sector IT projects: weak skills, problematic behaviour, poor information, weak communication/listening, unreliable reporting, inadequate business cases/specifications/contracts, and weak supplier/department/central-agency relationships [OAG, 2000](https://oag.parliament.nz/2000/it-oversight/docs/part3.pdf). Those categories appear again in Novopay's weak governance, customisation, absent IQA accountability, and optimistic ministerial reporting [Beehive, 2013](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/ministerial-inquiry-report-novopay-released), and in BCU's optimism bias, late/bypassed governance, missing requirements, poor fit-gap analysis, and ignored assurance warnings [MBIE independent review, 2026](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/32155-review-of-the-biometric-projects-biometric-capability-update-bccu-and-ipe-projects).

The cost category matters for public accountability. INCIS is best described as a gross project cost of $107.5m reduced to $82.5m after settlement, not simply an $82.5m write-off [Beehive, 1999](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/settlement-cuts-incis-cost-825-million). Novopay's $56.8m figure is a "total cost to date" at the inquiry release point, with separate later settlement and audit-cost figures [Beehive, 2013](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/ministerial-inquiry-report-novopay-released); [OAG, 2014](https://ao.parliament.nz/2014/school-audits/part3.htm); [Beehive, 2014](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/government-owned-company-take-over-school-payroll). DIA's $22.915m is an impairment of an intangible asset after work halted, not the total Te Ara Manaaki programme cost [DIA annual report 2023/24](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2023-24-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2023-2024-Internal-Affairs-Annual-Report.pdf). BCU's about $35m is an independent-review estimate, explicitly caveated by difficulty establishing total costs [MBIE independent review, 2026](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/32155-review-of-the-biometric-projects-biometric-capability-update-bccu-and-ipe-projects).

## What would change this conclusion

- A Treasury, agency, or OIA-released reconciliation that separates capital spend, operating spend, impairment/write-off, settlement receipts, remediation, and reusable assets for each project would change the catalogue's dollar totals and confidence levels.
- A public independent review of DIA's Te Ara Manaaki/Civil Registration failure would change the causes row; the current public record supports impairment and termination more strongly than root-cause diagnosis.
- A final Public Service Commission or Privileges Committee outcome for BCU could change the integrity/reporting conclusions; this finding uses the MBIE independent review and official releases available on 3 July 2026.
- I could not verify a primary-source Budget 2026 line for the reported $31.2m BCU write-off, so I did not use that as the cost anchor.
- I could not pin public costs for the National Library NDIS project or Oranga Tamariki IT reset tightly enough to include them in the main catalogue.
