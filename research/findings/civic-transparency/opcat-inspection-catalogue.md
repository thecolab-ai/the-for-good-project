# The Ombudsman's mental-health and intellectual-disability OPCAT record is a fragmented facility-by-facility inspection catalogue — no live facility-level register, only two dated thematic roll-ups — with repeated unresolved infrastructure, seclusion, occupancy, staffing, and mixed-status findings

> **Re-research note (8 July 2026).** This finding was originally produced (4 July 2026) with the Ombudsman's filtered OPCAT category page and the Ward 10a report page Cloudflare-blocked to the local fetch tooling, so the catalogue's completeness and the Ward 10A 2026 status were rated Medium "due to block." After the 8 July 2026 fetch-ladder upgrade those sources are reachable: I browsed the **entire** OPCAT category (all three result pages) and fetched the Ward 10a page and the 2026 media release directly. This version replaces the block-caveated conclusions with directly-verified ones, adds catalogue entries the blocked pass missed (the two health-sector thematic roll-ups, the Ward 6C initial report, and the three Puna forensic-ward initial reports), and gives an exact category denominator.

## Executive answer

- **The public record is not one national dataset.** The OPCAT record is published as individual per-facility resource pages inside a single "OPCAT" resource category that, on 8 July 2026, contained **exactly 93 resources across three result pages** — mixing mental-health, intellectual-disability, prison, aged-care, refugee, youth-justice-residence, MIQ, thematic, and annual OPCAT reports together, with no live facility-by-facility register or dashboard. [Ombudsman OPCAT category (filtered)](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993)
- **Within that category I enumerated at least 47 individual mental-health / intellectual-disability / forensic inpatient facility reports, plus two dated thematic roll-ups.** The two thematic reports are the only roll-ups that exist — "Thematic report on inspections of secure intellectual disability facilities" (13 Apr 2022, covering 17 secure residences across six providers, inspected 14 Aug 2019–4 Jun 2020) and the "OPCAT COVID-19 report … mental health facilities" (15 Jun 2020) — and both are point-in-time documents, not an ongoing register. [Secure-ID thematic report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/opcat-report-thematic-report-inspections-secure-intellectual-disability-facilities-under); [OPCAT category](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993)
- **Repeat, unfixed recommendations are the norm, not the exception, in the visible follow-up record.** Across 17 follow-up/re-inspection reports with published achievement counts, "not achieved" or "still progressing" dominates: e.g. HRBC Waikato had 23 of 42 earlier ward recommendations not achieved plus two unable to assess; Wards 34/35/36 Waikato had nine of 12 not achieved; Southland had one of 10 achieved and nine not achieved; Te Awhina had two of 14 achieved and ten not achieved; Te Whare o Matairangi had five of nine not achieved; Te Whare Maiangiangi had nine of 14 not achieved. [HRBC follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-announced-follow-inspection-henry-rongomau-bennett-waiora-waikato-hospital); [Wards 34/35/36 follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-follow-inspection-wards-34-35-and-36-waikato-hospital-under-crimes); [Southland follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-announced-follow-inspection-inpatient-mental-health-unit-southland-hospital); [Te Awhina follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-announced-follow-inspection-te-awhina-unit-whanganui-hospital)
- **The recurring themes** are: buildings/sub-areas not fit for purpose; seclusion rooms, admissions rooms, courtyards, or other non-bedroom spaces used restrictively or as bedrooms; over-occupancy and capacity pressure; staff shortages/training gaps; privacy and gender-separation gaps; unreliable recording of seclusion/environmental restraint; and voluntary or non-forensic people exposed to locked or forensic-level restrictions. [Te Whare Maiangiangi follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-follow-inspection-te-whare-maiangiangi-unit-tauranga-hospital-under-0); [Ward 10a report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); [Ward 21 report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-21-palmerston-north-hospital-under-crimes-torture-act)
- **Ward 10A, Wakari is the sharpest case of the "inspect → recommend → not fixed → re-inspect" cycle** — and its full March 2026 report is still not published. The 2021 report (published Feb 2022) found the ward "not fit-for-purpose" (an issue open since 2014) and made 15 recommendations. The re-inspection of 17–20 March 2026 is so far documented only in the Ombudsman's 3 July 2026 media release, which reports "some of the worst practices we have observed anywhere" — including one person confined to their bedroom for ~18 months with minimal human contact, a bariatric mat used to restrain people causing injury, and "next to no improvement to the physical structure" — after which Health NZ closed the ward to new admissions and the Ministry of Health accepted a recommendation for an independent investigation. [Ward 10a 2021 report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); [Ombudsman media release, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need)

**Overall confidence:** High for the category size (93 resources), the existence/dates/facility names/published follow-up counts of the cited reports, the existence of the two thematic roll-ups, and the recurring-theme pattern — all verified by directly browsing the full OPCAT category and the individual report pages on 8 July 2026. Medium only for anything requiring non-public data (internal Health NZ implementation records, unpublished follow-up visits) and for the exact "no later follow-up" count, which is now a strong statement within the published category but cannot see the Ombudsman's internal CMS.

## Evidence

### Scope and method

The Ombudsman says OPCAT monitoring covers places of detention including mental-health and intellectual-disability facilities, and publishes each inspection as a resource page with PDF/Word report files. [Ombudsman monitoring places of detention](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/what-ombudsman-can-help/monitoring-places-detention)

The filtered OPCAT category page (`?f[0]=category:1993`) — which the original research pass could not open because it returned a Cloudflare challenge to the local `curl`/browser rungs — is now fully reachable through the upgraded fetch ladder. On 8 July 2026 I browsed all three result pages of that category and read the complete list. It contained **93 resources**: 32 on page 1, 32 on page 2, and 29 on page 3. [Ombudsman OPCAT category, page 1](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993); [page 2](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993&page=1); [page 3](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993&page=2)

The 93 resources are a single undifferentiated OPCAT category: they interleave mental-health inpatient units, secure intellectual-disability units, forensic units, prisons, aged-care and MIQ thematic reports, the Māngere Refugee Resettlement Centre, the Oranga Tamariki–run Matawhāiti Residence, the Prisoners of Extreme Risk Unit, and the OPCAT annual reports 2011/12–2016/17. There is no separate mental-health/intellectual-disability filter and no facility-level status register. This confirms the stream's core legibility gap directly rather than by inference. [Ombudsman OPCAT category](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993)

Counting only the individual mental-health / intellectual-disability / forensic **inpatient facility** reports inside the category (excluding prisons, aged care, MIQ, refugee, youth-justice residence, PERU, thematic, and annual reports), I identified **at least 47** report pages, spanning public issue dates from March 2020 to December 2025. The verified set includes adult acute units, older-persons/psychogeriatric units, forensic units, hospital secure units, and secure intellectual-disability units. [Ombudsman OPCAT category](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993); [Ward 10a report page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); [Kingsley Mortimer report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-kingsley-mortimer-unit-north-shore)

### Are there any roll-ups? Two, both dated

The closest thing to a national roll-up is not a live dataset but two point-in-time thematic reports found in the category:

- **"OPCAT Report: Thematic report on inspections of secure intellectual disability facilities under the Crimes of Torture Act 1989"** (published 13 Apr 2022) is a genuine cross-facility roll-up: it covers **17 secure residences across six service providers**, inspected between 14 August 2019 and 4 June 2020, and found "significant variation in the treatment and conditions across the residences," making recommendations to five of the six providers. It is a snapshot of a fixed inspection round, not an ongoing register. [Secure-ID thematic report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/opcat-report-thematic-report-inspections-secure-intellectual-disability-facilities-under)
- **"OPCAT COVID-19 report: Report on inspections of mental health facilities under the Crimes of Torture Act 1989"** (published 15 Jun 2020) is the other mental-health-sector thematic document. [Ombudsman OPCAT category](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993)

So the earlier framing that the record shows "no roll-up at all" needs one correction: two thematic roll-ups exist, but both are dated one-offs (2020 and 2022) and neither is a maintained facility-by-facility mental-health/ID register with per-recommendation status — which remains the legibility gap. [Ombudsman OPCAT category](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993); [Secure-ID thematic report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/opcat-report-thematic-report-inspections-secure-intellectual-disability-facilities-under)
