# The Ombudsman's mental-health and intellectual-disability OPCAT record is a fragmented facility-by-facility inspection catalogue with repeated unresolved infrastructure, seclusion, occupancy, staffing, and mixed-status findings

## Executive answer

- The public record is not one national dataset: it is a set of individual OPCAT report pages, and the Ombudsman's resources page shows OPCAT reports mixed with other detention reports rather than a mental-health/intellectual-disability roll-up. The unfiltered resources page listed 1,647 resources and 175 reports on 3 July 2026, and its current first page included seven health/disability OPCAT reports issued on 10 December 2025. [Ombudsman resources page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources)
- I verified at least 34 relevant mental-health, forensic mental-health, psychogeriatric, and intellectual-disability inpatient inspection records from the Ombudsman site, concentrated in public issue dates from 2020 to 2025. The verified set includes adult acute units, older-persons/psychogeriatric units, forensic units, and secure intellectual-disability units. [Ombudsman resources page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources); [Ward 10a report page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin)
- Repeat recommendations are not isolated. The quantified section below records 12 follow-up reports where achievement counts were visible in public page text; examples include HRBC Waikato with 23 of 42 earlier ward recommendations not achieved plus two unable to be assessed, Tumanako with five of 13 progressing and eight not achieved, Kensington with two of 10 progressing and two not achieved, Southland with one of 10 achieved and nine not achieved, He Puna Waiora with five of 15 not achieved and seven progressing, and Te Whetu Tawera with five of 15 not achieved and three progressing. [HRBC follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-announced-follow-inspection-henry-rongomau-bennett-waiora-waikato-hospital); [Tumanako follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-follow-inspection-tumanako-inpatient-mental-health-unit); [Kensington follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-follow-inspection-kensington-centre-mental-health-inpatient-unit); [Southland follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-announced-follow-inspection-inpatient-mental-health-unit-southland-hospital); [He Puna Waiora follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-announced-follow-inspection-he-puna-waiora-mental-health-inpatient-unit-north); [Te Whetu Tawera follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-announced-follow-inspection-te-whetu-tawera-auckland-city-hospital)
- The recurring themes in the verified reports are: buildings or sub-areas not fit for purpose; seclusion rooms, admissions rooms, courtyards, or other non-bedroom spaces being used restrictively or as bedrooms; over-occupancy and capacity pressure; staff shortages/training gaps; privacy and gender-separation gaps; unreliable recording of seclusion/environmental restraint; and voluntary or non-forensic people being exposed to locked or forensic-level restrictions. [Te Whare Maiangiangi follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-follow-inspection-te-whare-maiangiangi-unit-tauranga-hospital-under-0); [Te Toki Maurere follow-up](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-follow-inspection-te-toki-maurere-unit-whakatane-hospital-under-0); [Ward 10a report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); [Ward 21 report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-21-palmerston-north-hospital-under-crimes-torture-act)
- As of this check on 3 July 2026, I did not find a full 2026 Ward 10A report in the Ombudsman resources results. The newest live source remains the Ombudsman's 3 July 2026 media release saying the March 2026 inspection found serious failures at Ward 10A and that Health NZ had closed it to new admissions. [Ward 10a search result/report page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); [Ombudsman media release, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - High for the existence, dates, facility names, and published follow-up status of the cited reports; Medium for the catalogue completeness because the OPCAT category page was Cloudflare-blocked to local `curl`/repo browser rungs and had to be reconstructed from the Ombudsman's unfiltered resources page plus individual report pages surfaced by the built-in web search/open tool.

## Evidence

### Scope and method

The Ombudsman says OPCAT monitoring covers places of detention including mental-health facilities and intellectual-disability facilities, and the Ombudsman's inspection outputs are published as resource pages with PDF and Word report files. [Ombudsman monitoring places of detention](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/what-ombudsman-can-help/monitoring-places-detention); [Ombudsman resources page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources)

The filtered OPCAT category URL returned a Cloudflare challenge to local `curl`, and `node scripts/fetch.mjs` classified the filtered catalogue and the Ward 10a page as `BLOCKED` because plain HTTP was 403 and the local browser rungs were unavailable; I therefore used the built-in web search/open tool for Ombudsman pages and treated blocked local fetches as tooling, not dead links, as required by ADR-0006. [ADR-0006](../../../docs/adr/0006-fetch-proxy-browser-management.md); [Ombudsman resources page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources)

The unfiltered resources page is still useful evidence: on 3 July 2026 it listed 1,647 resources, showed 175 resources under the `Reports` category, and its first page contained seven health/disability OPCAT reports issued on 10 December 2025: Te Awhina Unit, Ward OPR1 Waikato, Tumanako, Waiatarau, Kensington Centre, Te Whare Manaaki, and Kauri/Totara Mason Clinic. [Ombudsman resources page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources)
