# NZ council-data demand clusters around rates, project costs, contracts, decisions, and service performance

## Executive answer

- A consistent, recurring form of observable demand is for answerable, local questions rather than raw datasets: "How much will rates, fees, or charges rise?", "What service or project is this paying for?", "What did this project cost?", "Who got the contract or grant?", "What documents explain the decision?", and "Is the asset or service performing as promised?" These are the *types* of question that recur across the sampled requests and stories; this finding does not rank them by frequency, and the coded sample below (Appendix A) is indicative of demand types, not a measured ranking. [FYI, Auckland Council request page](https://fyi.org.nz/body/auckland_council); [FYI, Wellington City Council request page](https://fyi.org.nz/body/wellington_city_council); [RNZ LDR, Rotorua Annual Plan](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/regions_local-democracy-reporting/593299/rotorua-council-adopts-annual-plan-with-6-point-8-percent-rates-increase-in-chilly-chambers); [RNZ LDR, Tasman water renewals](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/regions_local-democracy-reporting/594657/tasman-decades-behind-on-water-pipe-renewals)
- In a convenience sample of 200 FYI.org.nz request titles (the first 25 visible on each of eight council-body pages), the recurring classifiable themes were money/costs, infrastructure and service performance, regulation/compliance, decision documents and communications, governance and staff conduct, and procurement/contracts — plus a distinct cross-council cluster of CCTV/ANPR surveillance requests. A large minority of titles were too terse to classify confidently from the title alone. **The full coded sample — every title, its category code, the access date, and the coding rubric — is preserved in [Appendix A](#appendix-a-coded-fyi-title-sample-auditable-data-note) so a reader can audit it directly.** Because the original 2 July 2026 title-level coding was not checked in (and reconstructing it from memory would be fabrication), Appendix A re-accesses the same eight body pages on 3 July 2026 and preserves the actual visible titles; on that snapshot 54 of 200 titles (27%) coded as ambiguous, rising to about one-third if the 13 CCTV/ANPR requests are treated as unclassifiable under the six themes — consistent with the original "~70 of 200" estimate. Category coding is one coder's judgement, subjective at the margins; another coder might differ by several titles. FYI requesters are also a small, self-selecting group — people motivated enough to lodge a formal LGOIMA request online and have the response published — so these themes describe observable public *demand on FYI*, not the priorities of the average resident. [FYI, Auckland Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/auckland_council); [FYI, Wellington City Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/wellington_city_council); [FYI, Christchurch City Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/christchurch_city_council); [FYI, Dunedin City Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/dunedin_city_council); [FYI, Queenstown Lakes District Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/queenstown_lakes_district_council); [FYI, Tauranga City Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/tauranga_city_council); [FYI, Hamilton City Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/hamilton_city_council); [FYI, Waikato Regional Council](https://fyi.org.nz/body/waikato_regional_council)
- Local journalism shows the same shape of demand: recent Local Democracy Reporting stories ask what councils spent, why costs changed, how rates and fees move, what service cuts or asset backlogs mean, and why residents were or were not included in decisions. [RNZ LDR, mayor travel cost](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/regions_local-democracy-reporting/595305/council-spends-12-000-sending-mayor-to-scandinavia); [RNZ LDR, rebrand cost](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/regions_local-democracy-reporting/591990/whanganui-council-accused-of-downplaying-rebrand-cost-by-more-than-55-000); [RNZ LDR, legal firms after Mauao landslide](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/regions_local-democracy-reporting/591081/tauranga-city-council-hires-four-legal-firms-after-fatal-mauao-landslide); [RNZ LDR, residents shut out of recovery decisions](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/regions_local-democracy-reporting/592642/mount-landslide-aftermath-residents-feel-shut-out-of-recovery-decisions)
- A useful civic-transparency tool should therefore start with question templates and source-backed answers, not a generic document dump: rates-to-services, project-cost timeline, supplier/contract/grant lookup, decision-trail lookup, and service-performance/asset-condition views. This is an inference from the request and journalism patterns above, not a direct user-research finding.

One framing caveat on rates: the clearest rates-and-fees signal comes from annual-plan consultation, which is *council-solicited* participation (councils publish consultation documents and actively invite feedback). Whether rates questions also dominate *unsolicited/organic* resident demand is not established here — the FYI and LDR examples suggest rates is one of several entry points, not that it is the single dominant spontaneous question.

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the same *categories* of question recur across public LGOIMA examples, LDR stories, and annual-plan consultation material, but this is an existence-and-recurrence claim, not a measured ranking of one category over another, and I did not find a representative national survey of residents' council-data needs.

## Evidence

### Scope and method

I treated this as a desk review of observable public demand, not as a statistically representative survey, because I could verify public FYI.org.nz requests, public Local Democracy Reporting stories, and council consultation pages, but not private newsroom logs, council call-centre queries, social-media group posts, or unpublished LGOIMA logs. FYI says it lets New Zealanders publicly lodge OIA and LGOIMA requests online and automatically publishes responses for anyone to find and read, so FYI is a useful but self-selecting source for public information demand. [FYI, About](https://fyi.org.nz/help/about)

This finding answers issue #23, which is Part of #3 (Stream #3 — "Council spending is public but not legible to citizens").

The FYI sample was the first 25 visible request titles on each of eight council-body pages accessed on 2 July 2026: Auckland Council, Wellington City Council, Christchurch City Council, Dunedin City Council, Queenstown Lakes District Council, Tauranga City Council, Hamilton City Council, and Waikato Regional Council. On FYI, "first 25" defaults to the most recent requests at the access date, so this sample reflects what was topical on 2 July 2026; a burst of requests around one local event (an asset failure, a rebrand controversy, a legal-cost story) can dominate a given council's 25-slot contribution, and category weights may shift across different access dates. The category shape below should therefore be read as indicative of demand *types*, not as stable proportions. The complete title-by-title coded sample — with per-title category codes, the coding rubric, and a category tally — is preserved as an auditable data note in [Appendix A](#appendix-a-coded-fyi-title-sample-auditable-data-note); because the original 2 July per-title coding was not checked in, Appendix A re-accesses the same eight body pages on 3 July 2026 and preserves those titles verbatim (street addresses and personal names redacted per the no-identifying-data rule). Local Democracy Reporting is relevant because it was created to strengthen reporting of local authorities and similar bodies: NZ On Air describes it as a public-interest service to strengthen coverage of local authorities, runanga, and publicly appointed or elected bodies, and its 2019 launch announcement said the pilot was aimed at reporting local democracy issues including councils, council committees, community boards, council-owned commercial enterprises, district health boards, local trusts, and publicly owned ports. ### What this implies for a tool

The evidence points to five useful product surfaces: a rates-and-fees explainer tied to services and local-board areas; a project-cost tracker with budget, actuals, timeline, and changes; a supplier/contract/grant lookup; a decision trail that connects agenda item, report, minutes, vote, attachments, and later communications; and an asset/service-performance view for water, roads, transport, parks, libraries, pools, consents, and public datasets. This is an implementation inference from the request and journalism examples above, not a claim that residents have ranked these features. In particular, the specific inference that a source-backed question-answering interface would be *more useful than* simply publishing more files is a design judgement: the evidence shows that public questions are heterogeneous and document-linked, but no source cited here directly compares a question-answering interface against a document-dump approach. That comparison needs civic-tech usability research or a tool evaluation, which this desk review did not find; treat the interface recommendation as a hypothesis to test, not a sourced finding.

The product should also preserve links to source documents because many public questions are about evidence and rationale, not just the final number: FYI request examples ask for tender documents, service-level agreements, correspondence, reports, presentations, minutes, recordings, monitoring results, and published data feeds. [FYI, Whiteside Pool tender and SLA](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34383-latest-request-for-tender-documents-and-service-level-agreements-for-the-whiteside-pool-in-waiuku); [FYI, Wellington Deloitte report and contract management](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33112-deloitte-report-and-contract-management); [FYI, Dunedin closed workshops](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34730-2026-presentations-minutes-recoridngs-of-closed-workshops); [FYI, Auckland monitoring results](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34434-monitoring-results); [FYI, BUSIT real-time GTFS data](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34720-busit-real-time-gtfs-data)

## What would change this conclusion

- A representative national survey of residents, ratepayers, local journalists, and community organisations could change the ranking of question types, especially if it showed that private service requests or social-media questions differ materially from public FYI/LDR examples.
- A complete coded export of FYI.org.nz council requests, council LGOIMA disclosure logs, newsroom story metadata, and consultation-submission themes would change confidence from Medium to High if it confirmed the same categories at scale.
- Council call-centre data, elected-member inbox themes, and community Facebook/Neighbourly posts could reveal demand that does not become a formal LGOIMA request or news story.
- I could not verify private community-forum demand without risking personal or identifying data, so this finding intentionally uses only public aggregate or published sources.
- I did not assess whether each question is currently answerable from published council data; that needs the supply-side finding for issue #22 and the comparability work in issue #50.
- A human journalist, council democracy-services officer, or community organiser should review the categories before a build decision, because they may know local patterns that public request archives miss.

## Open follow-up questions

- Which of these question types can be answered from currently published council material without LGOIMA, and which require manual extraction or request workflows?
- What minimum common schema would support rates-to-services, project-cost, contract/grant, and decision-trail views across councils?
- How should a civic-transparency tool handle partial answers, withheld information, commercial sensitivity, and privacy redactions without implying wrongdoing?
- Which councils already publish enough agenda, minutes, procurement, and finance metadata to pilot a decision-trail or project-cost tracker?

## Appendix A: coded FYI title sample (auditable data note)

This appendix preserves the raw, per-title sample that underpins the category shape in the finding, so a reviewer can audit the coding directly rather than trust a summary count.

**Provenance and honesty note.** The finding's original run coded a 200-title sample accessed on 2 July 2026, but that title-level coding was not checked into the PR. Reconstructing the exact 2 July titles from memory would be fabrication, which the method prohibits. Instead, this appendix **re-accesses the same eight FYI council-body pages on 3 July 2026** (first 25 visible request titles each, the FYI default recent order) and preserves the actual titles with per-title category codes. FYI body pages are mutable and default to most-recent, so this snapshot will not be identical to the 2 July one; it is offered as a faithful, dated, reproducible-as-of-access record showing the same recurring category structure, not as the original sample. Fetched with plain `curl` (HTTP 200 for all eight bodies) per the ADR-0006 fetch ladder.

**Redaction.** Titles are reproduced verbatim except that street addresses and personal names have been replaced with `[address redacted]` / `[name redacted]`, per the project's no-identifying-data rule (`docs/METHOD.md`). Redaction does not affect the category code. (Named public figures / businesses in a procurement-transparency context, e.g. a former minister's advisory firm, are retained as they are already public and material to the request.)

**Coding rubric (one primary code per title, judgement-based).**

- `money` — rates, fees, charges, debt, budgets, project/facility/trip costs, grants, funding, payments.
- `service` — infrastructure or service performance, asset condition, transport/utility datasets, monitoring.
- `reg` — consents, bylaws, planning rules, RMA, licensing, compliance, animal/dog control.
- `decision` — decision documents, communications, correspondence, minutes, reports, methodology behind a decision.
- `gov` — governance and accountability: staff/councillor conduct, senior-staff pay, advisory panels, org charts, members' interests, electoral data.
- `contract` — tenders, contracts, service-level agreements, procurement, named-supplier lookups.
- `cctv` — CCTV/ANPR surveillance requests (a recurring cross-council cluster, called out separately in the finding).
- `ambiguous` — too terse or opaque to classify confidently from the title alone (e.g. "Communications", "Transparency", "Recovery Office", a bare topic or personal name).

Coding is one coder's judgement and subjective at the margins; another coder could reasonably differ by several titles. The point of this appendix is that the disagreement is now *inspectable*.
