# NZ community and residential mental-health bed capacity and the acute discharge bottleneck

This is research child **#252 of Stream #224**. It answers one question: **how many community, residential, and step-down mental-health and addiction beds NZ funds, what the waitlists look like, and how the shortage of discharge placements drives acute-ward over-capacity.**

> **Re-research note (8 July 2026).** An earlier version of this finding rated its Budget-2025 conclusions **Low** and flagged the core "no published community bed count" claim as only partly checkable, because key government sources (Beehive, Health NZ / MoH data pages) returned 403 bot-challenges to the fetch tooling then in use. After the 8 July fetch-ladder upgrade (Jina reader + stealth-browser rungs), those sources were re-fetched. The re-fetch showed two things: (1) the specific URLs the earlier run guessed at (`health.govt.nz/our-work/mental-health/community-mental-health`, `healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/our-data/`, `beehive.govt.nz/feature/budget-2025`) genuinely **404** — they were never the right pages, the block had masked that; and (2) the real Budget 2025 and monitoring pages can be checked through the fetch ladder or archived/search-indexed copies. A review then caught that one RNZ line-item article had been misdated and misattributed: the $51.72m/20-bed, $28.45m psychology-assistant, $20.18m maternal-mental-health and $1m/year community-fund package is **Budget 2026**, not Budget 2025 [ODT/RNZ republication, 15 Jun 2026](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/national/concerns-mental-health-funding-may-not-match-need-rnz). This corrected version keeps the core "no published national community bed count" finding at **High** confidence and rates the narrower Budget 2025 no-community-bed-expansion conclusion **Medium**.

## Executive answer

- **No one publishes a national community MH&A bed count — confirmed by reaching the actual publishers.** There is no published, defined national time series for community, residential, or step-down mental-health and addiction beds, in contrast to the acute inpatient series where headline figures exist (NZ funds **more than 1,300 mental-health and addiction hospital beds, including more than 900 acute** beds) [RNZ, 20 Dec 2024](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/537203/no-budget-for-psychiatric-beds-despite-being-over-capacity). Health NZ's public MH&A data is a **service-use** web tool, not a bed inventory [Health NZ, MH&A service-use web tool](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/health-data/data-sets-and-collections/mental-health-and-addictions-data/mental-health-and-addiction-service-use-web-tool), and the dedicated monitor, **Te Hiringa Mahara** (Mental Health and Wellbeing Commission), tracks access, wait times, seclusion and workforce — not a residential/community bed count [Te Hiringa Mahara, MH&A service monitoring](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/our-work/mental-health-and-addiction-system/mental-health-and-addiction-service-monitoring/). **Confidence: High** that no consolidated public community-bed figure exists; **Low** on any specific national community-bed number.
- **The discharge bottleneck is the binding constraint on acute flow.** Acute wards run 115%–140% of capacity, and the most-repeatedly cited reason patients cannot leave is **"a lack of beds in mental health and addiction residential services"** — many facilities "already full and had a waitlist" — not a lack of acute beds themselves [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse). **Confidence: High** on the pattern; the 140% figure is a single frontline account.
- **Community providers will not disclose numbers.** RNZ contacted major Waikato providers (Pathways, Manaaki Trust, Ember Korowai Takitini) about demand and waitlists; none would comment, and one referred back to Health NZ. The PSA described a **"culture of fear"** among providers that they would be punished by Health NZ or government if they spoke publicly [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse). **Confidence: High** on the refusal to comment and the culture-of-fear claim.
- **Community mental health is acknowledged as under-resourced.** The Public Service Association (main union for mental-health nurses) said community mental health is "resource-short" and called for "major investment and commitment" from government [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse). Health NZ admitted acute wards "still experienced spikes in demand" despite "working with community partners over the past year to improve the flow of patients to community settings" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse). **Confidence: High** on the under-resourcing pattern; **Medium** on the one-year timeline.
- **Budget 2024 made no bid for more psychiatry beds; Budget 2025 funded crisis-response, facility-safety and compulsory-care safeguards, but no identifiable national community/residential bed expansion.** Health NZ confirmed no 2024 Budget bid for more psychiatry beds was made "despite being over capacity" [RNZ, 20 Dec 2024](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/537203/no-budget-for-psychiatric-beds-despite-being-over-capacity). The genuine Budget 2025 mental-health lines include about **$28m over four years** for a multi-agency 111 mental-distress response, **$50m** to improve safety, privacy and dignity at mental-health facilities, and **more than $9m** for stronger protections for people receiving compulsory assessment and treatment [Beehive, 22 May 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/mental-distress-111-calls-get-mental-health-response); [Budget 2025 Health advice bundle, Sep 2025 proactive release](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-09/Budget-2025-Health-Bundle-2-Advice-on-Specific-Initiatives.pdf). The Vote Health Budget 2025 page also lists mental-health 111 response, forensic Midland capacity funded by reprioritisation outside Budget 2025 allowances, and "interim inpatient bed capacity" in a broader infrastructure tranche, but does not identify a community MH&A residential or step-down bed programme [Health NZ / MoH, Vote Health Budget 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/vote-health/budget-2025). **Confidence: Medium** (strong summary evidence, but no full line-by-line Estimates audit).
- **Respite services are also maxed out.** Beyond residential beds, patients need emergency housing options, and respite capacity was described as fully stretched [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse). **Confidence: Medium** (single-source account, consistent with the broader resource-shortage narrative).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the *direction* (community-bed shortage drives acute over-capacity; providers won't disclose numbers; the community sector is under-resourced; no targeted community-bed expansion is identifiable in the Budget 2025 material checked here) is well-evidenced and multiply attested, and the key **negative** finding (no published national community-bed inventory) is now **High** confidence after reaching the actual publishers. The *precise numbers* remain absent — not because they don't exist, but because they are not published and providers refuse to share them. That absence is itself the finding.

## Evidence

### 1. The core finding: no published national community MH&A bed count

In contrast to acute inpatient beds — where headline figures exist (NZ funds **more than 1,300 MH&A hospital beds, including more than 900 acute** [RNZ, 20 Dec 2024](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/537203/no-budget-for-psychiatric-beds-despite-being-over-capacity); ~608 adult acute inpatient, reportedly unchanged 2017→2022 [RNZ, 23 Nov 2022](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/479350/mental-health-system-struggling-to-keep-ahead-of-demand-for-acute-services-little)) — there is **no published, defined national count of community, residential, or step-down mental-health and addiction beds.**

This re-research reached the candidate publishers directly (rather than being bot-blocked, as the earlier run was) and confirmed the gap:

- **Health NZ public MH&A data** is delivered as a **service-use web tool** — counts of *people seen* and *service contacts*, not a funded-bed inventory [Health NZ, MH&A service-use web tool](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/health-data/data-sets-and-collections/mental-health-and-addictions-data/mental-health-and-addiction-service-use-web-tool).
- **Te Hiringa Mahara** (Mental Health and Wellbeing Commission), the statutory monitor, still active with monitoring data to 30 June 2025, reports against the **He Ara Āwhina** framework — access, wait times, seclusion/CCTO, equity and workforce — with **no residential/community bed-count measure** in its published Summary Set or dashboard [Te Hiringa Mahara, MH&A service monitoring](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/our-work/mental-health-and-addiction-system/mental-health-and-addiction-service-monitoring/); [Te Hiringa Mahara, He Ara Āwhina dashboard](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/our-work/mental-health-and-addiction-system/he-ara-awhina-dashboard/).
- The MoH and Health NZ pages the earlier run tried (`.../mental-health/community-mental-health` and `.../about-us/our-data/`) now **resolve but return 404** — restructured URLs, not a suppressed bed table [verified via `scripts/fetch.mjs`, 8 Jul 2026].

The absence is notable. If the community-bed count were a tracked metric, it would appear in these data products, the Commission's monitoring, or Budget documents. Its absence across all of them indicates it is simply **not collected or published as a national figure.** This does not mean the beds don't exist — providers such as Pathways, Manaaki Trust and Ember Korowai Takitini clearly operate residential services — but there is no authoritative national denominator. **Confidence: High** that no consolidated public figure exists.

### 2. The discharge bottleneck: community beds are the binding constraint

The central mechanism driving acute-ward over-capacity is not insufficient acute capacity (already over-subscribed) but the **inability to discharge patients to appropriate community settings:**

- A mental-health nurse at Waikato Hospital reported (Aug 2025) that **"one of the key factors contributing to problems transitioning patients to the community was a lack of beds in mental health and addiction residential services."** Patients often lack a supportive household and need 24/7 care and medication oversight. "Many facilities were already full and had a waitlist, and respite services were maxed out" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).
- The same nurse reported acute wards at **140% capacity** with patients sleeping on mattresses on the floor in interview rooms, seclusion rooms and other wards [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).
- Earlier in August 2025, RNZ reported an **entire ward at Auckland's North Shore Hospital** that "could not be discharged as they had nowhere to go for non-hospital care" [cited within RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse). (This is cited indirectly within the Waikato article; the standalone article was not separately located.)
- In April–May 2024, a Waikato facility (HRBC) was at **up to 115% occupancy**, with patients sleeping on floor mattresses [RNZ, 2024](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/517433/waikato-mental-health-facility-patient-forced-to-sleep-on-floor-due-to-overcrowding).
- The Dec 2024 report tied the same mechanism to prison: high demand meant "people getting turned away or discharged early," including a person in psychosis who "ended up in prison" after being unable to access mental-health accommodation [RNZ, 20 Dec 2024](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/537203/no-budget-for-psychiatric-beds-despite-being-over-capacity).

This is a **flow problem, not just a stock problem.** Adding acute beds (the later Budget 2026 package funds 20 inpatient beds) is necessary but not sufficient — if patients can't leave acute wards, new acute beds fill up too. The sibling finding (#233) documented the stock problem; this finding documents the flow constraint. **Confidence: High** on the pattern (multiply attested across 2024–2025 from frontline and institutional sources); **Medium** on the specific 140% and North Shore figures (single-account / cited indirectly).

### 3. Why we don't know the numbers: providers won't disclose

RNZ contacted several major Waikato MH&A residential providers about current demand and waitlists:

> "RNZ asked some of the major providers of mental health and addiction residential care in Waikato about current demand and waitlists — including Pathways, Manaaki Trust and Ember Korowai Takitini — but none would comment, and one referred RNZ back to Health NZ" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).

The PSA described a **"culture of fear"** among community providers:

> "Fitzsimons said there was a 'culture of fear' among many community providers that they would be punished by HNZ or the government if they spoke out about the reality they were facing. Fitzsimons said she wanted HNZ and the government to be clear with providers that they could present their realities, so New Zealand could know how many beds it was short of." [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse)

Health NZ declined to speak for community providers:

> "Any decision to make comment to media is one for our community providers to make and is not for Health New Zealand to speak to" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).

This creates a **double blind spot:** providers won't disclose, and Health NZ won't publish a bed inventory. The exact community-bed deficit is therefore unknowable from public sources. **Confidence: High** on the refusal to comment and the culture-of-fear claim (directly sourced, named actors).

### 4. Community mental health is under-resourced

Multiple sources converge on community mental health being under-resourced:

- **PSA (main union for mental-health nurses):** "Community mental health was resource-short, with workers having their pay equity claims scrapped by the government, and a shortage of beds and purpose-built facilities. 'Major investment and commitment is needed by the government in community mental health.'" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse)
- **Labour's mental-health spokesperson Ingrid Leary:** "What was happening in Waikato showed underfunding in both the public health and community provider systems." [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse)
- **Health NZ's own admission:** Vicki Aitken (HNZ MH&A lead for Te Manawa Taki) said HNZ "had been working with community partners over the past year to improve the flow of patients to community settings, but admitted that the acute wards still experienced spikes in demand" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse). This implicitly acknowledges community placement capacity has not increased enough to absorb demand.
- **RANZCP** (Royal Australian and New Zealand College of Psychiatrists, NZ office): its chair Dr Hiran Thabrew called the later Budget 2026 mental-health package **"piecemeal and reactive"**, with decisions made "somewhat reactively and in response to lobbying, rather than in a transparent, planned and equitable manner," and questioned whether the funding matches need [ODT/RNZ republication, 15 Jun 2026](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/national/concerns-mental-health-funding-may-not-match-need-rnz).

**Confidence: High** on the pattern of under-resourcing (converging union, opposition, professional-college and government sources).

### 5. Budget 2024/25/26: no targeted community-bed expansion

This is the section the earlier block gutted; it is now rebuilt from directly-read sources.

- **Budget 2024:** Health NZ confirmed in December 2024 that **no Budget bid for more psychiatry beds was made** that year, "despite being at or over capacity" [RNZ, 20 Dec 2024](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/537203/no-budget-for-psychiatric-beds-despite-being-over-capacity).
- **Budget 2025, corrected:** the Budget 2025 material checked here is *not* the later RNZ ~$100m package. The genuine Budget 2025 mental-health items visible in the official summary and Beehive release are:
  - **About $28m over four years** for a multi-agency response to 111 mental-distress calls, including mental-health and addiction telehealth and expanded response teams [Health NZ / MoH, Vote Health Budget 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/vote-health/budget-2025); [Beehive, 22 May 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/mental-distress-111-calls-get-mental-health-response).
  - **$50m** to improve safety, privacy and dignity at mental-health facilities, described as part of the Government's response to the Royal Commission of Inquiry into Abuse in Care; the proactive Budget 2025 advice bundle describes in-depth review, scoping and safety upgrades for inpatient units [Beehive, 22 May 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/mental-distress-111-calls-get-mental-health-response); [Budget 2025 Health advice bundle, Sep 2025 proactive release](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-09/Budget-2025-Health-Bundle-2-Advice-on-Specific-Initiatives.pdf).
  - **More than $9m** for stronger protections for people receiving compulsory assessment and treatment; the proactive advice bundle records the scalable initiative as **$9.36m** for independent oversight and complaints/investigation capacity [Beehive, 22 May 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/mental-distress-111-calls-get-mental-health-response); [Budget 2025 Health advice bundle, Sep 2025 proactive release](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-09/Budget-2025-Health-Bundle-2-Advice-on-Specific-Initiatives.pdf).
  - Budget 2025's $1.013b health-infrastructure tranche lists "interim inpatient bed capacity" among six initiatives, but gives no mental-health community/residential/step-down bed count or dollar line [Health NZ / MoH, Vote Health Budget 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/vote-health/budget-2025).
  - Forensic MH capacity is being expanded in HNZ's Midland region (linked to the Waikeria Prison expansion) but is **managed outside Budget 2025 allowances through reprioritised funding**, and is forensic/high-complexity capacity rather than a general national community-bed programme [Health NZ / MoH, Vote Health Budget 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/vote-health/budget-2025); [Treasury Budget 2025 mental-health initiative advice](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2025-09/b25-initiatives-doocey-5143241.pdf).
- **Budget 2026:** the previously cited RNZ line-item package belongs here, not to Budget 2025. It reports **$51.72m for 20 new inpatient beds**, **$28.45m** for 150 psychology assistants, **$20.18m** for maternal mental-health support, and a **$1m annual** community-groups fund, and explicitly says the money was included in the **2026 budget** [ODT/RNZ republication, 15 Jun 2026](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/national/concerns-mental-health-funding-may-not-match-need-rnz).
- **Conclusion:** across the Vote Health Budget 2025 page, the Beehive release, the proactive Budget 2025 advice bundle and the Treasury advice located for the forensic item, **no dedicated national community MH&A residential or step-down bed-expansion programme is identifiable in Budget 2025.** The $50m facility line is relevant to mental-health facilities and should not be ignored, but the available descriptions are safety/privacy/dignity upgrades and assessment/scoping, not new community residential beds. **Confidence: Medium** — this is supported by official summaries and advice papers, but I did not exhaustively audit every underlying Estimates appropriation.

### 6. The broader context: respite, emergency housing, and systemic barriers

The discharge bottleneck extends beyond residential beds:

- **Respite services** described as "maxed out" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).
- **Emergency housing** also lacking, with patients who "do not have a supportive household to go to" after hospital [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).
- **Staffing pressures** compounding the problem: "short staffing and the shortage of senior nurses" on acute wards, with staff on ACC leave from patient assaults [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).
- **Methamphetamine-related admissions** increasing demand: HNZ's Vicki Aitken attributed rising demand partly to "meth abuse and wider system issues" [RNZ, 14 Aug 2025](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/national/569914/waikato-mental-health-patients-sleeping-on-floor-mattresses-because-of-bed-shortage-nurse).

**Confidence: Medium** on the broader context (consistent with the primary source but largely single-account).

## What would change this conclusion

- **A published national community MH&A bed inventory.** If Health NZ or MoH released a defined count of community residential/step-down beds, the "we don't know" gap closes. The PSA explicitly asked for this so "New Zealand could know how many beds it was short of." An Official Information Act request to Health NZ for community bed numbers and waitlist data by district would resolve it — this is the single most impactful next step.
- **A line-by-line audit of the Budget 2025 Estimates/Vote Health appropriations.** This re-research read the Vote Health summary page, the Beehive Budget 2025 release, the proactive Budget 2025 advice bundle located for the facility-safety and safeguards initiatives, and Treasury advice located for the forensic item, and found no national community-bed line — but did not walk every underlying appropriation. A full Estimates read would move the Budget conclusion from Medium to High (or refute it).
- **A second journalist or researcher verifying the "culture of fear" among providers.** The RNZ account is detailed and named, but one corroborating source would lift this from Medium to High.
- **Te Hiringa Mahara adding a residential/community capacity measure.** The Commission is active and monitoring to 30 June 2025 but does not publish a bed count; if it added one, the metric would exist.
- **Could not verify:** the exact community-bed deficit (providers won't tell; Health NZ won't publish); the standalone RNZ North Shore Hospital article (cited only indirectly); whether the "culture of fear" extends nationally beyond Waikato; the precise national community/residential/step-down bed number (genuinely unpublished, not a fetch limitation).
- **A human with sector authority or an OIA** is needed to get the actual community-bed numbers — this data exists inside Health NZ and community providers but is not public. No fetch-tooling upgrade can recover a figure that is not published anywhere.

## Open follow-up questions

- **What is the actual national community MH&A residential bed count?** An OIA request or sector insider could resolve this — the single biggest remaining data gap.
- **How does community MH&A funding compare per patient to acute inpatient funding?** The Budget 2026 line-item package put $51.72m into 20 inpatient beds and $1m/year into a community-groups fund; Budget 2025 separately put money into crisis response and facility safety rather than a visible community-bed expansion. Quantifying the per-patient split would be a policy question worth its own finding.
- **Does the He Ara Āwhina framework deliberately exclude bed capacity, and why?** Understanding why the dedicated monitor tracks access but not residential stock would clarify whether the gap is by design.
- **Are there regional variations in the community-bed deficit, or is it national?** Waikato and Auckland are documented; other districts are unknown.
