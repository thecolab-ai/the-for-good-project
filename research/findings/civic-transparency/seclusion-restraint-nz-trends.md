# Official NZ seclusion data shows a falling national trend, persistent Maori inequity, uneven district patterns, and a public record lagging 15-21 months

## Executive answer

- National adult-inpatient seclusion is down on the Ministry of Health's own long-run series: in 2023/24, 561 adults were secluded in adult inpatient mental-health services, with 1,050 events and 24,610 hours; the Ministry reports a 48% reduction in people secluded and a 73% reduction in hours since 2009. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)
- The headline improvement has not removed ethnic inequity: in 2023/24 Maori were 55% of adults secluded while Maori were 17% of the general population, and the adult-inpatient rate was 62.8 Maori people secluded per 100,000 versus 9.6 non-Maori per 100,000, a 6.6x rate ratio. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara, 31 Mar 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/news-and-resources/deepening-inequities-in-the-mental-health-system-call-for-action/)
- The public district tables show the national share of adult-inpatient admissions with seclusion fell from 8.3% in 2020/21 to 6.4% in 2023/24, but district-level changes should be read only as approximate: the 2020/21 table included Mental Health Act patients treated in regional intellectual-disability secure services, while the 2023/24 table excluded regional intellectual-disability secure services. [Ministry of Health, 2022](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2022-09/odmhas-regulatory-report-sep22.pdf); [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)
- Public restraint reporting is weaker than seclusion reporting: the 2023 Ministry guidelines cover seclusion and restraint together, but Te Hiringa Mahara's 2026 monitoring says it wants to monitor restraint in future years and that data to support a wider human-rights approach is not currently collected. [Ministry of Health, 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/guidelines-for-reducing-and-eliminating-seclusion-and-restraint-under-the-mental-health-compulsory); [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)
- The public record is materially stale: the 2023/24 regulatory report covers data to 30 June 2024, uses PRIMHD/manual data extracted on 10 June 2025, and was published on 31 March 2026; Te Hiringa Mahara says manual validation means the most recent available seclusion data in its June 2026 monitoring was still 2023/24. [Ministry of Health publication page, 31 Mar 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/office-of-the-director-of-mental-health-and-addiction-services-regulatory-report-2023-to-2024); [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - High for the latest official counts, rates and publication dates; Low-to-Medium for district trend comparisons because the public tables give admission percentages rather than full district time-series counts and the 2020/21 and 2023/24 district tables differ in whether regional intellectual-disability secure services are included; Low for restraint trends because I did not find an equivalent official public national restraint time series.

## Evidence

### What is being measured

The Director reports define the main public series as seclusion for people under the Mental Health Act in adult inpatient wards, and the 2023/24 report excludes forensic inpatient services, regional intellectual-disability secure services and youth services from the adult-inpatient trend figures. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

The Ministry's 2023 guidelines say seclusion and restraint are "not therapeutic" and should be a last resort in emergency situations after less restrictive strategies have been tried; the same publication page says the guidelines support safe reduction and elimination of seclusion and restraint for people subject to the Mental Health Act. [Ministry of Health, 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/guidelines-for-reducing-and-eliminating-seclusion-and-restraint-under-the-mental-health-compulsory)

Te Hiringa Mahara's 2026 system-performance report treats seclusion as one of three human-rights measures, says it would like to monitor restraint in future years, and says wider data on human rights, indigenous rights and agency is not currently collected. [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)

### National adult-inpatient trend

The 2023/24 adult-inpatient series shows fewer people secluded and fewer hours than both 2022/23 and 2009: 561 people were secluded in 2023/24, down from 699 in 2022/23, while the Ministry reports hours fell 24% compared with 2022/23 and 73% since 2009. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf); [Ministry of Health, 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-02/Office-of-the-Director-of-Mental-Health-and-Addiction-Services-Regulatory-Report-2022-23.pdf)

The recent adult-inpatient time series is:

[Ministry of Health, 2021](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2021-11/office_of_the_director_of_mental_health_and_addiction_services_-_2020_regulatory_report_final_v2.pdf)

HQSC's programme reporting points in the same direction but uses a different programme measure: its 2024/25 annual report says the mental-health and addiction improvement programme reduced mean monthly seclusion rates across all ethnicities from 6.4% at the 2018/19 baseline to 4.3% as of September 2024. [Te Tahu Hauora HQSC, 2025](https://www.hqsc.govt.nz/assets/Core-pages/About-us/Annual-reports/Annual-Report-2024-25.pdf?hash=8f61fbfd213c7ff427bcb7a2d1e8a66e6c54d588)

### Maori and Pacific inequity

The latest official adult-inpatient report says Maori made up 55% of all adults secluded in 2023/24 while Maori made up 17% of the general population, and it reports Maori were secluded at 62.8 people per 100,000 compared with non-Maori at 9.6 per 100,000. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

Te Hiringa Mahara independently highlighted the same release-day inequity, saying Maori were 6.6 times and Pacific peoples 1.7 times more likely to be placed in seclusion than non-Maori and non-Pacific peoples, and that Maori accounted for 55% of adults placed in seclusion despite being 17% of the population. [Te Hiringa Mahara, 31 Mar 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/news-and-resources/deepening-inequities-in-the-mental-health-system-call-for-action/)

The inequity improved in absolute rate terms from 2020 to 2023/24 but did not disappear: the Maori adult-inpatient seclusion rate fell from 97.9 per 100,000 in 2020 to 62.8 per 100,000 in 2023/24, while the non-Maori comparison rate fell from 18.0 "other ethnicities" per 100,000 in 2020 to 9.6 non-Maori per 100,000 in 2023/24. [Ministry of Health, 2021](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2021-11/office_of_the_director_of_mental_health_and_addiction_services_-_2020_regulatory_report_final_v2.pdf); [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

HQSC's Zero Seclusion programme data also shows improvement but not parity: its 2024/25 annual report says Maori seclusion rates fell from 9.9% to 6.0%, and non-Maori non-Pacific rates fell from 4.0% to 2.5%, between the 2018/19 baseline and September 2024. [Te Tahu Hauora HQSC, 2025](https://www.hqsc.govt.nz/assets/Core-pages/About-us/Annual-reports/Annual-Report-2024-25.pdf?hash=8f61fbfd213c7ff427bcb7a2d1e8a66e6c54d588)

HQSC's summative evaluation of the mental-health and addiction quality-improvement programme reported earlier Zero Seclusion improvements from baseline to the latest update available to that evaluation: Maori mean monthly seclusion rates fell from 7.84% to 6.68%, non-Maori/non-Pacific rates fell from 4.0% to 3.06%, and Maori mean monthly duration hours per inpatient fell from 4.46 hours to 3.18 hours. [Te Tahu Hauora HQSC, 2023](https://www.hqsc.govt.nz/assets/Our-work/Mental-health-and-addiction/MHA_QI_summative_evaluation_report.pdf?hash=e9e67f9df58954640f78b11fabcca94a88b37037)

### District table signals and caveats

The cleanest district-by-district public table available across recent reports is the percentage of adult-inpatient admissions with seclusion recorded, but it is not a clean performance ranking. The Ministry warns that some services may have low admission rates, which can make their percentage higher than other services; it also changed the district-table scope, with the 2020/21 table including Mental Health Act patients treated in regional intellectual-disability secure services and the 2023/24 table excluding regional intellectual-disability secure services. [Ministry of Health, 2022](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2022-09/odmhas-regulatory-report-sep22.pdf); [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

With that caveat, the tabled admission-percentage figures show apparent reductions from 2020/21 to 2023/24 for Lakes, Nelson Marlborough, Capital & Coast, South Canterbury, MidCentral and Bay of Plenty, and apparent increases for West Coast, Canterbury, Whanganui and Auckland. These are signals for follow-up, not proof that one district performed better or worse than another, especially for small-population districts where admission counts can move percentages sharply. [Ministry of Health, 2022](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2022-09/odmhas-regulatory-report-sep22.pdf); [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

On the latest 2023/24 snapshot alone, the highest tabled percentages of admissions with seclusion recorded were Hutt Valley 10.8%, West Coast 10.7%, Tairawhiti 10.6%, Bay of Plenty 10.5%, Southern 10.4% and Waikato 9.5%, while the lowest were Northland 1.3%, Auckland 2.3%, Waitemata 2.3%, MidCentral 3.0% and Whanganui 3.2%. The Ministry's low-admission warning is directly relevant to small districts such as West Coast, Tairawhiti and South Canterbury, so these percentages should be treated as monitoring flags rather than a district league table. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

Te Hiringa Mahara says there is significant regional variation, including extended periods of zero seclusion in some areas, and treats that variation as evidence that further reduction is possible. [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)

### Forensic and intellectual-disability services

The 2023/24 report separately records 114 people, 315 events and 16,878 hours in forensic mental-health services after excluding three people with prolonged and/or frequent seclusion, with Auckland regional forensic services accounting for 53 people and 8,820 hours. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

The same report separately records 18 people secluded under the IDCCR Act and 7 under the Mental Health Act in intellectual-disability services after excluding five people with prolonged and/or frequent seclusion; including prolonged/frequent intellectual-disability cases changes intellectual-disability-service hours from 15,652 to 49,674 and events from 229 to 1,143. [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)

The Ombudsman release on Wakari Ward 10A is not part of the national annual time series, but it shows why the exclusions and lag matter: on 3 July 2026, the Chief Ombudsman reported inspectors had found one person secluded in a bedroom for around 18 months and other people subjected to prolonged environmental restraint and/or long-term seclusion in an adult intellectual-disability inpatient ward. [Ombudsman, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need)

## What would change this conclusion

- A Ministry, Health NZ or HQSC machine-readable dataset with district-by-district counts, hours, rates, ethnicity, restraint measures and consistent inclusion/exclusion rules through 2024/25 or 2025/26 would change the district and lag conclusions. [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)
- A public restraint dataset equivalent to the seclusion series would change the restraint conclusion; I found policy guidance on restraint and monitoring intent, but not a national public time series for restraint use by district and ethnicity. [Ministry of Health, 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/guidelines-for-reducing-and-eliminating-seclusion-and-restraint-under-the-mental-health-compulsory); [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)
- A human with lived-experience, Maori health, disability-rights or clinical authority should interpret whether "seclusion", "solitary confinement", "environmental restraint" and other coercive practices are being measured in ways that match people's actual experience. [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)
- I did not re-digitise chart-only district rates from Ministry figures; the district comparison uses the tables that expose exact percentages of admissions with seclusion recorded, but those tables are not perfectly comparable because the 2020/21 table included Mental Health Act patients treated in regional intellectual-disability secure services and the 2023/24 table excluded regional intellectual-disability secure services. [Ministry of Health, 2022](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2022-09/odmhas-regulatory-report-sep22.pdf); [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)
- I verified sources by direct PDF download with `curl` where possible; Ministry publication pages were also checked through the built-in web search/open tool because health.govt.nz pages can bot-block plain HTTP clients, while the direct PDF URLs used here downloaded successfully. ## Open follow-up questions

- Can Health NZ publish a quarterly, district-level coercive-practice dataset covering seclusion, physical restraint, mechanical/environmental restraint, ethnicity, age, disability and event duration without compromising privacy? [Te Hiringa Mahara, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)
- Which specific district practices explain sustained low or falling admissions with seclusion recorded, and are those practices transferable to districts with higher tabled percentages such as Hutt Valley, West Coast, Tairawhiti, Bay of Plenty, Southern and Waikato? [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf)
- How should prolonged/frequent seclusion cases in intellectual-disability and forensic services be publicly reported so they remain visible while protecting privacy? [Ministry of Health, 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-03/odmhas-regulatory-report-2023-24.pdf); [Ombudsman, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need)
