# Wakari Ward 10A: public records support 2026 official findings, while 2014 recommendation detail remains Ombudsman-sourced

## Executive answer

- The 2026 public record confirms the Ombudsman inspected Wakari Hospital's Ward 10A on 17-20 March 2026 and publicly found potential human-rights abuses including prolonged seclusion, prolonged environmental restraint, harmful EVAC/bariatric-mat restraint, restriction of dental care and money, toilet/dental care used as behavioural rewards, and possible unlawful medication administration; the full inspection report was not public in the Ombudsman's resource catalogue/search results I checked on 3 July 2026, so these findings currently rest on the Ombudsman's media release plus same-day reporting. [Ombudsman, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [ODT, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/horror-conditions-wakari-ward-some-worst-we-have-observed); [Ombudsman resources search: Wakari](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?search=Wakari)
- The Ombudsman's own 2022 OPCAT report, summarising its September 2014 inspection, says it had already recommended in 2014 that Ward 10A be upgraded/redeveloped, that Southern DHB should consider a purpose-built replacement, and that patients should be able to use the telephone privately; the May 2021 inspection repeated the fit-for-purpose and phone-privacy concerns and made 15 recommendations. I found no independent 2014-era public source for the specific 2014 recommendation wording, so this finding treats that detail as single-origin to the Ombudsman and Low-to-Medium confidence; 1News corroborates only that Ward 10A was acknowledged not fit for purpose as far back as 2014 and appears downstream of the same 2022 Ombudsman statement, while the 2021 Oversight report supports later system-level/Ward 10A concerns rather than the specific September 2014 recommendation wording. [Ombudsman 2022 Ward 10A report page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); [1News, 2 Feb 2022](https://www.1news.co.nz/2022/02/02/chief-ombudsman-scathing-of-environment-at-wakari-hospital-ward/); [Ombudsman 2021 Oversight report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/sites/default/files/2021-12/OMB%20-%20Oversight%20-%20Final%20opinion.pdf); [independent 2014-source absence search, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.google.com/search?q=%22Ward+10A%22+Wakari+2014+Ombudsman+recommendation+upgrade)
- Health NZ and the Ministry have made public commitments, but most are not yet confirmed outcomes: Health NZ says admissions stopped on 24 April 2026, its board agreed on 1 July 2026 to close Ward 10A as a forensic intellectual-disability unit, patient relocation planning began in May, and oversight/care-plan/seclusion-reduction/governance actions have been taken; Health NZ also says some matters involve complex clinical circumstances still subject to further review, that it is working with patients/families, and that staff support/redeployment discussions are continuing. The Ministry says it agreed to investigate and that a District Inspector investigation into two complaints is underway. [Health NZ, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/news-and-updates/health-new-zealand-acknowledges-ombudsman-statement-on-wakari-ward-10a); [Ministry of Health, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/news/ministry-to-investigate-dunedins-ward-10a-concerns-following-ombudsmans-call)
- I found no published independent-investigation terms of reference on 3 July 2026; the Ministry's page says the Ministry and District Inspector are considering legal options for further investigation, which means scope, independence, powers, reviewer identity, affected-person participation, deadlines, and publication commitments are not yet verifiable from public documents. [Ministry of Health, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.health.govt.nz/news/ministry-to-investigate-dunedins-ward-10a-concerns-following-ombudsmans-call); [web search for Ward 10A terms of reference, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.google.com/search?q=%22Ward+10A%22+%22terms+of+reference%22); [Ministry site search, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.google.com/search?q=site%3Ahealth.govt.nz+%22Ward+10A%22+%22terms+of+reference%22)

### 2021 recommendations: what was still live before 2026

The 2021 inspection made 15 recommendations, including separating forensic from non-forensic patients, not imposing medium-secure restrictions on non-forensic patients, providing gender separation where possible, aligning local seclusion definitions with the Ministry definition, recording seclusion correctly, prohibiting facial coverings during restraint, using only approved restraint techniques, making the sensory modulation room operational, providing independent consumer advocacy, rebuilding or at least upgrading the ward, fixing cleanliness/maintenance, giving independent access to drinks unless risk-assessed otherwise, extending activities to evenings/weekends, providing regular Maori cultural support, and enabling private telephone calls independent of staff. [Ombudsman 2022 Ward 10A report page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin)

The Ombudsman's 2026 release does not itemise the status of each 2021 recommendation, but the overlap is material: the 2026 findings again concern restrictive practices, seclusion/environmental restraint, dignity/basic necessities, care delivery, and the ward's physical structure, while Health NZ says the 2026 issues include restrictive practices, prolonged seclusion, rehabilitation opportunities and aspects of care delivery. [Ombudsman, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [Health NZ, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/news-and-updates/health-new-zealand-acknowledges-ombudsman-statement-on-wakari-ward-10a)
