# Votes plus annual reports can reconstruct only a partial floor for recent NZ government digital/IT actuals, not a complete all-of-government total

## Executive answer

- A defensible bottom-up *floor* can be reconstructed for named digital appropriations and capitalised software/intangible additions, but Votes plus agency annual reports still do not support a complete all-of-government digital/IT actuals total. The missing component is mainly operating IT: maintenance, cloud/software subscriptions expensed as services, managed services, telecommunications, cyber, embedded technology staff and vendors, and Crown-entity spend that is not consistently tagged as digital/ICT in public financial statements [Treasury, Estimates 2026/27 introduction](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/est26-v4-intro.pdf); [MBIE Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31275-mbie-annual-report-2024-25); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf).
- The clearest central-government digital line is the Government Digital Services / Government Digital Delivery Agency function: Vote Internal Affairs' 2025/26 Government Digital Services MCA was $35.056m final budget and $32.873m estimated actual before transfer, while Vote Public Service's new Government Digital Delivery Agency appropriation showed a 2025/26 comparator total of $53.966m and a 2026/27 budget of $50.070m after adding the transferred DIA Government Digital Services and Civic Information Services comparators [Vote Internal Affairs Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v4/est26-v4-intaff.pdf); [Vote Public Service Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v4/est26-v4-pubser.pdf).
- Annual reports materially improve the reconstruction and provide some multi-year actual cells: MBIE reports IT costs for actual 2020/21 to 2024/25 of $142.902m, $166.243m, $190.358m, $194.742m, and $204.326m; IR annual reports expose intangible additions/capitalisation for 2020/21 to 2024/25 of $53.444m, $28.083m, $14.346m, $13.196m, and $8.574m; DIA exposes GDS MCA actuals, computer costs, and internally generated intangible additions for 2020/21 to 2024/25; Health NZ has computer-software additions for 2022/23, 2023/24, and 2024/25 [MBIE Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31275-mbie-annual-report-2024-25); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2022](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2022.pdf); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf); [DIA Annual Report 2021/22](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/annual-report/%24file/DIA_AnnualReport_2022_v-2.pdf); [DIA Annual Report 2023/24](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2023-24-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2023-2024-Internal-Affairs-Annual-Report.pdf); [DIA Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report.pdf); [Health NZ Annual Report 2022/23](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Te-Whatu-Ora-HNZ-Purongo-a-tau-Annual-Report-22-23.pdf); [Health NZ Annual Report 2024/25](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Annual-Report-2024-25-Health-New-Zealand-Te-Whatu-Ora.pdf).
- The government's $13b/five-year figure is a forward projection, not a measured actual-spend series: the Beehive release says centralising digital investment and procurement could save up to 30% on "projected $13 billion technology spend across the public sector in the next five years" [Beehive, 22 Sep 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/government-digital-changes-bring-big-savings). The observed named central digital lines are tens of millions per year and the sampled agency annual-report proxies are still well below an implied $2.6b per year, so the projection can only be consistent with recent actuals if it includes much broader agency IT operating, procurement, cloud/software, contractors, and capital spend that is not separately visible in the public-source lines reviewed here.

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the named-line extraction is high confidence where the Vote documents itemise a line, but the all-of-government conclusion is medium because the method proves non-comparability and a floor, not a complete denominator.

## Evidence

### Method and scope

This finding answers whether a multi-year digital/IT actuals total can be reconstructed from the major digital-spending Votes named in issue #319: Vote Internal Affairs, Vote Revenue, Vote Business, Science and Innovation, Vote Public Service, and Vote Health. I used the 2026/27 Estimates and 2025/26 Supplementary Estimates for those Votes, older Vote Internal Affairs Estimates where needed to trace the former Government Digital Services MCA, the 2024/25 annual reports for DIA, MBIE, IR, and Health NZ, and older IR annual reports where the same annual-report intangible-asset line was needed to fill the 2020/21 to 2022/23 cells. Where overlapping annual reports gave current-year and prior-year versions of the same IR line, I used the latest available/restated value for that fiscal year [Vote Internal Affairs Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v4/est26-v4-intaff.pdf); [Vote Public Service Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v4/est26-v4-pubser.pdf); [Vote Revenue Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v4/est26-v4-reven.pdf); [Vote Business, Science and Innovation Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v1/est26-v1-buscin.pdf); [Vote Health Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v5/est26-v5-health.pdf); [DIA Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report.pdf); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2022](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2022.pdf); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2023](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2023.pdf); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2024](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2024.pdf); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf); [MBIE Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31275-mbie-annual-report-2024-25); [Health NZ Annual Report 2024/25](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Annual-Report-2024-25-Health-New-Zealand-Te-Whatu-Ora.pdf).

The strict inclusion rule was: include a line in the reconstructed floor only when the Vote line or annual-report line itself is digital/ICT tagged, capitalised software/intangible spend, IT/ICT equipment, computer costs, or explicitly scoped to digital transformation, digital services, digital identity, system capabilities, digital technologies, e-invoicing/business digital enablers, or technology-capital/intangible expenditure. Broad agency output appropriations were excluded from the strict floor, even where they obviously rely on IT, because the Estimates and annual reports usually do not expose the IT share [Treasury, Estimates 2026/27 introduction](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/est26-v4-intro.pdf); [Health NZ Annual Report 2024/25](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Annual-Report-2024-25-Health-New-Zealand-Te-Whatu-Ora.pdf).

### Annual-report capital and operating disclosures

The annual-report extraction helps most where an agency publishes the same category over several years, as MBIE does for IT costs and software assets and IR does for intangible additions/capitalisation across successive annual reports. It also helps for DIA and Health when older annual reports are included: DIA annual reports provide 2020/21 to 2024/25 GDS, computer-cost, and internal intangible-additions cells, and Health NZ 2022/23 provides a comparable intangible additions line [MBIE Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31275-mbie-annual-report-2024-25); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2022](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2022.pdf); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf); [DIA Annual Report 2021/22](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/annual-report/%24file/DIA_AnnualReport_2022_v-2.pdf); [DIA Annual Report 2023/24](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2023-24-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2023-2024-Internal-Affairs-Annual-Report.pdf); [DIA Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report.pdf); [Health NZ Annual Report 2022/23](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Te-Whatu-Ora-HNZ-Purongo-a-tau-Annual-Report-22-23.pdf); [Health NZ Annual Report 2024/25](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Annual-Report-2024-25-Health-New-Zealand-Te-Whatu-Ora.pdf).

The annual-report lines also use different accounting boundaries. IR states internally generated software, business-process design, and software licences are intangible assets, but maintenance, SaaS configuration/customisation, training, procurement change management, and support are expensed; IR also discloses $22.453m of operating contractors/consultants and $7.725m of capitalised contractors/consultants in 2024/25 without tagging the ICT share [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf). MBIE separately reports IT costs, but its intangible-asset note says ongoing SaaS/service fees that do not create a controlled asset are expensed as service contracts [MBIE Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31275-mbie-annual-report-2024-25). DIA's computer costs are a broad departmental category, and its SaaS implementation costs are capitalised only when control criteria are met [DIA Annual Report 2024/25](https://www.dia.govt.nz/diawebsite.nsf/Files/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report/%24file/2024-25-DIA-Annual-Report.pdf). Health NZ's intangible additions are computer software, but the report does not present a single Data and Digital operating-cost total; its named payroll data-and-digital initiative spent $0 against an $8m 2024/25 budget [Health NZ Annual Report 2024/25](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Annual-Report-2024-25-Health-New-Zealand-Te-Whatu-Ora.pdf).

### Cross-check against the $13b/five-year projection

The Beehive announcement is explicit that $13b is a projected five-year public-sector technology spend and that the possible $3.9b saving is 30% of that projection; it does not present a historical actual-spend series [Beehive, 22 Sep 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/government-digital-changes-bring-big-savings). The December 2025 Beehive release repeated the same $13b/30%/$3.9b formulation while announcing the GDDA transition into the Public Service Commission from 1 April 2026 [Beehive, 16 Dec 2025](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/public-service-digital-transformation-accelerates).

Dividing $13b over five years implies about $2.6b per year of technology spend. That is not contradicted by the Vote and annual-report evidence, because the evidence reviewed here exposes only a small central digital-government floor plus inconsistent agency-level capital and operating proxies; even the broadest 2024/25 subtotal from the target agencies is about $594.4m before the unobserved parts of Health, IR, DIA, MBIE, other departments, and Crown entities are added. The $13b projection also cannot be verified from public financial statements alone, because the statements do not consistently isolate agency operating IT, contractors, managed services, cloud/software subscriptions, embedded digital components of health and tax service delivery, and Crown entity technology spend [Vote Public Service Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v4/est26-v4-pubser.pdf); [Health NZ Annual Report 2024/25](https://static.info.content.health.nz/docs/publications/Annual-Report-2024-25-Health-New-Zealand-Te-Whatu-Ora.pdf); [Inland Revenue Annual Report 2025](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/annual-and-corporate-reports/annual-reports/annual-report-2025.pdf).

The PSC Digital Reset Plan supports the transparency gap: it says the system is poorly informed about the nature and value of technology investment across government, identifies waste/duplication and poor delivery as Cabinet concerns, and recommends resetting prioritisation, repositioning the GDDA, and reforming funding and delivery settings [PSC, Digital Reset Plan 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf). That is an independent reason not to treat the visible Vote lines as a complete actual-spend denominator [PSC, Digital Reset Plan 2026](https://www.publicservice.govt.nz/assets/DirectoryFile/Digital-Reset-Plan-2026.pdf).

## What would change this conclusion

- A GDDA, Treasury, Cabinet, or OIA-released dataset listing annual public-sector technology spend by agency, expense/capital type, Crown entity coverage, and category would change the conclusion from "partial floor and proxies only" to a measured actuals series.
- Agency-level general-ledger or procurement extracts for software, cloud, telecommunications, contractors, outsourced ICT, cyber, devices, data platforms, and capitalised software would allow the hidden operating component to be reconstructed and would test whether annual-report categories such as "IT costs" and "computer costs" are comparable.
- Health NZ data-and-digital operating actuals are the largest unresolved gap in the target Votes; the public Vote Health and Health NZ annual-report documents identify digital investment, cyber work, technology assets, and one payroll data-and-digital initiative, but do not expose a reusable annual Data and Digital operating total.
- I could not verify the methodology behind the $13b projection, the agency coverage, whether Crown entities outside the core public service are included consistently, or whether GST/capitalisation/accounting eliminations are treated consistently.
- I fetched Budget, Treasury, DIA, Health NZ, and IR annual-report PDFs directly with `curl` where direct PDF URLs were available, including IR's 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, and 2025 annual-report PDFs. Budget index pages were Cloudflare-blocked in plain `curl`, and MBIE's document endpoint returned an HTML interstitial to plain `curl`, so I used the built-in web/PDF extraction for MBIE and official direct PDF URLs or official publication pages where available; I did not call blocked index pages dead.

## Open follow-up questions

- What agency coverage and spend categories make up the Government's $13b/five-year projection?
- Can GDDA or Treasury produce an annual actuals table for technology spend using the same definitions as the $13b projection?
- Can Health NZ publish a Data and Digital operating-plus-capital actuals table for 2022/23, 2023/24, 2024/25, and 2025/26, separate from hospital infrastructure and clinical-equipment capital?
- Can annual-report intangible-asset additions, IT/computer costs, IT/ICT equipment, and capitalised contractor disclosures be reconciled to Vote capital expenditure across IRD, DIA, MBIE, Health NZ, and other large agencies without double-counting?
