# NZ mental-health inpatient inspections: the Wakari Ward 10A findings are primary-sourced and follow a warning trail back to 2014, the "Waikato mental-health ward slipped to 2027" starting fact is a conflation, and the national record is fragmented across ~93 per-facility reports with a ~21-month statistics lag

This is the **discover baseline for Stream #224**: it verifies the starting facts, corrects the ones that don't hold, and frames the fan-out. It is deliberately not the deep dive — each deep dive is a research child (#230, #231, #233, #234, #235).

## Executive answer

- **The Wakari findings are real and primary-sourced.** The Chief Ombudsman, John Allen, inspected **Ward 10A at Wakari Hospital, Dunedin — a secure inpatient unit for adults with intellectual disabilities — unannounced, 17–20 March 2026**, under the Crimes of Torture Act 1989 (NZ's OPCAT implementation). His 3 July 2026 media release reports "compelling evidence of punitive and coercive treatment": one person secluded in their bedroom for **around 18 months** with almost no meaningful human contact, restraint on an EVAC/bariatric mat causing physical harm (carpet burns), and staff conditioning access to basic necessities — a toilet, dental care, personal money — on "good" behaviour. He called it "some of the worst practices we have observed anywhere" [Ombudsman media release, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [RNZ, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman); [ODT, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/horror-conditions-wakari-ward-some-worst-we-have-observed).
- **This was a re-inspection failure, not a surprise discovery.** The Ombudsman inspected the same ward in May 2021 and reported (February 2022) that it was "not fit-for-purpose and was in critical need of upgrade and redevelopment," an issue **unresolved since 2014**, making 15 recommendations [Ombudsman 2021 inspection report page](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); the ODT reports Allen noting "next to no improvement" since [ODT](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/horror-conditions-wakari-ward-some-worst-we-have-observed). The 2014→2021→2026 warning trail is the spine of this stream.
- **One starting fact does not hold.** The RNZ story the issue cites for "Waikato Hospital's new mental-health ward has slipped to 2027" is about a **general 28-bed assessment and diagnostic ward near the emergency department** — the article contains no mention of mental health [RNZ, Jun 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/politics/659839/waikato-hospital-ward-will-not-be-ready-until-2027-health-minister-says). Waikato's actual new **64-bed adult acute mental-health facility** ($131.2m) was reported by Health NZ on 30 April 2026 as weather-tight and "expected to open in late December this year" (2026) [Health NZ via Scoop, 30 Apr 2026](https://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/GE2604/S00085/major-milestone-reached-for-waikatos-new-adult-acute-mental-health-facility.htm) — that is the operator's own timeline, a claim, not confirmation. Child #233 should verify the current date rather than inherit the conflation.
- **The fragmentation premise verifies, with a refinement.** The Ombudsman monitors mental-health and intellectual-disability facilities as places of detention under the Crimes of Torture Act 1989 / OPCAT [Ombudsman: monitoring places of detention](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/what-ombudsman-can-help/monitoring-places-detention), and inspection findings land as **~93 individual per-facility reports** in a filterable list, with no evident facility-level national roll-up [OPCAT reports catalogue](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993). As of 3 July 2026 the **full 2026 Ward 10A report was not yet in that catalogue** — the findings are public only via media release and news coverage. Refinement: Ward 10A patients are detained under the **Intellectual Disability (Compulsory Care and Rehabilitation) Act 2003**, not (only) the Mental Health Act 1992 the issue names — the stream must hold both frameworks.
- **National seclusion statistics exist but are badly lagged, and the inequity is stark.** The Office of the Director of Mental Health and Addiction Services **Regulatory Report 2023/24** (successor to the "Director of Mental Health Annual Report" the issue names) covers the year to 30 June 2024 but was published **31 March 2026** — a ~21-month lag [MoH publication page (Wayback snapshot, 31 Mar 2026)](https://web.archive.org/web/20260331002805/https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/office-of-the-director-of-mental-health-and-addiction-services-regulatory-report-2023-to-2024). Te Hiringa Mahara's same-day response reports **Māori were 6.6× more likely to be secluded than non-Māori (62.8 vs 9.6 per 100,000)** [Te Hiringa Mahara release, 31 Mar 2026](https://livenews.co.nz/2026/03/31/health-deepening-inequities-in-the-mental-health-system-call-for-action/); [The Spinoff, 16 Jun 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/the-bulletin/16-06-2026/our-mental-health-services-are-improving-unless-youre-young-maori-pasifika-or-disabled). Meanwhile the **Mental Health Bill** to replace the 1992 Act — introduced 1 October 2024, reported back 17 April 2025 — was still awaiting its second reading as of mid-2026, with advocates warning the planned mid-2027 commencement is at risk [Te Hiringa Mahara](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/news-and-resources/new-mental-health-bill-are-we-there-yet/); [Te Ao Māori News, 1 May 2026](https://www.teaonews.co.nz/2026/05/01/advocates-urge-the-government-to-pass-mental-health-bill/).

**Overall confidence:** High — every load-bearing claim above is either primary-sourced (Ombudsman, Health NZ, MoH publication record) or double-sourced, and the two starting facts that failed verification are corrected, not repeated. Agency commitments (ward closure, relocations, independent investigation) are marked as claims throughout.

## Evidence

### What the Ombudsman found at Ward 10A (March 2026)

The inspection was **unannounced, 17–20 March 2026**, of Ward 10A — described in the Ombudsman's release as an inpatient unit for adults with intellectual disabilities at Wakari Hospital, Dunedin — under the Crimes of Torture Act 1989, which implements OPCAT [Ombudsman media release, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need) ([archived press release text](https://web.archive.org/web/20260703093820/https://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/GE2607/S00008/failures-at-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-highlight-need-for-strengthened-commitment-from-health-agencies.htm)). Findings reported by the Chief Ombudsman and carried consistently by RNZ and the ODT:

- "Compelling evidence of punitive and coercive treatment", with some people "subjected to prolonged environmental restraint and/or long-term seclusion" [Ombudsman release](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman).
- One person **secluded in their bedroom for around 18 months**, with an almost complete lack of meaningful human contact and no access to the outdoors [Ombudsman release](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [ODT](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/horror-conditions-wakari-ward-some-worst-we-have-observed).
- Restraint using an EVAC/bariatric mat that caused physical harm including carpet burns; restrictions on dental care; denial of access to people's own money; staff "incentivising 'good' behaviour by rewarding people with access to basic necessities including dental treatment or the use of a toilet" [Ombudsman release](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need); [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman); [ODT](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/horror-conditions-wakari-ward-some-worst-we-have-observed).
- RNZ additionally reports concerns about **potentially unlawful medication administration** [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman) — this detail was in RNZ's account of the findings; treat as Medium until the full report is published.

No patient or staff member is identified in any source used, and none is identified here.

### The warning trail: 2014 → 2021 → 2026

The Ombudsman (then Peter Boshier) inspected Ward 10a and Helensburgh Cottage on **3–7 May 2021** and published the report on **2 February 2022**, finding "the Ward was not fit-for-purpose and was in critical need of upgrade and redevelopment" — an issue the report noted had been **unresolved since 2014** — alongside concerns about mixing forensic and non-forensic patients and an improperly applied restraint technique; it made **15 recommendations**, including rebuilding or substantially upgrading the ward, and repeated a recommendation from 2014 [Ombudsman 2021 inspection report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin). The ODT independently anchors the trail: the 2022 report "deemed ward 10a 'not fit for purpose'", and in 2026 Allen noted "next to no improvement" to the physical environment since [ODT](https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/horror-conditions-wakari-ward-some-worst-we-have-observed). **Confidence: High.** A repeat-findings pattern across the wider ~93-report corpus is exactly what child #230 should quantify.

### Agency responses — claims, not confirmations (docs/METHOD.md)

Per RNZ, Health NZ's director of mental health Phil Grady said Health NZ **ceased admissions to Ward 10A in late April 2026 and began relocating patients in May 2026**, with commitments to strengthen clinical oversight and seclusion-reduction approaches; the Ministry of Health accepted the Ombudsman's recommendation of an **independent investigation**, and a district inspector was already reviewing two complaints [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/health/660871/potential-human-rights-abuses-uncovered-at-dunedin-s-wakari-hospital-ombudsman). The Ombudsman's release confirms the ward is closed to new admissions and says he will monitor both agencies' progress [Ombudsman release](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/news/serious-failures-adult-intellectual-disability-inpatient-ward-dunedin-highlight-need). **These are the agencies' statements about themselves — claims, not verified outcomes** (relocation destinations, investigation terms of reference, and completion are all unverified). **Confidence in "the agencies said this": High; in "this has happened": not assessed — child #234's core job.**

### The inspection regime, and where its outputs actually live

New Zealand ratified OPCAT in 2007; the Ombudsman's inspection powers come from the Crimes of Torture Act 1989, and the mandate covers "prisons, court facilities, mental health facilities, intellectual disability facilities, aged care facilities, immigration detention centres", with announced and unannounced inspections and published expectations documents for health and disability places [Ombudsman: monitoring places of detention](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/what-ombudsman-can-help/monitoring-places-detention); [Ministry of Justice: OPCAT](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/constitutional-issues-and-human-rights/human-rights/international-human-rights/cat/optional-protocol/). The published output is a filterable list of **~93 OPCAT inspection reports, one document per facility inspection** — recent examples include Te Awhina Unit (Whanganui), Ward OPR1 (Waikato), and Tumanako Inpatient Mental Health Unit (Whangārei), all December 2025 [OPCAT reports catalogue](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?f%5B0%5D=category%3A1993). A site search for "Wakari" returns the 2022 Ward 10a report and 2024 follow-ups for Wards 9A/9B, but **no 2026 Ward 10A report as of 3 July 2026** [Ombudsman resources search](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources?search=Wakari) — the most consequential inspection finding of the year is currently legible only through a media release. Two refinements to the issue's premise: (1) Ward 10A patients are subject to the **Intellectual Disability (Compulsory Care and Rehabilitation) Act 2003** compulsory-care regime (the 2021 report describes the units as secure units for patients with intellectual disabilities requiring compulsory care), so the stream spans both that Act and the Mental Health (Compulsory Assessment and Treatment) Act 1992; (2) whether the Ombudsman's *annual* OPCAT reporting or the Human Rights Commission's central-NPM reporting already aggregates findings usefully is unassessed here — child #230 must check before asserting "no national picture" as more than "no facility-level roll-up found". **Confidence: High** on regime and catalogue facts.

### National seclusion/restraint statistics: they exist, lag badly, and show stark inequity

The national statistics vehicle the issue calls the "Director of Mental Health Annual Report" is now the **Office of the Director of Mental Health and Addiction Services: Regulatory Report**; the 2023/24 edition covers 1 July 2023 – 30 June 2024, spans the Mental Health Act 1992, the IDCCR Act 2003 and the Misuse of Drugs Act 1975, includes seclusion, compulsory treatment orders and ECT — and carries a publication date of **31 March 2026**, ~21 months after the period it reports [MoH publication page, via Wayback snapshot of 31 Mar 2026](https://web.archive.org/web/20260331002805/https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/office-of-the-director-of-mental-health-and-addiction-services-regulatory-report-2023-to-2024) ([live page](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/office-of-the-director-of-mental-health-and-addiction-services-regulatory-report-2023-to-2024) — bot-blocked from this environment, see Sources). Te Hiringa Mahara (Mental Health and Wellbeing Commission) responded the same day: **"Māori were 6.6 times more likely to be secluded than non-Māori"**, with Māori adult-inpatient seclusion at **62.8 per 100,000 vs 9.6** for non-Māori, and Māori 1.6–2.3× more likely to be under compulsory treatment orders depending on order type [Te Hiringa Mahara release, 31 Mar 2026](https://livenews.co.nz/2026/03/31/health-deepening-inequities-in-the-mental-health-system-call-for-action/) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20260331040332/https://livenews.co.nz/2026/03/31/health-deepening-inequities-in-the-mental-health-system-call-for-action/)). The Spinoff, citing Commission monitoring, repeats the 6.6× figure and reports **755 people placed in seclusion in 2024/25** [The Spinoff, 16 Jun 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/the-bulletin/16-06-2026/our-mental-health-services-are-improving-unless-youre-young-maori-pasifika-or-disabled) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20260629185343/https://thespinoff.co.nz/the-bulletin/16-06-2026/our-mental-health-services-are-improving-unless-youre-young-maori-pasifika-or-disabled)); Te Ao Māori News carries the Commission's Director of Māori Health citing Māori as 17% of the population but **55% of those in seclusion** [Te Ao Māori News, 1 May 2026](https://www.teaonews.co.nz/2026/05/01/advocates-urge-the-government-to-pass-mental-health-bill/). Note honestly: the 6.6×/62.8/9.6 figures all trace to the one authoritative origin (the 2023/24 Regulatory Report, as analysed by the Commission) — that is an official statistic rather than a two-independent-sources situation, and the report PDF itself could not be fetched from this environment (bot-blocked; not in Wayback). **Confidence: High that these are the official figures as stated by the Commission; the underlying time series is child #231's job.**

### Corrections and refinements to the issue's starting facts

1. **"Waikato Hospital's new mental-health ward has slipped to 2027" — does not hold as stated.** The cited RNZ article concerns "an assessment and diagnostic centre near the emergency department, providing brief observations and treatment outside the main wards" — a 28-bed general ward, originally expected in the second half of 2026, now expected Q2 2027; the article never mentions mental health [RNZ, Jun 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/politics/659839/waikato-hospital-ward-will-not-be-ready-until-2027-health-minister-says). Waikato's actual mental-health build — the 64-bed, $131.2m adult acute inpatient facility replacing Henry Rongomau Bennett Centre capacity — was reported by Health NZ on 30 April 2026 as weather-tight and expected to open **late December 2026** [Health NZ via Scoop, 30 Apr 2026](https://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/GE2604/S00085/major-milestone-reached-for-waikatos-new-adult-acute-mental-health-facility.htm). That timeline is the operator's own claim; child #233 verifies it. **Confidence in the correction: High** (the article text itself is the evidence).
2. **"The Mental Health Act 1992 governs the units" — incomplete.** Ward 10A is an intellectual-disability compulsory-care unit under the **IDCCR Act 2003** [Ombudsman 2021 report](https://www.ombudsman.parliament.nz/resources/report-unannounced-inspection-ward-10a-and-helensburgh-cottage-wakari-hospital-dunedin); the Regulatory Report explicitly covers both Acts [MoH page via Wayback](https://web.archive.org/web/20260331002805/https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/office-of-the-director-of-mental-health-and-addiction-services-regulatory-report-2023-to-2024). **Confidence: High.**
3. **"Director of Mental Health Annual Report" — renamed.** Now the "Office of the Director of Mental Health and Addiction Services: Regulatory Report" [MoH page via Wayback](https://web.archive.org/web/20260331002805/https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/office-of-the-director-of-mental-health-and-addiction-services-regulatory-report-2023-to-2024). **Confidence: High.**

### The law in motion

The Mental Health Bill — repealing and replacing the 1992 Act — was introduced **1 October 2024** and reported back by the Health Committee on **17 April 2025** after 358 submitters; as of mid-2026 it had not had its second reading, the Commission judges its coercive-practice provisions (including on seclusion) "do not go far enough", and advocates and the Commission warn the stall risks the planned **July 2027** commencement [Te Hiringa Mahara: "New Mental Health Bill — are we there yet?"](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/news-and-resources/new-mental-health-bill-are-we-there-yet/); [Te Ao Māori News, 1 May 2026](https://www.teaonews.co.nz/2026/05/01/advocates-urge-the-government-to-pass-mental-health-bill/) (which quotes the Minister for Mental Health attributing the delay to legislative congestion). **Confidence: High** on the dates and stage; the Bill's substantive effect on seclusion/restraint is child #235's question.

## What would change this conclusion

- **Publication of the full 2026 Ward 10A report.** Everything on the 2026 findings currently rests on the Ombudsman's release and same-day reporting from it. The full report could refine details (e.g. the medication-administration concern RNZ carried) — it should be re-checked immediately by children #230/#234, and could also falsify the "not yet published" claim at any moment.
- **Evidence of an existing national roll-up.** If the Ombudsman's annual OPCAT reporting, the Human Rights Commission's central-NPM reporting, or Te Hiringa Mahara monitoring already aggregates facility-level inspection findings legibly, the stream's "no legible national picture" premise weakens from "doesn't exist" to "exists but isn't reaching decision-makers" — a different problem. Child #230 must check this first.
- **The Regulatory Report PDF itself.** health.govt.nz bot-blocks this environment and the PDF is not in Wayback; direct reading could adjust the seclusion figures as relayed by the Commission (I could not verify totals like people-secluded counts or hours for 2023/24, and deliberately omitted numbers I saw only in un-fetchable or synthesized form).
- **Verification of agency follow-through.** If Health NZ's relocations, the MoH independent investigation, or ward remediation are confirmed by records rather than statements, the claims-vs-confirmed picture changes materially (in either direction).
- **Could not verify:** the full 2026 report text; 2023/24 national seclusion totals (people/hours); the current legislative stage beyond "awaiting second reading" (bills.parliament.nz returns an application error to non-browser fetches — tooling, not absence); which specific facility the relocated Ward 10A patients went to (and no attempt was made to find out at individual level — see below).
- **A human gate is required here.** This is a maximally sensitive domain: detained disabled people, allegations at the threshold of torture, and live agency responses. Per Constitution Art. III, a person with clinical and/or human-rights authority should review before any synthesis of this stream is treated as settled, and lived-experience/whānau perspectives are essential before anything moves past research.
