# Southland, Otago and Bay of Plenty public consent layers can extend dairy expiry screening, but Northland cannot from its current public layers

## Executive answer

- Southland, Otago and Bay of Plenty public consent layers can be classified into dairy water-take and dairy-effluent expiry buckets, but the result is an expiry-screening denominator rather than a production-at-risk denominator because none of the three layers inspected here joins consent rows to milk production or exposes a clean water-allocation volume comparable to Waikato's seasonal allocation layer [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2), [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0), [BOPRC BayMaps web map data](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/11eba444ec124100b7ffc540ca44a7b2/data?f=json).
- Southland is the strongest of the four follow-up regions: its current-consents layer has structured `Type`, `Subtype`, `Primary_`, `Status` and `ExpiryDate` fields, and a conservative classifier found 878 distinct dairy water-take IRIS IDs and 1,181 distinct dairy-effluent IRIS IDs, with 818 water-take IDs and 1,106 effluent IDs expiring by 31 December 2036 [data.govt.nz Southland dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/southland-current-resource-consents3), [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2).
- Otago is classifiable but less clean: its public layer has `ConsentType`, `ConsentStatus`, `ConsentExpiryDate` and free-text `PurposeActivity`; a text classifier found 172 distinct dairy water-take consent numbers and 316 distinct dairy-effluent consent numbers, with 114 water-take IDs and 294 effluent IDs expiring by 31 December 2036 [data.govt.nz ORC dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/orc-resource-consents26), [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0).
- Bay of Plenty is classifiable from the current BayMaps web-map FeatureServer, not from the stale data.govt.nz GeoService link found first: the live web map exposes `ConsentID`, `Subtype`, `Category`, `Status`, `Purpose` and `ExpiryDate`; a conservative classifier found 93 current dairy water-take consent IDs and 523 current dairy-effluent IDs, with 38 water-take IDs and 215 effluent IDs expiring by 31 December 2036 [BOPRC BayMaps web map](https://maps.boprc.govt.nz/maps/11eba444ec124100b7ffc540ca44a7b2), [BOPRC BayMaps web-map data](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/11eba444ec124100b7ffc540ca44a7b2/data?f=json), [BOPRC Consents FeatureServer](https://services1.arcgis.com/TWsoAGve3wb3oFHK/arcgis/rest/services/Consents/FeatureServer/0).
- Northland cannot extend the expiry denominator from the current public point, line and polygon layers: those layers expose `IRISID`, `ActivityType`, `ActivitySubType` and `CurrentStatus` but no expiry date, no dairy classifier and no effluent term in the inspected activity fields [data.govt.nz Northland point dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-point4), [data.govt.nz Northland line dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-line4), [data.govt.nz Northland polygon dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-polygon4), [Northland ArcGIS FeatureServer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - official public layers support the classification result, but Southland, Otago and Bay of Plenty use different schemas, Otago relies heavily on free text, Bay of Plenty required discovery through a web-map item because the older catalogue GeoService is stale, and Northland may hold expiry information outside the public layers inspected here.

## Evidence

### Scope and method

This finding answers issue #890 by testing whether the four regions left unresolved by the earlier denominator finding - Southland, Otago, Bay of Plenty and Northland - have public consent fields sufficient to classify dairy water-take and dairy-effluent records with expiry dates [previous denominator finding](dairy-consent-expiry-denominator.md). I used the vendored `data-govt-nz` skill to locate official catalogue records, then queried official ArcGIS REST services for field metadata and aggregate counts on 2026-07-09 [data.govt.nz Southland dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/southland-current-resource-consents3), [data.govt.nz ORC dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/orc-resource-consents26), [data.govt.nz BOPRC dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents12), [data.govt.nz Northland point dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-point4).

The classifier deliberately uses public purpose/category fields and reports only aggregate counts; it does not publish consent-holder names, coordinates, site addresses, public-document links or individual consent records [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2), [BOPRC Consents FeatureServer](https://services1.arcgis.com/TWsoAGve3wb3oFHK/arcgis/rest/services/Consents/FeatureServer/0). "By 2036" in the tables means expiring on or before 31 December 2036, including the "by 2031" rows; the cutoffs match the previous denominator finding's 2031 and 2036 expiry horizons [previous denominator finding](dairy-consent-expiry-denominator.md).

The classifier rules were intentionally narrow. Southland dairy water-take required a water permit/take signal plus a dairy signal from `Type`, `Subtype`, `Details` or `Primary_`; Southland dairy effluent required an effluent signal plus a dairy signal [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2). Otago dairy water-take required a water-take signal in `ConsentType` or `PurposeActivity` plus "dairy" in `PurposeActivity`; Otago dairy effluent required both "dairy" and "effluent" in `PurposeActivity` [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0). Bay of Plenty dairy water-take required current status, water-take or water-use subtype, and "dairy" in `Purpose`; Bay of Plenty dairy effluent required current status and either `Category = Dairy`, a farm-dairy-effluent purpose, or both "dairy" and "effluent" in `Purpose` [BOPRC Consents FeatureServer](https://services1.arcgis.com/TWsoAGve3wb3oFHK/arcgis/rest/services/Consents/FeatureServer/0).

### Southland: classifiable, with the strongest public schema

Environment Southland's data.govt.nz record says the Southland Current Resource Consents dataset comes from the Consents module of IRIS and is updated overnight, so the data is "never more than 24 hours old" even when the catalogue metadata date implies otherwise [data.govt.nz Southland dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/southland-current-resource-consents3). The ArcGIS layer inspected on 2026-07-09 had 6,932 feature rows and 4,998 distinct `IRISID` values, with fields including `IRISID`, `Type`, `Subtype`, `Details`, `StartDate`, `ExpiryDate`, `Primary_` and `Status` [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2).

Southland's public schema has structured values that directly support dairy classification: `Type` includes `Dairy Consent` and `Water Permit`; `Subtype` includes `Groundwater Take (Consumptive)`, `Surface Water Take (Consumptive)`, `Effluent Storage`, `Establish Dairy Farm`, `Expanded Dairy Farm` and `New Dairy Farm`; and `Primary_` includes `Dairy`, `Dairy Support`, `Dairying - Cows`, `Milk and dairy industries`, `Existing Effluent Storage` and `New Effluent Storage` [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2).

All Southland counts below were computed from the live Environment Southland ArcGIS layer on 2026-07-09, using distinct `IRISID` as the consent identifier where present [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2).

| Southland class | Feature rows | Distinct IRIS IDs | Rows expiring by 2031 | IDs expiring by 2031 | Rows expiring by 2036 | IDs expiring by 2036 | Rows with missing expiry |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Dairy water-take | 1,354 | 878 | 583 | 402 | 1,251 | 818 | 7 |

**Claim confidence:** High for Southland being classifiable into the requested categories with expiry dates; Medium for the exact counts, because a council officer would still need to confirm whether feature rows or distinct `IRISID` values are the right denominator for every renewal question [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2).

### Otago: classifiable, but mostly by free-text purpose

Otago Regional Council's data.govt.nz record says the ORC Resource Consents dataset contains spatial locations of RMA and Building Act consents, permits and certificates and is updated nightly from ORC's consent-management system [data.govt.nz ORC dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/orc-resource-consents26). The ArcGIS layer inspected on 2026-07-09 had 201,818 feature rows and fields including `ConsentNumber`, `ConsentType`, `ConsentStatus`, `ConsentExpiryDate`, `DistrictAuthority` and `PurposeActivity` [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0).

Otago's public schema supports water-take screening through structured `ConsentType` values such as `Groundwater Take Permit` and `Surface Water Take Permit`, but it does not expose a structured dairy-land-use field in the layer inspected; dairy classification therefore depends on the free-text `PurposeActivity` field [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0). This makes Otago usable for screening and expiry buckets, but less reliable than Southland for automated dairy/non-dairy classification [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0).

All Otago counts below were computed from the live ORC ArcGIS layer on 2026-07-09, using distinct `ConsentNumber` as the consent identifier where present [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0).

| Otago class | Feature rows | Distinct consent numbers | Rows expiring by 2031 | IDs expiring by 2031 | Rows expiring by 2036 | IDs expiring by 2036 | Rows with missing expiry |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Dairy water-take | 4,160 | 172 | 432 | 18 | 2,720 | 114 | 48 |
| Dairy effluent | 7,738 | 316 | 856 | 35 | 7,210 | 294 | 24 |

The large gap between Otago feature rows and distinct consent numbers is a material warning: the layer can support expiry-category screening, but feature-row counts should not be read as consent counts without deduplication and council interpretation [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0).

**Claim confidence:** Medium for Otago being classifiable into the requested categories with expiry dates; Low-to-Medium for exact automated counts, because the dairy signal is free text and the feature-row-to-consent relationship is unusually many-to-one [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0).

### Bay of Plenty: classifiable from the current web map, but not from the stale catalogue GeoService

The data.govt.nz "Resource Consents" record for Bay of Plenty says the dataset shows all current, expired and lapsed BOPRC consents and points to an ArcGIS GeoService URL under `gis.boprc.govt.nz/server2/.../MapServer/45` [data.govt.nz BOPRC dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents12). Direct HTTP to that GeoService timed out, the linked GeoJSON download returned a server-error JSON response, and the repo fetch ladder reached the GeoService through Jina and received `404 Service not found`, so this finding does not use the stale catalogue GeoService for counts [data.govt.nz BOPRC dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents12).

The current BayMaps web map is usable: ArcGIS search found the public BOPRC "Resource Consents" web map, and its web-map JSON references `https://services1.arcgis.com/TWsoAGve3wb3oFHK/arcgis/rest/services/Consents/FeatureServer/0` for both "All Consents" and "Current Consents" layers [BOPRC BayMaps web map](https://maps.boprc.govt.nz/maps/11eba444ec124100b7ffc540ca44a7b2), [BOPRC BayMaps web-map data](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/11eba444ec124100b7ffc540ca44a7b2/data?f=json). The FeatureServer inspected on 2026-07-09 had 29,716 feature rows and fields including `ConsentID`, `Subtype`, `Category`, `Status`, `Purpose`, `ExpiryDate`, `GrantedDate` and public-document link fields [BOPRC Consents FeatureServer](https://services1.arcgis.com/TWsoAGve3wb3oFHK/arcgis/rest/services/Consents/FeatureServer/0).

Bay of Plenty's schema is sufficient for expiry screening because `Subtype` distinguishes `Water Take`, `Water Use` and `Discharge`, `Category` includes `Dairy`, and `ExpiryDate` is available, but the layer inspected does not expose allocation-volume or milk-production fields [BOPRC Consents FeatureServer](https://services1.arcgis.com/TWsoAGve3wb3oFHK/arcgis/rest/services/Consents/FeatureServer/0). ### Northland: not classifiable into expiry buckets from the current public point, line or polygon layers

Northland's data.govt.nz point, line and polygon records say they are locations of applications to Northland Regional Council for resource consents and are updated nightly [data.govt.nz Northland point dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-point4), [data.govt.nz Northland line dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-line4), [data.govt.nz Northland polygon dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-polygon4). The public FeatureServer layers inspected on 2026-07-09 exposed only `OBJECTID`, `IRISID`, `ActivityType`, `ActivitySubType`, `CurrentStatus` and geometry-derived fields such as length or area; they did not expose an expiry date, consent-purpose narrative, dairy category, effluent category, allocation volume or water-use volume [Northland point layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/0), [Northland line layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/1), [Northland polygon layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/2).

The Northland layers do contain water-category records: the inspected point layer had 35,785 rows, 12,399 of which matched water in `ActivityType` or `ActivitySubType`; the line layer had 710 rows and 209 water-category rows; and the polygon layer had 591 rows and 324 water-category rows [Northland point layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/0), [Northland line layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/1), [Northland polygon layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/2). The same public layers returned zero rows matching dairy and zero rows matching effluent in `ActivityType` or `ActivitySubType`, and without expiry fields they cannot answer the issue's expiry-denominator question even for broad water categories [Northland point layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/0), [Northland line layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/1), [Northland polygon layer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer/2).

**Claim confidence:** High that the public point, line and polygon layers inspected cannot extend the expiry denominator; Low for any broader claim about what Northland Regional Council holds internally, because the council may hold expiry, purpose and volume data outside these public layers [data.govt.nz Northland point dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents-point4).

## What would change this conclusion

- A Northland public layer or data extract with `IRISID`, expiry date, activity purpose, dairy/effluent category and allocation-volume fields would change Northland from "not classifiable from public layers" to "classifiable", because the current public point, line and polygon layers expose only broad activity type/subtype and status [Northland ArcGIS FeatureServer](https://services2.arcgis.com/J8errK5dyxu7Xjf7/arcgis/rest/services/Resource_Consents_/FeatureServer).
- Council confirmation of the correct consent denominator - feature row, `IRISID`, `ConsentNumber`, `ConsentID`, permit component or replacement-consent group - would change the exact counts for Southland, Otago and Bay of Plenty, because all three public services can represent multiple features or activities under one consent identifier [Environment Southland ArcGIS layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2), [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0), [BOPRC Consents FeatureServer](https://services1.arcgis.com/TWsoAGve3wb3oFHK/arcgis/rest/services/Consents/FeatureServer/0).
- A BOPRC-maintained catalogue update replacing the stale `server2` GeoService route with the current BayMaps FeatureServer would improve reproducibility for Bay of Plenty, because this run found the usable service through the public web-map item rather than through the data.govt.nz GeoService resource [data.govt.nz BOPRC dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents12), [BOPRC BayMaps web-map data](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/11eba444ec124100b7ffc540ca44a7b2/data?f=json).
- A public join from consent IDs to water-allocation volumes, actual use, irrigated area, dairy herd size or milk-solids production would change this from an expiry-screening result to a production-at-risk denominator; DairyNZ/LIC publishes regional dairy production, but that report does not join production to council consent IDs [DairyNZ/LIC NZ Dairy Statistics 2024/25](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf).
- I could not verify renewal outcomes, consent-condition tightening, actual water use, consent-holder production, or whether councils would classify ambiguous free-text records the same way this automated classifier does [ORC ArcGIS layer](https://services6.arcgis.com/ALxeDxaeNjQo1Mf0/arcgis/rest/services/ORC_Resource_Consents/FeatureServer/0).
