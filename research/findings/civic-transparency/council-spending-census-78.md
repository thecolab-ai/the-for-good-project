# A 78-council desk census finds statutory finance documents everywhere in principle, but very little council-published structured spending data

## Executive answer

- This desk census used LGNZ's official list of all 78 regional, territorial, and unitary council websites as the frame, because LGNZ says Aotearoa New Zealand has 78 councils and publishes a comprehensive council-website list. [LGNZ council types](https://www.lgnz.co.nz/local-government-in-nz/councils-in-aotearoa/); [LGNZ council websites](https://www.lgnz.co.nz/local-government-in-nz/councils-in-aotearoa/council-websites-and-maps/)
- National, machine-readable finance baselines exist: Stats NZ publishes local-authority financial/statistical releases in CSV/XLSX form, DIA's Local Councils site publishes historic long-term-plan financial tables in CSV/XLSX, and MBIE says GETS award notices are published as CSV open data. [Stats NZ local authority statistics](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/local-authority-statistics-march-2026-quarter/); [DIA Local Authority Long-Term Plans](http://www.localcouncils.govt.nz/lgip.nsf/wpg_URL/Resources-Download-Data-Local-Authority-Long-Term-Plans?OpenDocument); [MBIE procurement open data](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/cross-government-functions/new-zealand-government-procurement-and-property/open-data)
- The council-by-council website pass found many council pages for plans, annual reports, grants, tenders, suppliers, or procurement, but it did **not** find a general council-published structured supplier-payment or transaction-spending ledger in the bounded homepage/root-sitemap search. The negative claim is **Low confidence**, because council search indexes are uneven and a structured file could sit outside the homepage/root sitemap or behind a document library. [LGNZ council websites](https://www.lgnz.co.nz/local-government-in-nz/councils-in-aotearoa/council-websites-and-maps/)
- The strongest directly verified council-published structured spending-adjacent examples remain narrow: Christchurch City Council's ArcGIS Capital Works Programme has project, activity, budget-band and date fields under CC BY 4.0, while Wellington City Council's ArcGIS `FundingDataWCCbyfund` is an Excel grants workbook last updated in 2018 and has unclear item-level licensing. [Christchurch ArcGIS metadata](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/a96f3f9e87c84608b164557d87874ecb?f=json); [Christchurch ArcGIS layer fields](https://gis.ccc.govt.nz/arcgis/rest/services/CorporateData/Administrative/MapServer/23?f=pjson); [WCC FundingDataWCCbyfund metadata](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/eef4e55911fe40e39a9ec0c0d2640479?f=json)
- The practical answer for a civic tool is: start with national aggregates and named council document pages, treat GETS as procurement-notice evidence rather than a spending ledger, and pilot only where a council has a directly verified structured dataset such as Christchurch capital works. [NZ Government Procurement data and reporting](https://www.procurement.govt.nz/data-and-reporting/)

**Overall confidence:** Medium. The existence of national datasets and named structured examples is High confidence; the "not found" claim about full transaction-level council data is Low confidence because it is based on a reproducible public-web desk search, not council confirmation or LGOIMA requests.

## Evidence

### Scope and method

This finding answers issue #187 by checking the supply side across all 78 councils named by LGNZ, rather than only Auckland, Wellington and Christchurch as in the earlier limited scan. [Issue #187](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/187); [earlier data census](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/civic-transparency/council-spending-data-census.md); [LGNZ council websites](https://www.lgnz.co.nz/local-government-in-nz/councils-in-aotearoa/council-websites-and-maps/)

I treated "spending, budget, and procurement data" as four different evidence levels: statutory financial documents such as long-term plans, annual plans and annual reports; structured all-council finance datasets; procurement/tender/award-notice data; and council-published line-item, project, grants, supplier, or transaction datasets. That separation is necessary because LGNZ describes Long-term Plans, Annual Plans and Annual Reports as the core council planning/reporting documents, while MBIE's GETS data is procurement award-notice data rather than a consolidated finance ledger. [LGNZ how councils work](https://www.lgnz.co.nz/local-government-in-nz/councils-in-aotearoa/how-councils-work/); [MBIE procurement open data](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/cross-government-functions/new-zealand-government-procurement-and-property/open-data); [NZ Government Procurement data and reporting](https://www.procurement.govt.nz/data-and-reporting/)

For each council, I used the LGNZ official website URL, fetched the homepage and root `sitemap.xml` where available with plain HTTP, and searched URLs/link text for `annual report`, `long term plan`, `annual plan`, `budget`, `procurement`, `tender`, `contract`, `spending`, `expenditure`, `capital works`, `open data`, and `grant`. This is a reproducible desk-search method, but it is not equivalent to a council records request or a full crawl of every document-library endpoint. [LGNZ council websites](https://www.lgnz.co.nz/local-government-in-nz/councils-in-aotearoa/council-websites-and-maps/)

Data.govt.nz and MBIE asset downloads intermittently returned Incapsula/bot-protection pages to command-line fetches in this environment, so I used the built-in web fetch/search rung for the pages and marked download-level claims only where an official page or directly fetched ArcGIS metadata supported them. A bot-protection response was not treated as a dead citation, consistent with this repo's ADR-0006 fetch guidance. [Data.govt.nz local councils showcase](https://data.govt.nz/catalogue-guide/showcase/local-councils); [MBIE procurement open data](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/cross-government-functions/new-zealand-government-procurement-and-property/open-data); [ADR-0006](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/docs/adr/0006-fetch-proxy-browser-management.md)

Within the bounded search hits, I did not find CSV, XLS/XLSX, JSON, or GeoJSON URL extensions for finance/procurement/spending pages; that supports a Low confidence conclusion that structured council-published spending data is not easily discoverable from council website front doors. It does not rule out downloads generated by JavaScript, files without extensions, search-only document libraries, ArcGIS services, PDFs, or council-specific portals. ### Licences and reuse terms

The national finance data has clearer reuse terms than many council-level examples: Stats NZ uses CC BY 4.0 unless indicated otherwise, DIA Local Councils pages display CC BY 3.0 NZ, and data.govt.nz says content is CC BY 4.0 unless indicated otherwise. [Stats NZ local authority statistics](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/local-authority-statistics-march-2026-quarter/); [DIA download index](http://www.localcouncils.govt.nz/lgip.nsf/wpg_url/Resources-Download-Data-Index); [data.govt.nz requests CSV result/footer](https://www.data.govt.nz/requests/csv)

Council portal licences are item-specific enough to require per-dataset checks: Christchurch's capital-works ArcGIS item states CC BY 4.0, while the Wellington grants item has no licence value in its item metadata even though WCC's general GIS open-data terms refer to CC BY 4.0. [Christchurch ArcGIS metadata](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/a96f3f9e87c84608b164557d87874ecb?f=json); [WCC FundingDataWCCbyfund metadata](https://www.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/eef4e55911fe40e39a9ec0c0d2640479?f=json); [WCC open-data terms](https://wellington.govt.nz/wellington-city/maps/open-data-portal-terms-and-conditions)

## What would change this conclusion

- A council-by-council confirmation request asking each council whether it publishes reusable supplier-payment, procurement-register, capital-project, grants, chart-of-accounts, LTP spreadsheet, or annual-report data would raise or overturn the Low-confidence negative claim from this web-only census.
- A full crawl of each council document library, including JavaScript search endpoints and PDFs, could find structured downloads that the homepage/root-sitemap method missed.
- A current DIA replacement for Local Councils 2021-31 or 2024-34 LTP tables would change the "historic/dates through 2018-28" assessment for all-council budget data.
- A successful non-bot-blocked download of MBIE GETS CSV files would allow a council-name count of GETS award notices; this pass verified the official MBIE page, but command-line CSV downloads returned Incapsula pages and were treated as blocked tooling, not dead links.
- A direct licence opinion is needed before reusing item-level council data at scale, because general website copyright terms and ArcGIS item metadata can diverge.
- I did not contact councils, lodge LGOIMA requests, or verify whether internal finance systems can export the missing ledgers; this finding is a public-source desk census only.

## Open follow-up questions

- Which councils, if any, will confirm by email or LGOIMA that they publish supplier-payment or transaction-level spending data, and under what reuse terms?
- Can the 2024-34 LTP financial tables be reconstructed into a common CSV/XLSX from council-published plans without hand-keying?
- Which citizen question types from the demand-side finding are answerable from national aggregates alone, and which require council-specific project, contract, or supplier data?
- Would Christchurch's capital-works schema generalise to other councils' capital-programme reporting, or is it too Christchurch-specific?
