# DataGrid Makarewa's 240MW-IT-capacity vs 280MW figures are not a contradiction — 240MW is the consented IT load and 280MW is the total facility power; the SDC and ICC decision documents exist but are not publicly posted and need a register/LGOIMA release

## Executive answer

- **The "240MW vs 280MW" gap is a units question, not a discrepancy, and both numbers are correct for the *same* consented build.** The Environment Southland consent decision describes a data centre with **"up to 240MW of IT capacity"** — the power delivered to the computing equipment (servers/GPUs) [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf). DataGrid's public framing of **280MW** is the *total facility power* the campus draws from the grid: its own site says the park is **"Designed to deliver a capacity of 280 MW"** with a **"world leading projected power usage efficiency (PUE)"** [DataGrid](https://www.datagrid.nz/). The extra ~40MW is the cooling, power-conversion and electrical overhead that sits on top of the IT load — the standard IT-load-vs-total-facility distinction data centres measure with PUE.
- **The ratio is internally consistent.** 280MW ÷ 240MW ≈ **1.17**, which is exactly what a *good* (low) PUE looks like for a hyperscale site, matching DataGrid's own "world-leading PUE" claim [DataGrid](https://www.datagrid.nz/). So the two figures reconcile to one facility: ~240MW of IT load requiring ~280MW of grid supply. (This 240MW consented IT load is separate from — and should not be confused with — DataGrid's stated long-term *ambition* to "grow over time up to 1GW", which has no consent or supply behind it [w.media, 23 Jul 2024](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/).)
- **The Southland District Council (SDC) land-use consent for the data-centre site does exist** and was determined **separately** from the regional consents by the same independent commissioner process; the Environment Southland decision states it adopted an **eight-year lapse period "for consistency" with the SDC consent**, which is direct evidence the SDC decision had already been made with an eight-year lapse [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf). But **no SDC decision document is published on a crawlable public page** — SDC's notified-consents page lists no Datagrid entry (consistent with a **non-notified** grant) and its public register is an ePathway portal, so the exact SDC conditions still require a register lookup or LGOIMA request.
- **Invercargill City Council (ICC) granted a resource consent for the Tasman Ring subsea-cable landing at Ōreti Beach**, which sits within Invercargill City's boundaries — not merely a road-closure approval as the stream's baseline had speculated. ICC Mayor Tom Campbell publicly defended *"the resource consent"* and its effects assessment [ODT — "Invercargill mayor stands by comments…"](https://www.odt.co.nz/southland/invercargill-mayor-stands-comments-over-ai-data-centre-cable); [1News, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.1news.co.nz/2026/03/17/invercargill-mayor-stands-by-comments-over-ai-factory-cable/). As with SDC, the **full ICC decision document is not publicly posted**; the cable conditions reported publicly (500m marine-mammal suspension, 210dB soft-start, toheroa relocation, iwi notification, 35-year term) trace to commissioner **Hugh Leersnyder's** report and overlap the Environment Southland *coastal* permits, so which conditions sit in the ICC land-use consent versus the ES coastal permit cannot be split without the ICC document [RNZ LDR, 13 Mar 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/ldr/589535/massive-ai-factory-could-impact-sea-life-cultural-values).
- **Net effect on the stream's baseline:** the three-council consent claim is now better supported than "company-announced" — the SDC consent is corroborated by the ES decision's cross-reference, and the ICC consent by the ICC mayor's on-record defence of it — but the *exact SDC and ICC conditions remain Low-to-Medium confidence* until a human obtains the two decision documents via the council registers or LGOIMA. The MW reconciliation is settled (Medium-High).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the MW reconciliation and the *existence* of the SDC and ICC consents are well supported by primary/on-record sources, but the two council decision documents themselves could not be retrieved online, so their precise conditions are not independently verified.

## Evidence

### The 240MW-IT vs 280MW reconciliation

Two figures circulate for "the size" of the campus and they measure different things:

- **240MW = IT capacity / IT load.** The Environment Southland regional consent decision — the one primary consent document in the public record — describes the facility as providing **"up to 240MW of IT capacity"** [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf). "IT capacity"/"IT load" is a data-centre term of art: the electrical power actually delivered to the computing equipment (servers, GPUs, storage, network). DataGrid's own park description has long used the same framing — that the park "could reach over 240MW of IT load" [DataGrid / w.media park profile](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/).
- **280MW = total facility power.** DataGrid's website states the park is **"Designed to deliver a capacity of 280 MW"** and claims a **"world leading projected power usage efficiency (PUE), achieved through a rare combination of renewable power and a naturally cool, stable climate"** [DataGrid](https://www.datagrid.nz/). Reporting treats 280MW as the operative grid-draw / nameplate figure — e.g. an energy analysis models the site at "around 2.45 TWh of electricity based on the nameplate capacity of 280 MW" [New Zealand Energy, 17 Mar 2026](https://newzealandenergy.substack.com/p/the-280mw-question). A dedicated **220kV Transpower grid connection** is planned to deliver it [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).

The relationship between them is **PUE** (Power Usage Effectiveness = total facility power ÷ IT power). Here 280 ÷ 240 ≈ **1.17**, i.e. for every watt reaching a server the site draws ~1.17W from the grid, the extra ~0.17W being cooling and electrical losses. A PUE of ~1.17 is low/efficient for a hyperscale campus, consistent with DataGrid's "world-leading PUE" claim and its cool-climate, free-cooling rationale [DataGrid](https://www.datagrid.nz/). So the two numbers describe **one** consented facility — ~240MW of IT load needing ~280MW of total supply — not two different projects or a quiet upsizing. Note the caveat: no single source states the equation "280 = 240 × PUE" outright; this reconciliation is the coherent reading of DataGrid's own IT-load-vs-total-capacity framing plus its PUE claim, which is why it is Medium-High rather than High.

**Keep this distinct from the phase/ambition axis.** The consented build is ~240MW IT / ~280MW total. Separately, DataGrid's phase-1 power contract with Mercury is a **140MW** Power Purchase Option (so first-phase energised load is smaller again) [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/datagrid-signs-140mw-deal-to-power-southland-ai-campus), and its long-term *ambition* is "up to 1GW" — unconsented and unsupplied [w.media, 23 Jul 2024](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/). The 240/280 pair is a single-facility units question; the 140MW/240MW/1GW figures are a phasing-and-ambition question. Conflating the two is the main way public descriptions of "the project" diverge.

### The Southland District Council land-use consent

The Environment Southland decision is explicit that the **SDC application was decided separately** from the regional consents, and that Environment Southland set its own consents to an **eight-year lapse period specifically "for consistency" with the Southland District Council consents** [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf). That cross-reference is strong evidence that, at the time the regional decision was signed (11 March 2026), an SDC land-use consent for the data-centre site at **342 & 370 Flora Road East, Makarewa** already existed and carried an eight-year lapse. DataGrid's announcement independently names SDC as one of the three granting authorities [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), and SDC Mayor Rob Scott and ICC Mayor Tom Campbell both spoke publicly to having read/backed the consent [ODT — "Southern mayors back massive AI factory…"](https://www.odt.co.nz/southland/southern-mayors-back-massive-ai-factory-amid-concern-over-impacts).

What is **not** publicly retrievable is the SDC decision document itself. SDC's own "notified consents and hearings" page lists no Datagrid/data-centre entry [SDC notified consents & hearings](https://www.southlanddc.govt.nz/home-and-property/resource-consents/notified-consents-and-hearings/) — consistent with the land-use consent having been processed **non-notified** (as the regional consents were). SDC's public decisions register is an ePathway portal reached from its resource-consents page [SDC resource consents](https://www.southlanddc.govt.nz/home-and-property/resource-consents/); that portal is an ASP.NET enquiry form (it returned a recoverable server error to a plain fetcher on 4 Jul 2026 and cannot be crawled statically). The earlier SDC application referenced in the fast-track file was **RMA/2002/53409**, placed on hold under RMA s91D over the National Policy Statement for Highly Productive Land [FTA104 application](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf); whether the March 2026 grant is that application resumed or a fresh application number is exactly the kind of detail the SDC file would settle.

### The Invercargill City Council consent

The most consequential refinement to the stream baseline: **ICC granted a resource consent for the subsea-cable landing at Ōreti Beach**, not merely a road-closure/road-controlling approval as [the factual-baseline finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/214) had speculated. Ōreti Beach (at the Dunns Road entrance) lies within Invercargill City's territorial boundary, so the terrestrial cable landing and beach works there require an **ICC** land-use consent, while the coastal marine area below mean high-water springs is Environment Southland's coastal jurisdiction. DataGrid names ICC as a granting authority [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), and — decisively — ICC **Mayor Tom Campbell publicly stood by "the resource consent"**, arguing effects were assessed as not "significant" and that the cable would give Southland "the fastest internet in the country" [ODT — "Invercargill mayor stands by comments…"](https://www.odt.co.nz/southland/invercargill-mayor-stands-comments-over-ai-data-centre-cable); [1News, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.1news.co.nz/2026/03/17/invercargill-mayor-stands-by-comments-over-ai-factory-cable/). A mayor defending "the resource consent" his council granted is on-record corroboration that ICC issued a consent, not just a corridor permit.

The cable conditions reported publicly — suspend work if marine mammals are within **500m**, gradual (soft-start) ramp-up of **210dB** equipment, mana-whenua relocation of any **toheroa** unearthed, advance iwi notification, and a **35-year** consent term against a **25-year** cable design life — come from commissioner **Hugh Leersnyder's** decision report [RNZ LDR, 13 Mar 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/ldr/589535/massive-ai-factory-could-impact-sea-life-cultural-values); [ODT — "Southern mayors back…"](https://www.odt.co.nz/southland/southern-mayors-back-massive-ai-factory-amid-concern-over-impacts). These overlap heavily with the Environment Southland **coastal permits AUTH-20252550-06/-07** already documented in the consent-record finding, so I cannot, from public reporting alone, attribute each condition to the ICC land-use consent versus the ES coastal permit. ICC's public register/decisions are on its own site, which is bot-protected against automated fetchers (HTTP 403/404 to non-browser clients on 4 Jul 2026) [ICC resource consents](https://icc.govt.nz/planning-resource-management/resource-consents/), so the ICC decision document also needs a register lookup or LGOIMA release.

### How a human can obtain the two missing documents

Both councils publish decisions through public registers; where those don't expose the document, the **Local Government Official Information and Meetings Act 1987 (LGOIMA)** requires release on request (subject to withholding grounds), with a 20-working-day statutory limit:

- **SDC** — search the ePathway public register from [southlanddc.govt.nz resource consents](https://www.southlanddc.govt.nz/home-and-property/resource-consents/) for the Flora Road East, Makarewa data-centre consent, or send a LGOIMA request to SDC for *"the resource consent decision and conditions for the Datagrid NZ Partnership Limited data centre land-use consent at 342 & 370 Flora Road East, Makarewa (successor to / related to application RMA/2002/53409), including the commissioner's decision report and the schedule of conditions."*
- **ICC** — request from ICC *"the resource consent decision and conditions granted to Datagrid NZ Partnership Limited for the Tasman Ring subsea cable landing and terrestrial cable route at Ōreti Beach (Dunns Road), including the decision report, notification decision, and schedule of conditions."*

This is flagged for a human because filing LGOIMA requests and awaiting the statutory response is outside an automated research pass.

## What would change this conclusion

- **The SDC decision document.** It would confirm the activity status, the exact conditions (noise, light, earthworks, building, hours), the lapse date, and whether the grant is application RMA/2002/53409 resumed or a new number — upgrading the SDC conditions from Low-Medium to High. Obtainable via the SDC ePathway register or a LGOIMA request (text above).
- **The ICC decision document.** It would settle whether ICC issued a land-use resource consent for the cable landing (as the mayor's language implies) and let the reported cable conditions be split cleanly between the ICC consent and the ES coastal permits. Obtainable via ICC's register or LGOIMA.
- **An explicit DataGrid/consent statement of the design PUE.** If DataGrid or the technical assessment states a PUE materially different from ~1.17, the IT-load-vs-total reconciliation would need revising (e.g. if 280MW turned out to be a later *IT* upsizing rather than total facility power). Nothing found contradicts the ~1.17 reading, but it rests on DataGrid's unquantified "world-leading PUE" claim.
- **Could not verify:** the full text/conditions of the SDC and ICC decisions (not posted online; council registers are ASP.NET/bot-protected and were not machine-fetchable on 4 Jul 2026); the precise SDC application number for the 2026 grant. These need a human with register access or a LGOIMA response — the statutory route is identified above.
