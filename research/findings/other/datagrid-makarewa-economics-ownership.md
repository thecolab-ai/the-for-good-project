# DataGrid Makarewa: ownership, backers, OIO approvals, and defensible capex/jobs figures

## Executive answer

- Companies Office records show a controlled New Zealand holding stack for the Makarewa entities, culminating in `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED`, which is directly controlled by a private Singapore entity: `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited`.
- The same records list **Remi Galasso** and **Perrine Dhalluin (Pagniez/Dhalluin)** as directors of the core Datagrid New Zealand entities, while the local corporate records do not identify individual private investors beyond those registered entities.
- Overseas Investment Office/LINZ evidence shows approval activity for the Makarewa landholding extension (c.6.25 hectares at 342 Flora Road East), confirming foreign ownership scrutiny but not the full capital structure at the limited-partner layer.
- Public capex figures remain inconsistent: RNZ and ODT coverage reference **~$3b**, Newsroom/Spinoff cover **$3.5b**, and NZ Herald labels **$5.1b**; no official public source in this pass provides a single audited “final” capex number.
- Jobs claims are also not harmonised by type: Datagrid-reported construction activity is **~1,200** while RNZ reports a lower **~550**; both converge on **~50** permanent jobs. The 1,200/550 difference is likely definitional (total roles over project life vs peak concurrent on-site jobs) rather than an absolute contradiction.

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the corporate and approval documents are authoritative where available, but ownership-beneficiary details beyond registered entities and a single independently auditable cost/job figure are not fully public.

## Evidence

### 1) Ownership and corporate structure (Companies Office primary records)

Companies Office filings show the following legally recorded structure:
- `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED` (NZ Reg. `9410701`) has directors **Perrine Dhalluin** and **Remi Galasso** and one listed shareholder: **Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited** with 100% control.
  [Companies Office 9410701](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410701)
- `DATAGRID NZ LIMITED` (Reg. `9410784`) is 100% owned by `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED`.
  [Companies Office 9410784](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410784)
- `DATAGRID NZ SERVICES LIMITED` (Reg. `9410788`) is 100% owned by `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED`.
  [Companies Office 9410788](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410788)
- `DATAGRID NEW ZEALAND GENERAL PARTNER LIMITED` (Reg. `8160662`) is itself 100% owned by `DATAGRID NZ LIMITED`, with directors including **Remi Galasso** and **Perrine Anne Laure Pagniez DHALLUIN**.
  [Companies Office 8160662](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/8160662)

These are the strongest non-press sources available in public records for legal ownership links and director identity. The immediate holding parent appears to be an unexposed private partner entity (`Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited`), so claims about individual backers beyond this chain should be treated as secondary.

### 2) OIO / overseas ownership approvals

- LINZ records show **Datagrid New Zealand Limited Partnership** receiving the relevant overseas-investment decision in 2024 for land at **342 Flora Road East, Makarewa**, for an area of **approximately 6.2517 hectares**, consistent with the land expansion context in project reporting.
  [LINZ decision page (202400315)](https://www.linz.govt.nz/our-work/overseas-investment-regulation/decisions/2024-07/202400315)
- The archived Notice of Decision PDF confirms the consent approval letter is addressed to the Datagrid partnership and records the land parcel and associated approvals.
  [Notice of Decision PDF](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/202400315%20Notice%20of%20Decision.pdf), [Archive copy](https://web.archive.org/web/20260704050540/https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/202400315%20Notice%20of%20Decision.pdf)

These approvals establish that the foreign-investment review pathway has occurred for land-use/control steps, but do not alone validate the final stated capex/job envelope.

### 5) Scope mismatch risks (important for interpretation)

- The same debate mixes at least two project scopes: the announced regional consent footprint and longer-term ambition language (for example, broader scaling aspirations in filings and commentary). Without a single canonical baseline document that maps “planned phase 1” and “full buildout,” cost and employment figures should not be treated as directly comparable.
- In this research path, the defensible baseline is therefore: `reported project cost and jobs figures are public statements by outlet/company, not yet reconciled official totals`, and the only confirmed legally recorded entities are the Companies Office and OIO/LINZ filings.

## What would change this conclusion

- If Datagrid published a full, audited pro-forma with a reconciled project scope (phase-by-phase) and independent verification of capex and job impacts, this conclusion would be materially stronger and could be shifted to High confidence.
- If Companies Office, LINZ, or OIO were to release the partner-level beneficial ownership documents publicly (or court-obtained filings disclosed), a currently uncertain “beneficial/backer” conclusion could become High confidence.
- If the MBIE/energy or council records include a definitive single “economic impact statement” for the Makarewa approval package, current uncertainty around GDP impact claims would reduce substantially.

## What could not verify

- No publicly discoverable, independently audited, single-source capex or jobs/GDP reconciliation report was found for the same final scope (e.g., phase 1 versus all phases).
- Final beneficial-owner details for the ultimate parent `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited` were not publicly available through Companies Office records in this pass.
- The project appears to be represented through multiple scopes (announced/buildout vs consented phase), so “the” headline capex/jobs number is not directly derivable from a single cited public line-item source.
