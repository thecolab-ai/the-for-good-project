# DataGrid Makarewa is now held through a corporate NZ/Singapore chain; public capex and jobs figures mix different scopes, currencies, and job measures

## Executive answer

- The current Companies Office record does **not** show an individual directly owning the NZ operating companies. As at 4 July 2026, `DATAGRID NZ LIMITED` and `DATAGRID NZ SERVICES LIMITED` are wholly owned by `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED`; `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED` is wholly held by `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited`; and the older `DATAGRID NEW ZEALAND GENERAL PARTNER LIMITED` is wholly owned by `DATAGRID NZ LIMITED` [Companies Office, DATAGRID NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410784); [Companies Office, DATAGRID NZ SERVICES LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410788); [Companies Office, DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410701); [Companies Office, DATAGRID NEW ZEALAND GENERAL PARTNER LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/8160662).
- The best public ownership reconstruction is: the project began through `Datagrid New Zealand Limited Partnership`, OIO-approved in 2021 for the Makarewa land; BW Digital/BW Group then appears in 2022-2023 records as a major upstream backer; by 2025, an OIO farm-land exemption records an upstream transaction in which `DG Founders General Partner Pte Ltd` could acquire up to 100% of `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited` [LINZ 2021 OIO assessment report](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/oio_assessment-report_datagrid-nz_20220228.pdf); [LINZ 2023 BW Digital exemption](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/doc/202300050%20Decision%20Letter.pdf); [LINZ 2025 DG Founders exemption](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/doc/Notice%20of%20Decision-%20202500359-%20Exemption.pdf). Current ultimate beneficial ownership behind `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited` is **not visible** in the NZ Companies Office record.
- The defensible current capex range is **NZ$3.5b to NZ$5.1b**, not a clean single number. NZ$3.5b is the repeated 2026 public figure for the Makarewa AI/data-centre project [Newsroom, 16 Mar 2026](https://newsroom.co.nz/2026/03/16/from-dairy-to-data-the-3-5-billion-bet-on-southland/); [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). The Herald's NZ$5.1b headline is best read as the same project framed as a **US$3b** investment converted to NZ dollars, but the article also refers to a **US$3.5b** data centre, so the precise basis remains unclear without DataGrid financing or consent-economic schedules [NZ Herald, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/). The official 2021 OIO record only supports an earlier "over NZ$1b" development expectation for a smaller 100-150MW plan [LINZ 2021 OIO assessment report](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/oio_assessment-report_datagrid-nz_20220228.pdf).
- Jobs figures are not directly comparable. The most defensible public statement is: about **50-72 permanent operational roles**, plus construction-phase impacts reported as **550 on-site workers**, **1,200 short-term construction jobs**, or **close to 5,400 construction-phase jobs** depending on source and likely definition [RNZ, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power); [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent); [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project); [NZ Herald, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/). I found no independent public economic model separating direct jobs, peak on-site workers, job-years, and wider induced employment.
- The University of Otago anchor-tenant story is real but older: Otago announced in 2022 that a strategic agreement paved the way for it to become an anchor tenant of a then-150MW/43ha Datagrid facility, while MBIE's 2025 briefing still described Otago as "set to be an anchor tenant" [University of Otago, 3 Jun 2022](https://www.otago.ac.nz/news/newsroom/datagrid-welcomes-university-of-otago-as-future-anchor-tenant); [MBIE briefing, 27 May 2025](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025?utm=). I found no 2026 Otago procurement or service-start record confirming that the 2022 tenant arrangement survived unchanged into the 280MW AI-factory proposal.

**Overall confidence:** Medium — Companies Office and OIO records are strong for the visible company chain and land/transaction history, but ultimate beneficial ownership, current backers after the 2025 upstream transaction, financing, and job/GDP definitions are not fully public.

## Evidence

### Current NZ corporate structure

The NZ register shows four relevant registered companies using the DataGrid name. `DATAGRID NZ LIMITED` (company no. 9410784, incorporated 5 March 2026) is a registered limited company wholly owned by `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED` [Companies Office, DATAGRID NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410784). `DATAGRID NZ SERVICES LIMITED` (company no. 9410788, incorporated 5 March 2026) is also wholly owned by `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED` [Companies Office, DATAGRID NZ SERVICES LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410788). `DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED` (company no. 9410701, incorporated 5 March 2026) is wholly held by `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited` [Companies Office, DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410701). `DATAGRID NEW ZEALAND GENERAL PARTNER LIMITED` (company no. 8160662, incorporated 17 February 2021) is wholly owned by `DATAGRID NZ LIMITED` [Companies Office, DATAGRID NEW ZEALAND GENERAL PARTNER LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/8160662).

The current registered directors visible across these entities are Rémi Galasso and Perrine Dhalluin/Pagniez ep. Dhalluin [Companies Office, DATAGRID NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410784); [Companies Office, DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410701). I have deliberately not reproduced residential-address details from the register because they are not needed to answer the public-interest question.

This means the NZ public register can support a **corporate ownership chain**, but not a clean statement that any named individual currently owns a given percentage of the project. Press reports saying Rémi Galasso is the majority owner are consistent with older OIO and press material, but they are not independently derivable from the current NZ register alone [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project); [NZ Herald, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/).

### OIO record and backer history

The 2021 OIO assessment identifies the applicant as `Datagrid New Zealand Limited Partnership`, a New Zealand limited partnership formed on 1 March 2021, seeking consent to acquire about 42.8167ha of sensitive land at Taylors Road / Flora Road East, Makarewa for a carbon-neutral hyperscale data centre [LINZ 2021 OIO assessment report](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/oio_assessment-report_datagrid-nz_20220228.pdf). That 2021 report also says the applicant expected to spend more than NZ$100m before operations and that Datagrid would cost over NZ$1b to develop, with an initial 100MW plan scalable to 150MW [LINZ 2021 OIO assessment report](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/oio_assessment-report_datagrid-nz_20220228.pdf). LINZ granted that consent on 23 December 2021 [LINZ 2021 OIO decision page](https://www.linz.govt.nz/our-work/overseas-investment-regulation/decisions/2021-12/202100622).

In 2022, the University of Otago's announcement described Datagrid New Zealand LP as a subsidiary of BW Digital and said Hawaiki shared the same parent as Datagrid, BW Digital [University of Otago, 3 Jun 2022](https://www.otago.ac.nz/news/newsroom/datagrid-welcomes-university-of-otago-as-future-anchor-tenant). DCD reported that BW Group had acquired a 37.5% stake in Datagrid's holding company and that the remaining Datagrid/Hawaiki structure was led by co-founder Rémi Galasso [DataCenterDynamics, 1 Jun 2022](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/university-of-otago-named-anchor-tenant-of-datagrids-proposed-southland-data-center/). A 2023 OIO exemption letter says BW Digital Pte. Ltd was one of two shareholders in `H2 Cable General Partner Pte. Limited` and sought to acquire the remaining shares in that general partner, which held the land/project interest indirectly through Datagrid New Zealand Limited Partnership [LINZ 2023 BW Digital exemption](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/doc/202300050%20Decision%20Letter.pdf).

The more recent 2025 OIO farm-land offer exemption records another upstream step: `DG Founders General Partner Pte Ltd` was exempted for a qualifying transaction involving acquisition of up to 100% of shares in `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited`, including indirect interests in both the original 42.8167ha parcel and an additional 6.2517ha parcel at 342 Flora Road East [LINZ 2025 DG Founders exemption](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/doc/Notice%20of%20Decision-%20202500359-%20Exemption.pdf). The same decision explains that the transaction was between the holding company's current majority and minority shareholders, and that the majority shareholder was the only appropriate purchaser under the shareholder agreement [LINZ 2025 DG Founders exemption](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/doc/Notice%20of%20Decision-%20202500359-%20Exemption.pdf). Herald reporting gives the more specific but secondary account: the 2021 application had the Galasso Family Trust as ultimate beneficial owner of 62.5% and BW Digital/BW Group at 37.5%, and a November 2025 OIO update said BW's stake had been sold to a Galasso-associated firm [NZ Herald, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/).

Malcolm Dick is best treated as an early founder/backer, not a current visible owner. DCD and earlier technology press report DataGrid as founded/backed by Hawaiki Cable founder Rémi Galasso and CallPlus founder Malcolm Dick [DataCenterDynamics, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [Bill Bennett, 17 Dec 2020](https://billbennett.co.nz/datagrid-southland-data-centre-submarine-cables/). I found no current Companies Office shareholding or directorship for Malcolm Dick in the four NZ DataGrid companies listed above [Companies Office, DATAGRID NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410784); [Companies Office, DATAGRID HOLDING GROUP NZ LIMITED](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410701).

### Land approvals

There are three relevant OIO/LINZ land or upstream-transaction records. First, the 2021 consent covered about 42.8167ha at Taylors Road / Flora Road East, Makarewa [LINZ 2021 OIO assessment report](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/oio_assessment-report_datagrid-nz_20220228.pdf). Second, LINZ approved Datagrid New Zealand Limited Partnership's acquisition of about 6.2517ha at 342 Flora Road East on 16 July 2024 [LINZ 2024 notice of decision](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/202400315%20Notice%20of%20Decision.pdf). ### Capex reconciliation

The 2021 OIO record is useful as a floor and historical baseline, not as a current capex figure. It describes an initial 100MW plan scalable to 150MW, with more than NZ$100m of pre-operational spend and over NZ$1b expected development cost [LINZ 2021 OIO assessment report](https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/oio_assessment-report_datagrid-nz_20220228.pdf). The 2022 University of Otago announcement similarly described a first module costing more than NZ$100m and Datagrid plus Hawaiki Nui costing over NZ$1b [University of Otago, 3 Jun 2022](https://www.otago.ac.nz/news/newsroom/datagrid-welcomes-university-of-otago-as-future-anchor-tenant).

The 2026 public project is materially larger: DataGrid announced a 280MW, 78,000sqm consented hyperscale data-centre initiative [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), and existing stream research has verified regional consents for up to 240MW IT capacity and 280MW power consumption [#214 finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/datagrid-makarewa-consents.md). Newsroom and The Spinoff both frame the current project as a NZ$3.5b bet/build [Newsroom, 16 Mar 2026](https://newsroom.co.nz/2026/03/16/from-dairy-to-data-the-3-5-billion-bet-on-southland/); [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project).

The Herald's NZ$5.1b figure does not appear to be a separate official capex estimate. The Herald article describes the project as US$3b / NZ$5.1b near the top, while later referring to a US$3.5b data centre; it also focuses on funding, power, Transpower connection, and the separate Tasman Ring cable as unresolved hurdles [NZ Herald, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/). Because the article itself contains both US$3b and US$3.5b wording, the safest reconciliation is: NZ$5.1b is a currency-converted US-dollar framing in a press article, not proof of a different consented build scope. The exact basis would need DataGrid's financing materials, the economic assessment lodged with consents, or an official investor/debt document.

### Jobs, GDP, and "who gains"

The jobs numbers describe different things and should not be averaged. DataGrid's own March 2026 release says the initiative is expected to create more than 1,200 skilled and technical jobs during construction [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent). The Spinoff repeats a similar "1,200 short-term jobs during construction" figure and says ongoing staff would be about 50 people [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). RNZ reports up to 550 construction workers on-site and about 50 permanent staff [RNZ, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power). The Herald, citing the environmental-consent application, reports 72 full-time operational jobs, nearly 5,400 construction-phase jobs, and NZ$36m GDP per year [NZ Herald, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/).

The defensible interpretation is that 550 is likely a peak or on-site workforce figure; 1,200 is a proponent construction-job claim; and 5,400 is likely a broader construction-phase economic-impact/job-year measure from application material. However, I found no public primary economic report defining those units, so that interpretation is Medium confidence rather than a settled finding. The "who gains" public question is active: ODT's March 2026 story frames the project around who benefits from a NZ$3.5b AI/data-centre build near Invercargill [ODT, 15 Mar 2026](https://www.odt.co.nz/southland/who-gains-southlands-massive-new-ai-data-centre).

### University of Otago tenant status

The University of Otago anchor-tenant report is not just trade-press repetition. Otago announced in June 2022 that a strategic agreement with Datagrid paved the way for it to become an anchor tenant of the proposed 150MW facility, with Datagrid offering customised hosting and colocation services to the university [University of Otago, 3 Jun 2022](https://www.otago.ac.nz/news/newsroom/datagrid-welcomes-university-of-otago-as-future-anchor-tenant). DCD reported the same arrangement and described Otago as the first customer of the planned facility [DataCenterDynamics, 1 Jun 2022](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/university-of-otago-named-anchor-tenant-of-datagrids-proposed-southland-data-center/). MBIE's 2025 ministerial briefing still described the University of Otago as set to be an anchor tenant [MBIE briefing, 27 May 2025](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025?utm=). Because the 2022 arrangement was attached to a smaller, 2024-targeted 150MW project, and because no 2026 Otago contract or procurement record was found, this finding treats Otago as a reported/proposed anchor tenant, not a verified current operating customer.

## What would change this conclusion

- Full current upstream filings for `Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Limited`, `DG Founders General Partner Pte Ltd`, and any relevant limited-partnership register entries would change the ownership conclusion from "visible corporate chain" to a firmer beneficial-ownership finding.
- The unredacted OIO ownership-structure attachment, shareholder agreement, or 2025 OIO transaction file would confirm whether the Herald's Galasso/BW percentages and BW-sale account are exactly right.
- A DataGrid financing close, investor memorandum, or consent economic assessment with a clear project scope and currency would settle whether the current project should be described as NZ$3.5b, US$3b/NZ$5.1b, or another figure.
- A primary economic-impact report defining "jobs", "FTE", "job-years", peak site workforce, operational FTE, and GDP contribution would settle the 550 / 1,200 / 5,400 construction jobs spread and the NZ$36m annual GDP claim.
- A current University of Otago contract/procurement record or statement would show whether the 2022 anchor-tenant arrangement still applies to the 2026 280MW AI-factory version of the project.
