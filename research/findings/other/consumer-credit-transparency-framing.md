# NZ small-credit transparency should be framed as a total-cost help front door, not a flat fee comparison

## Executive answer

- The core problem is real enough to research as a stream: small consumer credit in New Zealand spans personal loans, overdrafts, credit cards, BNPL, and high-cost credit, and the law treats those products differently enough that a plain fee table would risk misleading people [Consumer Protection](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/help-product-service/borrowing-money/what-lenders-must-do), [MBIE](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/consumer-protection/buy-now-pay-later), [Commerce Commission](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/business/credit-providers/buy-now-pay-later/).
- The right framing is "total cost of credit for realistic scenarios, plus eligibility and risk notes, plus a route to human help", because the CCCFA covers lender disclosure, responsible lending, reasonable fees, and high-cost-credit limits, while MoneyTalks is a public financial-mentoring front door funded through MSD's Building Financial Capability work [Consumer Protection](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/help-product-service/borrowing-money/what-lenders-must-do), [Commerce Commission](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/business/credit-providers/changes-to-credit-laws/), [MSD](https://www.msd.govt.nz/what-we-can-do/providers/building-financial-capability/moneytalks/index.html).
- Regulator-facing value is plausible: MBIE records the Commerce Commission's 2024 personal-banking market study as finding that New Zealand's four largest banks do not face strong competition in personal banking services and as making 14 recommendations to promote competition, while FMA CoFI material says financial institutions must have fair conduct programmes designed to ensure compliance with the fair conduct principle [MBIE](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/business/competition-regulation-and-policy/market-studies/competition-study-into-personal-banking-services), [FMA](https://www.fma.govt.nz/assets/Reports/Fair-conduct-programme-insights-report.pdf).
- The stream should fan out before ideation: I opened five research children on total-cost scenarios, disclosure comparability, regulatory/advice boundaries, vulnerable-borrower impacts, and data/referral requirements [#67](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/67), [#68](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/68), [#69](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/69), [#70](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/70), [#71](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/71).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the regulatory ownership and public-source landscape are clear from official sources, but this discover memo does not yet calculate product-level total costs or validate user needs with borrowers, financial mentors, lenders, or regulators.

## Evidence

### Problem statement and affected users

The discover issue frames the problem as the difficulty of seeing the all-in cost of small borrowing across personal loans, overdrafts, credit cards, BNPL, and high-cost or short-term loans, with particular risk for lower-income households and people under financial pressure [issue #60](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/60). Stats NZ reported that 9.1 percent of people lived in households in material hardship in the year ended June 2025, which supports prioritising clarity for people with little room to absorb extra borrowing costs [Stats NZ](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/household-income-and-housing-cost-statistics-year-ended-june-2025/). MoneyTalks is funded through Building Financial Capability to provide free advice to individuals and whanau who want to improve financial wellbeing, and MSD says trained financial mentors can connect people with local BFC providers [MSD](https://www.msd.govt.nz/what-we-can-do/providers/building-financial-capability/moneytalks/index.html).

### Product scope

Consumer Protection says CCCFA responsible-lending rules apply to providers of mortgages, loans, agreed overdrafts, BNPL accounts, buying on credit, truck shops, consumer leases, and home buy-backs, and that responsible lending covers advertising, loan agreements, affordability assessments, and limits on interest and fees [Consumer Protection](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/help-product-service/borrowing-money/what-lenders-must-do). RBNZ publishes lending and monetary statistics that include bank and non-bank sector lending, credit-card balances and average interest rates, credit-card spending, and credit conditions, so aggregate official data exists for some parts of the small-credit landscape but not for every product-level fee or eligibility rule [RBNZ](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/statistics/series/lending-and-monetary).

BNPL needs separate treatment from ordinary interest-bearing loans because MBIE says BNPL lets consumers access goods or services now and pay later in interest-free instalments, and because BNPL providers were not automatically required to comply with CCCFA rules before the 2024 changes when they did not charge interest, credit fees, or take a security interest [MBIE](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/consumer-protection/buy-now-pay-later). The Commerce Commission says the law changed on 2 September 2024 so BNPL contracts are declared consumer credit contracts and BNPL lenders need to comply with lender obligations unless an exemption applies [Commerce Commission](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/business/credit-providers/buy-now-pay-later/).

High-cost credit also needs separate treatment because the CCCFA caps the recoverable rate of charge under a high-cost consumer credit contract at 0.8 percent per day, and the Commerce Commission summarises related changes as including a total cost cap, a ban on compound interest, and a presumption that default fees above $30 are unreasonable unless the lender proves otherwise [NZ Legislation](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2003/52/en/latest/), [Commerce Commission](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/business/credit-providers/changes-to-credit-laws/).

### Current information map

The public information landscape appears to have at least four layers that need separate provenance: regulator and consumer-protection guidance, statutory rules, lender-owned product pages or disclosure documents, and third-party calculators or comparators. Consumer Protection summarises lender duties for borrowers, NZ Legislation publishes the CCCFA text, and RBNZ publishes aggregate credit statistics [Consumer Protection](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/help-product-service/borrowing-money/what-lenders-must-do), [NZ Legislation](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2003/52/en/latest/), [RBNZ](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/statistics/series/lending-and-monetary). Lender-owned examples show that rates, fees, eligibility, and terms can sit in different places: ASB's personal-loan page gives an example total interest cost and says assessment considers regular income, account operation, and credit history, while ANZ's personal-loan rates page states a fixed-rate range, default-rate treatment, and rate-change caveats [ASB](https://www.asb.co.nz/personal-loans), [ANZ](https://www.anz.co.nz/rates-fees-agreements/personal-loans/).

Existing public education and comparison material is useful but not sufficient as the only artifact. Sorted's personal-loans guide tells users to add up total costs and notes that many personal loans have establishment or documentation fees, while MoneyHub's personal-loan comparison lists rates and fees across lenders but is a third-party guide rather than a regulator-owned source of canonical lender data [Sorted](https://sorted.org.nz/guides/tackling-debt/personal-loans/), [MoneyHub](https://www.moneyhub.co.nz/personal-loans.html).

### Regulatory desks and product guardrails

The Commerce Commission is the natural desk for CCCFA disclosure, responsible lending, and high-cost-credit rules because its credit-law guidance summarises lender obligations and statutory changes for credit providers [Commerce Commission](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/business/credit-providers/changes-to-credit-laws/). FMA is the natural desk for CoFI fair conduct because its fair conduct programme report says CoFI requires financial institutions to be licensed by FMA and to establish, implement, maintain, and comply with an effective fair conduct programme designed to ensure compliance with the fair conduct principle [FMA](https://www.fma.govt.nz/assets/Reports/Fair-conduct-programme-insights-report.pdf). MBIE is a natural policy desk because it publishes consumer-credit policy material including the BNPL reforms and the personal-banking market-study government response page [MBIE BNPL](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/consumer-protection/buy-now-pay-later), [MBIE banking study](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/business/competition-regulation-and-policy/market-studies/competition-study-into-personal-banking-services).

The proposed AI "reality-check" layer must stay on the information-and-education side until a qualified human confirms the advice boundary. FMA says a financial advice provider is an individual or entity that provides a financial advice service, and that a person providing advice to retail clients must hold a FAP licence or be an authorised body under someone else's licence [FMA FAP](https://www.fma.govt.nz/business/services/financial-advice-provider/). FMA also says financial advisers must be registered, while nominated representatives have less discretion and can provide advice only under provider processes and controls [FMA adviser](https://www.fma.govt.nz/business/services/financial-adviser/).

## What would change this conclusion

- This framing would weaken if child research finds that product-level public disclosures are too unstable, too licence-restricted, or too non-standardised to support defensible total-cost scenarios.
- This framing would change if FMA, Commerce Commission, MBIE, a qualified NZ financial-services lawyer, or a licensed FAP concludes that the intended reality-check layer cannot be safely delivered as general information and education.
- This framing would change if financial mentors or hardship-service providers say a comparison front door would distract from better first-line interventions, or if they do not want referral/warm-handoff integration.
- I could not verify current product-level costs across enough lenders to recommend any named comparison set; that belongs in [#67](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/67) and [#70](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/70).
- I did not contact regulators, lenders, borrowers, MoneyTalks, FinCap, or other NFPs; human validation is required before the stream moves from research into ideation.
