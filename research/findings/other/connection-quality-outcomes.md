# Round-2 rural connection-quality evidence mostly confirms the prior observed-outcomes finding

## Executive answer

- This round-2 finding should be read as a reconciliation and update to the round-1 answer for issue #587, not as an independent second answer: issue #587 already found that rural connectivity evidence is clearest for service access, farm operations, and learning continuity, while public sources still do not provide national causal outcome totals [issue #587](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/587), [round-1 finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-observed-outcomes.md), [issue #683](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/683).
- The additional round-2 sources do not overturn the round-1 conclusion. MPI and National Infrastructure add useful service-use and farm-technology examples, and MBIE's 2021 report adds a business-connectivity barrier, but none provides a national count of missed health care, learning loss, farm incidents, lost farm production, revenue loss, or business closures caused by rural connection quality [MPI, 2022](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/news/media-releases/boosting-rural-connectivity-aims-to-deliver-sustainable-benefits-to-kiwi-farmers), [National Infrastructure, 2026](https://nationalinfrastructure.govt.nz/our_project/video-life-saving-connectivity-under-threat-in-remote-far-north/), [MBIE, 2021](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/17055-understanding-the-digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses).
- The strongest health evidence remains the same as round 1: the Ministry of Health's 2025 rural roadshow records poor rural broadband limiting telehealth expansion in places including Hanmer Springs and Thames, and the NZ Telehealth Leadership Group's DHB stocktake found 15 of 20 DHBs cited patient access to devices and 12 cited patient access to fast internet as telehealth barriers [Ministry of Health, 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/rural-health-roadshow-insights-report.pdf), [NZ Telehealth Leadership Group, 2020](https://www.telehealth.org.nz/assets/Uploads/Telehealth-Full-Report-2020-V7-Pages.pdf).
- The strongest farm-adoption evidence also remains the same as round 1: AgriTechNZ's 2022 baseline surveyed more than 1,000 farmers and growers and found connectivity matters for adoption decisions, but on-farm internet-connectivity ratings alone were not a significant differentiator of adoption segments [AgriTechNZ, 2022](https://trustalliance.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/AgriTechNZ-Digital-Adoption-in-Primary-Industries-Report-2022.pdf).
- The practical implication is unchanged: a volunteer or policy intervention should start with service-quality measurement, outage resilience, and narrow sector workflows rather than claiming a national outcome total that the public evidence does not support [Commerce Commission, 2026](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/2025-Telecommunications-Monitoring-Report-29-June-2026.pdf), [round-1 finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-observed-outcomes.md).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - multiple NZ sources support access, continuity, service-quality, and adoption-barrier links, but the best available public evidence still stops short of national causal outcome totals by rural connection quality.

## Evidence

### Relationship to the round-1 finding

Issue #683 asks what observed rural health, education, farm, and small-business outcomes can be linked to connection quality, while issue #587 asked what observed rural outcomes can be linked to poor connectivity quality [issue #683](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/683), [issue #587](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/587). Those questions substantially overlap, and the already-merged round-1 finding is the primary answer for the shared question [round-1 finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-observed-outcomes.md).

This update narrows the round-2 contribution to three things: confirming that no public source checked here changes the round-1 bottom line; adding MPI, National Infrastructure, and MBIE 2021 as useful but non-quantifying sources; and making explicit that the stronger DHB telehealth stocktake and AgriTechNZ farm-adoption baseline should carry more evidential weight than generic telehealth guidance or isolated project examples [MPI, 2022](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/news/media-releases/boosting-rural-connectivity-aims-to-deliver-sustainable-benefits-to-kiwi-farmers), [National Infrastructure, 2026](https://nationalinfrastructure.govt.nz/our_project/video-life-saving-connectivity-under-threat-in-remote-far-north/), [MBIE, 2021](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/17055-understanding-the-digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses), [NZ Telehealth Leadership Group, 2020](https://www.telehealth.org.nz/assets/Uploads/Telehealth-Full-Report-2020-V7-Pages.pdf), [AgriTechNZ, 2022](https://trustalliance.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/AgriTechNZ-Digital-Adoption-in-Primary-Industries-Report-2022.pdf).

### Health: confirmed access constraint, still not a national outcome count

The Ministry of Health's 2025 Rural Health Roadshow visited 13 rural locations and recorded digital connectivity as a theme raised by rural communities [Ministry of Health, 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/rural-health-roadshow-insights-report.pdf). In its digital health and telehealth theme, the Ministry reported that telehealth expansion is limited by poor rural broadband in Hanmer Springs and Thames and that some patients are not comfortable with or able to use digital services [Ministry of Health, 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/rural-health-roadshow-insights-report.pdf).

The best independent corroboration is the NZ Telehealth Leadership Group's 2020 DHB stocktake, not the generic NZ Telehealth Resource Centre page used in the first PR draft. In the 2019 national DHB telehealth survey, all 20 DHBs responded to the organisational survey, 18 responded about clinical services, 15 cited patient access to devices as a barrier, and 12 cited patient access to fast internet as a barrier [NZ Telehealth Leadership Group, 2020](https://www.telehealth.org.nz/assets/Uploads/Telehealth-Full-Report-2020-V7-Pages.pdf). That supports an observed access constraint, but neither source provides public counts of failed video consults, phone substitutions, missed appointments, avoided travel, or health outcomes caused by poor broadband [Ministry of Health, 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/rural-health-roadshow-insights-report.pdf), [NZ Telehealth Leadership Group, 2020](https://www.telehealth.org.nz/assets/Uploads/Telehealth-Full-Report-2020-V7-Pages.pdf).

**Per-claim confidence:** Medium - the access constraint is directly observed and independently supported, but the outcome size is unverified.

### Education: continuity support, rural connection-quality effect not isolated

The Ministry of Education's COVID-era evaluation says government provision of internet connections and devices likely supported learner engagement, achievement, progress, and wellbeing, but it also warns that strong causal conclusions about the size of the impact are not warranted because the evidence was incomplete and not detailed enough [Ministry of Education, 2021 evaluation](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-03/12.-BN-1278617-Evaluation-Report-Provision-of-Internet-Connectivity-and-Dev..._Redacted.pdf?VersionId=C0OS1Kl_.9kVLTtO67kveAKgOmS2M4jx). A separate Ministry briefing says about 40,000 internet connections had been allocated before the August 2021 Alert Level 4 move, and that learners in rural communities were less likely to have reliable and affordable internet because of limited digital infrastructure [Ministry of Education, 2021 connected households](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/06.-ER-Keeping-households-connected-into-the-2022-school-year-127209.._Redacted.pdf?VersionId=lZsMyfJRRhorLL3A0JQspk4gTzX3Vh0w).

The education conclusion therefore remains a continuity finding rather than a rural effect-size finding. Public Ministry sources link connectivity support to learning continuity and identify rural learners as less likely to have reliable and affordable internet, but they do not isolate rural connection quality from devices, household circumstances, school support, duration of access, or COVID disruption [Ministry of Education, 2021 evaluation](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-03/12.-BN-1278617-Evaluation-Report-Provision-of-Internet-Connectivity-and-Dev..._Redacted.pdf?VersionId=C0OS1Kl_.9kVLTtO67kveAKgOmS2M4jx), [Ministry of Education, 2021 connected households](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/06.-ER-Keeping-households-connected-into-the-2022-school-year-127209.._Redacted.pdf?VersionId=lZsMyfJRRhorLL3A0JQspk4gTzX3Vh0w).

**Per-claim confidence:** Medium - the continuity benefit is supported by Ministry evaluation evidence, but a rural connection-quality effect size is not public.

### Farms: project examples add colour, AgriTechNZ remains the stronger adoption source

MPI's 2022 rural-connectivity release says patchy network connection remains a significant barrier to many farmers adopting agricultural technology, and it gives examples including sensors for remote traps, grain silos, and water systems [MPI, 2022](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/news/media-releases/boosting-rural-connectivity-aims-to-deliver-sustainable-benefits-to-kiwi-farmers). National Infrastructure's Far North rural connectivity page gives direct service-use examples from Rural Connectivity Group sites, including remote work, online banking, telehealth, education, emergency calls, payment and information services, hazard apps, farming data, environmental programmes, and Civil Defence response [National Infrastructure, 2026](https://nationalinfrastructure.govt.nz/our_project/video-life-saving-connectivity-under-threat-in-remote-far-north/).

Those sources are useful for intervention design, but AgriTechNZ remains the strongest quantitative farm-adoption source in this stream. Its 2022 baseline engaged more than 1,000 farmers and growers through a random telephone survey and qualitative follow-up, found that perceived local connectivity can influence digital-adoption decisions, and also found that on-farm internet-connectivity ratings alone were not a significant differentiator of adoption segments [AgriTechNZ, 2022](https://trustalliance.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/AgriTechNZ-Digital-Adoption-in-Primary-Industries-Report-2022.pdf). The same report says poor internet connectivity was slightly more significant as an adoption barrier for sheep, beef, and sheep-and-beef farmers, and that conservative segments were more likely to be restricted by poor internet service and cellphone coverage [AgriTechNZ, 2022](https://trustalliance.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/AgriTechNZ-Digital-Adoption-in-Primary-Industries-Report-2022.pdf).

Federated Farmers reporting through Farmers Weekly adds a current farm-experience signal by describing a 2025 survey in which mobile coverage averaged 57 percent of farmland, but the article is stakeholder reporting and the underlying PDF was not directly extractable through the repo fetch ladder from this environment [Farmers Weekly / Federated Farmers, 2025](https://www.farmersweekly.co.nz/news/connectivity-frustrations-deepen-for-farmers/), [ComCom-hosted Federated Farmers PDF, fetch classified BLOCKED](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0026/329039/Federated-Farmers-of-NZ-Submission-on-Market-study-into-personal-banking-services-Preliminary-Issues-paper-7-September-2023-Attachment-2.pdf). The Commerce Commission independently confirms the rural service-quality substrate: rural broadband satisfaction trailed urban satisfaction across all measured service dimensions, and rural consumers face choices across coverage, performance, reliability, device compatibility, upfront costs, and monthly price [Commerce Commission, 2026](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/2025-Telecommunications-Monitoring-Report-29-June-2026.pdf).

**Per-claim confidence:** Low-Medium - the adoption and service-use channels are supported, but I did not find public national counts of lost production, compliance failures, injuries, or emergency delays caused by connection quality.

### Small businesses: connectivity is a digital-adoption barrier, not proven rural revenue harm

MBIE's 2021 digital-capability report says internet connectivity or speed was identified as a barrier to digital adoption by one in four businesses, and that this barrier was particularly relevant to agriculture, forestry, and fishing businesses [MBIE, 2021](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/17055-understanding-the-digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses). MBIE's 2024 business digital monitor says an emerging digital divide means some businesses are seizing digital opportunities while others are falling behind, and it identifies barriers including lack of time, lack of knowledge, cybersecurity concerns, and the need for support choosing appropriate and affordable digital tools [MBIE, 2024](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/business/support-for-business/better-for-business/research-and-reports/digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses).

Those sources support a business digital-adoption barrier, especially for rural-adjacent primary industries, but they do not show rural connection quality causing revenue loss, employment loss, productivity change, or business exit [MBIE, 2021](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/17055-understanding-the-digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses), [MBIE, 2024](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/business/support-for-business/better-for-business/research-and-reports/digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses).

**Per-claim confidence:** Low - the business digital divide is real, but the rural connection-quality outcome link is indirect.

## What would change this conclusion

- A public Health NZ dataset reporting telehealth attempt, failure, phone-substitution, no-show, completion, or travel-avoidance outcomes by rurality and connection quality would change the health conclusion from an access constraint to a quantified health-service outcome [Ministry of Health, 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/rural-health-roadshow-insights-report.pdf), [NZ Telehealth Leadership Group, 2020](https://www.telehealth.org.nz/assets/Uploads/Telehealth-Full-Report-2020-V7-Pages.pdf).
- A Ministry of Education analysis linking rural household connection quality to attendance, engagement, assessment participation, NCEA outcomes, or learning progress would change the education conclusion from continuity support to rural effect measurement [Ministry of Education, 2021 evaluation](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-03/12.-BN-1278617-Evaluation-Report-Provision-of-Internet-Connectivity-and-Dev..._Redacted.pdf?VersionId=C0OS1Kl_.9kVLTtO67kveAKgOmS2M4jx), [Ministry of Education, 2021 connected households](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/06.-ER-Keeping-households-connected-into-the-2022-school-year-127209.._Redacted.pdf?VersionId=lZsMyfJRRhorLL3A0JQspk4gTzX3Vh0w).
- Provider, NAIT, ACC, emergency-service, insurer, or farm-management-system data linking outages or weak mobile coverage to compliance failures, delayed emergency calls, injuries, lost production, or extra labour would change the farm conclusion [AgriTechNZ, 2022](https://trustalliance.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/AgriTechNZ-Digital-Adoption-in-Primary-Industries-Report-2022.pdf), [Farmers Weekly / Federated Farmers, 2025](https://www.farmersweekly.co.nz/news/connectivity-frustrations-deepen-for-farmers/).
- A linked firm-level dataset combining rural service quality, digital adoption, revenue, productivity, export activity, employment, and business survival would change the small-business conclusion [MBIE, 2021](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/17055-understanding-the-digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses), [MBIE, 2024](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/business/support-for-business/better-for-business/research-and-reports/digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses).
- What I could not verify: national counts of rural telehealth failures caused by broadband, rural learning loss caused by connection quality, farm incidents or compliance failures caused by outages, or rural small-business revenue and survival outcomes caused by poor connection quality. Those may exist in provider, agency, education, health, farm-management, or commercial datasets, but I did not find them in public sources checked for issue #683 [Commerce Commission, 2026](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/2025-Telecommunications-Monitoring-Report-29-June-2026.pdf), [MBIE, 2024](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/business/support-for-business/better-for-business/research-and-reports/digital-capability-of-new-zealand-businesses).
- Human validation is still needed from rural health providers, schools, farmers, rural SMEs, iwi, WISPs, councils, emergency managers, and disabled or medically dependent rural residents because public reporting cannot show lived trade-offs among price, data caps, latency, outage backup, devices, skills, and trust [Ministry of Health, 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/rural-health-roadshow-insights-report.pdf), [Commerce Commission, 2026](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/2025-Telecommunications-Monitoring-Report-29-June-2026.pdf).

## Open follow-up questions

- No sub-issues opened; after reconciliation with issue #587, the remaining gaps require access to non-public or unpublished administrative datasets rather than another public-source sweep [round-1 finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/rural-connectivity-observed-outcomes.md).
- Can Health NZ publish anonymised rural telehealth quality measures that distinguish video failure, phone substitution, completed video, in-person fallback, and avoided travel?
- Can the Ministry of Education safely evaluate rural learner outcomes by household connection quality without exposing learner identity?
- Can ComCom, MBIE, providers, NAIT, ACC, emergency services, and rural insurers publish aggregate farm-outcome or rural-business-outcome indicators linked to outage or service-quality measures?
