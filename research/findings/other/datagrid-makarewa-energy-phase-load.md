# Datagrid Makarewa: consented 280 MW, 140 MW Mercury PPOA, and the staged grid-path versus 1 GW claims

## Executive answer

- The best-supported public project-wide figure remains a **280 MW Makarewa AI campus**; DataGrid’s consent announcement and multiple outlets consistently repeat the **280 MW, 2028 target** framing, but the public record did not provide a clear public split by phase beyond that campus total and a **100 MW Stage 1 connection request** in PowerNet’s planning docs [DataGrid, 2026a](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), [PowerNet AMP, 2026](https://powernet.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/TPCL-Asset-Management-Plan-2026-36.pdf).
- The confirmed Mercury arrangement is a **140 MW Power Purchase Option Agreement (PPOA)**, not a full 280 MW firm-supply contract for the campus. It is described as a **15-year long-term option contract**, with about **1.2TWh/year** associated with the buildout [DataGrid, 2026b](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/mercury), [DCD, 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/).
- Grid evidence is mixed but coherent: DataGrid says a **dedicated 220 kV Transpower connection** is part of the campus plan, while PowerNet files show a **100 MW Stage 1 NMK GXP connection concept** and line upgrade to 66 kV, indicating staged reinforcement rather than all-power immediate firming in the public file [DataGrid, 2026a](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), [PowerNet AMP, 2026](https://powernet.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/TPCL-Asset-Management-Plan-2026-36.pdf).
- A Transpower SOSA draft explicitly models DataGrid with a **140 MW announced PPOA** and a **120 MW load increase in the medium scenario by winter 2029**, suggesting a staged near-term load representation, not an instantly finalized 280 MW firm peak [Transpower, 2026](https://static.transpower.co.nz/public/bulk-upload/documents/2026%20SOSA%20-%20-%20Draft%20Report.pdf?VersionId=cmUJGFenPEt._MANxTJ8VtT5x9ZoDDx).
- Against Tiwai, **280 MW is ~49% of Tiwai’s 572 MW** public baseline; it is closer to “about half,” not “double,” and 1.2TWh/yr is about **2.9%** of NZ demand if compared against the 2025 EA dataset total (41,618.322 GWh) [EA, 2024](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/the-tiwai-point-smelter-demand-response-in-winter-2024/), [EA, 2025 data](https://www.emi.ea.govt.nz/Wholesale/Download/DataReport/CSV/W_GD_C?DateFrom=20250101&DateTo=20251231&Format=CSV).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the confirmed 280 MW figure, 140 MW PPOA terms, and staged/transmission evidence are consistent across public sources, but explicit “phase-by-phase MW” for later expansion stages is not documented in a single consented, final public schedule.

## Evidence

### Power-by-phase and what is actually confirmed
- DataGrid’s consent announcement says the facility will consume **280 MW of power** when operational and frames the campus as the country’s second-largest user after Tiwai [DataGrid, 2026a](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).
- The same company-facing page links to phase context with wording around being in a **resource consent** for the campus (280 MW scale), but does not disclose a 130 MW + 150 MW split [DataGrid, 2026a](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).
- An MEUG press release states Datagrid’s plan is for **up to 280 MW in phase one** and an **ultimate objective of 1 GW** [MEUG, 2026](https://backend.meug.co.nz/assets/Documents/MEUG-Press-Release_New-member-Datagrid-NZ.pdf).
- A separate MfE fast-track response paper says the proposed park could reach **over 240 MW of IT load** [MfE Fast-Track ANON, 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf).
- Transpower’s published grid connection planning (PowerNet AMP) requests a **100 MW Stage 1 connection to NMK GXP** and a 33→66 kV line relocation to support future development, indicating staged network sizing [PowerNet AMP, 2026](https://powernet.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/TPCL-Asset-Management-Plan-2026-36.pdf).
- Interpretation: 280 MW appears as the announced campus target and phase-one envelope in public documents; publicly available staged evidence is 100 MW stage-one grid planning and Transpower SOSA’s 120 MW modeled increment.

### PPOA structure and grid security context
- DataGrid describes its Mercury contract as a **140-megawatt long-term Power Purchase Option Agreement (PPOA)** [DataGrid, 2026b](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/mercury).
- DataGrid says the PPOA provides a **long-term price hedge** and cites **~1.2TWh per annum** [DataGrid, 2026b](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/mercury).
- DCD and other outlets repeat the 140 MW/15-year framing and also say the deal underpins the first phase of a 280 MW AI campus [DCD, 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealan/).
- BusinessDesk and DCD both describe it as an **option contract** for 140 MW (which is structurally different from a fully firmed 280 MW take-offering) [BusinessDesk, 2026](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/datagrid-signs-electricity-deal-with-mercury-for-southland-ai-data-centre), [DCD, 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/).

### Grid impact and transmission evidence
- DataGrid repeatedly says the campus is powered through a dedicated **220 kV connection to Transpower** [DataGrid, 2026a](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).
- PowerNet’s AMP states the NMK GXP connection is proposed as a **100 MW Stage 1** request and includes a **66 kV relining** to support future development [PowerNet AMP, 2026](https://powernet.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/TPCL-Asset-Management-Plan-2026-36.pdf).
- Transpower’s 2026 SOSA draft uses DataGrid with a **140 MW announced PPOA** and models a **120 MW load increase in the medium demand scenario by winter 2029** [Transpower, 2026](https://static.transpower.co.nz/public/bulk-upload/documents/2026%20SOSA%20-%20Draft%20Report.pdf?VersionId=cmUJGFenPEt._MANxTJ8VtT5x9ZoDDx).
- Interpretation: the public transmission story is that of staged or incremental loading assumptions rather than a singular immediately-firm 280 MW grid transfer point.

### Tiwai and national comparison math
- EA identifies Tiwai Point smelter as NZ’s single largest consumer at **572 MW** [EA, 2024](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/the-tiwai-point-smelter-demand-response-in-winter-2024/).
- If Tiwai is 572 MW, then 280 MW is **48.9%** of that load (`280 / 572 = 0.489`).
- The EA dataset for 2025 shows NZ total demand **41,618.322 GWh** for UNI+CNI+LNI+USI+LSI across half-hour records [EA, 2025 data](https://www.emi.ea.govt.nz/Wholesale/Download/DataReport/CSV/W_GD_C?DateFrom=20250101&DateTo=20251231&Format=CSV).
- At that denominator, 1.2 TWh is approximately **2.88%** (`1200 / 41618.322`) of one-year demand.
- So the public “twice Tiwai” framing does not match current confirmed figures unless one assumes speculative later expansion; it matches “high-growth, potential-to-grow” narratives, not confirmed end-state.

## What would change this conclusion
- Officially published, phase-broken consent or connection-stage documents from the regulator/utility showing a clearly consented, firm, phase-by-phase MW schedule for all stages.
- A public confirmation that the full 280 MW is either already firmed under one contract and already grid-committed today, or that first-stage load is definitively fixed at a different value than the public 120 MW staged estimate.
- A public, current revision to Transpower’s load treatment that supersedes the cited SOSA assumptions.
- Any official update that retracts or changes the 572 MW Tiwai baseline used for public comparison.

## What could not verify
- I could not confirm, from one primary company or regulator document, a clean 130 MW + 150 MW phase split for the current announcement window.
- I could not confirm public firm-contract documentation showing **all** 280 MW as already secured supply from Mercury or another single PPA/contract.
- I could not verify the full Tiwai comparative “twice” claim using all media narratives because some older paywalled or dynamic RNZ pages were inaccessible from raw HTML in this environment.
