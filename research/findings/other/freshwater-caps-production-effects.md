# NZ freshwater caps have produced clear nutrient and consent changes, but not a proven broad dairy-production drop

## Executive answer

- **Lake Taupo is the clearest observed outcome:** the cap-and-trade/buy-back package retired the intended 20 percent manageable-nitrogen reduction, required dairy and drystock farms to operate under nitrogen discharge allowances and consents, and included land purchase or management change to lower-leaching uses; it is evidence of land-use and nitrogen-headroom change, not a public milk-production time series for the catchment [Waikato Regional Council, Variation 5](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/regional-plan/protecting-lake-taupo/); [Motu, Taupo Nitrogen Market](https://www.motu.nz/assets/Documents/our-work/environment-and-resources/nutrient-trading-and-water-quality/Motu-Note-20-Taupo-Nitrogen-Market.pdf).
- **Lake Rotorua has binding nitrogen-reduction architecture, but the strongest production numbers found are modelled forecasts rather than observed results:** Plan Change 10 became operative on 16 November 2022, targets a reduction from about 755 tonnes of nitrogen entering the lake each year to 435 tonnes by 2032, and the section 32 modelling projected that dairy area and milk solids could fall materially by 2032 under the preferred sector-range scenario [BOPRC, PC10 page](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/your-council/plans-and-policies/plans/regional-plans/regional-natural-resources-plan/lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10/); [BOPRC, PC10 section 32 report](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/media/509000/s-final-section-32-lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10-pdf-copy.pdf).
- **Selwyn-Waihora has observed compliance machinery now operating:** about 200 farms with land-use consent conditions must meet percentage nitrogen-loss reductions from 1 January 2022, including 30 percent for dairy above the 15 kgN/ha/yr threshold, and Environment Canterbury says audits resumed from the 2022/23 season; that is an observed regulatory/compliance effect, but not yet a public catchment milk-production attribution [Environment Canterbury, Selwyn Waihora nitrogen reductions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/your-region/farmers-hub/farming-practices/reducing-nitrogen-loss/selwyn-waihora).
- **Waikato-Waipa, Southland and Otago are still mostly transition cases:** Waikato PC1 remains in the Environment Court process after a May 2025 interim decision, Southland's water and land plan has strengthened farming-related rules and still has litigation around incidental farming discharges, and Otago's proposed land and water plan states that it does not promote land-use change at this stage [Waikato Regional Council, PC1](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/healthy-rivers-plan-for-change/); [Environment Southland, pSWLP](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/plans-and-strategies/regional-plans/proposed-southland-water-and-land-plan); [Otago Regional Council, LWRP ministerial paper](https://www.orc.govt.nz/media/bfzbwm5x/paper-6-2-updated-draft-08-05-2024.pdf).
- **The dairy baseline runs against a simple "freshwater caps have cut NZ production" story:** DairyNZ/LIC reports national milksolids rose 2.9 percent in 2024/25 to 1.937 billion kg while cow numbers fell 0.5 percent to 4.68 million, and Stats NZ reports total dairy cattle fell 1 percent to 5.75 million at 30 June 2025 [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf); [Stats NZ, Agricultural production statistics 2025](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/agricultural-production-statistics-year-to-june-2025/).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the observed rule/consent outcomes are well supported by council records, but causal production attribution is weak because public data does not join catchment rules, individual consents, stocking, milk solids, land-use transfers and weather/price confounders.

## Evidence

### The dairy production baseline does not show a national production fall

DairyNZ/LIC reports that in 2024/25 New Zealand dairy companies processed 21 billion litres of milk containing 1.937 billion kg of milksolids, up from 1.882 billion kg in 2023/24, while total cows fell to 4.68 million and herds fell to 10,370 [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf). Stats NZ separately reports 5.75 million dairy cattle at 30 June 2025, down 1 percent from 2024 and below the 2014 peak of 6.698 million in its published 2002-2025 series [Stats NZ, Agricultural production statistics 2025](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/agricultural-production-statistics-year-to-june-2025/).

The regions relevant to this issue remain major producers in the 2024/25 DairyNZ/LIC regional tables: Waikato produced 429.1 million kg MS, Bay of Plenty 61.4 million kg MS, North Canterbury 325.2 million kg MS, South Canterbury 109.0 million kg MS, Otago 114.5 million kg MS, and Southland 273.0 million kg MS [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf). Those regional tables are not catchment-level enough to isolate Taupo, Rotorua, Selwyn-Waihora or Waikato-Waipa rule effects, so they are a baseline check rather than proof of causation [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf).

### Lake Taupo: observed nitrogen headroom and land-use change, not a catchment milk-series

Waikato Regional Council says Variation 5 became operative on 7 July 2011, requires dairy and drystock farming in the Lake Taupo catchment to have resource consents, limits annual average nitrogen leaching from rural land uses, allows nitrogen offsetting between properties, and uses public funds through the Lake Taupo Protection Trust to reduce manageable nitrogen losses by 20 percent [Waikato Regional Council, Variation 5](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/regional-plan/protecting-lake-taupo/). The Protecting Lake Taupo project page says the project had to reduce the manageable load of nitrogen by at least 20 percent, remove 170 tonnes of nitrogen in the catchment by 2018, and use $79.2 million excluding GST from central government, Waikato Regional Council and Taupo District Council to support conversion of high nitrogen-leaching land uses into low nitrogen-leaching land uses over 15 years [Protecting Lake Taupo, project story](https://www.protectinglaketaupo.nz/about/the-project/).

Motu's evaluation says the trust achieved its nitrogen-reduction target through a mix of land purchase, conversion to low-leaching activities, and direct NDA purchases where landowners changed land use or management, and it reports more than 5000 ha of reforestation to pine plantations as a complementary effect [Motu, Taupo Nitrogen Market](https://www.motu.nz/assets/Documents/our-work/environment-and-resources/nutrient-trading-and-water-quality/Motu-Note-20-Taupo-Nitrogen-Market.pdf). The same Motu note cautions that lake-water response cannot yet prove the full environmental outcome because Lake Taupo has long water and nutrient residence times; for this issue, that means the strongest observed claim is "nitrogen allowances and some land use changed", not "dairy production visibly fell" [Motu, Taupo Nitrogen Market](https://www.motu.nz/assets/Documents/our-work/environment-and-resources/nutrient-trading-and-water-quality/Motu-Note-20-Taupo-Nitrogen-Market.pdf).

**Claim confidence:** High for the nitrogen cap/buy-back and consent effect; Medium for land-use change scale because the public summaries do not provide a full parcel-by-parcel production dataset.

### Lake Rotorua: binding pathway, modelled dairy impact, observed production still not public

Bay of Plenty Regional Council says PC10 introduced rules limiting nitrogen entering Lake Rotorua from land use, sets out how Nitrogen Discharge Allocation is allocated to individual rural properties, and became operative on 16 November 2022 [BOPRC, PC10 page](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/your-council/plans-and-policies/plans/regional-plans/regional-natural-resources-plan/lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10/). The PC10 section 32 report states the target is to reduce nitrogen entering Lake Rotorua from about 755 tonnes per year to 435 tonnes by 2032, mostly from pastoral sources including dairy, sheep, beef, deer and grazed lifestyle blocks [BOPRC, PC10 section 32 report](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/media/509000/s-final-section-32-lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10-pdf-copy.pdf).

The same section 32 report says the integrated framework uses farm nutrient plans, resource consents, NDAs by 2032, a $40 million incentive fund to permanently remove 100 tonnes of nitrogen, and a separate gorse programme [BOPRC, PC10 section 32 report](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/media/509000/s-final-section-32-lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10-pdf-copy.pdf). Its economic modelling projected that, by 2032 under the sector-range scenario, existing dairy area may reduce by 43 percent and milk-solids production may reduce by nearly 40 percent; those are forecast impacts in the policy analysis, not observed post-2022 production results [BOPRC, PC10 section 32 report](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/media/509000/s-final-section-32-lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10-pdf-copy.pdf).

**Claim confidence:** High for PC10 status and target; Medium for expected production/land-use effect because the production figures are modelled and pre-implementation, not measured outcomes.

### Selwyn-Waihora: consented nitrogen reductions are now auditable

Environment Canterbury says Plan Change 1 for Selwyn-Waihora restricts agricultural nitrogen losses, requires farming activities to operate at good management practice and make further improvements, and sets catchment reductions to meet the nitrogen load limit by no later than 2037 [Environment Canterbury, Selwyn Waihora nitrogen reductions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/your-region/farmers-hub/farming-practices/reducing-nitrogen-loss/selwyn-waihora). ECan says about 200 farms with nitrogen losses greater than 15 kgN/ha/yr have land-use consent conditions requiring percentage nitrogen-loss reductions from 1 January 2022, with reductions including 30 percent for dairy and 22 percent for dairy support [Environment Canterbury, Selwyn Waihora nitrogen reductions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/your-region/farmers-hub/farming-practices/reducing-nitrogen-loss/selwyn-waihora).

ECan says those reductions are checked through scheduled Farm Environment Plan audits, that audits had been on hold while the Overseer interim solution was resolved, and that audits have resumed from the 2022/23 season [Environment Canterbury, Selwyn Waihora nitrogen reductions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/your-region/farmers-hub/farming-practices/reducing-nitrogen-loss/selwyn-waihora). This is stronger than "rules on paper" because consent conditions and audits are operating, but it still does not identify how much milk production changed in the Selwyn-Waihora catchment after 2022 [Environment Canterbury, Selwyn Waihora nitrogen reductions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/your-region/farmers-hub/farming-practices/reducing-nitrogen-loss/selwyn-waihora); [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf).

**Claim confidence:** High for consent/audit requirements; Medium for production interpretation because the public production data is regional, not catchment-attributed.

### Waikato-Waipa: still appeal-stage, so observed production effects are premature

Waikato Regional Council describes PC1 as the first step in an 80-year journey for the Waikato and Waipa rivers, with rules for point-source discharges and non-point agricultural discharges covering nitrogen, phosphorus, sediment and bacteria [Waikato Regional Council, PC1](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/healthy-rivers-plan-for-change/). The council says the Environment Court released its first interim decision on appeals on 28 May 2025, PC1 still needs further process before replacing operative regional-plan provisions, and the current Waikato Regional Plan remains unchanged alongside PC1 until the final version updates it or is treated as operative under RMA section 86F [Waikato Regional Council, PC1](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/healthy-rivers-plan-for-change/).

Because the main Waikato-Waipa plan change was still not final on the public council page checked, this finding treats Waikato-Waipa as a regulatory-exposure case rather than an observed production-reduction case [Waikato Regional Council, PC1](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/healthy-rivers-plan-for-change/). Waikato remains the largest dairy region in the 2024/25 DairyNZ/LIC table, with 3,029 herds, 1.085 million cows and 429.1 million kg MS, so any future measured PC1 effect would matter nationally [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf).

**Claim confidence:** High for PC1 procedural status; Medium for production-risk significance because regional scale is clear but future rule effects are not yet measured.

### Southland: stronger farming controls and farm-plan deadline, but production still high

Environment Southland says the proposed Southland Water and Land Plan introduced new rules for intensive winter grazing, stock access to waterways, land-use intensification, industrial discharges and wastewater discharges, and that further rules were added or strengthened through the Environment Court process for intensive winter grazing, farm plans, pasture-based wintering and discharge consents [Environment Southland, pSWLP](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/plans-and-strategies/regional-plans/proposed-southland-water-and-land-plan). Environment Southland also says farmers have until 27 November 2027 to submit farm plans for certification, and MPI's Ground Rules page says NES-F agricultural intensification consents issued before 1 January 2025 remain valid until expiry while new or expanded intensification is now managed under the Southland Water and Land Plan and other applicable regulations [Environment Southland, Southland Farm Plans](https://www.es.govt.nz/community/farming/southland-farm-plans); [MPI Ground Rules, Southland agricultural intensification](https://www.groundrules.mpi.govt.nz/rule/3503-agricultural-intensification-in-southland).

Southland is not showing up in the public record as a region where freshwater caps have already reduced dairy output; DairyNZ/LIC reports Southland had 983 herds, 601,434 cows and 273.0 million kg MS in 2024/25, with average production of 454 kg MS per cow [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf). The public council record is better evidence of new compliance obligations and legal uncertainty than of completed production reduction [Environment Southland, pSWLP](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/plans-and-strategies/regional-plans/proposed-southland-water-and-land-plan).

**Claim confidence:** High for rule/farm-plan status; Medium for "no observed production reduction found" because absence in public sources is not proof that no farm-level changes occurred.

### Otago: freshwater planning is explicit about not yet forcing land-use change

Otago Regional Council's May 2024 ministerial paper says Otago had around 440 dairy farms, mostly in Clutha and Waitaki, and that expansion had tailed off recently [Otago Regional Council, LWRP ministerial paper](https://www.orc.govt.nz/media/bfzbwm5x/paper-6-2-updated-draft-08-05-2024.pdf). The same paper says the proposed Land and Water Regional Plan is intended to enable existing land uses to operate with certainty while maintaining or improving water quality and quantity, but also says the plan does not propose land-use change and therefore will not achieve target attribute states by itself [Otago Regional Council, LWRP ministerial paper](https://www.orc.govt.nz/media/bfzbwm5x/paper-6-2-updated-draft-08-05-2024.pdf).

For water quantity, the ORC paper says new minimum flows are proposed to align with when most water permits in a catchment expire and that water users may respond through infrastructure investment, storage, on-farm management or land-use change; for rural water quality, it says the draft region-wide provisions require Good Management Practice and do not promote land-use change because more work is needed to understand GMP benefits [Otago Regional Council, LWRP ministerial paper](https://www.orc.govt.nz/media/bfzbwm5x/paper-6-2-updated-draft-08-05-2024.pdf). DairyNZ/LIC reports Otago produced 114.5 million kg MS from 433 herds and 265,647 cows in 2024/25, so Otago remains a material dairy region, but the public planning record found here does not show a completed freshwater-cap production reduction [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf).

**Claim confidence:** High for ORC's stated planning position; Medium for production interpretation because catchment-level consent-renewal outcomes were not joined to dairy production.

## What would change this conclusion

- A council, processor or audited research dataset joining individual consent conditions, nitrogen discharge allowances, land-use changes, herd numbers and milk-solids production for Taupo, Rotorua, Selwyn-Waihora, Waikato-Waipa, Southland or Otago would change this from a medium-confidence "observed rules, limited production proof" finding to a stronger causal estimate [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf).
- Public post-2022 Lake Rotorua monitoring showing how many pastoral properties have sold NDAs, changed land use, reduced cows or reduced milk solids would test whether the section 32 forecasts are becoming observed outcomes [BOPRC, PC10 section 32 report](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/media/509000/s-final-section-32-lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10-pdf-copy.pdf).
- ECan publication of Selwyn-Waihora FEP audit outcomes by sector, including actual nitrogen reductions achieved and any dairy-system changes, would strengthen or weaken the Selwyn conclusion [Environment Canterbury, Selwyn Waihora nitrogen reductions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/your-region/farmers-hub/farming-practices/reducing-nitrogen-loss/selwyn-waihora).
- Final Waikato PC1 provisions, Southland Rule 24/discharge outcomes and Otago LWRP decisions could shift Waikato-Waipa, Southland and Otago from "transition" to "implemented cap/renewal" cases [Waikato Regional Council, PC1](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/healthy-rivers-plan-for-change/); [Environment Southland, pSWLP](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/plans-and-strategies/regional-plans/proposed-southland-water-and-land-plan); [Otago Regional Council, LWRP ministerial paper](https://www.orc.govt.nz/media/bfzbwm5x/paper-6-2-updated-draft-08-05-2024.pdf).
- A human with regional-council consent expertise, dairy farm-system expertise or access to processor/catchment production data is needed before this finding should be used as a numeric estimate of production at risk.
