# NZ rest-home audit reports support facility-level quality aggregation, but not a complete provider-level audit-and-complaints dataset

## Executive answer

- New Zealand's published rest-home audit pages are rich enough to build a **facility-level** dataset covering certified premises, beds, services, current auditor, certification end date, certification period, latest audit date/type, public audit-report links, and current corrective-action rows with risk rating and action status [Ministry of Health, certified rest homes](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes); [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports).
- A **legal-entity provider** aggregation is possible because the Ministry CSV and facility pages publish provider/legal names, but an **ultimate-owner or brand-level** dataset would need a separate ownership crosswalk because related groups can appear through multiple legal entities and public facility pages do not provide a stable parent-company field [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [NZBN Register search example for Bupa Care Services NZ Limited](https://www.nzbn.govt.nz/mynzbn/nzbndetails/9429034659267/).
- A complete public dataset that joins audit attainment, corrective actions, certification periods and **complaints** at provider level is **not currently feasible from published data alone**: HDC publishes annual and thematic complaint reporting, and the Ministry pages mention serious-complaint inspections and district issues-based audits, but I found no current public machine-readable complaint table keyed to rest-home provider or facility [HDC research and data reports](https://www.hdc.org.nz/our-work/research-and-data-reports/); [HDC Annual Report 2025](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media/ha5ntqul/hdc-annual-report-2025.pdf); [Ministry of Health, rest-home certification and audits](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits).
- The official certified-provider CSV snapshot I downloaded on 7 July 2026 contains **686 premises rows, 43,312 beds and 402 distinct legal provider names**; the largest legal provider names by premises were Bupa Care Services NZ Limited (39 premises / 3,029 beds), Summerset Care Limited (33 / 2,743), Oceania Care Company Limited (25 / 1,919), Metlifecare Retirement Villages Limited (25 / 1,172), Radius Residential Care Limited (24 / 1,961), Heritage Lifecare Limited (24 / 1,849), CHT Healthcare Trust (21 / 1,490) and The Ultimate Care Group Limited (19 / 1,007) [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [archived CSV snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260707192700/https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv).
- The audit signal shows real variation, but it should be presented as **audit-measured compliance variation**, not a simple "best rest home" score: certification periods range from 1 to 4 years based on performance, reports vary by audit type and standards era, and corrective actions include both low-risk documentation issues and moderate/high clinical, staffing, medication, notification and care-planning issues [Ministry of Health, rest-home certification and audits](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits); [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports).

**Overall confidence:** Medium -- the public audit and certification fields are well evidenced and repeatable, but I did not bulk-parse all facility pages or all historical PDFs/DOCX reports, and the complaint-linkage conclusion rests on absence from current public HDC/Ministry publication surfaces rather than access to internal HealthCERT, HNZ or HDC databases.

## Evidence

### What can be aggregated

The Ministry says all rest homes and aged residential care facilities in New Zealand must be certified and audited to ensure safe and appropriate care and compliance with the Health and Disability Services (Safety) Act 2001, and it publishes audit reports for all rest homes in the public certified-provider database [Ministry of Health, rest-home certification and audits](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits). The certified-provider listing includes a downloadable CSV described as a full list of certified providers in New Zealand, updated on the page as 8 July 2026, and the page notes that current corrective actions from the latest audit appear on facility pages and update as actions are completed [Ministry of Health, certified rest homes](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes).

The official CSV is immediately useful for a baseline table because it includes premises name, service types, beds, address, former DHB area, certificate/licence name, certification period, certificate/licence end date, current auditor, legal name, legal-entity address, and legal-entity website [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [archived CSV snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260707192700/https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv). My parse of that CSV found 686 premises rows, 43,312 beds, 402 distinct legal provider names, and four auditing agencies: BSI Group New Zealand Ltd on 515 rows, The DAA Group Limited on 155 rows, Quality Health Authority on 15 rows, and Central Region's Technical Advisory Services Limited on one row [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [archived CSV snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260707192700/https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv).

Facility pages add fields not present in the CSV. For example, Accadia Manor Rest Home's public page lists its provider as Bupa Care Services NZ Limited, 29 beds, a 36-month certification period, a 23 April 2026 surveillance audit, three low-risk corrective actions, one still in progress, and DOCX/PDF links for current and older audits [Accadia Manor Rest Home](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes/accadia-manor-rest-home). Summerset Cambridge's page lists Summerset Care Limited, 116 beds, a 12-month certification period, a 5 December 2025 audit, and two low-risk corrective actions reported complete by 20 February 2026 [Summerset Cambridge](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes/summerset-cambridge). Karori Village's page lists Radius Residential Care Limited, 97 beds, a 12-month certification period, a 16 April 2026 provisional audit, one low-risk building-warrant action in progress and one moderate first-aid staffing action reported complete [Karori Village](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes/karori-village).

The audit reports themselves are structured enough to support deeper extraction. The Ministry guide says audit reports include rest-home details, audit type, services, bed occupancy, an executive summary, a summary-of-attainment table, attainment against standards, criteria requiring corrective actions, and criteria recorded for continuous improvement [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports). The same guide defines attainment levels as CI, FA, PA, UA and NA, and says PA/UA items receive risk ratings from negligible to critical; this makes an attainment/CAR data model plausible if a parser normalises audit type, standards era and report format [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports).

### What the first aggregation shows

The CSV shows a market with both large legal providers and many smaller entities: eight legal provider names account for 210 of 686 premises rows and 15,120 of 43,312 beds in the downloaded snapshot, while 402 legal provider names appear overall [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [archived CSV snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260707192700/https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv). The certification-period field has variation that can act as a first compliance proxy because the Ministry says certification and surveillance audits occur every 1 to 4 years and that the length of certification depends on how well the facility performs [Ministry of Health, rest-home certification and audits](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits). In the CSV snapshot, certification-period values were: 36 months on 268 rows, 48 months on 171 rows, 12 months on 30 rows, 24 months on 10 rows, "No change" on 206 rows and "Other" on one row, so a public comparison tool would need to resolve or explain "No change" rather than treating the CSV as a clean numeric field [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [archived CSV snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260707192700/https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv).

The facility pages show quality variation that is visible to families but not yet packaged for comparison. Te Mahana Limited had a 23 July 2024 audit with five corrective actions including a high-risk medication-system action and moderate registered-nurse-hours and medication-competency actions, all reported complete in March 2025 [Te Mahana Limited](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes/te-mahana-limited). Mt Herbert House and Forget-Me-Not-Village had a 4 February 2026 audit with multiple moderate corrective actions still in progress, including internal audits/falls documentation, registered-nurse coverage, care-plan detail and essential notification reporting [Mt Herbert House and Forget-Me-Not-Village](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes/mt-herbert-house-and-forget-me-not-village). Elderslea Rest Home had a 14 April 2026 audit with moderate in-progress actions covering interRAI/care-plan timeliness, medication documentation/temperature monitoring and detailed care interventions [Elderslea Rest Home](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes/elderslea-rest-home).

The signal is not purely negative. The Ministry guide says audit reports highlight strengths as well as areas for improvement and include continuous-improvement ratings where criteria exceed full attainment [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports). A provider-comparison dataset should therefore include both corrective-action burden and positive/continuous-improvement fields where available, rather than only counting CARs [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports).

### Why provider-level and complaint linkage remain the blockers

The Ministry's public provider field is a legal-provider name, not necessarily an ultimate ownership group or consumer-facing brand [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv). NZBN can identify a legal entity such as Bupa Care Services NZ Limited by NZBN 9429034659267, but the NZBN Register lookup is an entity register, not a ready-made rest-home ultimate-owner crosswalk [NZBN Register, Bupa Care Services NZ Limited](https://www.nzbn.govt.nz/mynzbn/nzbndetails/9429034659267/). This means legal-entity provider comparison is feasible now, but brand/group comparison would need maintained matching rules and review for acquisitions, trading names and related entities [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [NZBN Register, Bupa Care Services NZ Limited](https://www.nzbn.govt.nz/mynzbn/nzbndetails/9429034659267/).

Complaint linkage is weaker. HDC says its complaints data reflects the issues people using services care most about and that it publishes reports on individual complaints and complaint trends, but its research-and-data page currently lists general HDC/Health NZ complaint reports, a residential disability support report, and one pre-2015 residential-aged-care complaint report for 2010-2014 rather than a current provider-keyed ARC complaint dataset [HDC research and data reports](https://www.hdc.org.nz/our-work/research-and-data-reports/). HDC's 2025 annual report says about 18% of complaints to HDC are about care provided to older people, that the Aged Care Commissioner is a statutory decision-maker on complaints, and that complaints management provides oversight of issues older people are concerned about; it does not publish a rest-home facility/provider complaint table in the annual-report text I checked [HDC Annual Report 2025](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media/ha5ntqul/hdc-annual-report-2025.pdf).

The Ministry page also points to non-public or separately held complaint/issue pathways: serious complaints can lead to unannounced Ministry inspections, and districts can conduct issues-based audits under the Age Related Residential Care Contract, with readers told to contact their district for information on those audits [Ministry of Health, rest-home certification and audits](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits). That supports the conclusion that public audit pages are a strong starting point, but they are not the same as a complete regulator-wide quality-variation dataset [Ministry of Health, rest-home certification and audits](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits); [HDC research and data reports](https://www.hdc.org.nz/our-work/research-and-data-reports/).

## What would change this conclusion

- A Ministry, HealthCERT or Health NZ export/API with facility IDs, full attainment rows, CAR rows, certification history, issues-based audits, serious-complaint inspection links and provider ownership fields would move the feasibility conclusion from Medium to High and remove most scraping risk [Ministry of Health, certified rest homes](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes).
- A current HDC or cross-agency complaint dataset keyed to rest-home facility or legal provider, with privacy-safe suppression for small counts, would change the "complaints cannot be joined" conclusion [HDC research and data reports](https://www.hdc.org.nz/our-work/research-and-data-reports/); [HDC Annual Report 2025](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media/ha5ntqul/hdc-annual-report-2025.pdf).
- A maintained ownership crosswalk from Ministry legal-provider names to NZBN, Companies Office identifiers, consumer-facing brands and ultimate owners would make brand/group-level comparison defensible; without it, only legal-entity aggregation is safe [Ministry of Health CSV](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv); [NZBN Register, Bupa Care Services NZ Limited](https://www.nzbn.govt.nz/mynzbn/nzbndetails/9429034659267/).
- I could not verify the national distribution of CAR counts, attainment ratings or continuous-improvement ratings because I did not bulk-parse all facility pages or all PDF/DOCX audit reports; the page-level examples are feasibility probes, not a statistically representative national benchmark [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports).
- Several Ministry HTML pages were blocked to plain command-line fetches by Cloudflare-style 403 responses but resolved through the built-in web fetch/search; the CSV itself fetched by plain HTTP and was archived, so I treated page 403s as tooling limits rather than dead citations [Ministry certified rest homes listing](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/certified-providers/rest-homes); [archived CSV snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260707192700/https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/LegalEntitySummaryAgedCare.csv).
- A human aged-care sector reviewer should assess whether public CARs and certification-period signals are fair for family-facing comparison, because audit compliance signals can understate resident experience, culture, staffing reality and complaints that never reach public decisions [Ministry of Health, understanding audit reports](https://www.health.govt.nz/regulation-legislation/certification-of-health-care-services/for-residents-and-families/rest-home-certification-and-audits/understanding-rest-home-audit-reports); [HDC Annual Report 2025](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media/ha5ntqul/hdc-annual-report-2025.pdf).

## Open follow-up questions

- What minimum viable scoring model would be defensible: latest CAR count by risk/status, certification period, latest audit age/type, continuous-improvement count, or a no-composite "evidence panel" for each facility?
- Can HealthCERT, Health NZ or HDC provide a privacy-safe complaint/inspection extract by facility through OIA, or would small-count suppression make provider comparison too noisy?
- How often do Ministry legal-provider names diverge from consumer-facing brand or ultimate ownership, and what review process would keep an ownership crosswalk current?
