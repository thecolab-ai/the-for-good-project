# Existing NZ credit comparators already serve rate-lookup and general education, but none combine fee-inclusive total cost, a missed-payment stress scenario, no-interest community finance, and a support-first handoff for money-tight borrowers

## Executive answer

- **The counter-hypothesis is only half right.** The stream's framing finding warned that if Sorted plus commercial comparators already serve the target cohort, the case for a new artifact "weakens to a distribution/trust problem" ([framing finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/consumer-credit-transparency-framing.md), issue [#178](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/178)). This assessment tests that directly against all seven named tools. Result: the **education and rate-lookup** portions of the need are genuinely well served — so *that* part of the case does collapse — and, correcting an earlier draft, **fee-inclusive total cost is now served by at least two tools** (Glimp's calculator and Finance.co.nz both compute a fee-inclusive total for a chosen scenario). What remains unserved is the **combination this finding's title names**: none of the seven pairs that fee-inclusive total with a **missed-payment/default "stress cost"**, **no-interest community-finance** rows, and a **support-first handoff** for money-tight borrowers.
- **What is already covered well:** free, non-commercial, plain-English guidance on how credit costs work and where to get help. [Sorted](https://sorted.org.nz/guides/tackling-debt/personal-loans/) — run by the Crown entity Te Ara Ahunga Ora Retirement Commission [retirement.govt.nz](https://retirement.govt.nz/financial-capability/sorted) — tells borrowers to "add up the total costs," explains establishment fees, and routes people to free [MoneyTalks](https://fincap.org.nz/moneytalks) financial mentors. Duplicating Sorted's *education* layer would add little.
- **What NO tool does:** pair a fee-inclusive total cost with a **"stress cost" if a payment is missed** (late/default fees). Fee display varies widely — [Glimp's calculator](https://www.glimp.co.nz/personal-loans/personal-loans-calculator) and [Finance.co.nz](https://finance.co.nz/personal-loans/) compute a fee-inclusive total, [Money Compare](https://www.moneycompare.co.nz/compare/borrowings.personal-loans) shows the establishment fee, [Interest.co.nz](https://www.interest.co.nz/borrowing/personal-loan) is headline-rate-only, and [MoneyHub](https://www.moneyhub.co.nz/personal-loans.html)'s calculator explicitly states "Establishment fees, monthly account fees, and other lender charges are not included" — but **none of the seven** adds a missed-payment/default "stress cost." That default-fee scenario is the single axis absent from every tool, and it is exactly the axis the stream's cost-scenarios finding identified as decisive for very small amounts.
- **Coverage skews to mainstream/near-prime, and no-interest community finance is invisible.** The comparators rate banks and near-prime non-bank lenders; none surface no-interest community finance (Good Shepherd NZ, Ngā Tāngata Microfinance) as a peer option, despite those being materially cheaper for eligible money-tight borrowers per the stream's own cost-scenarios finding.
- **Routes to human help are real but uneven.** Editorial/guidance tools ([Sorted](https://sorted.org.nz/guides/tackling-debt/personal-loans/), [Canstar](https://www.canstar.co.nz/personal-loans/)) point to MoneyTalks; the pure transactional comparison surfaces ([Interest.co.nz](https://www.interest.co.nz/borrowing/personal-loan), [Money Compare](https://www.moneycompare.co.nz/compare/borrowings.personal-loans), [Glimp](https://www.glimp.co.nz/personal-loans), [Finance.co.nz](https://finance.co.nz/personal-loans/)) do not — so a borrower who lands on a rate table gets no support-first off-ramp.

**Overall confidence:** Medium — each tool's observable behaviour was checked directly and is High-confidence, but the synthesis rests on a point-in-time snapshot of pages that change, and on inferring cohort *sufficiency* rather than testing it with users (which #71/#69 are assigned to do).

## Evidence

### How each tool was assessed

Each page was fetched and read on 3 July 2026. Where a plain fetch was bot-blocked (tooling, not a dead link), I escalated per [ADR-0006](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/docs/adr/0006-fetch-proxy-browser-management.md): MoneyHub's JS body was rendered via stealth Chromium (`cloak-fetch`); Canstar returned HTTP 406 at every live rung, so it was read from a Wayback snapshot ([captured 2026-04-12](https://web.archive.org/web/20260412135049/https://www.canstar.co.nz/personal-loans/)); Sorted is a JS SPA that did not render for the fetchers, so its content was read via search-engine extraction of the live page and cross-checked against the stream's earlier citation of the same URL. **Glimp was re-assessed after review:** an earlier draft read only Glimp's `/personal-loans` marketing page and missed its linked `/personal-loans/personal-loans-calculator`, which fetched cleanly via plain HTTP and *does* carry a live per-lender rate/fee comparison with a fee-inclusive total (see §6). Each tool was scored on four axes: (1) coverage, (2) provenance/freshness, (3) vulnerable-borrower (missed-payment) scenarios, (4) routes to human help.

### 1. Sorted — govt education, not a comparator (serves the education need)

[Sorted](https://sorted.org.nz/guides/tackling-debt/personal-loans/) is a free service of Te Ara Ahunga Ora Retirement Commission, a Crown entity funded by central government [retirement.govt.nz](https://retirement.govt.nz/financial-capability/sorted), [Wikipedia: Te Ara Ahunga Ora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Te_Ara_Ahunga_Ora_Retirement_Commission). Its personal-loans guide is **education, not a live comparison surface**: it explains that "Many personal loans have establishment or documentation fees" and that "you should add up the total costs," and it points borrowers to banks, building societies and credit unions as typically lower-cost, and to free MoneyTalks financial mentors [Sorted personal loans](https://sorted.org.nz/guides/tackling-debt/personal-loans/). It carries no per-lender rate/fee table, no default-fee stress scenario, and no coverage of named high-cost lenders. **Verdict:** fully serves the *education + human-help* axes; does not attempt the *comparison* axis.

### 2. MoneyHub — dated, per-product fees, discloses affiliate revenue, but calculator excludes fees

[MoneyHub](https://www.moneyhub.co.nz/personal-loans.html) is a commercial, affiliate-funded site that styles itself "New Zealand's independent consumer finance website" (its own site schema) and carries an explicit "Updated 1 July 2026" date. It shows per-product detail (e.g. AA Money/Nectar "Interest rate: 9.95% p.a – 29.95% p.a … Establishment or Loan Fee: $240"), discloses commercial revenue ("Advertising Disclosure: We may earn a commission from loans, but our top picks are always based on rigorous research"; "MoneyHub may earn a referral bonus for anyone that's approved through some of the below links"), and links to Bad Credit Loans, Payday Loans and Debt Help guides [MoneyHub personal loans](https://www.moneyhub.co.nz/personal-loans.html). **Critical gap:** its repayment figures reflect interest only — "Important: Calculations use the standard amortisation formula and reflect the interest rate only. Establishment fees, monthly account fees, and other lender charges are not included and may apply on top of these figures" [MoneyHub personal loans](https://www.moneyhub.co.nz/personal-loans.html). So even the most complete commercial tool does not present a fee-inclusive total, and it presents no missed-payment "stress cost." It is also the strongest on the counter-hypothesis: dated, per-product, affiliate-disclosed, with a bad-credit path.

### 3. Canstar — annual "Best Value" star ratings, methodology + "How we get paid", routes to MoneyTalks

[Canstar NZ](https://www.canstar.co.nz/personal-loans/) rates products **annually** rather than live: "each year Canstar's expert research team rates and compares the major personal loan lenders." Its latest set assessed 13 providers — ANZ, ASB, Avanti, BNZ, Gem, Harmoney, Heartland Bank, Lending People, MTF Finance, Nectar, The Co-operative Bank, Unity and Westpac — i.e. mainstream banks plus near-prime non-bank lenders plus one credit union (Unity); no high-cost/short-term lenders and no no-interest community finance are in the rated set [Canstar personal loans (Wayback 2026-04-12)](https://web.archive.org/web/20260412135049/https://www.canstar.co.nz/personal-loans/). It publishes a "How we get paid" page and an award methodology, discusses fees editorially ("Loan establishment fees can be as much as $380"), and — unusually among the seven — routes distressed borrowers to human help: "Don't be embarrassed about reaching out for help from places such as the free financial helpline MoneyTalks" [Canstar personal loans (Wayback 2026-04-12)](https://web.archive.org/web/20260412135049/https://www.canstar.co.nz/personal-loans/). **Gap:** its output is a "best value" award, not a fee-inclusive total-cost for the user's own scenario, and it has no missed-payment stress figure.

### 4. Interest.co.nz — headline rate table with a fee caveat, no fees, no dates, no help route

[Interest.co.nz](https://www.interest.co.nz/borrowing/personal-loan) publishes a **headline-rate table only**, covering banks, peer-to-peer lenders, building societies, credit unions and finance companies. It carries an honest caveat — establishment and monthly fees "can push the effective cost of credit (debt) up significantly" and it points to its own cost-of-credit calculator — but the table itself shows no fees, no total cost, no default-fee scenario, no per-row effective dates, and no route to financial mentors, and it excludes both high-cost short-term lenders and no-interest community finance [Interest.co.nz personal loan](https://www.interest.co.nz/borrowing/personal-loan).

### 5. Money Compare — filterable table (rate + establishment fee), no default fees, no dates, no help route

[Money Compare](https://www.moneycompare.co.nz/compare/borrowings.personal-loans) shows interest rate, establishment fee, amount range, term and payment frequency across a mix of banks (Westpac, ASB, Kiwibank, ANZ, BNZ, SBS, Co-operative), credit unions (NZCU Auckland/Central) and non-bank lenders (Harmoney, Nectar, Instant Finance, Thorn/Finance Direct). It discloses no default/late fees or total cost, no commercial-revenue statement, no effective dates, no missed-payment scenario, and no route to human help; no-interest community finance is absent [Money Compare personal loans](https://www.moneycompare.co.nz/compare/borrowings.personal-loans).

### 6. Glimp — a live fee-inclusive calculator (corrected after review), but no stress scenario, no community finance, no help route

**Correction (post-review):** an earlier draft assessed only Glimp's `/personal-loans` marketing overview and wrongly called Glimp a "thin marketing overview … no actual rate/fee comparison table." That was wrong. The marketing page links (7 times, with "Compare Now" buttons) to a live tool at [`/personal-loans/personal-loans-calculator`](https://www.glimp.co.nz/personal-loans/personal-loans-calculator), which fetched via plain HTTP. Its heading is "Personal Loan Calculator — Compare interest rates, repayments, and fees": the user sets an amount, a term (2–7 years) and a repayment frequency (weekly/fortnightly/monthly), and it returns per-lender cards showing **Interest Rate From, Establishment Fee from, Monthly Service Fee, loan Type, the periodic Repayment, and — notably — a fee-inclusive "Amount Repaid" total**. For example, at a 5-year term it showed Harmoney at "Interest Rate From: 7.99%, Establishment Fee from: $150.00, Monthly Service Fee: $0.00 … Repayment: $405.43 … Amount Repaid: $24,475.80" and ANZ at "13.9% … Establishment Fee: $0.00 … Amount Repaid: $27,859.80" [Glimp personal loans calculator](https://www.glimp.co.nz/personal-loans/personal-loans-calculator). The "Amount Repaid" figure is genuinely fee-inclusive: 60 × $405.43 + $150 establishment = $24,475.80. So Glimp is in fact **one of the more fee-complete of the seven**, not the thinnest. **What it still does not do** (verified on the calculator page: zero occurrences of "default", "late", "missed", "penalty" or "MoneyTalks"/"financial mentor"): it presents **no missed-payment/default "stress cost"**, no **no-interest community finance** rows, no **route to human help**, no commercial-revenue disclosure, and no per-row effective dates. **Verdict:** serves the fee-inclusive total-cost axis; still misses the missed-payment-scenario, community-finance and support-handoff axes that define this finding's gap.

### 7. Finance.co.nz — richest fee fields, but non-bank-only and no support route

[Finance.co.nz](https://finance.co.nz/personal-loans/) has the most complete **fee** fields of any of the seven: interest rate, a worked monthly payment, total payment over term, establishment fee, monthly fee, other fees (PPSR, broker), early-repayment fee, and term/amount ranges. But its panel is **non-bank lenders only** (Harmoney, Lending Crowd, MTF, Simplify, Driva, LoanOptions, Nectar) — no banks, no credit unions, no community finance — its links carry affiliate parameters with no explicit disclosure, it shows no effective dates, no missed-payment "stress cost," and no route to financial mentors [Finance.co.nz personal loans](https://finance.co.nz/personal-loans/).

### Cross-cutting picture on the four axes

- **Coverage:** every tool anchors on mainstream/near-prime personal loans. None presents **no-interest community finance** (Good Shepherd NZ, Ngā Tāngata Microfinance) as a peer option, even though the stream's cost-scenarios finding shows those are materially cheaper for eligible borrowers and should be shown separately [cost-scenarios finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/small-credit-cost-scenarios.md). High-cost/short-term lenders appear as editorial explainer topics (Canstar, MoneyHub) but not as compared products.
- **Provenance/freshness:** uneven. Only MoneyHub carries an explicit page date; Canstar has an annual methodology and "How we get paid"; the four rate/table tools (Interest.co.nz, Money Compare, Glimp, Finance.co.nz) show no effective dates and mostly no methodology or affiliate disclosure. Affiliate-revenue models (MoneyHub, Finance.co.nz, and implicitly Glimp) create a ranking-incentive that the transparency governance in the stream's front-door finding was designed to counter [front-door requirements finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/credit-help-front-door-requirements.md).
- **Vulnerable-borrower (missed-payment) scenarios:** **none of the seven** present a default/late-fee "stress cost" — this held even on [Glimp's calculator](https://www.glimp.co.nz/personal-loans/personal-loans-calculator), which shows a fee-inclusive total but carries zero "default"/"late"/"missed" content. (MoneyHub, separately, strips fees out of its headline repayment figure.) This is the single most consistent gap and maps exactly onto the "show both on-time cost and stress cost if a payment is missed" requirement the cost-scenarios finding identified [cost-scenarios finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/small-credit-cost-scenarios.md).
- **Routes to human help:** present on the two guidance-heavy tools (Sorted, Canstar → MoneyTalks) and absent on the four transactional comparison surfaces. MoneyTalks itself is a free national helpline run by FinCap that connects people to 180+ financial mentoring services and has helped 175,000+ New Zealanders since 2018 [MoneyTalks / FinCap](https://fincap.org.nz/moneytalks) — i.e. the human-help infrastructure exists and is free; the gap is that a borrower on a rate table is not handed to it.

## What would change this conclusion

- **User testing that says education is the binding constraint.** If #71/#69 find that money-tight borrowers *do* consult tools before borrowing and that Sorted's education is what they actually need (not a total-cost engine), then the residual gap this finding identifies shrinks toward "distribution/trust," and the counter-hypothesis largely holds after all. I could not test cohort behaviour; that needs the demand-side work and lived-experience input.
- **A tool I under-sampled.** This already happened once: the first draft assessed Glimp from its marketing page and missed its calculator surface — the reviewer caught it and this version corrects it (§6). Sorted remains a JS SPA read via search extraction plus the stream's prior citation of the same URL, so a live human browse could still reveal a Sorted calculator that narrows the gap. The lesson generalises: for each named tool, the *comparison/calculator* surface (not just the landing page) is what must be checked, and a reviewer should re-open all seven in a normal browser before treating any single-tool claim as final.
- **A default-fee/stress-cost feature added by any incumbent.** These pages change; MoneyHub in particular is dated and iterates. If MoneyHub, Canstar or Finance.co.nz shipped a missed-payment scenario and community-finance rows, the core unmet need identified here would be filled by an incumbent and the case for a new artifact would collapse to partnership.
- **Whether "compared products" *should* include high-cost and no-interest finance.** The parent stream deliberately warns against flattening unlike products [framing finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/consumer-credit-transparency-framing.md). If a steward judges that showing no-interest community finance beside commercial loans is itself misleading, then the "coverage gap" is a design choice, not a defect — a human/steward call.
- **What I could not verify:** the *current* (July 2026) live state of Canstar (read from an April snapshot after live 406 blocks) and the full interactive behaviour of the Sorted single-page app. (Glimp's calculator was verified directly via plain HTTP this round, after being under-sampled in the first draft.) None of these blocks are dead links; they are tooling/bot-protection limits under [ADR-0006](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/docs/adr/0006-fetch-proxy-browser-management.md).
