# NZ's public VKT data shows BEV kilometres are still small, but it does not yet publish a clean hybrid/PHEV split

## Executive answer

- **The public official answer is narrower than the issue asks for.** The Ministry of Transport's latest annual fleet statistics app is the 2024 update, released in October/November 2025, and its downloadable workbook publishes annual light-vehicle VKT for petrol, diesel and pure electric vehicles but not a directly observed BEV/PHEV/ordinary-hybrid/petrol/diesel table for the period after EV RUC began on 1 April 2024 [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics); [annual fleet statistics Shiny app](https://www.mot-dev.link/fleet/annual-motor-vehicle-fleet-statistics/) ([archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260130000407/https://www.mot-dev.link/fleet/annual-motor-vehicle-fleet-statistics/)).
- **In the observed 2024 Ministry table, light petrol vehicles still dominate VKT.** Published 2024 light-vehicle travel was 32.013b km for light petrol, 12.775b km for light diesel and 0.987b km for light pure electric vehicles, equal to about 69.9%, 27.9% and 2.2% of total light travel respectively; this is my calculation from the Ministry workbook's "8.2a,b" and "1.4 to 1.7" sheets [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).
- **A transparent count-weighted allocation suggests ordinary hybrids are a bigger current revenue-proxy issue than BEVs or PHEVs.** Allocating the Ministry's 2024 petrol and diesel VKT across its 2024 light-fleet fuel counts gives an approximate split of 28.620b km petrol non-hybrid, 12.715b km diesel non-hybrid, 3.115b km ordinary hybrid, 0.338b km PHEV/range-extender and 0.987b km BEV; this allocation is not a Ministry-published VKT table and assumes equal average VKT within the published petrol and diesel travel pools [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).
- **At current light EV/PHEV RUC rates, the 2024 BEV/PHEV kilometre pool is small in road-revenue terms.** Applying GST-exclusive RUC rates to the observed BEV and allocated PHEV kilometres gives about $65m from BEVs and $11m from PHEVs, or about $76m combined; that scale is small beside 2024/25 net NLTF fuel excise duty of $1.926b and net RUC of $1.998b, but this is a calendar-year VKT sensitivity, not audited revenue [Ministry of Transport rates page](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/rates-of-petrol-excise-duty-and-road-user-charges); [Ministry of Transport PHEV Cabinet minute](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Road-User-Charges-Light-Electric-RUC-Vehicles-Amendment-Bill.pdf); [NZTA NLTF annual report 2024/25](https://www.nzta.govt.nz/assets/resources/annual-report-national-land-transport-fund-annual-report-202425/annual-report-2025-nltf.pdf); [IRD GST](https://www.ird.govt.nz/gst).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - high for the published petrol/diesel/pure-electric VKT and current rate arithmetic, but only medium for the powertrain split because hybrid and PHEV VKT are allocated from counts rather than observed in a public table.

## Evidence

### Scope and source path

I scoped this finding to light vehicles because the policy change that triggered the issue was the end of the light EV and plug-in hybrid RUC exemption from 1 April 2024, while heavy electric vehicles have a separate exemption timeline [Beehive, 16 January 2024](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/electric-vehicles-pay-road-user-charges); [Ministry of Transport RUC system](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/road-user-charges-system). The Ministry page says the annual fleet statistics use the Motor Vehicle Register as a key source, can be downloaded as a complete spreadsheet, and classify light vehicles as light passenger and light commercial vehicles up to 3,500kg [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).

I downloaded `NZVehicleFleet_2024.xlsx` from the Ministry's Shiny app on 7 July 2026 by opening `https://www.mot-dev.link/fleet/annual-motor-vehicle-fleet-statistics/` in Playwright/Chrome and clicking the generated `download_all_tables` link; direct `curl` to the official Ministry page returned an Incapsula challenge, so I treated the official page as bot-blocked tooling rather than dead, consistent with the repo fetch ladder [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics) ([archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260221104038/https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics)); [annual fleet statistics Shiny app](https://www.mot-dev.link/fleet/annual-motor-vehicle-fleet-statistics/) ([archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260130000407/https://www.mot-dev.link/fleet/annual-motor-vehicle-fleet-statistics/)).

The Ministry's Vehicle Fleet Model documentation confirms why a powertrain VKT table matters: the model projects fleet composition, VKT, energy use, emissions, registrations and scrappage, and it disaggregates by vehicle type and power type; however, that model update reports historic/estimated data only to 2022 and projects from 2023 to 2055, so it is not an observed post-RUC VKT table [Ministry of Transport Vehicle Fleet Model documentation](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Vehicle-Fleet-Model-Documentation_202405_v7.pdf).

### Observed VKT in the public annual workbook

The Ministry workbook's published light-fuel travel table gives the following observed VKT series for the latest three years available; "shown sum" is slightly below 100% because the workbook has small motorcycle, bus, truck and other lines outside the light petrol/diesel/pure-electric rows [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).

| Calendar year | Light petrol VKT | Light diesel VKT | Light pure electric VKT | Total light VKT | Observed light shares |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 2022 | 30.464b km | 12.294b km | 0.433b km | 43.201b km | Petrol 70.5%, diesel 28.5%, pure electric 1.0% |
| 2023 | 31.904b km | 12.700b km | 0.760b km | 45.374b km | Petrol 70.3%, diesel 28.0%, pure electric 1.7% |
| 2024 | 32.013b km | 12.775b km | 0.987b km | 45.782b km | Petrol 69.9%, diesel 27.9%, pure electric 2.2% |

The 2024 pure-electric VKT share is larger than the pure-electric vehicle count share in the same workbook: the fuel-count sheet records 78,834 light vehicles with main fuel `Electric` out of 4.310m light vehicles, or 1.8%, while the travel table records 0.987b pure-electric km out of 45.782b total light km, or 2.2%; this comparison uses the workbook's "8.4" and "1.4 to 1.7" sheets [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).

### Allocated powertrain estimate for 2024

Because the workbook does not publish hybrid and PHEV VKT separately, I allocated the workbook's 2024 light petrol VKT across petrol-side fuel counts and its 2024 light diesel VKT across diesel-side fuel counts, then kept pure-electric VKT as the observed BEV line; this is a transparent proxy, not a Ministry-published statistic [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics). The allocation treats `Petrol hybrid`, `Petrol electric hybrid`, `Diesel hybrid` and `Diesel electric hybrid` as ordinary hybrids, treats `Plugin petrol hybrid (PHEV)`, `Plugin diesel hybrid (PHEV)` and `Electric (petrol extended)` as PHEV/range-extender, and treats `Electric` as BEV/pure electric, matching the workbook's fuel labels [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).

| 2024 powertrain bucket | Source status | Estimated VKT | Share of 2024 light VKT | Revenue relevance |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| Petrol non-hybrid | Allocated from published petrol VKT and fuel counts | 28.620b km | 62.5% | Pays FED through petrol rather than RUC [Ministry of Transport RUC system](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/road-user-charges-system) |
| Diesel non-hybrid | Allocated from published diesel VKT and fuel counts | 12.715b km | 27.7% | Pays RUC as diesel light vehicles [Ministry of Transport RUC system](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/road-user-charges-system) |
| Ordinary hybrid | Allocated from published petrol/diesel VKT and hybrid fuel counts | 3.115b km | 6.8% | Mostly remains in the FED proxy problem unless and until petrol vehicles move to RUC [Ministry of Transport RUC system](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/road-user-charges-system) |
| PHEV/range-extender | Allocated from published petrol/diesel VKT and PHEV/range-extender fuel counts | 0.338b km | 0.7% | Pays reduced light PHEV RUC and still uses some petrol/diesel [Ministry of Transport PHEV Cabinet minute](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Road-User-Charges-Light-Electric-RUC-Vehicles-Amendment-Bill.pdf) |
| BEV/pure electric | Observed in workbook as light pure-electric travel | 0.987b km | 2.2% | Pays light EV RUC from 1 April 2024 [Beehive, 16 January 2024](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/electric-vehicles-pay-road-user-charges) |
| Other/unallocated | Residual from total light VKT | 0.008b km | 0.02% | Too small to affect this revenue question [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics) |

The allocation is useful for scale but should not be treated as a behavioural result, because the Vehicle Fleet Model documentation says VKT by sub-category is modelled from average VKT per vehicle and vehicle counts and then adjusted to broad VKT projections, which means same-average allocation is a simplifying assumption rather than a substitute for observed odometer-based hybrid/PHEV VKT [Ministry of Transport Vehicle Fleet Model documentation](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Vehicle-Fleet-Model-Documentation_202405_v7.pdf).

### Revenue implications

The current revenue system charges RUC by distance for diesel, heavy and electric vehicles, while fuel excise duty applies only to petrol and is embedded in the pump price [Ministry of Transport RUC system](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/road-user-charges-system). Light EVs and plug-in hybrids entered RUC from 1 April 2024; the public announcement said light EV owners would pay $76 per 1,000km and that plug-in hybrids would pay a reduced rate, and Cabinet later accepted the select committee's $38 per 1,000km PHEV rate [Beehive, 16 January 2024](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/electric-vehicles-pay-road-user-charges); [Ministry of Transport PHEV Cabinet minute](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Road-User-Charges-Light-Electric-RUC-Vehicles-Amendment-Bill.pdf).

For an NLTF-like comparison I removed GST from the retail RUC rates because IRD says GST is charged at 15%, giving about $66.09 per 1,000km for light EVs and $33.04 per 1,000km for PHEVs [IRD GST](https://www.ird.govt.nz/gst); [Ministry of Transport rates page](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/rates-of-petrol-excise-duty-and-road-user-charges). Applying those rates to the 2024 VKT estimate gives about $65m from BEV kilometres and $11m from PHEV/range-extender kilometres, or about $76m combined, before any allowance for compliance, timing, refunds, administration cost or the fact that the 2024 calendar year includes three pre-RUC months [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics); [Ministry of Transport rates page](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/rates-of-petrol-excise-duty-and-road-user-charges).

That $76m sensitivity is small compared with 2024/25 net NLTF revenue lines: NZTA reported net RUC of $1.998b and net fuel excise duty of $1.926b in 2024/25 [NZTA NLTF annual report 2024/25](https://www.nzta.govt.nz/assets/resources/annual-report-national-land-transport-fund-annual-report-202425/annual-report-2025-nltf.pdf). This supports the earlier stream finding that current EV RUC is not the main road-revenue hole, but it also strengthens the ordinary-hybrid concern because the allocated ordinary-hybrid VKT pool is about 3.2 times the observed BEV VKT pool and remains largely funded through fuel consumed rather than kilometres driven [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics); [Ministry of Transport RUC system](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/road-user-charges-system).

As a simple stress test, if the allocated 3.115b ordinary-hybrid kilometres paid FED at 5.0L/100km rather than at a 9.2L/100km petrol benchmark, the difference would be about $92m at the ordinary petrol excise rate of 70.024 cents/litre; this is my calculation using the Ministry's petrol excise rate and the earlier stream's 9.2L/100km benchmark, not a Ministry revenue forecast [Ministry of Transport rates page](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/rates-of-petrol-excise-duty-and-road-user-charges); [land-transport revenue-gap finding](land-transport-revenue-gap.md).

## What would change this conclusion

- A Ministry or NZTA observed odometer-based table for 2024, 2025 and 2026 that directly separates petrol, diesel, ordinary hybrid, PHEV and BEV VKT would replace the allocation model and could materially change the hybrid/PHEV shares [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).
- A post-April-2024 RUC revenue table split by light EVs, plug-in hybrids, light diesel and heavy vehicles would change the revenue section from a VKT-based sensitivity into an observed revenue finding [Ministry of Transport RUC system](https://www.transport.govt.nz/area-of-interest/revenue/road-user-charges-system).
- Evidence that hybrids or PHEVs have materially higher or lower annual VKT than conventional petrol/diesel vehicles would change the allocation, because this finding assumes equal average VKT within each published petrol/diesel travel pool [Ministry of Transport Vehicle Fleet Model documentation](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Vehicle-Fleet-Model-Documentation_202405_v7.pdf).
- I could not verify a public monthly or quarterly VKT-by-powertrain table after 1 April 2024, and I could not verify actual PHEV electric-versus-petrol use; those gaps matter for both revenue and emissions [Ministry of Transport annual fleet statistics](https://www.transport.govt.nz/statistics-and-insights/fleet-statistics/sheet/annual-fleet-statistics).
- I opened a separate `.skills` repo request for a Ministry fleet/VKT CLI so the next researcher can fetch and parse this workbook repeatably instead of driving the Shiny app manually [thecolab-ai/.skills#167](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/issues/167).
