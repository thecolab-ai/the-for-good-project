# DataGrid Makarewa's public consent record is mainly council RMA approvals, not a completed fast-track approval; Environment Southland granted nine regional consents, while exact Southland District Council and Invercargill City Council conditions still need the decision records

## Executive answer

- The primary public consent decision I could verify is the **Environment Southland / Southland Regional Council non-notified RMA decision dated 11 March 2026**, made by independent commissioner Hugh Leersnyder under delegated authority, granting nine regional consents for the Makarewa data centre, groundwater, wastewater, air discharges, wetland works, dewatering, and the Foveaux Strait / Oreti Beach datacable [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf); [Environment Southland public notice](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z).
- The regional decision describes a data centre providing **up to 240MW of IT capacity**, with a high-speed internet cable from Oreti Beach to Australia; this is not the same number as DataGrid's announced **280MW power consumption** and **78,000 sqm** campus [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf); [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).
- The Environment Southland consents lapse on **11 March 2034**, expire on **11 March 2061**, and are granted for **35 years**; the decision says the eight-year lapse period was chosen for consistency with the separate Southland District Council consents [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf); [groundwater take permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf).
- FTA104 was **not** the pathway that produced the March 2026 consents: MfE's FTA104 page is an application page last updated **14 January 2025**, the application itself proposed seeking approvals under the RMA from Southland District Council, Invercargill City Council, and Environment Southland, and an MBIE briefing dated **27 May 2025** says DataGrid applied to be listed in Schedule 2 of the Fast-track Approvals Act 2024 but **was not chosen for inclusion** [MfE FTA104 page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20260119175247/https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/)); [FTA104 application](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20250815124032/https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf)); [MBIE briefing](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025?utm=).
- I could not locate the actual Southland District Council land-use consent decision or Invercargill City Council approval/conditions in public council records; the exact SDC/ICC conditions therefore remain **Medium-to-Low confidence** until those decision documents are obtained by council release or LGOIMA [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf); [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the regional council record and fast-track status are well supported by primary documents, but the SDC and ICC decision documents were not found publicly.

## Evidence

### What Environment Southland approved

Environment Southland published a public notice on **13 March 2026** stating that the resource consents sought by Datagrid NZ Partnership Limited from Environment Southland were granted, and the same notice lists nine granted consents plus the s42A report and commissioner decision [Environment Southland public notice](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z).

### What Southland District Council and Invercargill City Council appear to cover

The regional decision says the same commissioner was engaged by **Environment Southland and Southland District Council** to decide resource consents for the datacable and datacentre, but says the Southland District Council application was decided separately [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).

The Environment Southland decision states that the applicant also applied to Southland District Council for land-use consent for the datacentre site at Flora Road East, Makarewa, and the earlier FTA104 application says the relevant RMA local authorities were **Southland District Council, Invercargill City Council, and Environment Southland** [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf); [FTA104 application](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf).

The FTA104 application identifies Invercargill City Council and Southland District Council as road-controlling authorities for roads along which cables would extend from the submarine cable to the Makarewa site, and the Environment Southland decision says cable trenching at Oreti Beach would require a partial road closure by Invercargill City Council for about two days [FTA104 application](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf); [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).

DataGrid announced on **11 March 2026** that approvals had been granted by **Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council**, and that both the Makarewa AI factory and the Tasman Ring landing at Oreti Beach were approved [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent). This is consistent with the public regional record, but it is still company-sourced for the exact SDC and ICC approvals because I did not locate those decision documents [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent); [Environment Southland public notice](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z).

### Fast-track record and relationship to the council consents

MfE's FTA104 page is titled **"Application FTA104 - Datagrid Sustainable Data Centre Park"**, is last updated **14 January 2025**, and lists redacted application and supporting documents rather than an approval decision or conditions [MfE FTA104 page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20260119175247/https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/)).

The FTA104 application, submitted **2 May 2024**, says the applicant was seeking approval under the **Resource Management Act 1991**, names Southland District Council, Invercargill City Council, and Environment Southland as the relevant local authorities, and describes the project as **49ha**, a submarine cable between Australia and Invercargill, a landing station for up to three cables, and a dedicated **220kV** Transpower grid-exit substation [FTA104 application](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20250815124032/https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf)).

The FTA104 application says an earlier Southland District Council application **RMA/2002/53409** was on hold under RMA section **91D** because of the National Policy Statement for Highly Productive Land, and that this SDC application would be withdrawn before lodgement to an expert panel under the Fast-track Approvals Act [FTA104 application](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf).

That planned fast-track route did not become the operative approval route in the public record: an MBIE briefing for a **5 June 2025** ministerial meeting says DataGrid applied to have the project listed within Schedule 2 of the **Fast-track Approvals Act 2024**, but the project **was not chosen for inclusion** [MBIE briefing](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025?utm=). The same MBIE briefing, dated **27 May 2025**, still describes the site as LUC 2 land and says the proposed site was prevented by NPS-HPL restrictions at that point [MBIE briefing](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025?utm=).

The best-supported conclusion is therefore: **FTA104 was an attempted fast-track listing/referral pathway that did not itself produce an approval; the March 2026 public approvals were ordinary council RMA approvals, at least for the Environment Southland consents** [MBIE briefing](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025?utm=); [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).

### Notification, submissions, and public comments

The Environment Southland decision is expressly a **non-notified** resource-consent decision, and the public notice provides the decision, s42A report, application documents, technical comments, RFI material, and notification consideration report rather than a public-submissions bundle [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf); [Environment Southland public notice](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z).

The Environment Southland decision records written approvals from several organisations and iwi entities and says the consent authority must not have regard to effects on persons who gave written approval under RMA section 104(3); this finding deliberately does not reproduce individual neighbouring landowner details or personal information [Environment Southland decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).

The MfE FTA104 page lists application documents and redacted letters/supporting material, but I did not find a posted public-submissions bundle or an expert-panel comments process for FTA104 [MfE FTA104 page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20260119175247/https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/)).

## What would change this conclusion

- The **Southland District Council decision document** would upgrade the SDC land-use approval from Medium to High confidence and settle the exact activity status, site-design controls, noise/light/building/earthworks conditions, and lapse date.
- The **Invercargill City Council approval document** would upgrade the ICC finding from Low-Medium to High confidence and settle whether ICC issued a resource consent, road-opening approval, road-closure approval, corridor-access approval, or another approval type for the Oreti Beach / road corridor works.
- An **EPA or MfE expert-panel record for FTA104 after 27 May 2025** would change the fast-track conclusion, but I found the opposite: MBIE says the project was not chosen for Schedule 2, and the public approvals found are council RMA approvals.
- I could not verify whether the earlier SDC application **RMA/2002/53409** was formally withdrawn, replaced, varied, or decided under a later application number; a council file release or LGOIMA response is needed.
- I did not find public submissions because the Environment Southland decision was non-notified and the MfE FTA104 page did not expose a submissions/comments bundle; if SDC or ICC separately notified any component, their submission records would change that part of the conclusion.
