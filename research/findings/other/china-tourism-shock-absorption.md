# Alternative visitor markets could absorb a small China tourism spend shock, but not quickly replace a large one

## Executive answer

- A 25% fall in China-origin visitor spend would remove about **$366m** from the **$1.463b** China spend baseline, while a 50% fall would remove about **$731m**; those figures are arithmetic from MBIE's year-ended-March-2026 IVS country spend table [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json).
- The best structural substitutes are a **portfolio** of USA, UK/Europe, Australia, and India, not a single market: the USA already spends more than China in the IVS table, UK and Germany have comparable or higher per-trip spend, Australia has much larger volume but lower spend per visitor, and India has high strategic demand but is still small and visa-required [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json); [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/); [Immigration New Zealand visa waiver list](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/visit/what-you-need-to-visit-new-zealand/visa-waiver-countries-and-territories/).
- Pricing is not the main barrier for the USA and Europe: MBIE reports mean spend per visitor of **$5,963** for the USA, **$5,475** for the UK, **$7,261** for Rest of Europe, and **$8,371** for Germany, compared with **$5,543** for China; Australia is the clear spend-dilution risk at **$3,079** per visitor [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json).
- India is the most plausible long-run growth option but the weakest short-run absorber: Tourism New Zealand says Indian visitors spent **$266.1m** in the year ending May 2025, only about **18%** of China's March-2026 IVS spend baseline, and Air New Zealand/Air India describe India-New Zealand access as connecting through Australia or Singapore while a direct service is only being explored by the end of 2028, subject to aircraft deliveries and approvals [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/); [Air New Zealand, 2025](https://www.airnewzealandnewsroom.com/press-release-2025-air-new-zealand-and-air-india-enter-new-codeshare-partnership-and-mou-to-explore-direct-flights-between-india-and-new-zealand).
- Aviation capacity makes rapid replacement difficult: Auckland Airport expected **up to 50 weekly Auckland-China flights** in summer 2025/26 and **5.8m international seats** across all international routes for November 2025 to March 2026, while Air New Zealand says widebody growth depends on returning grounded aircraft and taking two of ten new 787s, with 20-25% widebody growth over the next two years [Auckland Airport China connectivity, 2025](https://corporate.aucklandairport.co.nz/news/latest-media/2025/sichuan-airlines-boosts-akl-s-connectivity-to-china); [Auckland Airport summer seats, 2025](https://corporate.aucklandairport.co.nz/news/latest-media/2025/auckland-airport-gears-up-for-summer-with-207-000-more-internati); [Air New Zealand interim result, 2026](https://www.airnewzealandnewsroom.com/press-release-2026-air-new-zealand-announces-2026-interim-result-and-provides-full-year-guidance).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the spend, visa, and announced-route evidence is strong; the speed-of-absorption conclusion is Medium because I did not find a complete public route-level seat-capacity dataset by origin market, load factor, and season.

## Evidence

### Scale of the shock and candidate markets

This finding answers whether alternative visitor markets can structurally absorb a China-origin tourism spend shock, using the year-ended-March-2026 MBIE IVS China spend baseline from issue #498 and focusing on routes, visa settings, spend-per-visitor profiles, and market capacity rather than current diversification programme outcomes [Issue #564](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/issues/564); [China exposure baseline](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/china-export-exposure-baseline.md).

MBIE's IVS annual summary JSON reports year-ended-March-2026 visitor spend of **$13.735b** for all countries, **$4.238b** from Australia, **$1.969b** from the USA, **$1.463b** from China, **$1.227b** from Rest of Europe, **$986m** from the UK, **$914m** from Rest of world, **$538m** from Germany, and **$310m** from Canada [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json). A 25% China spend shock is therefore about **$366m**, and a 50% shock is about **$731m**, calculated from the same MBIE China value of **$1.463b** [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json).

The USA is the strongest single-market candidate by current spend because MBIE puts USA visitor spend at **$1.969b**, or about **135%** of China's **$1.463b**, and Tourism New Zealand says **382,000** American visitors arrived in the year ending May 2025, with **67%** holiday arrivals and **73m** people in the USA seriously considering visiting New Zealand [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json); [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

Europe is plausible as a high-value portfolio rather than a single origin market: MBIE's UK, Germany, and Rest of Europe categories sum to about **$2.751b**, or about **188%** of China's spend, while Tourism New Zealand reports **188,000** UK visitors and **73,000** German visitors in the year ending May 2025 [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json); [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

Australia has the largest scale and easiest access but is not a like-for-like spend substitute: MBIE puts Australia at **$4.238b**, or about **290%** of China spend, and Tourism New Zealand says Australia accounted for almost half of international visitor arrivals and **1.4m** arrivals in the year ending May 2025 [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json); [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

India is a strategic growth candidate but begins from a much smaller New Zealand base: Tourism New Zealand reports **81,000** Indian travellers, **$266.1m** total spend, **$67.4m** holiday spend, and **27m** serious considerers across New Delhi, Bangalore, and Mumbai in the year ending May 2025 [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/). Against China's **$1.463b** year-ended-March-2026 IVS baseline, India's Tourism New Zealand market spend is about **18%** of China spend, so India would need very large percentage growth to absorb even a 25% China shock by itself [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json); [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

### Pricing and spend-per-visitor fit

MBIE's year-ended-March-2026 IVS mean spend per visitor is **$5,543** for China, **$5,963** for the USA, **$5,475** for the UK, **$7,261** for Rest of Europe, **$8,371** for Germany, **$5,132** for Canada, **$4,462** for Asia, and **$3,079** for Australia [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json). Replacing Chinese visitors one-for-one with USA, Germany, or Rest of Europe visitors would not create an average-spend gap on MBIE's current means, while replacing them one-for-one with Australian visitors would lose about **$2,464** per visitor before considering different purpose mix, seasonality, and regional itinerary [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json).

India's pricing profile is closer to China than its current scale is: Tourism New Zealand reports Indian average spend per trip of **$5,335**, rising to **$5,402** for holiday visitors, compared with China's **$5,543** MBIE mean spend per visitor and Tourism New Zealand's **$5,809** China average spend per trip for the year ending May 2025 [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/); [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json). This makes India a better price-fit than Australia, but not a fast one-market replacement because its current spend base is much smaller [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

### Visa and regulatory barriers

Immigration New Zealand's visa-waiver list includes Germany, Japan, Singapore, the United Kingdom, and the United States, and says people travelling on a listed passport can travel without a visa but need an NZeTA first [Immigration New Zealand visa waiver list](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/visit/what-you-need-to-visit-new-zealand/visa-waiver-countries-and-territories/). The same page does not list India or mainland China among visa-waiver countries, and Immigration New Zealand's Visitor Visa page says people travelling on passports from some countries must apply for a Visitor Visa, with cost from **NZD $441** and **80%** processed within **2 weeks** [Immigration New Zealand visa waiver list](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/visit/what-you-need-to-visit-new-zealand/visa-waiver-countries-and-territories/); [Immigration New Zealand Visitor Visa](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/visas/visitor-visa/).

Visa-waiver travellers still face a pre-travel authorisation step: Immigration New Zealand says NZeTA requests should be allowed **72 hours** for processing, though many are processed faster [Immigration New Zealand Visa Waiver Visitor Visa](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/visas/visa-waiver-visitor-visa/). The practical regulatory gap is therefore largest for India because Indian passport holders generally need a visitor visa, while USA, UK, Germany, Japan, and Singapore travellers can use the NZeTA pathway [Immigration New Zealand visa waiver list](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/visit/what-you-need-to-visit-new-zealand/visa-waiver-countries-and-territories/); [Immigration New Zealand Visitor Visa](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/visas/visitor-visa/).

China has a partial facilitation channel that is not available to India: from 3 November 2025, Chinese passport holders travelling from Australia with an eligible Australian visa can visit New Zealand on an NZeTA for up to three months under a 12-month trial [Immigration New Zealand, 2025](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/about-us/news-centre/easier-travel-from-australia-to-new-zealand-for-chinese-visitors/). China-origin group tourism also has a specific outbound-travel dependency because Tourism New Zealand says Approved Destination Status is an arrangement with the Chinese Government that lets Chinese holiday travellers visit in a tour group, and inbound operators or guides need ADS approval before organising ADS tours to New Zealand [Tourism New Zealand ADS](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/partner-with-us/approved-destination-status/).

### Aviation logistics and lead times

Auckland Airport's summer 2025/26 statement expected **5.8m seats** to **43 international destinations** between November 2025 and March 2026, up **3.7%** from summer 2024/25 [Auckland Airport summer seats, 2025](https://corporate.aucklandairport.co.nz/news/latest-media/2025/auckland-airport-gears-up-for-summer-with-207-000-more-internati). Auckland Airport also expected **up to 50 weekly flights** between Auckland and China in summer 2025/26 after Sichuan Airlines' Chengdu return, and described Chengdu as Auckland's eighth mainland Chinese destination [Auckland Airport China connectivity, 2025](https://corporate.aucklandairport.co.nz/news/latest-media/2025/sichuan-airlines-boosts-akl-s-connectivity-to-china).

India's direct-air capacity is the clearest structural bottleneck among candidate markets: Air New Zealand and Air India announced codeshare connections from Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Chennai via Sydney, Melbourne, or Singapore to Auckland, Christchurch, Wellington, and Queenstown, and said a direct India-New Zealand service would be explored by the end of 2028, subject to new aircraft deliveries and government approvals [Air New Zealand, 2025](https://www.airnewzealandnewsroom.com/press-release-2025-air-new-zealand-and-air-india-enter-new-codeshare-partnership-and-mou-to-explore-direct-flights-between-india-and-new-zealand). Air India's own New Zealand booking page also says there are currently no direct flights to New Zealand and that Air India offers connecting services [Air India flights to New Zealand](https://www.airindia.com/en-in/book-flights/flights-to-new-zealand).

Europe has strong high-spend demand but lacks simple New Zealand flag-carrier direct access: Air New Zealand's Europe sales page says Europe-origin travellers depart on partner airlines and then connect onto Air New Zealand flights, commonly via Asia or North America, and Air New Zealand said in 2020 it would not resume its suspended Los Angeles-London route [Air New Zealand Europe flights](https://www.airnewzealand.eu/flight-deals-to-new-zealand); [Air New Zealand network revision, 2020](https://www.airnewzealandnewsroom.com/press-release-air-new-zealand-revises-international-network). This does not prevent European substitution, but it makes the pathway more dependent on partner-airline capacity, one-stop routings, fares, and long-haul connection quality [Air New Zealand Europe flights](https://www.airnewzealand.eu/flight-deals-to-new-zealand).

The USA/North America has better direct-flight fit than India or Europe, but still competes for scarce widebody aircraft: Air New Zealand says engine availability issues have taken longer than expected, four grounded Airbus neo and Boeing 787 aircraft are expected to return to service through the 2026 calendar year, and two of ten new 787 aircraft are expected later in the financial year, giving **20-25%** widebody capacity growth over the next two years [Air New Zealand interim result, 2026](https://www.airnewzealandnewsroom.com/press-release-2026-air-new-zealand-announces-2026-interim-result-and-provides-full-year-guidance). IATA's December 2025 global outlook separately forecasts **4.9%** passenger traffic growth in 2026 but says deceleration is partly due to persistent supply-side constraints, including limited aircraft availability and labour shortages [IATA Global Outlook for Air Transport, December 2025](https://www.iata.org/en/iata-repository/publications/economic-reports/global-outlook-for-air-transport-december-2025/).

### Buyer relationships and product-market fit

Tourism New Zealand already treats the assessed substitutes of Australia, USA, UK, Germany, and India as major markets, alongside China and Japan, and says it selects markets based on existing or potential airline connectivity plus a significant group of people seriously considering New Zealand for a holiday [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/). That supports the conclusion that the assessed candidate markets are not speculative, but it does not prove they can be scaled fast enough to replace a major China shock [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

The China market has a distinct trade-channel structure because Tourism New Zealand's China Market Development Unit approves and monitors ADS-accredited inbound operators and tour guides, and operators need ADS approval before organising ADS tours to New Zealand [Tourism New Zealand ADS](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/partner-with-us/approved-destination-status/). By contrast, the USA, UK, Germany, and India market snapshots emphasise broad holiday considerer pools, nature/landscape motivations, off-peak travel, and regional dispersal rather than a single ADS group-tour compliance channel [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

The itinerary fit is mixed: issue #498 found China visitor itinerary nights concentrated in Queenstown-Lakes, Auckland, Christchurch, and Mackenzie using MBIE IVS itinerary JSON, while Tourism New Zealand says Indian visitors commonly favour Auckland, Queenstown-Lakes, and Christchurch and **52%** visit four or more regions; UK and German visitors also show regional dispersal, with **55%** of UK visitors and **62%** of German visitors travelling to four or more regions [China exposure baseline](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/china-export-exposure-baseline.md); [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/). This suggests alternative markets could fit many of the same high-profile destinations, but the regional replacement would not be automatic because visitor purpose, tour style, length of stay, and gateway routes differ by market [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/).

### Capacity constraints and competition for travellers

India's outbound-travel growth is attractive but contested: Australia's India Economic Strategy projected Indian tourists to Australia could grow from **300,000** in 2017 to nearly **1.2m** by 2035, with about **70m** Indians travelling overseas annually by 2035, and Tourism Australia reports Indian traveller expenditure in Australia of **A$2.7b** in the year ending September 2025, up **15%** over 2024 [Australia DFAT India Economic Strategy](https://www.dfat.gov.au/publications/trade-and-investment/india-economic-strategy/ies/chapter-6.html); [Tourism Australia India insights](https://www.tourism.australia.com/en/insights/markets/india/market-insights-snapshot.html). Those two sources support the long-run India opportunity, but they also show Australia is already chasing and capturing the same India outbound segment at much larger spend scale than New Zealand's **$266.1m** India visitor spend reported by Tourism New Zealand [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/); [Tourism Australia India insights](https://www.tourism.australia.com/en/insights/markets/india/market-insights-snapshot.html).

The overall structural conclusion is that a **25%** China tourism spend shock is plausible to absorb over multiple seasons if the USA, Europe, Australia, and India all contribute growth, because the required replacement is about **$366m** and several assessed candidate markets already have large or high-spend bases [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json); [Tourism New Zealand markets overview](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/about-us/markets-overview/). A **50%** shock is materially harder to absorb quickly because the required **$731m** is larger than Tourism New Zealand's reported current India and Germany spends individually, aviation expansion is bounded by widebody delivery/availability and route lead times, and the most scalable nearby market, Australia, has much lower per-visitor spend [MBIE IVS annual summary JSON](https://teic.mbie.govt.nz/ste/data/views/theEconomy/demand/IVSAnnual/ivsAnnualSummary.json); [Auckland Airport summer seats, 2025](https://corporate.aucklandairport.co.nz/news/latest-media/2025/auckland-airport-gears-up-for-summer-with-207-000-more-internati); [Air New Zealand interim result, 2026](https://www.airnewzealandnewsroom.com/press-release-2026-air-new-zealand-announces-2026-interim-result-and-provides-full-year-guidance).

## What would change this conclusion

- A public route-level dataset showing origin-market seats, load factors, seasonality, aircraft type, and forward schedules for China, India, North America, Europe, Japan, Singapore, and Australia would materially improve the aviation-capacity conclusion; I found route announcements and airport/airline capacity statements, but not a complete public seat-capacity dataset.
- Evidence that airlines could redeploy aircraft from China routes to North America, Europe connections, India, or other Asia within one or two seasons at comparable load factors and yields would make a 50% China shock look more absorbable.
- A visa-waiver or materially faster visitor-visa pathway for Indian passport holders would improve India's short-run substitutability; current public INZ settings make India a visa-required market while USA, UK, Germany, Japan, and Singapore are visa-waiver/NZeTA markets.
- A current MBIE IVS country breakdown that separately reports India, Singapore, Japan, Korea, and other Asian markets in the same table as China would improve price and scale comparisons; I used Tourism New Zealand's year-ending-May-2025 India and Japan market figures where MBIE's public annual summary grouped some countries.
- A regional spend-by-origin dataset could change the regional conclusion, because itinerary-night overlap with Queenstown-Lakes, Auckland, Christchurch, and Mackenzie does not prove equivalent spend replacement in those regions.
- A human with tourism trade, airline network-planning, or inbound-tour-operator experience should review the route-lead-time and buyer-relationship interpretation before it is used for operational decisions.

I could not verify a complete public China-versus-alternative route capacity dataset, current India-specific MBIE IVS spend in the same March-2026 table, airline load-factor/yield assumptions, or the depth and conversion performance of Tourism New Zealand's travel-trade relationships by market.
