# NZ should adapt targeted incentives, workload relief, mentoring and workforce data, not import whole overseas teacher-career systems

## Executive answer

- The most transferable instruments for NZ's shortage points are **targeted scholarships/bonding, subject-and-place retention payments, earn-while-training pathways for existing unqualified/LAT teachers, funded workload relief, structured mentoring, and better linked workforce data**; these map to NZ's current secondary, te reo Māori/Māori-medium, rural and priority-staffing shortage signals rather than treating teacher supply as a uniform national headcount problem [Ministry of Education TDS release, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released), [Education Workforce TBS](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/principals-and-schools/initiatives-schools-and-kura/teacher-bonding-scheme), [Education Workforce EBITE](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award).
- **Australia transfers best as a toolkit architecture**, not as a single programme: its National Teacher Workforce Action Plan groups supply, ITE, retention, profession status and workforce-data actions, while its Commonwealth Teaching Scholarships combine large scholarships with a public-school teaching obligation but do not themselves place teachers in jobs [Australian Government, NTWAP](https://www.education.gov.au/national-teacher-workforce-action-plan), [Commonwealth Teaching Scholarships FAQ](https://www.education.gov.au/teaching-scholarships/frequently-asked-questions).
- **England transfers best on targeted early-career STEM retention and workload governance**: England pays school teachers in chemistry, computing, mathematics and physics a targeted retention incentive of £3,000-£6,000 in eligible high-need state-funded secondary schools, and its workload taskforce was set up around a five-hours-per-week reduction ambition [DfE targeted retention incentive](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/targeted-retention-incentive-payments-for-school-teachers), [DfE workload reduction taskforce](https://www.gov.uk/government/groups/workload-reduction-taskforce).
- **Singapore transfers best on career ladders, mentoring culture and professional-development design**, but not as a wholesale career-service model: Singapore MOE has three career tracks, scholarships with tuition/allowance support, and TALIS 2024 evidence of stronger mentoring/professional development than the OECD average, but NZ's school boards employ staff and pay/allowances sit in national collective or individual employment agreements [Singapore MOE career tracks](https://www.moe.gov.sg/careers/become-teachers/pri-sec-jc-ci/professional-development), [Singapore MOE TALIS 2024](https://www.moe.gov.sg/news/press-releases/20251007-singapore-teachers-embrace-digital-technologies-and-benefit-from-strong-professional-development-oecd-talis-2024-study), [NZ school boards](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/administration-and-management/what-school-board), [NZ secondary agreements](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/people-and-employment/secondary-teachers-collective-and-individual-employment-agreements).
- Options to **rule out or heavily redesign** are England-style academy/MAT pay-flexibility machinery, Singapore-style central career deployment, and Australia-style federal/state implementation machinery; NZ can use the underlying instrument logic, but the institutional carrier must fit NZ boards, kura, collective agreements and Ministry-funded national schemes [NZ school boards](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/administration-and-management/what-school-board), [DfE 6,500 teachers plan](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/every-child-achieving-and-thriving/6500-additional-teachers-delivery-plan-html-version), [Singapore MOE career tracks](https://www.moe.gov.sg/careers/become-teachers/pri-sec-jc-ci/professional-development).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - high for the existence and design of the comparator instruments, medium for transferability judgement, and lower for expected NZ impact because public sources rarely evaluate whether each instrument changes actual teacher placement and retention in STEM, Māori-medium and priority-staffing roles.

## Evidence

### NZ already has the right instrument categories, but not yet enough public evidence on scale and targeting

The Ministry's 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply report projects a primary surplus of 530 teachers in 2026 rising to 1,350 by 2028, while secondary has a national shortfall of about 710 teachers in 2026 narrowing to 190 by 2028, and the same release cautions that national and regional projections do not always reflect supply challenges experienced by individual schools and communities [Ministry of Education TDS release, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released). That means comparator instruments should be judged by whether they reach **secondary shortage subjects, Māori-medium/te reo Māori roles, and priority-staffing schools**, not by whether they increase aggregate teacher numbers alone [Ministry of Education TDS release, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released).

NZ's current toolkit already includes scholarships, bonding, rural placement support, employment-based Māori-medium training, early-career release time and local allowances. The Ministry says around 460 teacher-training scholarships are awarded each year and prioritised for difficult-to-recruit STEM and te reo Māori subjects; Te Tipu Whakarito offers 130 annual scholarships for students aiming to teach in Kaupapa Māori/Māori-medium settings or teach te reo Māori in English-medium secondary schools; Go Rural provides a $4,000 grant for student-teacher placements of five weeks or more in eligible rural or isolated schools; and EBITE provides 65 awards for LAT holders in Kaupapa Māori/Māori-medium and te reo Māori roles to train while employed [Ministry of Education TDS release, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released), [Te Tipu Whakarito](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/becoming-teacher-new-zealand/become-teacher-scholarships/te-tipu-whakarito-scholarship), [Go Rural](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/becoming-teacher-new-zealand/become-teacher-scholarships/go-rural-isolated-placements-fund), [EBITE](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award).

NZ's Teacher Bonding Scheme is already a place-based retention instrument: teachers recruited to eligible vacancies can receive payments accumulating to $40,000 before tax after five years, the scheme has 185 places per year, and its stated aims are reducing recruitment challenges at Priority Staffing Schools, improving distribution to those schools/kura, increasing retention and getting more beginning teachers onto longer-term contracts [Education Workforce TBS](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/principals-and-schools/initiatives-schools-and-kura/teacher-bonding-scheme). NZ also has a school-board-level 3R allowance of up to $4,000 per year and staffing incentive / priority teacher supply allowances for schools or kura identified by the Ministry as needing additional recruitment and retention support [NZ secondary agreements](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/people-and-employment/secondary-teachers-collective-and-individual-employment-agreements).

The transfer question is therefore not "should NZ invent overseas-style incentives from scratch?", but "which overseas designs show where NZ should make existing instruments more specific, larger, easier to administer, or better measured?" This is interpretation from the NZ programme set above and from the comparator instruments below.

### What to rule out

England's school TRI is transferable as a targeted payment design, but England's wider pay-flexibility route is not directly transferable. England's 6,500 teachers plan says it will remove the ceiling on pay and build additional flexibility into the School Teachers' Pay and Conditions Document, while England's eligible state-funded secondary schools include local-authority-maintained schools, academies, free schools and multi-academy trusts [DfE 6,500 teachers plan](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/every-child-achieving-and-thriving/6500-additional-teachers-delivery-plan-html-version), [DfE targeted retention incentive](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/targeted-retention-incentive-payments-for-school-teachers). NZ should not copy that institutional route because every state and state-integrated school or kura is governed by a board, boards employ staff, and secondary pay/allowances/classroom release are set in collective or individual employment agreements [NZ school boards](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/administration-and-management/what-school-board), [NZ secondary agreements](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/people-and-employment/secondary-teachers-collective-and-individual-employment-agreements).

Singapore's career ladder is transferable as a design pattern, but not as a plug-in operating model. Singapore MOE frames teachers as Education Officers in a ministry career structure with three tracks and lateral movement across tracks, while NZ's employment relationship is carried by boards and national agreements rather than a single centralised employer career service [Singapore MOE career tracks](https://www.moe.gov.sg/careers/become-teachers/pri-sec-jc-ci/professional-development), [NZ school boards](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/administration-and-management/what-school-board). NZ's safer adaptation is a negotiated specialist/mentor/career allowance structure for te reo Māori, Māori-medium, STEM and rural expertise, not central deployment of teachers between schools/kura [NZ secondary agreements](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/people-and-employment/secondary-teachers-collective-and-individual-employment-agreements).

Australia's national action-plan structure is transferable, but its federal/state machinery is not. Australia's plan was agreed by Education Ministers and is implemented across Commonwealth, state and territory responsibilities; NZ can use the action-plan categories, de-identified workforce-data design and pilot discipline, but does not need a federal coordination layer to run national Ministry-funded schemes [Australian NTWAP](https://www.education.gov.au/national-teacher-workforce-action-plan), [Australian NTWAP priority area 5](https://www.education.gov.au/national-teacher-workforce-action-plan/priority-area-5-better-understanding-future-teacher-workforce-needs).

## What would change this conclusion

- A public evaluation showing that NZ's current TeachNZ, Te Tipu Whakarito, Te Huawhiti, EBITE, Go Rural, TBS and 3R/priority allowances already fill STEM, te reo Māori, Māori-medium and priority-staffing vacancies at high retention rates would shift the answer from "adapt and target harder" to "scale proven NZ instruments" [Ministry of Education TDS release, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released), [Education Workforce TBS](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/principals-and-schools/initiatives-schools-and-kura/teacher-bonding-scheme).
- A robust evaluation showing England's school TRI changes retention in eligible STEM subjects, rather than merely paying teachers who would have stayed, would raise confidence in a NZ subject-specific retention payment; I found current official design details but not a completed causal evaluation for the school-teacher TRI [DfE targeted retention incentive](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/targeted-retention-incentive-payments-for-school-teachers).
- Evidence from Kaupapa Māori, Māori-medium and te reo Māori education leaders could change the ranking of scholarships, EBITE, mentoring and career-ladder options, because public documents cannot resolve how candidates weigh whakapapa, te reo proficiency, kura workload, tikanga responsibilities, pay, housing and career progression in practice [EBITE](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award), [Te Tipu Whakarito](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/becoming-teacher-new-zealand/become-teacher-scholarships/te-tipu-whakarito-scholarship).
- I could not verify a single public NZ dataset that links ITE enrolment, completion, Teaching Council registration/certification, subject specialism, Māori-medium setting, LAT use, vacancies, school priority status and retention; the checked data.govt.nz catalogue query for "teacher workforce" returned no datasets, while Education Counts publishes useful but separate teacher-number, ITE and movement sources [data.govt.nz CKAN search](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/api/3/action/package_search?q=teacher+workforce&rows=10), [Education Counts teacher numbers](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/teacher-numbers), [Education Counts ITE](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/initial-teacher-education-statistics).
- I did not open a follow-up skill request on the separate `.skills` repo because a generic data.govt.nz catalogue skill exists and the immediate gap is an education-domain data product or joined administrative release, which likely requires Ministry/Teaching Council data access rather than a straightforward public API wrapper.

## Open follow-up questions

- Can NZ publish a de-identified teacher workforce spine that links ITE, registration, vacancies, subject/language capability, LAT status, school setting and retention without exposing small-cell personal data?
- Which current NZ incentive has the best retention-per-dollar in shortage roles: Te Tipu Whakarito, Te Huawhiti, EBITE, Go Rural, TBS, 3R, staffing incentive allowance, priority teacher supply allowance, or beginning-teacher release time?
- What would a collectively agreed STEM / te reo Māori / Māori-medium subject-retention top-up look like if it had to avoid undermining kaupapa, collective-agreement equity and school-board staffing autonomy?
- How much mentor release time is required for a beginning or LAT teacher in Māori-medium, te reo Māori, STEM or rural roles before mentoring becomes credible rather than an additional workload pressure?
