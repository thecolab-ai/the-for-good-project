# Hard-to-staff school targeting should replace decile 1-3 with Equity Index More plus priority-staffing, rural, and Māori-medium flags

## Executive answer

- The old "decile 1-3" premise should not be reused as a current school-staffing label: decile was replaced by the Schooling Equity Index from 2023, and the Ministry now reports three EQI groups where the 2026 "More" group is EQI 494-569, formed from the "Many" and "Most" bands. [Education Counts, Schooling Equity Index](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/data-services/code-sets-and-classifications/the-equity-index); [Education Counts, EQI bands and groups](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/data-services/guidelines/school-equity-index-bands-and-groups); [Education Counts, teacher turnover definitions](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/teacher-turnover)
- The current operational proxy for "hard-to-staff" is the Teacher Bonding Scheme priority-staffing list: 630 schools/kura were eligible for the 2025/26 cohort, equal to 26% of state and state-integrated schools, using 2019-2023 retention/readvertising data plus Te Mahau and kaupapa Māori peak-body nominations. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa) ([archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260129033843/https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa))
- In a join of the TBS PDF to the July 2026 school directory, 627 of 630 TBS IDs matched current school records; among those matched TBS schools, 343 (54.7%) are EQI "More", 320 (51.0%) are in rural settlement/other areas, 250 (39.9%) are also Go Rural eligible, 181 (28.9%) appear in the Māori immersion Level 1/2 directory, and 111 (17.7%) have a kaupapa Māori peak-body affiliation. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa); [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv); [Education Workforce, Go Rural eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/Go%20Rural%20Eligible%20Schools_0.pdf?VersionId=upSdZFxT8nZXDNt88_okXKoHhjiff7_a) ([archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260705135633/https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/Go%20Rural%20Eligible%20Schools_0.pdf?VersionId=upSdZFxT8nZXDNt88_okXKoHhjiff7_a)); [Education Counts, Māori schools directory](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/directories/maori-schools-directory)
- The most affected public stratum is not "all disadvantaged schools"; it is the stacked group where priority staffing, high EQI, rural/isolation and Māori-medium indicators overlap: 65 matched TBS schools are simultaneously EQI "More", Go Rural eligible, and in the Māori immersion directory; 25 of those are in Northland, 13 in Bay of Plenty, 9 in Gisborne, and 6 in Waikato. Regionally, the highest official TBS share is Tai Tokerau/Northland at 79 eligible schools, or 54% of all schools in that TBS region. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa); [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv); [Education Workforce, Go Rural eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/Go%20Rural%20Eligible%20Schools_0.pdf?VersionId=upSdZFxT8nZXDNt88_okXKoHhjiff7_a); [Education Counts, Māori schools directory CSV](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/__data/assets/file/0009/63873/maori-schools-directory-01072026.csv)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the overlap counts are reproducible from current official public lists, but the Ministry does not publish the TBS criterion met by each school, so this cannot separate retention failure, readvertising failure, Te Mahau nomination, and kaupapa Māori nomination at school level.

## Evidence

### How I translated "decile 1-3"

The Ministry's current teacher-turnover definitions say decile was available up to 2022 and was replaced by the Schooling Equity Index from 2023 onward; the same page defines old low decile as decile 1-3, and says EQI groups collapse seven EQI bands into "Fewer", "Moderate" and "More". [Education Counts, teacher turnover definitions](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/teacher-turnover) The EQI bands page says schools receive EQI numbers from 344 to 569, with higher values indicating more socioeconomic barriers on average, and warns that bands/groups are for aggregate reporting rather than individual school quality labels. [Education Counts, EQI bands and groups](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/data-services/guidelines/school-equity-index-bands-and-groups)

For 2026, the Ministry's EQI group-range workbook gives "Fewer" as 344-429, "Moderate" as 430-493, and "More" as 494-569; the 2026 band workbook gives "Many" as 494-521 and "Most" as 522-569. [Education Counts, EQI bands and groups](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/data-services/guidelines/school-equity-index-bands-and-groups) That makes EQI Group "More" the closest current aggregate analogue to the old decile 1-3 concept, but it is not a one-to-one crosswalk because EQI and decile are different measures. [Education Counts, teacher turnover definitions](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/teacher-turnover)

The staffing translation should therefore use two lenses at once: EQI "More" for socioeconomic barriers, and TBS priority-staffing eligibility for actual staffing difficulty. [Education Counts, EQI bands and groups](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/data-services/guidelines/school-equity-index-bands-and-groups); [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa)

### Priority-staffing is broader than high EQI

The TBS PDF says a school/kura is eligible if it is a priority staffing school, and the six criteria are: five-year average teacher retention of 60% or less; five-year average teacher readvertising of 40% or more; retention of 70% or less plus readvertising of 20% or more; nomination by a Te Mahau regional office; nomination by Te Rūnanga Nui o ngā Kura Kaupapa Māori o Aotearoa; or nomination by Ngā Kura ā Iwi. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa) The same PDF says criteria 1-3 used every school's 2019-2023 retention and readvertising data, Te Mahau nominations concluded on 12 September 2025, TRN/NKāI nominations concluded on 19 September 2025, and 630 schools/kura were eligible. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa)

The July 2026 school directory has 2,464 open state or state-integrated schools in this analysis, and 750 of them (30.4%) are EQI "More"; the matched TBS set is much more concentrated, with 343 of 627 matched TBS schools (54.7%) in EQI "More". [data.govt.nz, school directory dataset](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/directory-of-educational-institutions); [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv); [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa) The reverse is also important: 284 matched TBS schools (45.3%) are not EQI "More", so high EQI alone would miss nearly half of the public priority-staffing list. [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv); [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa)

### Rural/isolation and Māori-medium flags compound the risk

The school-directory API documents `Urban_Rural_Indicator`, `EQi_Index`, and `Isolation_Index`; it explains EQI as a socioeconomic-barrier model and explains the isolation index as a distance-to-population-centres measure used for targeted isolation funding. [Education Counts, school directory API](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/directories/school-directory-api); [Education Counts, New Zealand schools directory](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/directories/list-of-nz-schools) In the matched TBS set, 320 schools (51.0%) are in `Rural settlement` or `Rural other`, compared with 726 of 2,464 open state/state-integrated schools (29.5%) in the same rural categories. [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv); [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa)

Go Rural adds a narrower rural-placement proxy: the programme page says student teachers must have a verified placement in one of 454 eligible Go Rural schools, and the eligible-school PDF lists those schools by region. [Education Workforce, Go Rural page](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/becoming-teacher-new-zealand/become-teacher-scholarships/go-rural-isolated-placements-fund) ([archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260313163400/https://workforce.education.govt.nz/becoming-teacher-new-zealand/become-teacher-scholarships/go-rural-isolated-placements-fund)); [Education Workforce, Go Rural eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/Go%20Rural%20Eligible%20Schools_0.pdf?VersionId=upSdZFxT8nZXDNt88_okXKoHhjiff7_a) The Go Rural PDF had 454 names; 453 matched current school-directory records by email or normalized name, and 250 of the matched TBS schools were also Go Rural schools. [Education Workforce, Go Rural eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/Go%20Rural%20Eligible%20Schools_0.pdf?VersionId=upSdZFxT8nZXDNt88_okXKoHhjiff7_a); [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv)

The Māori schools directory includes schools where at least one student is in Māori Language Immersion Level 1 or 2, meaning curriculum is taught in te reo Māori at least 51% of the time; it also records affiliation with Te Rūnanga Nui o ngā Kura Kaupapa Māori o Aotearoa or Ngā Kura ā Iwi o Aotearoa Inc. [Education Counts, Māori schools directory](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/directories/maori-schools-directory) In the joined data, 181 matched TBS schools (28.9%) were in that Māori immersion directory, and 111 matched TBS schools had a KME peak-body affiliation; across all current open state/state-integrated schools, the comparable counts were 360 (14.6%) and 112 (4.5%). [Education Counts, Māori schools directory CSV](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/__data/assets/file/0009/63873/maori-schools-directory-01072026.csv); [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv); [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa)

The strongest stacked group I could verify from public lists is the intersection of TBS eligibility, EQI "More", Go Rural eligibility, and Māori immersion Level 1/2: 65 matched schools/kura. ### Regional pattern

The official TBS PDF reports eligible schools by TBS education region, and the highest percentage of all schools in region is Tai Tokerau at 79 eligible schools and 54%; other high-share regions are Hawke's Bay/Tairāwhiti at 59 schools and 36%, Nelson/Marlborough/West Coast at 45 and 35%, Bay of Plenty/Waiariki at 56 and 32%, Taranaki/Whanganui/Manawatū at 64 and 30%, and Waikato at 75 and 29%. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa)

The Ministry's 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply projection says regional data highlights localised pressures that national figures mask, and it names Northland, Waikato, Taranaki and Bay of Plenty as projected primary-shortage regions in 2026. [Ministry of Education, TDS projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc) ([archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260705095323/https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc)) The same projection says 13 regions have secondary gaps in 2026, with Auckland North and West, Auckland South and Southwest, Otago, Manawatū-Whanganui, Hawke's Bay, Northland, Canterbury and Wellington among the most critical. [Ministry of Education, TDS projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc)

The joined school-level data and the TDS projection point to two different but compatible priority patterns: rural/high-EQI/kura concentration is clearest in Northland, Bay of Plenty, Gisborne/Tairāwhiti, Waikato and Hawke's Bay, while secondary regional shortages also make Auckland North/West, Auckland South/Southwest, Otago and Wellington relevant even when their TBS share is lower. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa); [Ministry of Education, TDS projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc); [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv)

## What would change this conclusion

- School-level TBS criterion flags would materially improve the answer: if the Ministry published which schools met retention, readvertising, Te Mahau nomination, TRN nomination or NKāI nomination criteria, the finding could distinguish measured staffing churn from nomination-based priority. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa)
- A public school-level vacancy/readvertising dataset by subject, region, EQI group and Māori-medium status would change this from a priority-list overlap analysis into a direct staffing-shortage analysis. [Ministry of Education, TDS projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc)
- The three TBS school IDs that did not match the current July 2026 school directory could slightly change joined counts if the Ministry publishes an updated TBS list or a historical directory keyed to October 2025. [Education Workforce, TBS eligible schools PDF](https://assets.workforce.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-10/TBS%20Eligible%20schools%20-%202025-26%20Cohort.pdf?VersionId=nsoqra3SK8D5d2E4xIhwL90eeDN9FzDa); [data.govt.nz, school directory CSV](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/c1923d33-e781-46c9-9ea1-d9b850082be4/resource/4b292323-9fcc-41f8-814b-3c7b19cf14b3/download/schooldirectory-05-07-2026-074522.csv)
