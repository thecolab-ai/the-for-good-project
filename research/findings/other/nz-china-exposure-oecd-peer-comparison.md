# NZ's China export exposure is high within a selected OECD peer set, but the exact OECD-wide rank is unverified

## Executive answer

- By **goods-export share**, NZ is highly exposed to China within the selected comparator set used here: NZ sent about **25% of goods exports** to China in 2024, above South Korea's 2023 China share (19.7%), Japan's 2024 China share (17.6%), Canada (~4% in 2024), and the Netherlands (2.8% in 2024), but below Australia (~33% in 2024-25) and Chile (~36% in 2024). This supports a narrower claim that NZ is in the upper tier of the selected peer set; it does **not** prove NZ's rank across all OECD members. [China Embassy NZ, 2025](https://nz.china-embassy.gov.cn/eng/zxgxs/202506/t20250605_11641456.htm); [ABS, 2024-25](https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/economy/international-trade/international-trade-supplementary-information-financial-year/latest-release); [World's Top Exports, 2024](https://www.worldstopexports.com/chiles-top-import-partners/); [PIIE, 2024](https://www.piie.com/research/piie-charts/2024/south-koreas-exports-united-states-and-japan-overtook-exports-china-2023); [KIET, 2023](https://www.kiet.re.kr/en/pub/ecoreviewDetailView?detail_no=1016); [Japan MOFA, 2025](https://www.mofa.go.jp/files/100540401.pdf); [CBS Netherlands, 2025](https://www.cbs.nl/en-gb/news/2025/08/dutch-exports-to-china-up-again-while-germany-s-have-fallen); [Global Affairs Canada, 2024](https://international.canada.ca/en/global-affairs/corporate/reports/chief-economist/annual/2024)
- A **goods-plus-services ranking** could only be directly supported for NZ and Australia from official current sources: MFAT reports NZ exports to China of **NZ$22.82b**, including NZ$19.77b in goods and NZ$3.05b in services for the year ended September 2024; DFAT reports Australia's goods-and-services exports to China of **A$196b**, or **29%** of Australia's global goods-and-services exports in 2025. I did not obtain a comparable Chile goods-plus-services share, so this finding should not claim NZ is below Chile on that metric. [MFAT, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.mfat.govt.nz/en/trade/free-trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements-in-force/nz-china-free-trade-agreement/key-facts-on-new-zealand-china-trade); [DFAT China country brief, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.dfat.gov.au/geo/china/china-country-brief)
- The **exports-to-GDP evidence is too mixed to rank every peer precisely**. It is still enough to show that NZ is not the highest-exposure country in this selected set: Chile's 2024 goods exports to China alone are about US$37.3b against 2024 GDP of about US$329b, and Australia's 2025 goods-and-services exports to China are A$196b against 2024 GDP of about US$1.76t, while NZ's year-ended September 2024 goods-and-services exports to China were NZ$22.82b against 2024 GDP of about US$261b. Some rows below are retained only as context because the latest verified bilateral export numerator, share, and denominator are not all on a single annual basis. [World's Top Exports, 2024](https://www.worldstopexports.com/chiles-top-import-partners/); [World Bank GDP API, 2024](https://api.worldbank.org/v2/country/NZ;AU;CL;CA;JP;KR;NL/indicator/NY.GDP.MKTP.CD?format=json&date=2024&per_page=20); [DFAT, 2025](https://www.dfat.gov.au/geo/china/china-country-brief); [MFAT, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.mfat.govt.nz/en/trade/free-trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements-in-force/nz-china-free-trade-agreement/key-facts-on-new-zealand-china-trade)
- NZ's exposure is distinctive because it combines goods and services dependence. China's share of NZ goods exports is high, MFAT reports NZ$3.05b of services exports to China in the year ended September 2024, and China is NZ's largest international-student source economy; Australia's education exposure to China is also substantial, so the services point is **not unique to NZ**, but goods-only comparisons understate NZ's relationship with China. [MFAT, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.mfat.govt.nz/en/trade/free-trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements-in-force/nz-china-free-trade-agreement/key-facts-on-new-zealand-china-trade); [Education New Zealand, 2026](https://www.enz.govt.nz/news-and-research/ed-news/latest-data-shows-continued-growth-in-international-enrolments); [Education Counts, 2026](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/international-students-in-new-zealand); [Australian Department of Education, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.education.gov.au/international-education-data-and-research/international-student-monthly-summary-and-data-tables)
- NZ's goods exports to China peaked at about **NZ$20.1b in 2022**, then fell to NZ$18.4b in 2023 and declined again in 2024; World Bank WITS separately reports 2023 NZ goods exports to China of **US$11.3b**, or **26.85%** of NZ goods exports. The difference is a currency and source-basis difference, not a US$20.1b peak. [VUW News, Feb 2025](https://www.wgtn.ac.nz/news/2025/02/nz-should-be-worried-about-chinas-trade-statistics); [WITS NZ Trade Summary 2023](https://wits.worldbank.org/CountryProfile/en/Country/NZL/Year/LTST/Summarytext)

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the goods-export-share comparison is relatively well supported for the selected countries, but the GDP-ratio discussion is intentionally limited to rough context, goods-plus-services comparability is limited to NZ and Australia, and this is not a full OECD-wide ranking.

## Evidence

### 1. Scope and comparison method

This finding uses a deliberately limited comparator set: Australia and Chile as commodity-export comparators, South Korea and Japan as large Asian-export comparators, and Canada and the Netherlands as lower-exposure OECD comparators. It is not a complete OECD dataset. A full OECD ranking would require one bilateral trade dataset applied consistently across all OECD members for the same reference year; UN Comtrade provides bilateral goods-trade data, while OECD inter-country input-output tables are a separate source for value-added trade analysis rather than a like-for-like services ranking. [UN Comtrade](https://comtradeplus.un.org/); [OECD Inter-Country Input-Output tables](https://www.oecd.org/en/data/datasets/inter-country-input-output-tables.html)

Within that scope, this finding uses China's share of each country's goods exports as the main comparator because it was the only metric for which I obtained peer rows across the whole selected set. Goods-plus-services exposure is discussed separately because comparable bilateral services data was not obtained for every peer. [UN Comtrade](https://comtradeplus.un.org/); [MFAT key facts](https://www.mfat.govt.nz/en/trade/free-trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements-in-force/nz-china-free-trade-agreement/key-facts-on-new-zealand-china-trade); [DFAT China country brief](https://www.dfat.gov.au/geo/china/china-country-brief)

### 5. Sector structure and services exposure

NZ's China-facing exposure is concentrated in primary goods and people-movement services. MFAT lists NZ's major goods exports to China as dairy, meat, forestry and wood products, and fruit, while its major services exports to China are tourism, education, and transportation services. [MFAT, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.mfat.govt.nz/en/trade/free-trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements-in-force/nz-china-free-trade-agreement/key-facts-on-new-zealand-china-trade)

China is a particularly important market for NZ international education. Education New Zealand reported full-year 2025 international enrolments of 92,580 and said China and India were the top two source countries, with China accounting for 34% of enrolments; Education Counts' international-students page says its Export Education Levy tables include international-student enrolments by selected country of citizenship. [Education New Zealand, 2026](https://www.enz.govt.nz/news-and-research/ed-news/latest-data-shows-continued-growth-in-international-enrolments); [Education Counts, 2026](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/international-students-in-new-zealand)

Tourism remains part of NZ's services exposure to China, but this finding treats the latest tourism market ranking as context rather than a load-bearing comparator claim. MFAT identifies tourism among NZ's major services exports to China, and Tourism New Zealand, citing international visitor data, reported that Chinese visitors were the third-largest visitor-spend market in 2025 after Australia and the United States. [MFAT, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.mfat.govt.nz/en/trade/free-trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements-in-force/nz-china-free-trade-agreement/key-facts-on-new-zealand-china-trade); [Tourism New Zealand, 2026](https://www.tourismnewzealand.com/news-and-activity/new-data-reiterates-importance-of/); [MBIE International Visitor Survey data release materials, 2026](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/immigration-and-tourism/tourism-research-and-data/tourism-data-releases/international-visitor-survey-ivs/international-visitor-survey-data-release-materials)

Australia is a relevant services-exposure peer for NZ in this finding. DFAT reports Australia's 2025 goods-and-services exports to China at A$196b, with education-related travel of A$14.5b listed among the top exports to China; the Australian Department of Education's monthly international-student summary reports that China accounted for 23% of international students in the latest summary. [DFAT China country brief, 2026](https://www.dfat.gov.au/geo/china/china-country-brief); [DFAT China factsheet, 2025](https://www.dfat.gov.au/sites/default/files/chin-cef.pdf); [Australian Department of Education, accessed 2026-07-07](https://www.education.gov.au/international-education-data-and-research/international-student-monthly-summary-and-data-tables)

The sector-risk conclusion should be kept narrow. For dairy, OECD-FAO projects that expected growth in China's domestic milk production will limit growth in whole milk powder imports, and USDA's 2025 China dairy report says fluid milk imports have fallen as the market adjusts to higher domestic substitution. [OECD-FAO Agricultural Outlook 2025-2034](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/07/oecd-fao-agricultural-outlook-2025-2034_3eb15914/full-report/dairy-and-dairy-products_1dd2e5a6.html); [USDA FAS China Dairy Annual, 2025](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dairy+and+Products+Annual_Beijing_China+-+People%27s+Republic+of_CH2025-0218) For forestry, USDA's 2025 China softwood market update says China is the world's largest importer, processor, and consumer of wood products, but imported softwood demand has slowed because of weaker real-estate demand and increased domestic timber production, with New Zealand and Russia identified as top suppliers. [USDA FAS China Softwood Market Update, 2025](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Softwood+Market+Update+2025_Shanghai+ATO_China+-+People%27s+Republic+of_CH2025-0227.pdf)

### 6. Historical trend

NZ merchandise exports to China rose sharply over the 2000s and 2010s: Treasury reported that NZ merchandise exports to China were 3.3% of NZ nominal GDP in 2012, up from 0.8% in 2000. [NZ Treasury WP 13/15](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/wp/chinas-recent-growth-and-its-impact-new-zealand-economy-wp-13-15) VUW's Jason Young reported that NZ exports to China peaked at NZ$20.1b in 2022, fell to NZ$18.4b in 2023, and decreased again in 2024; WITS reports NZ goods exports to China of US$11.3b in 2023, or 26.85% of NZ's total goods exports. [VUW News, Feb 2025](https://www.wgtn.ac.nz/news/2025/02/nz-should-be-worried-about-chinas-trade-statistics); [WITS NZ Trade Summary 2023](https://wits.worldbank.org/CountryProfile/en/Country/NZL/Year/LTST/Summarytext)

Peer trends show that NZ's recent decline from the China-share peak is not unique. AMP reports that Australia's goods-export share to China peaked at 42% in 2021 and later eased to roughly one-third; PIIE reports that South Korea's China share fell below 20% in 2023 for the first time in 20 years. [AMP Econosights, 2024](https://www.amp.com.au/resources/insights-hub/econosights-australia-and-the-chinese-economy); [PIIE, 2024](https://www.piie.com/research/piie-charts/2024/south-koreas-exports-united-states-and-japan-overtook-exports-china-2023)

The likely explanation is partly global rather than NZ-specific, but this is an inference from broader commodity-market evidence rather than a country-by-country decomposition. World Bank reported that commodity prices recovered in early 2021, lifted by the global economic rebound, and UNCTAD reported a 43% increase in its free-market commodity price index during January-December 2021, attributed to recovery after the pandemic's initial waves. [World Bank Commodity Markets Outlook, April 2021](https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2021/04/20/commodity-prices-to-stabilize-after-early-2021); [UNCTAD, 2022](https://indico.un.org/event/1000802/attachments/2184/7232/2211377E.pdf)

### 7. Countries not covered by this finding

This finding does not include full comparable data for Singapore, Ireland, Colombia, Costa Rica, the Baltic states, or all other OECD members. A complete answer would need a consistent OECD, WTO, IMF DOTS, or UN Comtrade extraction across all OECD members for the same year, and an OECD Trade in Value Added or inter-country input-output analysis would be needed to separate gross-export exposure from value-added exposure in supply-chain and hub economies. [UN Comtrade](https://comtradeplus.un.org/); [OECD Inter-Country Input-Output tables](https://www.oecd.org/en/data/datasets/inter-country-input-output-tables.html)

## What would change this conclusion

- A unified OECD, WTO, IMF DOTS, or UN Comtrade extract covering bilateral goods and services exports to China for all OECD members in the same reference year could materially change NZ's relative rank. [UN Comtrade](https://comtradeplus.un.org/)
- A verified Chile national-statistics source for bilateral goods-plus-services exports to China could show whether NZ is below Chile on total-export exposure as well as goods-only and rough GDP-share metrics; this finding only verified Chile's goods-export share from a secondary source. [World's Top Exports, 2024](https://www.worldstopexports.com/chiles-top-import-partners/)
- A value-added trade analysis could change the apparent exposure of economies where gross exports differ materially from domestic value added, because OECD inter-country input-output tables are designed to trace value added through cross-border production networks. [OECD Inter-Country Input-Output tables](https://www.oecd.org/en/data/datasets/inter-country-input-output-tables.html)
- Sector-level NZ export data over a full year would improve confidence about the relative importance of dairy, meat, forestry, and fruit within the China relationship because MFAT identifies those as major NZ goods exports to China, but this finding did not extract a full-year official sector table. [MFAT](https://www.mfat.govt.nz/en/trade/free-trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements-in-force/nz-china-free-trade-agreement/key-facts-on-new-zealand-china-trade)
- A formal decomposition of the post-2021 decline by price, volume, exchange rate, and destination switching would test the commodity-cycle inference, which this finding currently supports only with broader commodity-market evidence.
