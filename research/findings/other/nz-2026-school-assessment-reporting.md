# NZ's 2026 school assessment and reporting system is a three-layer model, not a replay of National Standards

## Executive answer

- The 2026 system has three distinct layers: unchanged twice-yearly plain-language parent reporting under regulation 21; new curriculum and assessment requirements issued through 2025 secondary legislation; and Ministry reporting guidance that is expected/encouraged but not all framed as regulation. [Education (School Boards) Regulations 2020, reg 21](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/secondary-legislation/pco-drafted/2020/193/en/latest/), [New Zealand Gazette 2025-sl7165](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2025-sl7165), [Tāhūrangi, 2026 requirements page](https://tahurangi.education.govt.nz/reporting-assessment-requirements-expectations-for-2026)
- Required from 2026 for most state and state-integrated schools: report against the revised Years 0-10 English and Mathematics & Statistics learning areas; use Phonics Checks for Years 0-2; assess each Year 3-8 student twice yearly in reading, writing, and maths using SMART, PATs, or e-asTTle in 2026; and use the five common progress descriptors in curriculum assessment decisions. [Tāhūrangi, reporting guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/reporting-to-parents-and-wh-nau/5637359076.p), [Required/Encouraged PDF](https://mapaoch00pr.blob.core.windows.net/mapaoch00pr/artefacts/pdf/3f3fbbb5-cc8a-4625-b6f7-1dd088a18d63/RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf?sv=2026-02-06&st=2026-07-01T00%3A01%3A03Z&se=2126-07-03T00%3A01%3A03Z&sr=b&sp=rw&rscd=attachment%3B+filename%3D%22RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf%22%3B+filename*%3DUTF-8%27%27RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf&sig=D%2BfMlyXdXW5%2F%2BP7OGCxpRAgJLl56aJlMeWky%2F1zu3x4%3D)
- Not required in the same way: SMART itself is optional because schools can choose SMART, PATs, or e-asTTle for 2026; Years 9-10 have no specified assessment tool requirement; and the five common report components are described by Ministry sources as encouraged/expected and as needing to be included in reports, but the PDF separates them from regulatory requirements. [SMART guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p), [Ministry parent portal](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress), [Required/Encouraged PDF](https://mapaoch00pr.blob.core.windows.net/mapaoch00pr/artefacts/pdf/3f3fbbb5-cc8a-4625-b6f7-1dd088a18d63/RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf?sv=2026-02-06&st=2026-07-01T00%3A01%3A03Z&se=2126-07-03T00%3A01%3A03Z&sr=b&sp=rw&rscd=attachment%3B+filename%3D%22RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf%22%3B+filename*%3DUTF-8%27%27RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf&sig=D%2BfMlyXdXW5%2F%2BP7OGCxpRAgJLl56aJlMeWky%2F1zu3x4%3D)
- The key difference from National Standards is narrower than "no central data flow": National Standards used NAG 2A to require schools to report individual children against National Standards and publish/submit school-level counts and proportions at/above/below/well-below the standards, while the 2026 system has no found current equivalent annual National Standards-style return but does give the Ministry SMART data pathways for system-level analysis where SMART is used. [New Zealand Gazette 2009-go8813](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2009-go8813), [ERO, 2012](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/assets/documents/Reporting-to-Parents-National-Standards-Years-4-to-8-September-2012.pdf), [SMART guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p), [Treasury OIA 20250889](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)
- Māori-medium and specified kura-board timing is offset: the 2025 Gazette notice makes the new statements effective on 1 January 2026 for state and state-integrated schools except those run by specified kura boards, where the effective date is 1 January 2027; the 2009 NAG 2A similarly gave boards working toward Te Marautanga o Aotearoa later dates for National Standards-era reporting. [New Zealand Gazette 2025-sl7165](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2025-sl7165), [New Zealand Gazette 2009-go8813](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2009-go8813)

## Evidence

### Current legal and policy basis

Regulation 21 of the Education (School Boards) Regulations 2020 requires a school board, principal, and teaching staff to use good-quality assessment or aromatawai information when reporting to each student and their parents on progress and achievement, and requires those reports to be plain-language, cover progress and achievement across relevant curriculum statements and in literacy/mathematics or te reo matatini/pangarau, and be provided at least twice per year. [Education (School Boards) Regulations 2020, reg 21](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/secondary-legislation/pco-drafted/2020/193/en/latest/)

The Ministry's 2026 reporting page says those reporting regulations have not changed, but schools must now report progress against the new Years 0-10 English and Mathematics & Statistics learning areas from 2026. [Tāhūrangi, reporting guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/reporting-to-parents-and-wh-nau/5637359076.p)

The legal instrument for the new curriculum and assessment layer is Gazette notice 2025-sl7165, which says the Minister issued foundation curriculum policy statements and national curriculum statements under section 90 of the Education and Training Act 2020, that those statements are secondary legislation, and that the English and Mathematics & Statistics statements include assessment requirements. [New Zealand Gazette 2025-sl7165](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2025-sl7165)

That Gazette notice makes the statements effective from 1 January 2026 for state and state-integrated schools except schools run by specified kura boards, and from 1 January 2027 for those specified kura boards. [New Zealand Gazette 2025-sl7165](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2025-sl7165)

### Required versus expected/encouraged elements in 2026

For Years 0-8, the Ministry's required/encouraged PDF says schools must teach the Years 0-10 English and Mathematics & Statistics curriculum statements in full to Years 0-8 students, keep meeting Years 0-8 reading/writing/maths teaching-time requirements, use Phonics Checks at 20 and 40 weeks, and run twice-yearly Year 3-8 reading/writing/maths assessments using SMART, PATs, or e-asTTle in 2026. [Required/Encouraged PDF](https://mapaoch00pr.blob.core.windows.net/mapaoch00pr/artefacts/pdf/3f3fbbb5-cc8a-4625-b6f7-1dd088a18d63/RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf?sv=2026-02-06&st=2026-07-01T00%3A01%3A03Z&se=2126-07-03T00%3A01%3A03Z&sr=b&sp=rw&rscd=attachment%3B+filename%3D%22RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf%22%3B+filename*%3DUTF-8%27%27RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf&sig=D%2BfMlyXdXW5%2F%2BP7OGCxpRAgJLl56aJlMeWky%2F1zu3x4%3D)

For Years 9-10, the same PDF says there are no specified assessments that must occur, while schools must still have systems and strategies for close monitoring, actively assess progress against year-by-year teaching sequences, and use the five common progress descriptors in assessment decisions. [Required/Encouraged PDF](https://mapaoch00pr.blob.core.windows.net/mapaoch00pr/artefacts/pdf/3f3fbbb5-cc8a-4625-b6f7-1dd088a18d63/RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf?sv=2026-02-06&st=2026-07-01T00%3A01%3A03Z&se=2126-07-03T00%3A01%3A03Z&sr=b&sp=rw&rscd=attachment%3B+filename%3D%22RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf%22%3B+filename*%3DUTF-8%27%27RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf&sig=D%2BfMlyXdXW5%2F%2BP7OGCxpRAgJLl56aJlMeWky%2F1zu3x4%3D)

The English and Mathematics & Statistics curriculum pages repeat that school boards and principals must ensure twice-yearly Year 3-8 assessments use SMART, PATs, or e-asTTle, and that staff must use the common progress descriptors: Emerging, Developing, Consolidating, Proficient, and Exceeding. [NZC English Years 0-10](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/nzc---english-years-0-6/5637239089.p), [NZC Mathematics & Statistics Years 0-10](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/new-zealand-curriculum-online/nzc---mathematics-and-statistics-phases-1-4-years-0-10/5637289330.p)

SMART is a permitted tool, not the sole mandated tool: the SMART guidance says schools can choose SMART, e-asTTle, or PAT for twice-yearly progression monitoring in 2026, and clarifies that all Year 3-8 students must be covered by one of the specified tools. [SMART guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

The reporting-format layer is not as simple as "optional": the required/encouraged PDF places the common report components under encouraged practice, while the Ministry parent portal says the five elements need to be included in some way in all reports from the start of 2026, and the Tāhūrangi release says the common components are expected in all reports from 2026. [Required/Encouraged PDF](https://mapaoch00pr.blob.core.windows.net/mapaoch00pr/artefacts/pdf/3f3fbbb5-cc8a-4625-b6f7-1dd088a18d63/RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf?sv=2026-02-06&st=2026-07-01T00%3A01%3A03Z&se=2126-07-03T00%3A01%3A03Z&sr=b&sp=rw&rscd=attachment%3B+filename%3D%22RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf%22%3B+filename*%3DUTF-8%27%27RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf&sig=D%2BfMlyXdXW5%2F%2BP7OGCxpRAgJLl56aJlMeWky%2F1zu3x4%3D), [Ministry parent portal](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress), [Tāhūrangi guidance release](https://tahurangi.education.govt.nz/reporting-to-parents-and-whanau-guidance-released)

Those five common report components are: progress descriptors, a visual representation of progress over time, information about how descriptors were selected and next learning steps, assessment results, and an attendance record. [Tāhūrangi, reporting guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/reporting-to-parents-and-wh-nau/5637359076.p), [Ministry parent portal](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress)

The descriptors carry different mid-year and end-year meanings: at mid-year Emerging/Developing means not yet on track and Consolidating/Proficient/Exceeding means on track; at end-year Proficient/Exceeding means expectations have been met and Emerging/Developing/Consolidating means they have not yet been met. [Ministry parent portal](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress)

### Data flows and system monitoring

SMART produces immediate foundation-report information, later scaled-score reports in 2026, three teacher reports, and school-level dashboards broken down by year level, strands/elements, and demographic group. [Tāhūrangi, 2026 requirements page](https://tahurangi.education.govt.nz/reporting-assessment-requirements-expectations-for-2026)

The SMART FAQ says student data transfers automatically when a student changes school or kura, and that SMART is compatible with student management systems using the same process and file formats as e-asTTle initially. [SMART guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

SMART also creates a central Ministry data pathway. The current SMART guidance says the Ministry is kaitiaki of all student and ākonga data in SMART and that, at system level, aggregated data helps the Ministry identify where additional resources or targeted investment may be required and informs policy design. [SMART guidance](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

The SMART business-case material released through Treasury is more explicit about the intended data flow: it says the Ministry needed access to data for system-level secondary analysis, lists increased individual-level information on student progress and achievement available for Ministry system-level purposes as a project benefit, and says the Ministry would use aggregate data to analyse trends and determine system-level interventions. [Treasury OIA 20250889](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)

For national target monitoring, a separate pathway remains representative sampling: the Ministry's Foundational Learning Action Plan says the Curriculum Insights and Progress Study assesses a representative sample of students annually in reading, writing, and maths at Years 3, 6, and 8 from 2025, and that a parallel te reo Maori study, Te Tirewa Matai, has been developed for Maori-medium settings. [Foundational Learning Action Plan](https://www.education.govt.nz/our-work/strategies-policies-and-programmes/attendance-and-achievement/foundational-learning-action-plan)

### How National Standards worked

The 2009 Gazette notice inserted NAG 2A for schools with Years 1-8 students, requiring boards, principals, and teaching staff to use National Standards to report individual students' progress and achievement in relation to National Standards and to provide plain-language written reports to parents at least twice a year. [New Zealand Gazette 2009-go8813](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2009-go8813)

The same NAG 2A required boards to report school-level National Standards data in annual reports, including school strengths, areas for improvement, planned actions, and numbers/proportions of students at, above, below, or well below the standards, including Maori, Pasifika, and gender breakdowns where privacy allowed. [New Zealand Gazette 2009-go8813](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2009-go8813)

ERO described the National Standards as introduced in 2010 for English-medium schools with Years 1-8 students, said the standards described what students should know and be able to do in reading, writing, and mathematics at different schooling points, and said the NAGs required schools to report to parents and students about progress and achievement in relation to the standards. [ERO, Reporting to Parents: National Standards Years 4 to 8, 2012](https://thehub.sia.govt.nz/assets/documents/Reporting-to-Parents-National-Standards-Years-4-to-8-September-2012.pdf)

The 2009 NAG 2A delayed application for boards working toward implementing Te Marautanga o Aotearoa until 2 February 2011 and delayed the first relevant annual reports for those boards to the 2012 school year. [New Zealand Gazette 2009-go8813](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2009-go8813)

The 2017 abolition announcement said the Government had stopped National Standards and Nga Whanaketanga Rumaki Maori, and that from 2018 schools would no longer be compelled to report National Standards annually to the Ministry of Education, while parents would still receive at least twice-yearly progress and achievement reports. [Beehive, 12 December 2017](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/national-standards-ended)

## What would change this conclusion

- A current Ministry circular, Gazette notice, regulation, or school-board compliance instruction requiring schools to submit annual whole-cohort Year 3-8 results to the Ministry in National Standards-style categories would change the narrower "no found equivalent annual return" comparison.
- A published SMART data-use policy, data dictionary, or operational access-control document showing exactly which identifiable, de-identified, aggregate, school-level, and cohort-level fields the Ministry can access would refine the SMART data-flow section.
- A definitive Ministry legal interpretation saying the five report components are enforceable regulatory requirements, rather than expected/encouraged administrative reporting guidance, would change the required-versus-expected split.
- I did not verify how private schools, partnership schools, or every special-school context is treated; the core finding is about state and state-integrated schools/kura covered by the cited Ministry and Gazette material.
- I did not test SMART in a school account, so data-flow claims are limited to public Ministry guidance rather than system inspection.
- Tāhūrangi pages were bot-blocked by raw `curl`/`scripts/fetch.mjs` in this environment, but the built-in web fetch resolved them; the `RequiredEncouraged_20260701.pdf` was fetched directly and extracted locally with `pdftotext`.
