# Major settled iwi have generally grown settlement capital into larger portfolios, but with very different risk exposures and distribution capacity

## Executive answer

- The baseline pattern is growth from settlement capital into multi-asset commercial groups, but scale varies sharply: the 2024 TDB comparison reports total assets of $2.399b for Waikato-Tainui, $2.097b for Ngāi Tahu, $443m for Tūhoe, $260m for Raukawa, $173m for Ngāti Awa and $1.539b for Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei; Te Rarawa's own 2023/24 report gives group total assets of $123m and net assets of $91.287m. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf); [Te Rūnanga o Te Rarawa, 2023/24 annual report](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/files/annual-reports/annual-report-2023.pdf)
- Settlement scale and later relativity payments matter: TDB's comparator table records redress amounts of $473m for Ngāi Tahu, $465m for Waikato-Tainui, $43m for Ngāti Awa, $18m for Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei, $50m for Raukawa and $169m for Tūhoe, while Te Rarawa's settlement page records a $33.84m financial quantum. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf); [Te Rūnanga o Te Rarawa settlement FAQs](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/pou/cultural/settlement/faqs)
- The portfolios are not one model: Waikato-Tainui and Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei are property-heavy, Ngāti Awa is primary-industry-heavy, Tūhoe is financial-assets-heavy, and Ngāi Tahu and Raukawa are more diversified across property, primary industries and financial assets. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf)
- Returns and debt move with portfolio exposure: 2024 return on assets was 7.5% for Waikato-Tainui, 7.4% for Tūhoe, 7.6% for Raukawa, 3.3% for Ngāi Tahu, -1.3% for Ngāti Awa and -1.8% for Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei; 2024 gearing was 18% for Waikato-Tainui, 19% for Ngāi Tahu, 14% for Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei, 8.3% for Ngāti Awa, and zero for Tūhoe and Raukawa. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf)
- Public distributions are real but uneven: in 2024 TDB reports $69m distributed by Ngāi Tahu, $55.6m by Waikato-Tainui, $0.6m by Ngāti Awa, $9.4m plus $2.6m Toi Tupu support by Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei, $469,000 by Tūhoe and $1.1m by Raukawa; Te Rarawa reports a commercial group operating profit of $2.842m and a $25,000 contribution to scholarship awards, but its public report does not give a single group-wide equivalent distribution number. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf); [Te Rūnanga o Te Rarawa, 2023/24 annual report](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/files/annual-reports/annual-report-2023.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium -- the public record is strong for the larger iwi in TDB's comparison and for current Ngāi Tahu/Waikato-Tainui/Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei reporting, but weaker for consistent long-run Te Rarawa metrics and for separating commercial-entity performance from whole-group iwi accounts.

## Evidence

### Scope and source basis

This finding answers issue #492 as a public-source baseline for Ngāi Tahu, Waikato-Tainui, Te Rarawa, Ngāti Awa, Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei, Tūhoe and Raukawa, using Te Tari Whakatau or settlement pages for settlement terms, iwi annual reports for latest reported finances where available, and TDB Advisory's 2024 iwi investment report for comparable 2014-2024 assets, returns, gearing and distributions. [Te Tari Whakatau quarterly reports page](https://whakatau.govt.nz/te-tira-kurapounamu-treaty-settlements/quarterly-reports); [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf)

The settlement system context is large enough to make comparison useful but not simple: the Auditor-General says about 150 public organisations had about 12,000 settlement commitments under about 80 settlements with about 70 groups, and that $2.738b of financial and commercial redress had been transferred through settlements by the time of its 2025 report. [Office of the Auditor-General, 2025](https://ao.parliament.nz/2025/treaty-settlements/overview.htm)

TDB says its 2024 report reviewed ten iwi that collectively managed about $8.2b, about 70% of all post-settlement iwi assets nationwide, and cautions that comparisons are affected by settlement dates, risk, valuation methods and reporting differences. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf)

### What the comparison shows

The largest iwi portfolios are now the two early, high-value settlements with relativity mechanisms: Waikato-Tainui and Ngāi Tahu both sit around the $2b total-assets scale in the 2024 comparison, while the smaller settlements in this sample range from Te Rarawa's $123m group asset base to Ngāti Awa's $173m and Raukawa's $260m in the same broad period. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf); [Te Rūnanga o Te Rarawa, 2023/24 annual report](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/files/annual-reports/annual-report-2023.pdf)

The simple "settlement capital became more money" story is incomplete because the balance sheets carry different risk: Waikato-Tainui's 2025 report says its property-heavy portfolio is exposed in economic duress and that diversification is key, Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei's 2024 and 2025 results show Auckland-property revaluation losses, and Ngāti Awa's 2024 loss was driven mainly by forestry-land valuation declines. [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf); [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf); [Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei financial statements 2024/25](https://ngatiwhatuaorakei.com/media/c51kskad/nwo-trust-nga-taua-ki-pu-tea-202425_digi.pdf)

Distributions are not a pure proxy for wellbeing because they include different things across entities: TDB includes grants, marae support, member savings schemes and iwi services in its distribution discussion, while Te Rarawa's public report gives commercial operating profit and selected contributions but not the same distribution denominator. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf); [Te Rūnanga o Te Rarawa, 2023/24 annual report](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/files/annual-reports/annual-report-2023.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A fuller audited dataset from Te Puni Kōkiri, Te Tari Whakatau, Te Ohu Kaimoana or iwi PSGEs that standardised settlement receipts, relativity payments, assets, net assets, debt, total return and distributions across all iwi would materially improve this baseline. [Office of the Auditor-General, 2025](https://ao.parliament.nz/2025/treaty-settlements/overview.htm)
- Direct 2024/25 audited reports for every iwi in the table would update the comparable picture; I found 2024/25 public reports for Ngāi Tahu, Waikato-Tainui and Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei, but not an extractable comparable 2024/25 source for every iwi in scope. [Ngāi Tahu, 2024/25 financial summary](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/TRONT-AR2025-FinancialSummaryA4.pdf); [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf); [Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei financial statements 2024/25](https://ngatiwhatuaorakei.com/media/c51kskad/nwo-trust-nga-taua-ki-pu-tea-202425_digi.pdf)
- This finding does not verify whether distributions reached whānau equitably, whether investments improved local employment or wellbeing, or whether the settlements were adequate compensation for loss; those questions need iwi-defined outcome measures and Māori domain review, not just balance-sheet analysis. [Office of the Auditor-General, 2025](https://ao.parliament.nz/2025/treaty-settlements/overview.htm)
- I could not reconcile every settlement "received" number into one clean basis because sources mix original quantum, land/options, cultural redress, interest and later relativity payments; the most reliable next step would be a spreadsheet of deed values and relativity payments by payment date. [TDB Advisory, 2024](https://www.tdb.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/TDB-Iwi-Investment-Report-2024-1.pdf); [Te Tari Whakatau quarterly reports page](https://whakatau.govt.nz/te-tira-kurapounamu-treaty-settlements/quarterly-reports)
