# NZ's 2026 school assessment data has privacy and OIA controls, but no published rule that clearly prevents school league tables

## Executive answer

- I found **no current, published Ministry rule that expressly bans school league tables** from 2026 SMART/PAT/e-asTTle assessment data; the practical safeguard is that no central public school-level SMART dataset is currently published, plus case-by-case OIA, privacy, confidentiality, and collation checks if school-level achievement data is requested. [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR), [Official Information Act 1982, ss 9 and 18](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1982/156/en/latest/)
- The 2026 data pathway is real: SMART gives school, class, and student reports; school reports aggregate by year level, gender, and ethnicity; schools can export student information, scores, and progress descriptors to their student management systems; and the Ministry says aggregated SMART data will inform resources, targeted investment, and policy design. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p), [SMART reporting guidance PDF](https://files-au-prod.cms.commerce.dynamics.com/cms/api/qwxsnqcpfm/binary/MLjQwt)
- The Ministry is explicitly aware of the league-table risk: a September 2024 released paper says future smarter assessment and reporting will likely improve the availability of standardised achievement data, and records concern that the current OIA approach could create unintended consequences that undermine standardisation for teaching and learning. [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR)
- Privacy law protects identifiable students, not school reputations as such; OIA section 9 can protect privacy, commercial/confidential information, free-and-frank advice, and improper gain, but it is a balancing test for each request rather than a categorical bar on releasing aggregate school-level achievement data. [Official Information Act 1982, s 9](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1982/156/en/latest/), [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 11](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/11/), [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, personal information guidance](https://www.privacy.org.nz/responsibilities/poupou-matatapu-doing-privacy-well/know-your-personal-information/)
- Reuse depends on the release route: Education Counts content is generally CC BY 4.0 unless marked otherwise, NZGOAL guides agencies to release government material for reuse under Creative Commons or clear non-copyright statements, and I found no SMART-specific public licence or reuse restriction for assessment-result extracts released via OIA. [Education Counts copyright statement](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/site-info/privacy), [NZGOAL](https://www.data.govt.nz/toolkit/policies/nzgoal)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the published-rule conclusion is supported by current official sources and the absence of any found explicit ban, but it remains possible that unpublished Ministry operational policy, contracts, privacy impact assessments, or future Cabinet decisions set stronger constraints.

## Evidence

### Publication: parent reports, school annual reporting, SMART reports, and OIA

The 2026 parent-facing reporting duty remains learner-facing, not public league-table reporting: regulation 21 requires school boards, principals, and teachers to use good-quality assessment information when reporting to each student and their parents, and the Ministry says SMART helps teachers plan teaching, track progress, share information with parents, and understand system-wide learner support. [Education (School Boards) Regulations 2020, reg 21](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/secondary-legislation/pco-drafted/2020/193/en/latest/), [Ministry parent SMART guide](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-smart-new-assessment-tool-your-child)

Schools also have public planning-and-reporting duties: the Ministry says the Education and Training Act 2020 and Education (School Planning and Reporting) Regulations 2023 require boards to create an annual report detailing the school's progress and finances, and the September 2024 released paper says annual reports must include detailed evaluations of student progress, especially for students with unmet needs, based on quality assessments and curriculum expectations. [Ministry school planning and reporting framework](https://www.education.govt.nz/education-professionals/schools-year-0-13/administration-and-management/school-planning-and-reporting-framework), [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR)

The Ministry's released OIA-position paper is the clearest current rule-like document for school-level achievement data. It says compiled tables of all New Zealand schools are typically released only through OIA requests, that the Ministry may release school-level achievement data in response to an OIA request, and that school-level achievement data not already public will be considered for release unless there is a good reason to withhold it. [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR)

The OIA default is availability: section 5 says information must be made available unless there is good reason for withholding it, section 9 allows withholding only where the interest is not outweighed by public-interest considerations, and section 18 allows refusal for reasons including good withholding grounds, public availability, non-existence/not held, or substantial collation or research. [Official Information Act 1982, ss 5, 9 and 18](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1982/156/en/latest/)

I found no current equivalent of National Standards-era mandatory public categories for every primary school. The earlier stream finding on the 2026 system found no current annual whole-cohort National Standards-style return, while the 2009 National Standards Gazette notice required boards to report school-level numbers and proportions at, above, below, or well below standards, including Māori, Pasifika, and gender breakdowns where privacy allowed. [2026 requirements finding](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/research/findings/other/nz-2026-school-assessment-reporting.md), [New Zealand Gazette 2009-go8813](https://gazette.govt.nz/notice/id/2009-go8813)

### Aggregation: school-level and system-level use is built in

SMART school reports are already aggregated inside the tool: the SMART overview says reading and maths reports provide school-level aggregated reports by year level, gender, and ethnicity, class reports with scaled scores and progress descriptors, and student reports with individual scaled scores, progress descriptors, demonstrated skills, and next learning focus. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

The detailed SMART reporting guidance says the reporting portal moves from aggregated views to individual reports; the school report gives aggregated reporting for a single school, the class report gives reporting for a specific class or year level, and the student report gives individual reporting. [SMART reporting guidance PDF](https://files-au-prod.cms.commerce.dynamics.com/cms/api/qwxsnqcpfm/binary/MLjQwt)

The same guidance says the school summary page provides an overview of classes and year levels, that reading and maths bars show the number and percentage of students assigned to each progress descriptor, and that ethnicity views show group counts plus average and median scaled scores. [SMART reporting guidance PDF](https://files-au-prod.cms.commerce.dynamics.com/cms/api/qwxsnqcpfm/binary/MLjQwt)

Schools can move SMART data into local systems: the SMART overview says exporting creates a CSV containing student information, assessment scores, and progress descriptors for each subject, for upload to a school student management system and use in parent/whānau reporting. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

The Ministry has central system-level use in mind. The SMART overview says the Ministry is kaitiaki of all student and ākonga data in SMART and that aggregated data helps identify where additional resources or targeted investment may be required and informs policy design. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

The March 2026 proactively released SMART business case says the Ministry needs access to data for system-level secondary analysis purposes within data-protection policies, and says targeting support to schools and kura will require different use policies from the current Ministry-provided tools. [Ministry SMART Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6)

### Privacy and confidentiality: real limits, but not a league-table ban

SMART data is personal information when it is about identifiable students: the Office of the Privacy Commissioner says personal information is any information that tells us something about a specific individual, including information where the individual can be identified by context or pieced-together identifiers. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, personal information guidance](https://www.privacy.org.nz/responsibilities/poupou-matatapu-doing-privacy-well/know-your-personal-information/)

Privacy Principle 11 generally limits disclosure of personal information to the purpose for which it was collected or obtained, with exceptions such as directly related purposes or permission from the person concerned. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Principle 11](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/11/)

OIA privacy protection is narrower than "no publication": section 9 allows withholding where necessary to protect natural persons' privacy, but that ground is subject to a public-interest override, and the Ministry's school-level achievement data paper says OIA requests must be assessed case by case. [Official Information Act 1982, s 9](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1982/156/en/latest/), [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR)

The Ministry's SMART page gives security and use assurances but not a public suppression rule: it says all SMART data is stored securely in Aotearoa New Zealand, backups are stored in Australia, Janison cannot use the data for its own purposes, and the Ministry has policies, systems, safeguards, contractual obligations, and operational processes for secure and ethical management. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

Those controls would make student-level or small-cell publication harder to justify, especially where learners could be re-identified, but they do not by themselves stop a requester or publisher from constructing rankings from sufficiently aggregated school-level data if that data is released or already public. [Office of the Privacy Commissioner, personal information guidance](https://www.privacy.org.nz/responsibilities/poupou-matatapu-doing-privacy-well/know-your-personal-information/), [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR)

### Reuse: open-publication rules permit reuse, while SMART/OIA reuse rules are unclear

NZGOAL is all-of-government guidance for agencies releasing copyright and non-copyright/public-domain material for reuse, and it aims to standardise Creative Commons licensing for government copyright works and recommended statements for non-copyright material. [NZGOAL](https://www.data.govt.nz/toolkit/policies/nzgoal)

Education Counts' copyright statement says Ministry copyright material on that site is generally licensed for reuse under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 unless a specific item says otherwise, allowing copying, distribution, and adaptation with attribution and licence compliance. [Education Counts copyright statement](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/site-info/privacy)

That means data or tables proactively published on Education Counts are likely reusable under that site licence unless marked otherwise, but I found no public SMART-specific reuse licence for CSV exports, SMART reports, or school-level SMART extracts released under OIA. [Education Counts copyright statement](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/site-info/privacy), [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p)

### What, if anything, prevents league tables?

The current answer is: **no explicit published league-table prohibition found; the barriers are indirect.** The strongest barriers are that SMART school reports are inside authenticated systems, no central school-level SMART results page was found, OIA release is case-by-case, personal or small-cell data can trigger privacy concerns, and the Ministry can consider confidentiality, substantial collation, and other OIA grounds. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p), [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR), [Official Information Act 1982, ss 9 and 18](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1982/156/en/latest/)

That is weaker than a statutory or policy ban. During National Standards, public comparison happened once school-level data were available: NZCER's 2013 paper says principals and others warned against media league tables and that this came to pass in 2012 using 2011 annual school reports. [NZCER, 2013](https://www.nzcer.org.nz/sites/default/files/downloads/NZARE%20National%20Standards%20conference%20paper.pdf)

The risk is therefore conditional: if school-level SMART/PAT/e-asTTle aggregates are centrally published, released via OIA, or replicated across school annual reports in comparable form, the public sources reviewed here do not show a rule that would clearly prevent third parties from ranking schools. [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR), [NZGOAL](https://www.data.govt.nz/toolkit/policies/nzgoal), [Education Counts copyright statement](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/site-info/privacy)

## What would change this conclusion

- A published Ministry SMART data-use policy, privacy impact assessment, Cabinet decision, Gazette notice, or contractual term that expressly prohibits school-level publication, ranking, or reuse for league tables would change the central conclusion. I found privacy assurances and OIA process rules, but not that explicit prohibition. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p), [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR)
- An Ombudsman decision, Ministry OIA response, or proactive release after SMART Assessment Window 1 that withholds or releases school-level SMART results would sharpen the practical OIA answer. This finding relies on the Ministry's general school-level achievement data release paper and the OIA, not a completed SMART-results OIA test case. [Ministry school-level achievement data release paper, 2025](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2025-08/4%20-%201335225%20Approach%20to%20releasing%20school%20level%20achievement%20data_Redacted.pdf?VersionId=faoyMMIPu8qND3Wulzr1xdaJNqIYasoR), [Official Information Act 1982](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1982/156/en/latest/)
- A public SMART data dictionary, role-access matrix, retention schedule, or small-cell suppression rule would improve the aggregation and privacy analysis. I could verify school/class/student report levels, system-level aggregated use, and general privacy assurances, but not the full operational controls. [Tāhūrangi SMART overview](https://newzealandcurriculum.tahurangi.education.govt.nz/smart-overview-and-access/5637300577.p), [SMART reporting guidance PDF](https://files-au-prod.cms.commerce.dynamics.com/cms/api/qwxsnqcpfm/binary/MLjQwt)
- A human with education-law, OIA, Privacy Act, or Ministry operational authority should review this before it is used as legal advice. This finding identifies public rules and governance gaps; it does not determine how a court, Ombudsman, Privacy Commissioner, or Minister would decide a specific request.
