# Current NZ youth mental-health baseline shows high distress, high self-harm need, and incomplete subgroup visibility

## Executive answer

- The current official baseline is that **22.9% of 15-24-year-olds, about 156,000 young people, had high or very high psychological distress in the 2024/25 New Zealand Health Survey**, about double the 11.2% recorded in 2019/20; the same 2024/25 row reports 29.2% for young women and 15.8% for young men. [Ministry of Health NZ Health Survey annual data explorer CSV, 2024/25](https://minhealthnz.shinyapps.io/nz-health-survey-2024-25-annual-data-explorer/data/nz-health-survey-2024-25-prevalences.csv); [MSD Child and Youth Wellbeing Strategy annual report 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf)
- The 2024/25 youth distress signal is inequitable: MSD reports high or very high distress for **27.1% of Māori young people, 30.8% of Pacific young people and 70.5% of disabled young people aged 15-24**, while the broader adult NZ Health Survey shows higher distress for Māori, Pacific peoples, disabled adults and the most deprived quintile. [MSD, 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf); [Ministry of Health NZHS CSV, 2024/25](https://minhealthnz.shinyapps.io/nz-health-survey-2024-25-annual-data-explorer/data/nz-health-survey-2024-25-prevalences.csv)
- Service need is visible but under-counted: the NZ Health Survey reports **13.5% of 15-24-year-olds, about 92,000 people, had unmet need for mental-health or addiction services in 2024/25**, and Te Hiringa Mahara reports that 0-18-year-olds had the longest specialist-service waits, with only 69.9% seen within three weeks and a 14.9% declined-referral rate in 2024/25. [Ministry of Health NZHS CSV, 2024/25](https://minhealthnz.shinyapps.io/nz-health-survey-2024-25-annual-data-explorer/data/nz-health-survey-2024-25-prevalences.csv); [Te Hiringa Mahara access summary, February 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/HAA-monitoring/MHWC-Access-data-summary-Feb-2026.pdf)
- Young people have the highest public-hospital self-harm admission rate: in 2024/25 around 6,800 people were hospitalised for intentional self-harm across about 9,000 events, and the 15-24 rate was **370 people per 100,000**, compared with 128 per 100,000 overall. [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report, June 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)
- The public data does **not** yet provide a single clean table for the issue's full ask - age by ethnicity by gender by region by deprivation for distress, self-harm, suicide and service need. The Health NZ suicide tool exposes age, sex, ethnicity, Māori/non-Māori, deprivation and district filters, but exact current youth subgroup suicide rates are small-count sensitive and some rates may be suppressed; the forthcoming Child and Youth Mental Health Study is designed to improve this gap for ages 5-24. [Health NZ suicide data and statistics](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/health-data/data-sets-and-collections/suicide-data-and-statistics); [Te Hiringa Mahara self-harm indicators working paper, October 2025](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/Suicide-and-self-harm-October-2025/Te-Hiringa-Mahara-Review-of-suicide-and-self-harm-indicators-Working-Paper.pdf); [Ministry of Health Child and Youth Mental Health Study](https://www.health.govt.nz/monitoring-statistics/surveys/child-and-youth-mental-health-study)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - High for national 2024/25 NZHS distress, unmet-need, specialist-access and self-harm-hospitalisation headline figures; Medium for subgroup interpretation because the current public record is fragmented and some youth suicide/self-harm subgroup cells are suppressed, provisional, dated or too small for stable rates.

## Evidence

### 1. What the current public sources can and cannot answer

The 2024/25 New Zealand Health Survey annual data explorer provides current adult prevalence estimates for high or very high psychological distress and unmet need for mental-health or addiction services by age group, gender within each row, ethnicity, disability status, NZDep quintile and four large Health NZ regions, but it does not expose a single public age-by-ethnicity-by-gender-by-region-by-deprivation cross-tab for youth. [Ministry of Health NZHS annual update page, 2024/25](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/annual-update-of-key-results-202425-new-zealand-health-survey); [NZHS prevalence CSV, 2024/25](https://minhealthnz.shinyapps.io/nz-health-survey-2024-25-annual-data-explorer/data/nz-health-survey-2024-25-prevalences.csv)

The MSD Child and Youth Wellbeing Strategy annual report adds 2024/25 youth indicators from the NZ Health Survey and Youth Health and Wellbeing Survey, including 15-24 K10 distress by Māori, Pacific and disabled status, and 13-19 K6 distress, self-harm, serious suicidal thoughts and attempted suicide by the same groups. [MSD, 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf)

Health NZ's suicide data page says the Suicide Data Web Tool provides confirmed suicide deaths and suspected self-inflicted deaths and supports downloadable data; the tool itself describes confirmed suicide data from the Mortality Collection and suspected self-inflicted deaths from Ministry of Justice coronial case management data, with variables including year, age group, sex, prioritised ethnicity, Māori/non-Māori, deprivation quintile and district. [Health NZ suicide data and statistics](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/health-data/data-sets-and-collections/suicide-data-and-statistics); [Health NZ suicide web tool](https://tewhatuora.shinyapps.io/suicide-web-tool/)

That suicide/subgroup data needs caution: Te Hiringa Mahara's self-harm indicators working paper says some rates may be suppressed where they are based on very small numbers, and the MSD annual report uses three-year rolling suicide averages for 15-24-year-olds because rates fluctuate significantly year to year due to small counts. [Te Hiringa Mahara self-harm indicators working paper, 2025](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/Suicide-and-self-harm-October-2025/Te-Hiringa-Mahara-Review-of-suicide-and-self-harm-indicators-Working-Paper.pdf); [MSD, 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf)

### 2. Psychological distress and unmet need in the 2024/25 NZ Health Survey

The clearest current national youth baseline is the NZ Health Survey 15-24 row: 22.9% of 15-24-year-olds had high or very high psychological distress in 2024/25, with a 95% confidence interval of 19.1%-27.0% and an estimated 156,000 people; the same row reports 15.8% for males and 29.2% for females. [Ministry of Health NZHS prevalence CSV, 2024/25](https://minhealthnz.shinyapps.io/nz-health-survey-2024-25-annual-data-explorer/data/nz-health-survey-2024-25-prevalences.csv)

The trend is materially worse than pre-pandemic: the NZHS prevalence CSV reports 11.2% high or very high psychological distress for 15-24-year-olds in 2019/20, 23.2% in 2023/24 and 22.9% in 2024/25; MSD reports the same 2019/20-to-2024/25 change as an 11.7 percentage-point worsening. [Ministry of Health NZHS prevalence CSV, 2024/25](https://minhealthnz.shinyapps.io/nz-health-survey-2024-25-annual-data-explorer/data/nz-health-survey-2024-25-prevalences.csv); [MSD, 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf)

### 3. Child and youth survey data show self-harm and suicidal-thought burden below age 15-24

The MSD annual report's Youth Health and Wellbeing Survey table reports 2024/25 K6 high or very high psychological distress for 21.2% of 13-19-year-olds, with 25.1% for Māori young people, 28.1% for Pacific young people and 48.5% for disabled young people. [MSD, 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf)

The same MSD table reports that in 2024/25, 24.2% of 13-19-year-olds had deliberately hurt themselves in the previous 12 months, 19.0% had seriously thought about killing themselves and 5.7% had attempted suicide; for disabled young people the corresponding figures were 46.3%, 42.4% and 15.3%. [MSD, 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf)

The Youth19/Youth2000 evidence is older and geographically limited, but it is still the most detailed public pre-2024 student-level breakdown. Youth19 surveyed 7,721 Year 9-13 students in Auckland, Northland and Waikato in 2019, plus alternative-education and NEET samples, and its mental-health report found significant depressive symptoms rose from 13% in 2012 to 23% in 2019. [Youth19 methods report](https://www.fmhs.auckland.ac.nz/assets/fmhs/faculty/ahrg/docs/2020/Youth19-Initial-Findings-Intro-and-Method.pdf); [Youth19 mental health report](https://www.youth19.ac.nz/s/Youth19-Mental-Health-Report.pdf)

Youth19 also reported a 6% past-year suicide-attempt prevalence among surveyed students in 2019, with higher attempt prevalence in low-decile schools than high-decile schools, and higher attempt prevalence in high-deprivation than low-deprivation areas. [Youth19 mental health report](https://www.youth19.ac.nz/s/Youth19-Mental-Health-Report.pdf)

The 2024 Youth2000 mental-health synthesis reports high-risk groups that national headline rows can hide: Pacific young people had 25.8% significant depressive symptoms, 26.4% serious suicidal thoughts and 11.7% past-year suicide attempts in the Youth19 Pacific analysis; trans and gender-diverse students had much higher reported depressive symptoms, suicidal thoughts and attempts than cisgender students; and alternative-education and NEET samples had very high distress and suicide-attempt signals. [Youth2000 mental-health synthesis, 2024](https://static1.squarespace.com/static/5bdbb75ccef37259122e59aa/t/67070339e1026c23bbd4fbe1/1728512837095/Youth19%2BMental%2BHealth%2Bsummaries_FINAL_Tuesday%2BOct%2B8.pdf)

### 4. Self-harm hospitalisations and suicide indicators

Te Hiringa Mahara's June 2026 system-performance report says the most recent confirmed-suicide year is 2020/21, with 649 confirmed suicide deaths and an age-standardised rate of 12.2 per 100,000; it reports Māori at 17.3 per 100,000 versus non-Māori at 11.2, and males at 18.3 versus females at 6.1. [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)

The same report says there were 630 suspected self-inflicted deaths in 2024/25, with an age-standardised suspected rate of 11 per 100,000; suspected data is timelier than confirmed data but remains provisional while coronial inquiries are underway. [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)

For self-harm severe enough to lead to public-hospital admission, Te Hiringa Mahara reports around 6,800 people hospitalised in 2024/25 across about 9,000 intentional self-harm events; the 15-24 rate was 370 people per 100,000, the highest age-group rate, compared with an overall rate of 128 per 100,000. [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)

The same source reports about 1,600 ambulance attendances for self-harm in 2024/25 and says people under 25 made up around half of those attendances. [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)

The Health and Māori Advisory Committee's June 2025 priority-insights paper gives a youth Māori inequity example: in 2023/24, Māori aged 15-19 had the highest reported youth hospitalisation rate for attempted suicide or self-harm at 75.2 per 10,000, and in 2020 rangatahi Māori aged 15-24 had a confirmed suicide-death rate of 24 per 100,000 versus 14 per 100,000 for non-Māori youth. [Health and Māori Advisory Committee Priority 4 key insights, June 2025](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-07/priority-4-key-insights%E2%80%93june-2025.pdf)

These suicide/self-harm indicators are not a complete measure of distress: Te Hiringa Mahara says intentional self-harm hospitalisation data excludes some emergency-department presentations because of ED data-collection limitations, and the indicators working paper says there is no population-level indicator of suicidal ideation or self-harm that does not involve health or emergency-service contact. [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara self-harm indicators working paper, 2025](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/Suicide-and-self-harm-October-2025/Te-Hiringa-Mahara-Review-of-suicide-and-self-harm-indicators-Working-Paper.pdf)

### 5. Service access and unmet need

Te Hiringa Mahara reports 183,356 people used specialist mental-health and addiction services in 2024/25, equal to 3.45% of the population, while ages 0-24 accounted for 36.1% of all specialist-service users. [Te Hiringa Mahara access summary, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/HAA-monitoring/MHWC-Access-data-summary-Feb-2026.pdf)

Within that youth group, 46,759 people aged 0-18 and 19,363 people aged 19-24 used specialist services in 2024/25; the 19-24 count fell from 24,282 in 2020/21 to 19,363 in 2024/25, and the population access rate fell from 6.1% to 4.9%. [Te Hiringa Mahara access summary, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/HAA-monitoring/MHWC-Access-data-summary-Feb-2026.pdf)

For 0-18-year-olds, Te Hiringa Mahara reports 69.9% were seen by specialist services within three weeks in 2024/25, compared with 80.9% overall; it also reports that 40% of people aged 0-18 waited longer than eight weeks for a third appointment and that 0-18-year-olds had the highest declined-referral rate at 14.9%. [Te Hiringa Mahara access summary, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/HAA-monitoring/MHWC-Access-data-summary-Feb-2026.pdf)

For lower-intensity or primary-care settings, Te Hiringa Mahara says 27,940 young people aged 12-24 used integrated primary mental-health and addiction services, 12,549 used youth primary mental-health services and 10,192 used Gumboot Friday services in 2024/25; its news summary says Access and Choice reached 236,300 people overall in 2024/25, below the 325,000 annual target. [Te Hiringa Mahara access summary, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/HAA-monitoring/MHWC-Access-data-summary-Feb-2026.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara news summary, 2026](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/news-and-resources/young-people-missing-out-on-access-to-mental-health-services/)

The public KPI Programme source is currently useful as metadata rather than an unauthenticated export: the vendored `mental-health-data-nz` skill found public indicator pages for child-and-youth wait times and seven-day follow-up, but the dashboards require registration or sign-in for stable data access. [MHA KPI wait-times indicator](https://www.mhakpi.health.nz/indicators/wait-times/); [MHA KPI 7-day follow-up indicator](https://www.mhakpi.health.nz/indicators/7-day-follow-up/)

### 6. Forthcoming Child and Youth Mental Health Study

The Child and Youth Mental Health Study is directly relevant but not yet a current baseline replacement. The Ministry of Health says the study will cover children and young people aged 5-24, estimate mental distress, mental-health conditions, substance use, harmful gambling, help-seeking, support received, barriers to access and unmet need, and focus on disabled young people, high-deprivation communities, Māori, Pacific, Asian and rainbow youth. [Ministry of Health Child and Youth Mental Health Study](https://www.health.govt.nz/monitoring-statistics/surveys/child-and-youth-mental-health-study)

Until that study is published, the best public baseline is a stitched view: NZHS for current national 15-24 distress/unmet need; MSD and Youth Health and Wellbeing Survey for current 13-19 self-harm and suicidal-thought indicators; Te Hiringa Mahara for specialist-service access and self-harm hospitalisations; Health NZ's suicide tool for detailed but small-count-sensitive suicide rates; and Youth19/Youth2000 for older but more granular subgroup detail. [Ministry of Health NZHS annual update page](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/annual-update-of-key-results-202425-new-zealand-health-survey); [MSD, 2024/25](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/child-youth-wellbeing/reports/annual-report-on-the-child-and-youth-wellbeing-strategy-and-previous-child-poverty-related-indicators-2024-25.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf); [Health NZ suicide data and statistics](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/health-data/data-sets-and-collections/suicide-data-and-statistics); [Youth19 mental health report](https://www.youth19.ac.nz/s/Youth19-Mental-Health-Report.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A Health NZ suicide-web-tool export or official table with current youth age-by-ethnicity-by-sex-by-district-by-deprivation counts, rates, confidence intervals and suppression flags would strengthen or change the suicide subgroup baseline; I did not produce that full export because the Shiny tool's downloadable data is session-generated and small counts need careful suppression handling. [Health NZ suicide data and statistics](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/health-data/data-sets-and-collections/suicide-data-and-statistics); [Te Hiringa Mahara indicators working paper](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/Suicide-and-self-harm-October-2025/Te-Hiringa-Mahara-Review-of-suicide-and-self-harm-indicators-Working-Paper.pdf)
- A machine-readable youth regional NZHS release with age-by-region-by-ethnicity or age-by-region-by-deprivation rows would change the regional conclusion; the public regional release page confirms 2024/25 regional tables exist, but this work did not extract a stable youth cross-tab from the bot-protected release page. [NZHS regional data release](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/regional-data-release-new-zealand-health-survey)
- Publication of the Child and Youth Mental Health Study would likely replace the stitched baseline for ages 5-24, especially for disabled, high-deprivation, Māori, Pacific, Asian and rainbow youth, and for unmet need/barriers to access. [Ministry of Health Child and Youth Mental Health Study](https://www.health.govt.nz/monitoring-statistics/surveys/child-and-youth-mental-health-study)
- A public ED-only self-harm dataset would change the self-harm burden estimate; Te Hiringa Mahara says hospitalisation data excludes some emergency-department presentations because of ED data-collection limits. [Te Hiringa Mahara system performance report](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/System-performance-monitoring-2025/MHWC-System-performance-report-June-2026.pdf)
- A human with Māori, Pacific, rainbow, disability, youth-clinical and lived-experience authority should interpret what the quantitative patterns mean for service design before this stream moves from research to ideation; the public numbers alone cannot explain help-seeking, trust, cultural safety or experience of care. [Youth2000 mental-health synthesis](https://static1.squarespace.com/static/5bdbb75ccef37259122e59aa/t/67070339e1026c23bbd4fbe1/1728512837095/Youth19%2BMental%2BHealth%2Bsummaries_FINAL_Tuesday%2BOct%2B8.pdf); [Ministry of Health Child and Youth Mental Health Study](https://www.health.govt.nz/monitoring-statistics/surveys/child-and-youth-mental-health-study)
- I could not verify a current public all-cells table for youth mental distress, self-harm, suicide and service need by every requested dimension at once; the finding therefore treats the issue as a baseline plus a public-data gap, rather than pretending the complete cross-tab exists.
