# Alcohol has the largest comparable economic burden, but alcohol, methamphetamine, and gambling harms are not additive cost buckets

## Executive answer

- **Best comparable headline:** alcohol is the largest measured burden, with NZIER estimating $6.0b-$9.1b in 2023 societal alcohol harm, equal to about 1.45%-2.21% of Stats NZ nominal GDP(E) for the year ended December 2023; the Ministry-commissioned 2025 gambling report estimates $4.219b in 2023-24 gambling social cost, about 1.00% of Stats NZ nominal GDP(E) for the year ended June 2024; and the Ministry of Justice gives a conservative 2024 methamphetamine social-harm estimate of $1.5b, about 0.35% of Stats NZ nominal GDP(E) for the year ended December 2024. [NZIER alcohol harm report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/costs-of-alcohol-harms-in-new-zealand-2may24-v2.pdf); [Browne et al. gambling social-cost report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/browne-et-al-economic-social-costing-of-gambling.pdf); [Ministry of Justice, actions to combat methamphetamine](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/key-initiatives/actions-to-combat-meth/); [Stats NZ GDP CSV](https://www.stats.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Gross-domestic-product/Gross-domestic-product-March-2026-quarter/Download-data/gross-domestic-product-march-2026-quarter.csv)
- **Do not add the three totals together.** NZIER explicitly warns that alcohol categories overlap, the Drug Harm Index uses personal/community harm categories and excludes intervention costs, and DIA gambling expenditure is "amount lost by gamblers" rather than a social-cost estimate. [NZIER alcohol harm report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/costs-of-alcohol-harms-in-new-zealand-2may24-v2.pdf); [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf); [DIA gambling expenditure](https://www.dia.govt.nz/gambling-statistics-expenditure)
- **Channel comparison is more useful than one grand total.** Alcohol has the strongest separated productivity and fiscal estimates: $3.972b in productivity losses and $810m in estimated government costs, including $766m Health and ACC, $43m Justice, and $1m Social Development. [NZIER alcohol harm report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/costs-of-alcohol-harms-in-new-zealand-2may24-v2.pdf)
- **Methamphetamine is the most volatile estimate.** The 2023 Drug Harm Index puts 2021 methamphetamine harm at $864.9m, while the Ministry of Justice gives a conservative 2024 social-harm estimate of $1.5b and Police Q1 2026 wastewater results put sample-site methamphetamine harm at $38.4m per week. [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf); [Ministry of Justice, actions to combat methamphetamine](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/key-initiatives/actions-to-combat-meth/); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/about-us/publication/national-drugs-wastewater-programme-quarter-1-2026)
- **DIA's $2.792b 2023-24 gambling expenditure is a scale/context number, not the burden numerator.** It is useful because it shows regulated gambling losses across TAB, Lotto, class 4 machines, and casinos, but the Browne et al. social-cost model is the better burden estimate because it costs financial, relationship, health, work/study, crime, life-course, and service channels. [DIA gambling expenditure](https://www.dia.govt.nz/gambling-statistics-expenditure); [Browne et al. gambling social-cost report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/browne-et-al-economic-social-costing-of-gambling.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the headline inputs are from official or Ministry-commissioned sources, but the three domains use different accounting designs and the methamphetamine series changes materially when wastewater volumes move.

## Evidence

### Comparison method

This finding treats a GDP percentage as defensible only when the numerator is an annual current-dollar estimate and the denominator is a matched-period nominal GDP(E) series from the Stats NZ gross domestic product CSV. [Stats NZ GDP CSV](https://www.stats.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Gross-domestic-product/Gross-domestic-product-March-2026-quarter/Download-data/gross-domestic-product-march-2026-quarter.csv)

The GDP calculations use Stats NZ series `SNEA.SG00NAC00B15D` for December-year nominal GDP(E), `SNEA.SG00NAC00B15J` for June-year nominal GDP(E), and values in millions of dollars because the Stats NZ CSV reports the series with magnitude `6`. [Stats NZ GDP CSV](https://www.stats.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Gross-domestic-product/Gross-domestic-product-March-2026-quarter/Download-data/gross-domestic-product-march-2026-quarter.csv)

I did not use real chain-volume GDP as the denominator because the harm estimates are current-dollar cost estimates, and I did not convert the Police Q1 2026 wastewater weekly estimate into the main GDP table because it is a single-quarter sample-site flow rather than a full-year national accounting estimate. [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/about-us/publication/national-drugs-wastewater-programme-quarter-1-2026); [Stats NZ GDP CSV](https://www.stats.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Gross-domestic-product/Gross-domestic-product-March-2026-quarter/Download-data/gross-domestic-product-march-2026-quarter.csv)

### What the ranking implies

On current public estimates, the defensible ranking is alcohol first, gambling second, and methamphetamine third by annual burden as a share of matched nominal GDP. [NZIER alcohol harm report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/costs-of-alcohol-harms-in-new-zealand-2may24-v2.pdf); [Browne et al. gambling social-cost report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/browne-et-al-economic-social-costing-of-gambling.pdf); [Ministry of Justice, actions to combat methamphetamine](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/key-initiatives/actions-to-combat-meth/); [Stats NZ GDP CSV](https://www.stats.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Gross-domestic-product/Gross-domestic-product-March-2026-quarter/Download-data/gross-domestic-product-march-2026-quarter.csv)

The ranking is not the same as prioritisation, because the Police wastewater series shows methamphetamine harm can move quickly with consumption, and the Ministry of Justice says wastewater methamphetamine levels doubled across the country in 2024. [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/about-us/publication/national-drugs-wastewater-programme-quarter-1-2026); [Ministry of Justice, actions to combat methamphetamine](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/key-initiatives/actions-to-combat-meth/)

The ranking also does not mean gambling expenditure is smaller than gambling harm: DIA's $2.792b expenditure number is operator profit/loss to gamblers, while Browne et al. separately estimate social costs such as excess spend, health-related quality-of-life effects, productivity loss, crime, and service costs. [DIA gambling expenditure](https://www.dia.govt.nz/gambling-statistics-expenditure); [Browne et al. gambling social-cost report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/browne-et-al-economic-social-costing-of-gambling.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A single NZ public-sector accounting framework that re-estimates alcohol, methamphetamine, and gambling harm with the same welfare, fiscal, productivity, family/whānau, and crime boundaries would change both the ranking and the channel comparison. [NZIER alcohol harm report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/costs-of-alcohol-harms-in-new-zealand-2may24-v2.pdf); [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf); [Browne et al. gambling social-cost report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/browne-et-al-economic-social-costing-of-gambling.pdf)
- A published technical note behind the Ministry of Justice's $1.5b conservative 2024 methamphetamine social-harm estimate would increase confidence in the current methamphetamine GDP share and might allow a better health/justice/fiscal split. [Ministry of Justice, actions to combat methamphetamine](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/key-initiatives/actions-to-combat-meth/)
- A full-year 2026 wastewater harm series, adjusted for population coverage, purity, seasonality, and method changes, could shift the methamphetamine estimate closer to the annualised Q1 2026 flow or back toward the 2024 annual estimate. [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/about-us/publication/national-drugs-wastewater-programme-quarter-1-2026)
- Better public data on unreported family violence, child harms, family theft, cultural harms, and affected others would probably raise the family/whānau channel in all three domains, because the alcohol, DHI, and gambling reports each identify undercounted family or affected-other harms. [NZIER alcohol harm report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/costs-of-alcohol-harms-in-new-zealand-2may24-v2.pdf); [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf); [Browne et al. gambling social-cost report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/browne-et-al-economic-social-costing-of-gambling.pdf)
- I could not verify a public methamphetamine productivity-loss estimate or a single pure fiscal total for gambling comparable to NZIER's alcohol government-cost table. [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf); [Browne et al. gambling social-cost report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/browne-et-al-economic-social-costing-of-gambling.pdf)
- Human domain review is needed before using this as a prioritisation recommendation, because economic burden is not the same as urgency, equity impact, tractability, or service-delivery readiness. [For Good Project streams and human gates](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/the-for-good-project/blob/main/docs/STREAMS.md)
