# A refreshed 20-SKU Grocer snapshot found NZ grocery price gaps up to 5.27x, with Papakura gaps up to 2.34x

## Executive answer

- In a fixed 20-SKU basket re-queried from Grocer's public current-price files on 2026-07-07, the largest observed same-SKU spread was loose lemons: NZ$1.99 at PAK'nSAVE Mill Street versus NZ$10.49 at New World Gate Pa, a 5.27x effective-price ratio [Grocer current price files: store 233](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_233.parquet), [store 294](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_294.parquet).
- The next largest gaps in the refreshed 20-SKU snapshot were lemons prepack 1kg at 2.86x, loose red tomatoes at 2.67x, Wattie's baked beans 420g at 2.34x, Coca-Cola Classic 1.5L at 2.29x, and Chelsea white sugar 1.5kg at 2.24x [Grocer current price files used in table rows below](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_440.parquet).
- The earlier Papakura potato, Coca-Cola, sugar, and Weet-Bix figures did not survive a fresh check: current files and product histories show those specific prices had changed before the 2026-07-07 recheck, so this revision removes them from the headline and replaces the Papakura table with refreshed current rows [Potatoes history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_2116.parquet), [Coca-Cola history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_6655.parquet), [Sugar history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_72.parquet), [Weet-Bix history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_21510.parquet).
- The refreshed Papakura subset still has local gaps, but the strongest few-kilometre examples are now smaller and different: Wattie's baked beans were NZ$1.79 at PAK'nSAVE Papakura versus NZ$4.19 at FreshChoice Papakura (2.34x), and Coca-Cola Classic 1.5L was NZ$2.49 at Woolworths Papakura versus NZ$4.99 at New World Papakura (2.00x) [Grocer Papakura price files: Woolworths 118](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_118.parquet), [FreshChoice 206](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_206.parquet), [PAK'nSAVE 230](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_230.parquet), [New World 307](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_307.parquet).
- Treat these as current-file price observations, not breach allegations or verified shelf audits: Grocer's public data exposes per-store price rows with original, sale, club, online, and multibuy fields, but Grocer also warns that prices can vary by location and time and does not guarantee displayed prices are accurate [Grocer skill notes](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/blob/34038374116ee537f8cb2fb8d0450d6b7dc21277/skills/grocer-nz/SKILL.md), [Grocer data-audit finding](grocer-nz-data-audit.md).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the refreshed arithmetic is reproducible from public Grocer files and product IDs are fixed. Confidence is not High because this is a same-fetch current-file snapshot from a third-party price collector, not an independently observed shelf audit; some current rows have `updated_at` timestamps before 2026-07-07; sale and club fields can materially change the effective price; and I did not verify each price in retailer apps or stores.

## Evidence

### Method and basket

I answered: for a fixed 20-SKU basket of everyday products, what were the largest cheapest-versus-dearest effective-price gaps in a Grocer current-price snapshot fetched on 2026-07-07, and which examples were local or same-banner? The `grocer-nz` skill documents Grocer's public base catalogue, per-store current-price parquet files, product search, current-price fields, and per-product price-history files; prices are stored in NZ cents [Grocer skill notes](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/blob/34038374116ee537f8cb2fb8d0450d6b7dc21277/skills/grocer-nz/SKILL.md), [Grocer API notes](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/blob/34038374116ee537f8cb2fb8d0450d6b7dc21277/skills/grocer-nz/references/api-notes.md).

The basket was fixed before ranking: product IDs `71`, `2279`, `4471`, `2116`, `42`, `5452`, `4081`, `7785`, `5795`, `5525`, `4085`, `20040`, `72`, `21510`, `1634`, `23717`, `6655`, `192`, `50692`, and `21610`. Product names, brands, sizes, and store IDs came from Grocer's public base catalogue and search index [Grocer base database](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/base_v3.duckdb.br), [Grocer skill notes](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/blob/34038374116ee537f8cb2fb8d0450d6b7dc21277/skills/grocer-nz/SKILL.md).

For the 2026-07-07 revision I refreshed the public current-price cache and loaded all 465 enabled store files listed in Grocer's public base catalogue. For each product-store row I used `effective_price_cent = least(sale_price_cent, club_price_cent, online_price_cent, original_price_cent)` after ignoring nulls; I excluded multibuy prices because the unit quantity can change the interpretation of the shopper-facing price [Grocer API notes](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/blob/34038374116ee537f8cb2fb8d0450d6b7dc21277/skills/grocer-nz/references/api-notes.md). That means the gap is "best visible current price in Grocer's row", not necessarily the non-member shelf price for every shopper [Grocer data-audit finding](grocer-nz-data-audit.md).

### Recheck of stale Papakura rows

A targeted recheck of the reviewer-flagged Papakura rows found that the previous draft's local headline examples were stale or used original price where an active sale price lowered the effective price. New World Papakura's potatoes Vivaldi Gold 2kg current row was NZ$8.99, not NZ$3.99; PAK'nSAVE Papakura Coca-Cola Classic 1.5L was NZ$3.99 while FreshChoice Papakura had an effective sale price of NZ$3.50; FreshChoice Papakura Chelsea white sugar had an effective sale price of NZ$4.40, not NZ$6.49; and New World Papakura Weet-Bix was NZ$6.99, not NZ$3.99 [Grocer current price files: 206](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_206.parquet), [230](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_230.parquet), [307](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/prices_per_store_v3/public_prices_307.parquet).

The product-history files support the same correction: for product `2116`, New World Papakura moved from NZ$3.99 on the rows timestamped 2026-07-02 17:00 UTC to NZ$8.99 by the 2026-07-03 17:00 UTC row; for product `6655`, FreshChoice Papakura moved to NZ$3.50 and PAK'nSAVE Papakura to NZ$3.99 by the 2026-07-05 17:00 UTC row; for product `72`, FreshChoice Papakura moved to NZ$4.40 by the 2026-07-05 17:00 UTC row; and for product `21510`, New World Papakura moved to NZ$6.99 by the 2026-07-05 17:00 UTC row [Potatoes history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_2116.parquet), [Coca-Cola history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_6655.parquet), [Sugar history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_72.parquet), [Weet-Bix history](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_21510.parquet).

## What would change this conclusion

- A later complete all-enabled-store crawl could change the top-ranked stores and ratios, because these are current rows and some rows visibly changed between the prior draft and this 2026-07-07 recheck [Grocer current-price file pattern documented in skill notes](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/blob/34038374116ee537f8cb2fb8d0450d6b7dc21277/skills/grocer-nz/SKILL.md), [Grocer price-history files](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/price_history_v3/price_history_6655.parquet).
- Independent in-store or retailer-app verification of the top examples would raise confidence from Medium to High; without that, these are Grocer current-row observations rather than independently confirmed shelf audits [Grocer terms and data-audit caveats](grocer-nz-data-audit.md).
- A different treatment of club prices could change ratios, because this finding used the lowest visible original/sale/club/online field and did not require that every shopper qualify for a member price [Grocer API notes](https://github.com/thecolab-ai/.skills/blob/34038374116ee537f8cb2fb8d0450d6b7dc21277/skills/grocer-nz/references/api-notes.md).
- Some loose produce rows have null size fields and are best read as retailer-matched product IDs, not proof that every piece of produce is like-for-like in quality, origin, or grade [Grocer base database](https://assets-prod.grocer.nz/public/base_v3.duckdb.br).
- I could not verify Grocer's upstream collection method, exact current-price refresh schedule, or whether the loaded current rows exactly matched in-store shelf labels at the time a shopper walked in [Grocer data-audit finding](grocer-nz-data-audit.md).
