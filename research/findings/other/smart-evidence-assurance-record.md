# SMART has a real business-case and assurance record, but the public evidence does not yet prove the rollout answers the National Standards failure modes

## Executive answer

- **The 2026 SMART/reporting system is not evidence-free.** Public records include a Single Stage Business Case and Cabinet pack, Treasury advice, a procurement-plan/RFP evaluation record, a privacy impact assessment, GETS award material for Māori-medium psychometric support, and Ministry/Beehive guidance on the new parent-reporting framework. [Ministry Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6); [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf); [FYI/MoE procurement OIA, 2025-2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/32602-procurement-of-smart); [FYI/MoE privacy OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342-oia-request-security-and-privacy-compliance-for-smart-assessment-tool); [GETS RFx 33957361, 2026](https://www.gets.govt.nz/MEDU/ExternalTenderDetails.htm?id=33957361)
- **The strongest public claim for consultation and trialling remains weakly evidenced.** The Minister said the approach followed consultation with principals' associations and teachers, plus trialling in 85 schools involving about 12,000 student assessment engagements, but I did not find a public trial report, trial dataset, independent evaluation, or released validation report that lets a reader test that claim. [Beehive, 2026](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/assessment-and-reporting-changes-parents-shine-light-learning); [FYI/MoE validation-process OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34749-assessment-content-review-and-validation-processes)
- **Treasury's record supports the concept but not the rushed timeline.** Treasury accepted that regular standardised assessment can support teaching and system policy, but it rated delivery only "Partial", warned the 2026 mandate was driving the tool timeline, and identified unresolved risks around change management, data privacy/ownership, stakeholder engagement, Māori-medium settings, item-development capacity, and the amber/red Gateway rating. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)
- **The public record only partly answers National Standards-era concerns.** The new report design uses progress descriptors, progress-over-time graphs, multiple evidence sources and teacher judgement; however, public evidence does not yet demonstrate solved moderation, workload, labelling, curriculum-narrowing, Māori-medium/aromatawai or data-misuse risks. [Ministry parent guide, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress); [NZCER, 2016](https://www.nzcer.org.nz/sites/default/files/downloads/National%20Standards%20in%20their%20seventh%20year.pdf); [Thrupp, 2017](https://ojs.victoria.ac.nz/nzaroe/article/download/4142/3661/5380)
- **Early sector evidence cuts against a clean "evidence-based rollout" story.** NZEI reported data-storage and AI-marking accuracy concerns, PPTA deputy-principal feedback reported workload, timetable, text-selection, fairness and AI-marking concerns, and the Privacy Impact Assessment itself recorded medium risks around transparency, accuracy, retention, scope creep and security. [NZEI Te Riu Roa, 2026](https://www.nzeiteriuroa.org.nz/about-us/media-releases/parents-urged-to-check-school-use-of-inaccurate-government-ai-tool); [PPTA, 2026](https://www.ppta.org.nz/news-and-media/smart-tool-not-ready-creates-more-concerns-than-solutions-deputy-principals-feedback); [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the existence and content of the public assurance record are well supported by official releases and OIA material, but the decisive evidence on trial quality, psychometric validity, Māori-medium fit and classroom burden is either unpublished, delayed, or only available through interested sector claims.

## Evidence

### What the Government publicly claims

The Government's 2 February 2026 announcement says 2026 is the first year of nationally consistent assessment and reporting in primary and intermediate schools, supported by twice-yearly progress check-ins and SMART for Years 3-8 reading, writing and maths. [Beehive, 2026](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/assessment-and-reporting-changes-parents-shine-light-learning)

The same announcement says the approach responds to ERO and NZAI advice, was developed after consultation with principals' associations and teachers, and was trialled in 85 schools involving about 12,000 student assessment engagements. [Beehive, 2026](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/assessment-and-reporting-changes-parents-shine-light-learning)

The new reporting framework requires five common elements in reports: a progress descriptor, an explanation of why that descriptor was chosen, assessment-tool results, a progress-over-time graph, and attendance information. [Ministry parent guide, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress)

The Ministry parent guide says no single evidence source gives a full picture and that teachers should combine observations, conversations, student work, classroom tasks and assessment-tool results when selecting progress descriptors. [Ministry parent guide, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress)

### What the released business case and Cabinet material add

The released Cabinet pack contains the "Standardised tool for assessment and aromatawai" Single Stage Business Case, dated through versions in late 2024, and says it followed Treasury Better Business Case guidance and the five-case model. [Ministry Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6)

The business case identifies the investment as a long-term dual-language standardised tool for twice-yearly assessment and aromatawai aligned to The New Zealand Curriculum and Te Marautanga o Aotearoa, intended to support teaching, parent/whānau reporting and system-level decisions. [Ministry Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6)

The business case describes existing e-asTTle problems that map directly to National Standards-era concerns: writing and tuhituhi marking/moderation workload, weaker reliability when moderation is bypassed, dated or culturally inappropriate items, low Māori-medium use, and reports that were not clear for teachers, whānau, students and administrators. [Ministry Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6)

The business case also shows delivery risk: the MVP was planned for early 2026 with minimal English-medium and Māori-medium item banks, the full version was planned for September 2026, and the item table showed 10,018 existing items against 19,200 required items. [Ministry Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6)

The business case says the Treasury Risk Profile Assessment rated the project "high risk" and planned assurance included Privacy and Security Certification and Accreditation, data-architecture review, technical/commercial viability review, Gateway reviews and Independent Quality Assurance reviews. [Ministry Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6)

### What Treasury warned

Treasury recommended that the Minister of Finance not approve the out-of-cycle funding request because key building blocks were unlikely to be ready before Cabinet considered a pre-commitment. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)

Treasury said the urgency was driven by the Minister of Education's intention to mandate standardised assessment tools from the beginning of 2026, and it suggested using existing tools for the 2026 mandate or introducing the mandate later. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)

Treasury's listed concerns included a more robust change-management plan, clear data privacy and ownership policies, confirmation that all delivery workstreams were achievable, and stronger stakeholder engagement, especially with Māori-medium education providers. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)

In its Budget 2025 assessment, Treasury rated delivery "Partial", said it did not agree the initiative could be implemented within the proposed timeframe, and warned that 19,200 new English-language assessment items plus te reo Māori item development created high delivery risk. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)

Treasury also said the benefit of funding in the Māori-medium context was unclear until the intervention logic explained how twice-yearly standardised assessment would improve learning support and policy compared with more flexible assessment arrangements, and until the implications for aromatawai were clearly articulated. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf)

### Procurement and assurance record

The FYI procurement OIA page shows the Ministry released a procurement response, a procurement plan for the Assessment and Aromatawai Tool, and RFP/tender documents in December 2025, and later pointed to the public Cabinet pack for the Single Stage Business Case. [FYI/MoE procurement OIA, 2025-2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/32602-procurement-of-smart)

The released procurement-plan text says the RFP objective was to identify an existing commercial off-the-shelf assessment solution delivered as software-as-a-service for online standardised assessments in English and te reo Māori, with evaluation criteria, weightings, demonstrations, dialogue, probity standards and price separation in the evaluation process. [FYI/MoE procurement plan attachment, 2025](https://fyi.org.nz/request/32602/response/136613/attach/5/Appendix%20A%20Procurement%20Plan%20Assessment%20and%20Aromatawai%20Tool%20Final%20Signed%20Redacted.pdf)

The PIA says SMART is delivered by Janison as software-as-a-service, hosted in Microsoft Azure New Zealand with disaster recovery in Sydney, and using controls including role-based access controls, encryption, access logging and audit trails. [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf)

The PIA says the Ministry remains accountable under the Privacy Act 2020, with Janison handling personal information as the Ministry's agent, and estimates SMART will process information for about 432,000 children, 22,400 teachers and 6,160 school/kura administrators. [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf)

The PIA's overall assessment is not a clean bill of health: it says risks are manageable with targeted controls, but records medium risks around transparency, accuracy, retention, scope creep and security, and says the PIA is not a formal security assessment because Certification and Accreditation is separate. [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf)

The GETS award notice for "Psychometrician Services for Te Hāpai Ākonga" says the Ministry awarded Monocle Education Limited work to refine Te Hāpai Ākonga data collection and assessment tools, support reliable and culturally appropriate measurement of mokopuna progress in pānui, tuhituhi, kōrero and pāngarau, and strengthen national reporting, programme evaluation and future investment decisions. [GETS RFx 33957361, 2026](https://www.gets.govt.nz/MEDU/ExternalTenderDetails.htm?id=33957361)

### Comparison with National Standards evidence

The National Standards comparison should be narrow: public SMART material is stronger than National Standards-era public debate in the sense that it includes a released business case, procurement record, Treasury advice and PIA; I did not verify a source that proves National Standards lacked every possible business-case or Treasury record, so this finding does not make that absence claim. [Ministry Cabinet pack, 2026](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-03/Cabinet%20Pack%20-%20Establishing%20a%20new%20standardised%20tool_Redacted_0.pdf?VersionId=h4gLPqajurvR8Cjn1c0RZxcJN3VJHYg6); [FYI/MoE procurement OIA, 2025-2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/32602-procurement-of-smart); [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf)

The relevant National Standards failure modes are still the same ones identified in existing research: weak comparability/moderation, workload, curriculum narrowing, labelling, and data used for ranking rather than learning support. [NZCER, 2016](https://www.nzcer.org.nz/sites/default/files/downloads/National%20Standards%20in%20their%20seventh%20year.pdf); [Thrupp, 2017](https://ojs.victoria.ac.nz/nzaroe/article/download/4142/3661/5380)

SMART's public record answers the policy-process criticism better than it answers the classroom-impact criticism: the assurance machinery exists, but the currently public evidence is not enough to show that trialling, validation, implementation and data governance have resolved the harms that made National Standards contested. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf); [FYI/MoE validation-process OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34749-assessment-content-review-and-validation-processes); [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A published independent evaluation of the 85-school trial, including sampling, item calibration, AI/writing-marking reliability, accessibility, Māori-medium/aromatawai validity, teacher workload, student/whānau comprehension and classroom-use findings, would materially strengthen or weaken the conclusion. [Beehive, 2026](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/assessment-and-reporting-changes-parents-shine-light-learning); [FYI/MoE validation-process OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34749-assessment-content-review-and-validation-processes)
- A completed public security Certification and Accreditation summary, privacy-statement release, retention schedule, data-use policy and anti-league-table rule would reduce the data-misuse concern. [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf); [FYI/MoE SMART data-use OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34630-smart-tool-data-use)
- Evidence from kura, Māori-medium leaders and whānau that SMART/Te Hāpai Ākonga supports aromatawai principles in practice would change the Māori-medium confidence rating. [Treasury OIA 20250889, 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-02/oia-20250889.pdf); [GETS RFx 33957361, 2026](https://www.gets.govt.nz/MEDU/ExternalTenderDetails.htm?id=33957361)
- Representative teacher, principal, student and whānau evidence after the first two 2026 reporting windows would change the workload, labelling and parent-clarity assessment. [Ministry parent guide, 2026](https://www.education.govt.nz/parents-and-caregivers/schools-year-0-13/parent-portal/guide-your-childs-reporting-and-progress); [PPTA, 2026](https://www.ppta.org.nz/news-and-media/smart-tool-not-ready-creates-more-concerns-than-solutions-deputy-principals-feedback)
- I could not verify a public final Gateway review, final trial report, full supplier evaluation report, final security C&A report, or complete SMART data-use/governance response; several relevant FYI requests were delayed, partial, or still not fully classifiable by the requester as of access on 7 July 2026. [FYI/MoE governance OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34790-smart-governance-and-oversight-structures); [FYI/MoE validation-process OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34749-assessment-content-review-and-validation-processes); [FYI/MoE SMART data-use OIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/34630-smart-tool-data-use)
- This finding needs human review from assessment specialists, Māori-medium/aromatawai leaders, teachers implementing SMART, disabled learner advocates, parents/whānau and privacy/security practitioners, because public documents cannot settle whether the tool is fair, mana-enhancing and useful in practice. [NZAI, 2021](https://www.nzai.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/NZAI_Assessment-Principles-Practices-and-Proof-Final.pdf); [FYI/MoE SMART PIA, 2026](https://fyi.org.nz/request/33342/response/141499/attach/5/APPENDIX%20A%20SMART%20PIA%20Redacted.pdf)

## Open follow-up questions

- What did the 85-school trial actually measure, and did it include independent psychometric, Māori-medium, accessibility, workload and student/whānau impact evaluation?
- What final rules govern publication, aggregation, reuse and external sharing of SMART data?
- After two reporting windows, are schools using SMART results to trigger funded support, or mainly to satisfy reporting and accountability expectations?
- What evidence do kura and Māori-medium leaders consider sufficient before national aromatawai data is used for system reporting or resource decisions?
