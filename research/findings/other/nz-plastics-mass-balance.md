# NZ's best current plastic mass balance is packaging-specific; all-plastics flows remain too incomplete for one current balance

## Executive answer

- The best current public mass balance is for **plastic packaging in 2022**, not for all plastics: PPPS/Valpak estimated 263,231 tonnes placed on the NZ market, 45,113 tonnes reprocessed onshore or exported for recycling, 28,954 tonnes onshore, 16,159 tonnes exported, and by subtraction 218,118 tonnes not recycled through those channels [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf).
- That 2022 packaging balance implies a **17.1% packaging recycling rate**, with onshore reprocessing equal to 11.0% of packaging placed on market and exports equal to 6.1%; MfE's current product-stewardship page separately repeats the headline that NZ produced about 263,000 tonnes of plastic packaging in 2022 and recycled 17% [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf); [MfE priority product stewardship, updated 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/what-government-is-doing/areas-of-work/waste/product-stewardship/priority-product-stewardship/).
- The older PMCSA all-plastics numbers are still useful as a warning label, not a current balance: PMCSA reported 575,000 tonnes/year of raw resin and plastic-material imports in 2018, 45,000 tonnes collected for recycling, 380,000 tonnes landfilled in 2015, and unknown finished-product imports, construction, agriculture, other use, marine leakage and land leakage [PMCSA, Quantifying Aotearoa's plastic](https://www.pmcsa.ac.nz/topics/rethinking-plastics/quantifying-aotearoas-plastic/).
- Public data can split some packaging flows by household/consumer versus non-consumer and by accepted kerbside polymer group, but cannot give a full polymer-by-fate balance: PPPS gives post-consumer and non-consumer onshore reprocessing but says offshore exports could not be split between consumer and non-consumer, while MfE/Eunomia says local plastic recycling data is missing and would need regular collection [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf); [Eunomia for MfE, 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/publications/New-Zealand-National-Recycling-Rate-Issues-and-Options-FINAL-30-01-25.pdf).
- Plastic-waste exports fell sharply after 2017 but did not disappear: MfE's Customs-based aide-memoire says exports fell from over 40,000 tonnes in 2017 to 21,000 tonnes in 2022, and the UN Comtrade-derived Our World in Data series reports NZ HS 3915 plastic-waste exports of 27,275 tonnes in 2024 [MfE aide-memoire BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf); [Our World in Data / UN Comtrade, downloaded 2026-07-05](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/plastic-waste-trade?metric=exports&rate=total).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the packaging baseline is the strongest current public estimate, but it is survey/interview-based and narrow; the all-plastics balance still depends on dated, one-off and incomplete sources.

## Evidence

### Scope: two balances, not one

New Zealand does not have one current public all-plastics mass balance from placed-on-market through collection, onshore reprocessing, export, landfill and leakage, because MfE's January 2025 recycling-rate model says most comprehensive recovered-material data is low or medium quality outside levy data, some territorial-authority reporting and product-stewardship data, and says plastic recovery has regular export data but missing local recycling data [Eunomia for MfE, 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/publications/New-Zealand-National-Recycling-Rate-Issues-and-Options-FINAL-30-01-25.pdf). The defensible current answer is therefore a **packaging balance for 2022**, with an older **all-plastics context balance** kept separate because PMCSA used 2015-2018 inputs and explicitly listed major unknowns for finished-product imports and leakage [PMCSA, Quantifying Aotearoa's plastic](https://www.pmcsa.ac.nz/topics/rethinking-plastics/quantifying-aotearoas-plastic/).

Within the 45,113 tonnes recycled, 28,954 tonnes onshore means 64.2% of recycled packaging was reprocessed domestically and 16,159 tonnes exported means 35.8% was exported; these are arithmetic shares of PPPS's reported onshore and offshore tonnages [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf). PPPS says its recycling estimates relied on surveys and interviews, identified major reprocessors and resins, and found that available capacity/export data was not specific enough to aggregate total tonnages by material and fate for each resin type [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf).

### Household/consumer versus commercial

PPPS's scope is **consumer or household-like plastic packaging**, not all commercial and industrial plastic packaging: it recommends commencing with "household and household-like" consumer-goods packaging and later considering all other plastic packaging, including commercial and industrial business-to-business packaging [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf). On placed-on-market tonnage, PPPS estimated 122,031 tonnes in scope if milk and a container-return-scheme category are excluded, 135,592 tonnes if milk is included and CRS is excluded, and 91,883 tonnes of out-of-scope C&I/other plastic packaging in its 2022 packaging estimate [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf).

For the recycling split, PPPS reports 15,080 tonnes of post-consumer packaging reprocessed onshore and 13,874 tonnes of non-consumer packaging reprocessed onshore, but the 16,159 tonnes exported has no consumer/non-consumer split [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf). That means public data supports a partial household/commercial split for **onshore reprocessing only**, not for exports, landfill or leakage [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf).

### Plastics 1/2/5 versus 3/4/6/7

Since 1 February 2024, council-managed household kerbside recycling has standardised accepted materials, including plastic bottles, trays and containers numbered 1, 2 or 5 [MfE household recycling page, updated 2024](https://environment.govt.nz/what-government-is-doing/areas-of-work/waste/improving-household-recycling-and-food-scrap-collections/). The export-control aide-memoire says mixtures of PET #1, HDPE/LDPE #2/#4 and PP #5, and separated streams of other plastic, do not require a Basel permit if almost free from contamination and destined for environmentally sound recycling, while mixed or contaminated plastic waste is restricted [MfE aide-memoire BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

Public data does not allow a complete 1/2/5 versus 3/4/6/7 fate balance. PMCSA's older import table gives raw-resin import shares by polymer, including PET #1 at 7%, HDPE #2 at 26%, PVC #3 at 12%, LDPE #4 at 31%, PP #5 at 18%, PS #6 at 4% and other #7 at 3%, but those are raw-resin import shares, not placed-on-market packaging fates [PMCSA, Quantifying Aotearoa's plastic](https://www.pmcsa.ac.nz/topics/rethinking-plastics/quantifying-aotearoas-plastic/). PPPS says producers, councils, operators and reprocessors should be required to report plastic packaging by resin type, format, colour, collection, sorting, reprocessing, disposal and system losses, which is a recommendation that the current public dataset is not yet sufficient [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf).

### All-plastics context and why it should not be merged with packaging

PMCSA's all-plastics data page is the clearest public map of the older whole-system problem: it lists 575,000 tonnes/year of raw resin and plastic-material imports in 2018, 75,000 tonnes/year of raw resin and plastic-material exports in 2018, 35,000 tonnes/year of waste-plastic exports in 2018, 150,000 tonnes/year of packaging in use from a 2015 Packaging NZ source, 45,000 tonnes/year collected for recycling from NRRT 2018, 380,000 tonnes/year of plastic waste landfilled from Eunomia 2015 and Perrot et al. 2018, and unknown tonnes for finished-product imports, finished-product exports, construction, agriculture, other uses, marine litter and land litter [PMCSA, Quantifying Aotearoa's plastic](https://www.pmcsa.ac.nz/topics/rethinking-plastics/quantifying-aotearoas-plastic/). PMCSA also states that there was no coordinated or standardised approach to measure or report plastic use and disposal by material type, which is why the 2015-2018 all-plastics table should not be treated as a current 2022 packaging trend line [PMCSA, Quantifying Aotearoa's plastic](https://www.pmcsa.ac.nz/topics/rethinking-plastics/quantifying-aotearoas-plastic/).

For all plastics, the only public export series I could update during this run was HS 3915 plastic-waste exports, not all plastic raw materials or all exported packaging. MfE's 2023 aide-memoire says plastic-waste exports fell from over 40,000 tonnes in 2017 to 21,000 tonnes in 2022 and that about 70% of 2022 plastic-waste exports were polyethylene [MfE aide-memoire BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf). Our World in Data's UN Comtrade-derived HS 3915 series reports NZ plastic-waste exports of 41,503 tonnes in 2017, 21,019 tonnes in 2022, 28,377 tonnes in 2023 and 27,275 tonnes in 2024, which broadly corroborates the 2022 MfE level but suggests export volumes had risen above 2022 by 2023-2024 [Our World in Data / UN Comtrade, downloaded 2026-07-05](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/plastic-waste-trade?metric=exports&rate=total).

### Landfill and leakage

For packaging, the difference between 263,231 tonnes placed on market and 45,113 tonnes reprocessed onshore or exported is 218,118 tonnes, but public sources do not split that residual into landfill, stockpiling, contamination losses, energy recovery, litter or other leakage [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf). For all plastics, PMCSA's 380,000-tonne landfill estimate is explicitly based on 2015 Class 1 landfill tonnage multiplied by an estimated 12% plastic composition, and PMCSA lists marine and land-litter tonnages as unknown while giving partial litter-density indicators [PMCSA, Quantifying Aotearoa's plastic](https://www.pmcsa.ac.nz/topics/rethinking-plastics/quantifying-aotearoas-plastic/).

## What would change this conclusion

- A current MfE, Stats NZ, Customs or accredited product-stewardship release giving all-plastics placed-on-market, collection, sorted output, onshore reprocessing, export, landfill, stockpiling and leakage by polymer would replace the two-balance conclusion [Eunomia for MfE, 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/publications/New-Zealand-National-Recycling-Rate-Issues-and-Options-FINAL-30-01-25.pdf).
- A direct Stats NZ/Customs extract for HS 3915 and any non-HS-3915 plastic raw-material export categories for 2023-2025 would strengthen or revise the export update; I found the UN Comtrade-derived OWID series but did not obtain a direct Stats NZ/Customs download during this run [Our World in Data / UN Comtrade](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/plastic-waste-trade?metric=exports&rate=total).
- A PPPS/Valpak underlying data appendix with resin, source, collection channel, contamination loss, and destination treatment would allow the household/commercial and 1/2/5-versus-3/4/6/7 splits that the public scheme report says are currently incomplete [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf).
- A national SWAP-style composition dataset after the 2024 kerbside standardisation and expanded OWLS/TA reporting would update the dated 380,000-tonne all-plastics landfill estimate [PMCSA, Quantifying Aotearoa's plastic](https://www.pmcsa.ac.nz/topics/rethinking-plastics/quantifying-aotearoas-plastic/); [Eunomia for MfE, 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/publications/New-Zealand-National-Recycling-Rate-Issues-and-Options-FINAL-30-01-25.pdf).
- Human validation from council waste managers, MRF operators, exporters and reprocessors would be needed before using these figures for operational design, because published sources do not expose contract-level tonnages, contamination rates, stockpiling, rejected bales or end-destination audits consistently [PPPS Scheme Design Report, 2025](https://ppps.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Plastic-Packaging-Product-Stewardship-Scheme-Design-Report-2025.pdf); [MfE aide-memoire BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).
