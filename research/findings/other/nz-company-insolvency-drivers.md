# IRD debt enforcement is the clearest evidenced near-term driver of elevated NZ company insolvency appointments, while macro pressure and post-Covid clean-up remain supporting context rather than proven causes

## Executive answer

- **The elevation is real and current.** Companies Office annual data shows company liquidations rose from **1,379 in 2021** to **1,823 in 2023**, **2,502 in 2024**, and **2,867 in 2025**; its monthly file shows **1,411** liquidations in January-June 2026, above **1,296** in January-June 2025 and **837** in January-June 2023. [Companies Office annual CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/yearly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv); [Companies Office monthly CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/monthly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv)
- **IRD enforcement is the strongest evidenced near-term driver, but not a complete causal decomposition.** IRD's January-March 2026 overdue-debt report says it issued **1,525 statutory demands** and **liquidated 605 companies** year-to-date to 31 March 2026, while its April 2026 tax-agent webinar says statutory demands in the first half of the financial year were almost double FY24. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405); [IRD tax-agent debt webinar PDF, Apr 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/no-number/presentations-and-webinar-pdfs/tax-agent-webinar-on-debt-april-2026.pdf?modified=20260420234851)
- **Tax-debt stock and composition explain why IRD has a large pipeline.** IRD reports total tax and entitlement debt of **$9.4b** at 31 March 2026, says **65%** of outstanding tax debt is held by micro and small-to-medium businesses, and says GST plus employer obligations account for **57%** of total debt. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405)
- **Lagged inflation, interest-rate, demand, and cash-buffer pressure are plausible background conditions, not proven appointment causes.** RBNZ says the economy was recovering but financial pressures on households and businesses were easing only as interest rates fell, that borrowing costs remain high for smaller businesses, and that many SMEs in hospitality and construction had margin pressure and reduced cash buffers since 2020. [RBNZ Financial Stability Report, May 2026](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/financial-stability/financial-stability-report/financial-stability-reports/2026/may/financial-stability-report-may-2026); [RBNZ FSR online report, SME lending section](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/financial-stability/financial-stability-report/financial-stability-reports/2026/may/financial-stability-report-may-2026/web-version)
- **"Post-Covid clean-up" and "zombie companies" are mostly expert interpretation.** Deloitte, using Companies Office and IRD material, calls IRD enforcement one of the main drivers and says Official Assignee liquidation appointments have increased significantly after COVID-19, but those claims should be treated as practitioner analysis unless reproduced from primary appointment-type or petitioning-creditor data. [Deloitte NZ Quarterly Insolvency Trends Q1 2026](https://www.deloitte.com/content/dam/assets-zone1/nz/en/docs/services/consulting/2026/2026-q1-new-zealand-insolvency-trends.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the trend and IRD enforcement activity are official and direct; the wider macro and post-Covid explanations are plausible and well-supported as context, but public data does not yet allocate each insolvency appointment to a cause or initiating creditor.

## Evidence

### What is elevated

Companies Office annual statistics show company liquidations rose from **1,379** in 2021 to **1,550** in 2022, **1,823** in 2023, **2,502** in 2024, and **2,867** in 2025. [Companies Office annual CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/yearly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv) The same annual file shows receiverships rose from **65** in 2022 to **102** in 2023, **185** in 2024, and **171** in 2025, while voluntary administrations rose from **31** in 2022 to **43** in 2023, **73** in 2024, and **42** in 2025. [Companies Office annual CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/yearly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv)

Companies Office monthly statistics show January-June liquidations of **1,411** in 2026, compared with **1,296** in the same six months of 2025, **1,131** in 2024, **837** in 2023, and **620** in 2022; adding receiverships and voluntary administrations gives **1,490** formal appointments in January-June 2026, compared with **1,381** in January-June 2025 and **924** in January-June 2023. [Companies Office monthly CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/monthly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv)

The safe trend statement is "appointments remain elevated", not "they are accelerating every month": Companies Office monthly data shows 2026 liquidations fell from **299** in March to **198** in April, then rose to **253** in May and **259** in June. [Companies Office monthly CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/monthly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv)

**Confidence: High** for the register-count trend because it comes from the official Companies Office series; **Medium** for interpreting 2026 because the year is incomplete and monthly register statistics can move as records are updated. [Companies Office latest company statistics](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/insights-and-articles/latest-company-statistics/)

### IRD enforcement is unusually direct evidence, but still partial

IRD's January-March 2026 overdue-debt report says total tax and entitlement debt was **$9.4b** at 31 March 2026, compared with **$9.3b** at March 2025, and says annual growth had slowed from **14.7%** at March 2025 to **1.6%** at March 2026. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405) The same report says **556,000** customers had tax debt at 31 March 2026, **23,000** fewer than at March 2025, and says collectable debt was **$5.9b**, or **62%** of total overdue tax and entitlement debt. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405)

IRD's tax-debt stock is business-heavy in the categories that matter for insolvency risk: the overdue-debt report says micro-businesses held **$3.6b** and SMEs held **$2.5b** of the **$9.4b** total at 31 March 2026, and says GST (**$3.4b**) plus employer activities (**$2.0b**) made up **57%** of total debt. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405) IRD's separate tax-statistics page says company tax and entitlement debt rose from **$4.8b** in June 2024 to **$5.7b** in June 2025, total tax and entitlement debt rose from **$7.9b** to **$9.3b**, and customers with tax debt rose from **512,000** to **527,000** over the same period. [IRD tax and entitlement debt statistics](https://www.ird.govt.nz/about-us/tax-statistics/tax-and-entitlement-debt-statistics)

IRD explicitly links serious-case management to liquidations. Its January-March 2026 report says it was actively managing about **4,500** serious cases covering **$1.4b** of tax debt, legal action was being taken in a quarter of cases, and **680** cases were before the courts. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405) It then says IRD issued **1,525** statutory demands year-to-date to 31 March 2026, that statutory demands mark the beginning of the liquidation process and result in many companies going into voluntary liquidation, and that IRD had liquidated **605** companies and bankrupted **118** individuals in that year-to-date period. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405)

IRD's April 2026 tax-agent webinar is consistent with that report but uses different cut points: it says IRD had warned **60** companies in a credit-reporting trial, more than half had been reported, more than half were in liquidation proceedings, and almost **1,000** statutory demands were issued in the first half of the financial year, up on the previous year and almost double FY24. [IRD tax-agent debt webinar PDF, Apr 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/no-number/presentations-and-webinar-pdfs/tax-agent-webinar-on-debt-april-2026.pdf?modified=20260420234851) IRD had also announced before the webinar that it would cover automated bank deductions, instalment arrangement offers, phone calls, visits, credit reporting, liquidation, and prosecution activity. [IRD live debt webinar notice, Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/updates/news-folder/2026/live-debt-webinar-for-tax-agents)

The limit is material: public Companies Office monthly and annual statistics count appointments, but the simple public CSVs do not identify the petitioning creditor or tell whether an appointment followed an IRD statutory demand, another creditor's application, a voluntary shareholder decision, or a secured-creditor step. [Companies Office monthly CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/monthly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv); [Companies Office annual CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/yearly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv) Therefore the evidence supports "IRD enforcement is a major evidenced contributor", not "IRD explains the whole rise." [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405)

**Confidence: High** that IRD enforcement is directly contributing to appointments; **Medium** on its share of the total because the public register series used here does not attribute each appointment to an initiating creditor.

### Macro pressure is real, but it explains vulnerability more than immediate appointment counts

RBNZ's February 2026 Monetary Policy Statement says the economy was at an early stage of recovery, households remained cautious in spending, unemployment remained elevated, the output gap was estimated at **-1.5%** of potential GDP in December 2025, and consumer spending had been constrained by low employment-income growth and falling real house prices. [RBNZ Monetary Policy Statement, Feb 2026](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/monetary-policy/monetary-policy-statement/monetary-policy-statement-filtered-listing-page/2026/feb-182/monetary-policy-statement-february-2026/web-version) The same statement says the OCR had been reduced from **5.50%** in mid-2024 to **2.25%** in November 2025, which indicates a shift from restrictive to more supportive settings but also a lagged path for relief. [RBNZ Monetary Policy Statement, Feb 2026](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/monetary-policy/monetary-policy-statement/monetary-policy-statement-filtered-listing-page/2026/feb-182/monetary-policy-statement-february-2026/web-version)

RBNZ's May 2026 Financial Stability Report says the economy had been showing signs of recovery before the Middle East conflict, financial pressures on households and businesses were easing as interest rates fell, but a slower recovery could make it harder for some households and businesses to meet loan repayments. [RBNZ Financial Stability Report, May 2026](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/financial-stability/financial-stability-report/financial-stability-reports/2026/may/financial-stability-report-may-2026) In its SME lending section, RBNZ says SMEs account for the vast majority of firms, are mainly dependent on bank lending for operating and growth capital, and that bank SME lending growth slowed after the pandemic in the period of higher interest rates. [RBNZ FSR online report, SME lending section](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/financial-stability/financial-stability-report/financial-stability-reports/2026/may/financial-stability-report-may-2026/web-version)

RBNZ is also specific about sectors that overlap with practitioner insolvency reports: it says many SMEs, particularly in hospitality and construction, have experienced margin pressures and run down cash buffers since 2020, and that credit quality of SME lending deteriorated as economic conditions worsened over the previous three years, while remaining better than during the Global Financial Crisis. [RBNZ FSR online report, SME lending section](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/financial-stability/financial-stability-report/financial-stability-reports/2026/may/financial-stability-report-may-2026/web-version)

Stats NZ data complicates any simple "current demand is collapsing" claim. Official GDP data for March 2026 shows production GDP rose **0.8%** from the previous quarter and **1.5%** from the same quarter a year earlier. [Stats NZ GDP CSV, Mar 2026](https://www.stats.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/Gross-domestic-product/Gross-domestic-product-March-2026-quarter/Download-data/gross-domestic-product-march-2026-quarter.csv) Stats NZ business financial data says all-BFD-industry sales were **$200b** in the March 2026 quarter, up **5.3%** from March 2025, and operating profit was **$29b**, up **11%** from March 2025. [Stats NZ Business Financial Data, Mar 2026](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/business-financial-data-march-2026-quarter/)

The macro reading is therefore lagged and distributional: high rates, weak demand, and cost pressure plausibly weakened balance sheets before 2026, but national March 2026 aggregates were recovering and do not prove that current insolvency appointments are being caused by current-quarter sales falls. [RBNZ Monetary Policy Statement, Feb 2026](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/monetary-policy/monetary-policy-statement/monetary-policy-statement-filtered-listing-page/2026/feb-182/monetary-policy-statement-february-2026/web-version); [Stats NZ Business Financial Data, Mar 2026](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/business-financial-data-march-2026-quarter/)

**Confidence: Medium** - official macro and credit sources support vulnerability, but do not causally allocate formal insolvency appointments.

### Practitioner reports are useful, but should be treated as secondary unless reproduced

Deloitte's Q1 2026 insolvency report says formal company insolvency activity remained high in Q1 2026, with **709** formal insolvencies in the quarter and liquidations making up **94.4%** of them. [Deloitte NZ Quarterly Insolvency Trends Q1 2026](https://www.deloitte.com/content/dam/assets-zone1/nz/en/docs/services/consulting/2026/2026-q1-new-zealand-insolvency-trends.pdf) Deloitte also says IRD enforcement is one of the main drivers of formal appointments, describing a shift from a support-first model to firmer enforcement through automation, third-party tools, credit reporting, statutory demands, and liquidation proceedings. [Deloitte NZ Quarterly Insolvency Trends Q1 2026](https://www.deloitte.com/content/dam/assets-zone1/nz/en/docs/services/consulting/2026/2026-q1-new-zealand-insolvency-trends.pdf)

Deloitte's analysis is directionally consistent with the official IRD sources: IRD itself reports increased bank-deduction notices, statutory demands, credit-reporting notifications, and company liquidations in 2025/26. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405); [IRD tax-agent debt webinar PDF, Apr 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/no-number/presentations-and-webinar-pdfs/tax-agent-webinar-on-debt-april-2026.pdf?modified=20260420234851) However, Deloitte's "zombie companies" explanation and Official Assignee panel trend should not be treated as primary evidence until reproduced from official appointment records, because the public Companies Office CSVs cited here do not expose that exact panel/petitioner cut. [Companies Office monthly CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/monthly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv); [Deloitte NZ Quarterly Insolvency Trends Q1 2026](https://www.deloitte.com/content/dam/assets-zone1/nz/en/docs/services/consulting/2026/2026-q1-new-zealand-insolvency-trends.pdf)

**Confidence: Medium** for Deloitte as secondary synthesis; **Low-Medium** for any claim that depends solely on its unreproduced Official Assignee/IRD panel series.

## What would change this conclusion

- **A public appointment-level or aggregate petitioning-creditor series would sharpen or overturn the IRD conclusion.** If official Companies Office, court, NZ Gazette, or IRD data showed IRD-initiated liquidations were only a small share of the rise after normalising for all appointments, then this finding should downgrade IRD from "strongest evidenced near-term driver" to "one visible contributor." [Companies Office monthly CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/monthly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv); [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405)
- **A sector-normalised bulk-data analysis could reweight macro explanations.** If Companies Office/NZBN/BIC/address joins show the increase is concentrated in sectors with the largest demand shock, financing spread, or cost-pressure exposure after normalising against enterprise counts, macro explanations would become stronger than this finding can currently support. [Companies Office bulk data guide](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/data-services/ways-to-get-our-data/bulk-data-help-guide/); [RBNZ FSR online report, SME lending section](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/financial-stability/financial-stability-report/financial-stability-reports/2026/may/financial-stability-report-may-2026/web-version)
- **IRD annual-report finalisation could change the 2025/26 enforcement numbers.** IRD's overdue-debt report says its data is subject to audit and is not final until sign-off and publication of IRD's annual report, so the statutory-demand, liquidation, and write-off counts should be checked against the final 2026 annual report when it is published. [IRD overdue debt report, Jan-Mar 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/about-us/publications/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt/managing-overdue-tax-debt---january-to-march-2026.pdf?modified=20260603234405)
- **What I could not verify:** I could not verify a public monthly series of IRD-initiated company liquidation applications, a public court/Gazette aggregate that separates IRD petitions from other creditors, or an official public decomposition of appointments by cause. [Companies Office monthly CSV](https://www.companiesoffice.govt.nz/assets/companies-office/company-statistics/monthly-company-statistics-since-2001.csv); [IRD tax-agent debt webinar PDF, Apr 2026](https://www.ird.govt.nz/-/media/project/ir/home/documents/no-number/presentations-and-webinar-pdfs/tax-agent-webinar-on-debt-april-2026.pdf?modified=20260420234851)
- **Human/domain judgement still matters.** Whether IRD's enforcement is best framed as overdue clean-up, fair-market enforcement, avoidable business failure, or a support-gap signal requires practitioner and small-business evidence that is not available from public aggregate data alone. [IRD struggling to file and pay tax](https://www.ird.govt.nz/situations/unable-to-pay-my-tax-debt); [Deloitte NZ Quarterly Insolvency Trends Q1 2026](https://www.deloitte.com/content/dam/assets-zone1/nz/en/docs/services/consulting/2026/2026-q1-new-zealand-insolvency-trends.pdf)
