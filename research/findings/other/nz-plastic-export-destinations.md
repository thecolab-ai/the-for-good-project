# NZ plastic-waste exports still go mainly to Southeast Asia, but public data reports declared destinations better than final treatment

## Executive answer

- New Zealand's public HS 3915 plastic-waste export total was **21,019 tonnes in 2022**, **28,377 tonnes in 2023**, **27,275 tonnes in 2024**, and **27,048 tonnes in 2025** when calculated from Stats NZ annual HS10-by-country export files using `total_export_qty`; OWID's UN Comtrade-derived grapher independently matches the 2022-2024 totals exactly after unit conversion [Stats NZ OMT datasets](https://www.stats.govt.nz/large-datasets/csv-files-for-download/overseas-merchandise-trade-datasets/) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20260520233455/https://www.stats.govt.nz/large-datasets/csv-files-for-download/overseas-merchandise-trade-datasets/)); [OWID plastic waste trade CSV](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/plastic-waste-trade.csv).
- The 2022 picture in MfE's aide-memoire still broadly holds, but the order shifted: **Malaysia and Indonesia remained the dominant HS 3915 destinations**, with Malaysia first in 2023 and 2024 and Indonesia first in 2025; Australia and the United States became more visible by 2025 [Stats NZ OMT datasets](https://www.stats.govt.nz/large-datasets/csv-files-for-download/overseas-merchandise-trade-datasets/); [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf) ([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20251112020245/https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf)).
- HS 3915 is not the full story: UNSD defines HS 3915 as waste, parings and scrap of plastics, while MfE says "plastic waste" made up about half of exported "plastic raw materials" in a 2021 analysis and separately reported shredded/flaked plastic exports rising from **340 tonnes in 2020** to **1,640 tonnes in 2022** under a different categorisation [UNSD HS 3915 classification](https://unstats.un.org/unsd/classifications/Econ/Detail/EN/32/3915); [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).
- The strongest public finding is a tracking gap: public data report declared destination countries and some permit/legal controls, but MfE says Customs self-declaration is the only tracking mechanism, there is **no ability to track waste once it has left New Zealand**, and officials had no official information about processing after arrival beyond anecdotal exporter due-diligence claims [Stats NZ OMT datasets](https://www.stats.govt.nz/large-datasets/csv-files-for-download/overseas-merchandise-trade-datasets/); [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).
- Public destination-country controls exist, but they are control evidence rather than consignment-level treatment evidence: Australia requires licences and single-polymer, cleaned, remanufacture-ready regulated waste plastic; Malaysia announced SIRIM Certificate of Approval requirements for waste plastic from 1 July 2025; Indonesia's public policy summaries describe import permits, pre-shipment inspection, importer facility requirements and homogeneity/non-contamination conditions [Australian DCCEEW waste-plastic export guidance](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/environment/protection/waste/exports/plastic); [Malaysia MITI announcement, 1 Jul 2025](https://www.miti.gov.my/index.php/announcements/view/906); [SWITCH-Asia Indonesia plastic policies, 2024](https://www.switch-asia.eu/site/assets/files/4409/plastic_policies_id.pdf).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — HS 3915 destination volumes are high confidence from Stats NZ and OWID/UN Comtrade, but the actual treatment of NZ material after arrival is low confidence because public official sources do not trace consignments through processors.

## Evidence

### Scope and data method

This finding answers issue #476 by updating the destination pattern for **HS 3915 "waste, parings and scrap, of plastics"**, then separating that from wider "plastic raw materials" such as shredded/flaked plastics that MfE says sit outside the plastic-waste categorisation used in its Customs extract [UNSD HS 3915 classification](https://unstats.un.org/unsd/classifications/Econ/Detail/EN/32/3915); [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

Stats NZ's overseas merchandise trade dataset page says the HS10-by-country export datasets contain export FOB values, export quantities, re-export values and quantities, total export values and quantities, and that exports use **country of destination**; the same page says data are provisional for three months and all files are updated after the annual confidentiality review [Stats NZ OMT datasets](https://www.stats.govt.nz/large-datasets/csv-files-for-download/overseas-merchandise-trade-datasets/).

I downloaded the official annual Stats NZ export-by-country ZIP files for 2022, 2023, 2024 and 2025, filtered rows whose HS10 code begins `3915`, and summed `total_export_qty`; those files list the HS 3915 unit as kilograms, so the table below divides by 1,000 to show tonnes [2022 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2022_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip); [2023 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2023_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip); [2024 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2024_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip); [2025 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2025_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip).

OWID's plastic-waste trade grapher defines the series as HS 3915 plastic-waste exports in tonnes, and its New Zealand rows report **21,018.926 tonnes in 2022**, **28,376.852 tonnes in 2023**, and **27,274.938 tonnes in 2024**, matching the Stats NZ extraction to rounding [OWID plastic waste trade CSV](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/plastic-waste-trade.csv); [OWID plastic waste trade grapher](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/plastic-waste-trade?metric=exports&rate=total).

MfE's 2023 aide-memoire reported **21,000 tonnes** of plastic-waste exports in 2022, Malaysia and Indonesia as the largest markets, and polyethylene as about **70%** of plastic-waste exports; the Stats NZ extraction gives 21,019 tonnes for HS 3915 in 2022, Malaysia plus Indonesia as the top two destinations, and ethylene-polymer waste as 68.9% [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf); [2022 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2022_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip).

The most important update since MfE's 2022 destination picture is that annual HS 3915 tonnage rose above the 2022 level in 2023-2025, while China fell from 1,803 tonnes in 2022 to 137 tonnes in 2025 and Australia rose from 1,309 tonnes in 2022 to 3,471 tonnes in 2025 in the Stats NZ extraction [2022 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2022_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip); [2025 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2025_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip).

### HS 3915 is not all exported plastic raw material

MfE explicitly warns that "`plastic waste` does not capture all plastic exported from New Zealand for recycling or remanufacture" and says a 2021 analysis found plastic waste made up half of all exported "plastic raw materials" [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

MfE also says some exported plastic raw materials are recycled commodities with basic processing such as shredded or flaked plastic, and that shredded/flaked plastic exports rose from **340 tonnes in 2020** to **1,640 tonnes in 2022** after China's National Sword policy [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

Appendix 3 of MfE's aide-memoire says the Customs plastic-waste extract does **not** include export consignments of flaked/shredded plastic because that material has a different categorisation, so the HS 3915 destination table above should not be read as a full account of all plastic exported for recycling or remanufacture [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

### Verification limits and actual treatment evidence

The most load-bearing official statement is MfE's: the only mechanism to track plastic-waste exports is Customs self-declaration, exporters are not required to submit export entries for consignments valued at $1,000 or less, and there is no ability to track waste after it leaves New Zealand [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

MfE's same memo says Customs data differ from public Stats NZ/Infoshare data because Customs includes all plastic-waste exports for which an export entry has been lodged, excludes exports where no entry has been lodged, has corrected some detected destination anomalies, and excludes flaked/shredded plastic; Stats NZ also does not report exports valued at $1,000 or less [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

Exporter due-diligence claims exist but are not official treatment verification: MfE says some recycling facilities reported no longer sending low-value mixed-grade plastics, using optical sorting or hand-picking, complying with importing-country checks such as Malaysian photo requirements, and visiting or auditing facilities, but MfE also says that beyond anecdotal information from individual exporters it had no official information about processing after arrival [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

The sector sample behind those due-diligence claims is partial: MfE says the recyclers it spoke to were four of twenty operators recorded as having exported plastic waste in 2022, were all material recovery facility operators, and likely represented less than half of plastic-waste exports because non-kerbside plastics such as PE films were likely a large share [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

MfE records general destination-risk evidence, including poor compliance and enforcement, limited resources, and poor waste-management infrastructure in some developing-country contexts, but the public record I found does not tie those general risks to audited treatment outcomes for specific NZ HS 3915 consignments [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).

### Controls at NZ and destination countries

New Zealand's EPA says hazardous plastic waste, most mixed plastic waste, separated plastic waste not listed in Schedule 3 Part 2A, PVC plastic waste even when separated and clean, and contaminated plastic waste need a permit; the EPA also says most separated plastic waste does not need a permit if it is almost exclusively one listed plastic type, destined for environmentally sound recycling, and almost free from contamination and other waste [EPA plastic waste import/export guidance](https://www.epa.govt.nz/hazardous-substances/certificates-permits-and-permissions/hazardous-waste-shipping/plastic-waste-import-and-export/).

MfE's Basel Convention page similarly says listed separated plastic waste can be imported or exported without a permit if almost free from contamination and destined for environmentally sound recycling, while importers and exporters must also follow importing-country requirements [MfE Basel Convention page](https://environment.govt.nz/what-government-is-doing/international-action/basel-convention/).

Australia, which received 12.8% of NZ HS 3915 tonnage in 2025, requires a waste export licence for regulated waste plastic and says qualifying plastic must be sorted into a single polymer, cleaned to remove contamination, and processed into a form ready for remanufacture; Australian Customs Notice 2022/27 says phase two from 1 July 2022 barred unprocessed, baled waste plastic from meeting the Plastic Rules [Australian DCCEEW waste-plastic export guidance](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/environment/protection/waste/exports/plastic); [Australian Customs Notice 2022/27](https://www.abf.gov.au/help-and-support-subsite/CustomsNotices/2022-27.pdf).

Malaysia, NZ's largest 2023 and 2024 HS 3915 destination, announced that SIRIM would issue a Certificate of Approval for waste plastic effective 1 July 2025, but the public MITI announcement page I could access provides the headline and date rather than consignment-level treatment results [Malaysia MITI announcement, 1 Jul 2025](https://www.miti.gov.my/index.php/announcements/view/906).

Indonesia, NZ's largest 2025 HS 3915 destination, is described in a SWITCH-Asia policy review as requiring non-hazardous waste importers to use imported plastic waste directly, have operational facilities, obtain pre-shipment inspection by authorised surveyors, obtain relevant ministry recommendations before import permits, and import only plastic scrap that is not from landfill, not mixed with other waste, not contaminated with hazardous material or waste, and homogeneous [SWITCH-Asia Indonesia plastic policies, 2024](https://www.switch-asia.eu/site/assets/files/4409/plastic_policies_id.pdf).

I did not find a primary Indonesian government English-language source sufficient to state a simple current plastic-scrap import ban for this finding; the continuing 2025 NZ export entries to Indonesia in Stats NZ data also show that any current restriction needs careful legal interpretation before being described as a simple ban [2025 Stats NZ exports HS10 by country ZIP](https://www3.stats.govt.nz/HS10_by_Country/2025_Exports_HS10_by_Country.zip).

## What would change this conclusion

- A current Customs or Stats NZ extract that separately identifies **flaked/shredded plastic raw-material exports by destination** would change the destination picture, because the HS 3915 table excludes a category MfE says reached 1,640 tonnes in 2022 [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).
- Audited processor-level records, Basel movement documents, destination-country inspection outcomes, or importer recycling-yield data for NZ-origin consignments would change the treatment conclusion, because current public evidence reports declared destinations and controls but not actual destination after transhipment risk or final processing [Stats NZ OMT datasets](https://www.stats.govt.nz/large-datasets/csv-files-for-download/overseas-merchandise-trade-datasets/); [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).
- A primary Indonesian government source in English or a translated legal instrument confirming the 2025 plastic-scrap import setting would change the Indonesia-controls section; the public source cited here is a 2024 policy summary, not a primary source strong enough to state a simple current ban [SWITCH-Asia Indonesia plastic policies, 2024](https://www.switch-asia.eu/site/assets/files/4409/plastic_policies_id.pdf).
- I could not verify the actual treatment shares for NZ-origin plastic waste in Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, China, Taiwan, Australia, Bangladesh, the United States, or Viet Nam from public sources, and MfE's 2023 aide-memoire says officials did not have official information about processing in receiving countries [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).
- A human with waste-export regulatory authority or direct industry audit access would be needed to test exporter due-diligence claims against processor-level records; public sources do not provide enough evidence to confirm whether material was recycled, stockpiled, dumped, burned, used as fuel, or re-exported after arrival [MfE BRF-3444, 2023](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/OIA/Files/Doc3-BRF-3444_Aide_Memoire-Information_on_plastic_waste_exports_out_of_New_Zealand-for_release.pdf).
