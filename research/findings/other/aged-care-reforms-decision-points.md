# Current NZ aged care reform actors and live decision points before the stream reaches G1

## Executive answer

- The core live reform axis is the **Aged Care Ministerial Advisory Group (ACMAG)**: it started in January 2026, is expected to report by mid-2026, and ministers then decide whether and how to implement its recommendations [MoH ACMAG](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/health-system-roles-and-organisations/health-system-and-boards?search=aged%20care%20ministerial%20advisory%20group) (official page currently at https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/health-system-roles-and-organisations/health-committees-and-boards/aged-care-ministerial-advisory-group).
- The Ministry says a parallel reform track is already running: Health NZ’s aged care funding and service-model review started in July 2023, completed phase one in Dec 2023, and moved to phase two in Jan 2024, with outputs explicitly described as informing broader policy advice rather than as final government decisions yet [Health NZ review page](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/what-we-do/programmes-and-initiatives/aged-care-funding-and-services-model-review).
- The Parliamentary Health Committee inquiry on aged care capacity has passed public evidence-gathering: submissions closed 19 Aug 2024 and the final report contains 14 recommendations and has been tabled to the House [Committee inquiry page](https://www3.parliament.nz/mi/pb/sc/make-a-submission/document/54SCHEA_SCF_68DED697-E85E-4888-DE1A-08DC7A1062A5/inquiry-into-the-aged-care-sectors-current-and-future) and [report press page](https://www3.parliament.nz/en/pb/sc/committees-press-releases/health-committee-reports-findings-of-aged-care-inquiry-to-the-house/).
- The Aged Care Commissioner’s 2024 report is still an external watchdog product (20 recommendations) and sits as an upstream quality/safety signal to sector and government, not a legally binding decision body [HDC media release](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media-resources/news/aged-care-commissioner-carolyn-cooper-calls-for-action-to-improve-access-to-health-and-disability-care-for-older-people/) and [Aged care report PDF](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media/uypbxcbp/media-release-aged-care-report-march-2024.pdf).
- A new operational funding decision is already made for 2026/27: the Beehive release on 17 June 2026 announced a 4% funded uplift (~$79m) for aged residential care, including weekend admissions requirements and base/incentive splits [Beehive dated 17 Jun 2026](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/funding-boost-aged-residential-care-sector).
- Pay-and-workforce settings are partially in policy and partially in workforce delivery: there is a published pay-disparity funding settlement for nurses and kaiāwhina (ongoing additional funding with eligibility rules), while immigration/work-pathway settings are active with a 24-month care-workforce work-stream rule and sector wage thresholds [Health NZ pay disparities (Wayback)](https://web.archive.org/web/20260705200702/https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/health-professionals/employment-information-agreements/pay-equity-framework/pay-disparities), and INZ care-workforce/work-to-residence pathway details plus 2026 wage rule references [INZ pathway page](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/live/resident-visas-to-live-in-new-zealand/skilled-residence-pathways-in-new-zealand/care-workforce-and-transport-sector-pathway-to-residence/) and [Amendment Circular 2026-01](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/assets/inz/documents/amendment-circulars/Amendment-Circular-2026-01.pdf).
- A hard user-charge governance point is already in place: Health’s maximum contribution rates apply from 1 July 2026, directly constraining resident costs in ARC [Health max contribution notice](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/maximum-contribution-applying-in-each-region-from-1-july-2026).

**Overall confidence:** High — multiple official NZ sources align on key timeline and decision points; low-confidence areas are downstream implementation dates and political execution details after recommendations are submitted.

## Evidence

### 1) Aged Care Ministerial Advisory Group (ACMAG): active policy architecture and decision hand-off

- The ACMAG was established to provide independent advice and began work Jan 2026. It says it is expected to report back by the middle of 2026, and its scope is to advise on aged care funding, cost-sharing, and contracting/regulatory settings [MoH ACMAG page](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/health-system-roles-and-organisations/health-committees-and-boards/aged-care-ministerial-advisory-group).
- The same page states that once ACMAG completes its final report, ministers will consider recommendations and may decide on changes to aged care settings, with sector engagement expected where recommendations progress [MoH ACMAG page](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/health-system-roles-and-organisations/health-committees-and-boards/aged-care-ministerial-advisory-group).
- The Government’s 2025 announcement of the integrated approach says this advisory body was tasked to identify changes, with recommendations expected by mid-2026 and implementation of funding-model changes anticipated in 2027 [Beehive, Building fit-for-purpose Aged Care (28 Oct 2025)](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/building-fit-purpose-aged-care-0).
- ACMAG explicitly says its work is informed by the Health NZ review and the Parliamentary Health Committee aged care inquiry [MoH ACMAG page](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/health-system-roles-and-organisations/health-committees-and-boards/aged-care-ministerial-advisory-group).

### 2) Health NZ aged care funding-and-service-model review: upstream evidence stream, not final output

- Health NZ states this review began July 2023; phase 1 ran in the second half of 2023 and was completed by Dec 2023; phase 2 started Jan 2024 with modelling and engagement on funding/service options [Health NZ review page](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/what-we-do/programmes-and-initiatives/aged-care-funding-and-services-model-review).
- The review page states its reports are analysis/options and “help inform advice currently being developed for the Government,” not itself the final policy instruction set [Health NZ review page](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/about-us/what-we-do/programmes-and-initiatives/aged-care-funding-and-services-model-review).
- The terms-of-reference PDF on the Health ministry page says the same review began July 2023, confirming that challenge points include funding model insufficiency and uneven access/ability to sustain services [MoH PDF via search results (TOR)](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-12/aged-care-ministerial-advisory-group-terms-of-reference-december-2025.pdf).

### 3) Parliamentary committee input: recommendations exist; implementation stage is external to the inquiry process itself

- The Health Committee inquiry page shows submissions were called and closed (11:59pm, 19 Aug 2024), meaning the evidentiary phase is complete for that process [Health Committee inquiry page](https://www3.parliament.nz/mi/pb/sc/make-a-submission/document/54SCHEA_SCF_68DED697-E85E-4888-DE1A-08DC7A1062A5/inquiry-into-the-aged-care-sectors-current-and-future).
- The House-press release on findings states the committee report contains 14 recommendations across care access, pricing transparency, dementia/neurological care capacity, and workforce sustainability [Committee report release](https://www3.parliament.nz/en/pb/sc/committees-press-releases/health-committee-reports-findings-of-aged-care-inquiry-to-the-house/).
- ACMAG documentation cites this same inquiry as part of its evidence base [MoH ACMAG page](https://www.health.govt.nz/about-us/new-zealands-health-system/health-system-roles-and-organisations/health-committees-and-boards/aged-care-ministerial-advisory-group).

### 4) HDC/Aged Care Commissioner: watchdog recommendations and where they sit

- The Aged Care Commissioner’s public statement says the March 2024 report makes 20 recommendations on quality/access/coordination and workforce support [HDC media page](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media-resources/news/aged-care-commissioner-carolyn-cooper-calls-for-action-to-improve-access-to-health-and-disability-care-for-older-people/).
- The same is reflected in the HDC report PDF (“The report makes 20 recommendations…”) [HDC report PDF](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media/uypbxcbp/media-release-aged-care-report-march-2024.pdf).
- On the HDC page, these are recommendations for sector improvement; they are not direct ministerial or Cabinet directives by themselves, and therefore should be treated as upstream pressure points [HDC media page](https://www.hdc.org.nz/media-resources/news/aged-care-commissioner-carolyn-cooper-calls-for-action-to-improve-access-to-health-and-disability-care-for-older-people/).

### 5) Current concrete fiscal settings: 2026/27 uplift and charge governance

- Beehive’s 17 June 2026 release states Health NZ will provide a 4% funding uplift (~$79m) to ARC in 2026/27, with a 2.66% base uplift and targeted service funding [Beehive release](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/funding-boost-aged-residential-care-sector).
- The same release says providers are expected to support clinical admissions over weekends and improve transitions from hospital to aged residential care [Beehive release](https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/funding-boost-aged-residential-care-sector).
- The Ministry of Health notice makes 1 July 2026 the effective date for applying maximum contribution rates for long-term ARC residents, which operationally changes what residents pay regionally [Max contribution notice](https://www.health.govt.nz/publications/maximum-contribution-applying-in-each-region-from-1-july-2026).

### 6) Pay-equity and immigration settings linked to workforce stability

- Health NZ’s pay-disparities material (archived 5 Jul 2026) says “Government funding has been set aside to reduce pay gaps” and gives phase funding (NZ$40m in 2022/23 then NZ$200m p.a. from 2023/24), including priority for aged residential care providers [Health NZ pay disparities (archived)](https://web.archive.org/web/20260705200702/https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/health-professionals/employment-information-agreements/pay-equity-framework/pay-disparities).
- That same archived page also states eligible workers are nurses/kaiāwhina in funded sectors, and that setting pay rates is an employer responsibility under bargaining [Health NZ pay disparities (archived)](https://web.archive.org/web/20260705200702/https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/health-professionals/employment-information-agreements/pay-equity-framework/pay-disparities).
- INZ’s care/workforce residence pathway page sets out work-experience/rule structure for this pathway: a care workforce route with a 24-month work requirement in approved roles, and qualifications/registration/wage criteria to apply [INZ care-workforce pathway](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/live/resident-visas-to-live-in-new-zealand/skilled-residence-pathways-in-new-zealand/care-workforce-and-transport-sector-pathway-to-residence/).
- The 2026 amendment circular sets relevant wage-rule timeline in circulation: for relevant pathways, the median wage value moved to $35 from 9 March 2026 [INZ Amendment Circular 2026-01 PDF](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/assets/inz/documents/amendment-circulars/Amendment-Circular-2026-01.pdf).
- Employment NZ also records a contemporaneous minimum wage rate reset (effective 1 Apr 2026) for baseline labour-cost context [Employment NZ minimum wage](https://www.employment.govt.nz/pay-and-hours/pay-and-wages/minimum-wage/minimum-wage-rates-and-types).

## What would change this conclusion

- If the ACMAG final report is published and ministers release a confirmed Cabinet paper with different timing/decision sequencing than “mid-2026 report, post-report ministerial decision,” the governance timeline above would change.
- If the Ministry of Health releases a later version of the aged care review that declares final decisions (as opposed to advice-only outputs), the review’s status in this map would need to shift from advisory to executable reform.
- If the Health Committee reports that recommendations were accepted, rejected, or substantially modified with exact Cabinet response dates, the current “implementation pending” framing should be narrowed.
