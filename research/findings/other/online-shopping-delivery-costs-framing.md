# The creeping cost of online shopping in NZ

## Executive answer

- NZ standard delivery fees rose from an average of $9.70 to $10 per parcel in one year; express delivery rose from $14.10 to $17.42 (a 23.5% increase) [Shippit, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).
- While most NZ retailers still advertise some form of free shipping, the minimum spend thresholds to qualify have been rising quietly — shoppers need to spend more to get it [RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).
- Only 10% of NZ retailers offer free returns, yet 50% of shoppers say they would be hesitant to buy from a retailer without free returns [Shippit, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).
- Restocking fees are common for change-of-mind returns: PB Tech charges 20%, Target Furniture 15%, Fishpond 17%, and at least one retailer charges shipping plus a $60 handling fee [Consumer NZ, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).
- Two-thirds of NZ consumers are unlikely to return to a retailer after a bad delivery experience [Shippit, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).
- The Consumer Guarantees Act does not cover "change of mind" returns — retailers set their own terms, creating a wide variance in what shoppers can expect [Consumer Protection, "Change of mind"](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/general-help/common-consumer-issues/change-of-mind) [Commerce Commission, "Buying online"](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/consumers/dealing-with-typical-situations/buying-goods-and-services/buying-online/).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the headline figures come from Shippit's research (a commercial delivery-software firm) as reported by RNZ; the raw Shippit report has not been independently reviewed here. The CGA framework is well-established law. Retailer-specific fees need individual verification against current store pages.

## Evidence

### Delivery cost trends

Research from Shippit, a delivery software firm, shows NZ standard delivery fees increased from an average $9.70 to $10 per shipment over a 12-month period, and express delivery costs rose from $14.10 to $17.42 — RNZ describes this as "increased by $3"; the precise arithmetic difference is $3.32, or approximately 23.5% [Shippit State of Shipping report, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees) [Shippit 2026 State of Shipping Report](https://www.shippit.com/state-of-shipping-report). The Shippit 2026 report landing page requires a download/login for the full report — the specific NZ figures are from RNZ's reporting.

### Free-shipping thresholds rising

Most NZ retailers offered some form of free shipping, but the amount shoppers had to spend to qualify had increased [RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees). The Australian example cited was Woolworths raising its "delivery saver" minimum order from $80 to $100, and raising the subscription price from $118 to $132.99 per six months [RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees). NZ-specific threshold changes were not quantified in the report — this needs dedicated research.

Retail consultant Chris Wilkinson (First Retail Group) noted free delivery is a "big driver of sales online" and that retailers use free-shipping offers strategically to clear overstock or drive cashflow at certain periods [RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees). Some NZ retailers encourage shoppers to join clubs/memberships where free-shipping offers are a feature [RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).

### Returns fees and restocking charges

Only 10% of NZ retailers offered free returns, even though half of shoppers said they would be hesitant to shop with a retailer that does not offer free returns [Shippit, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).

Specific restocking fees cited by Consumer NZ:
- PB Tech: 20% restocking fee for change-of-mind returns
- Target Furniture: 15% restocking fee
- Fishpond: 17% restocking fee
- One unnamed retailer: shipping costs plus a $60 handling/processing fee

[Consumer NZ, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees). Of these, only the PB Tech figure has been checked against a current store page: PB Tech's own returns policy confirms a 20% restocking fee for opened change-of-mind returns (see the surprising-claims table below). Target Furniture, Fishpond, and the $60-handling-fee retailer have not been independently verified against current store pages.

Massey University marketing expert Bodo Lang said free returns are "a very useful tactic to encourage shoppers to buy items that otherwise they might not buy online" because they remove the perceived risk of buying the wrong item [RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees).

### The CGA does not cover "change of mind"

Under the Consumer Guarantees Act 1993, sellers must honour guarantees for faulty goods, goods not matching their description, or goods not of acceptable quality. However, "change of mind" returns are not covered by the CGA: "Usually when you buy a product or service, the sale is final unless the item breaches a guarantee under the Consumer Guarantees Act," and "some stores have their own in-store policy to offer a refund, exchange or credit note for 'change-of-mind' purchases" [Consumer Protection, "Change of mind"](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/general-help/common-consumer-issues/change-of-mind). Because the CGA does not impose a statutory right to return non-faulty goods, the wide variance in restocking fees and return conditions described above operates within the legal framework, though the Fair Trading Act may still apply to misleading disclosures.

Consumer rights are the same whether a purchase is made online or in-store — "Your consumer rights are the same whether you buy online or in a store" — but online shopping carries one specific extra risk the Commerce Commission flags: "you can not inspect products before buying so it can be hard to judge the quality and authenticity of a product" [Commerce Commission, "Buying online"](https://www.comcom.govt.nz/consumers/dealing-with-typical-situations/buying-goods-and-services/buying-online/). Retailers, not consumers, are responsible for sorting out delivery delays and damage once an order is placed [Consumer Protection, "Delivery issues"](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/general-help/common-consumer-issues/delivery-issues). On return shipping specifically, the legal default (not a survey of retailer practice) is narrower than "many retailers": "If you want to return something that you have bought online, you are responsible for the cost of returning it, unless it's faulty" [Consumer Protection, "Delivery issues"](https://www.consumerprotection.govt.nz/general-help/common-consumer-issues/delivery-issues). That default is what the retailer-specific restocking fees above sit on top of — this finding does not have evidence on how many retailers additionally charge for return shipping beyond that baseline.

### Delivery experience drives loyalty

Two-thirds (approximately 66%) of NZ consumers are unlikely to return to a retailer after a bad delivery experience [Shippit, cited in RNZ, 2 Jul 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/653038/online-shoppers-facing-higher-delivery-costs-returns-fees). This suggests delivery quality — not just cost — is a significant competitive factor.

## What would change this conclusion

- **Access to the full Shippit report** would allow direct verification of sample sizes, methodology, and NZ-specific breakdowns (the RNZ article is a secondary summary).
- **Independent replication of the retailer fee audit** — visiting each named retailer's current returns/delivery pages to confirm the fees cited by Consumer NZ are still in effect.
- **NZ-specific free-shipping threshold data** — the RNZ article mentions rising thresholds but does not quantify NZ changes (only the Australian Woolworths example). A systematic snapshot of 20+ major NZ retailers would establish whether this is a NZ-wide pattern or anecdotal.
- **Rural/remote delivery surcharges** — the RNZ article and Shippit report do not address whether delivery costs vary by NZ region. Research into NZ Post zonal rates and e-commerce surcharges would clarify this gap.
- **E-commerce growth context** — Stats NZ retail sales data would show what proportion of NZ retail is now online, giving context for how many shoppers are affected.
