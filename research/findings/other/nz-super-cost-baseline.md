# NZ Super's reconciled cost baseline is about $26.5b gross and $22.0b net in 2026/27, rising to about $162b gross and $134b net by 2064/65 in the BEFU 2026 NZSF model

## Executive answer

- **Use gross appropriations for Budget cost, and net-of-tax NZS for fiscal-burden comparisons.** Vote Social Development appropriates $26.481340 billion for New Zealand Superannuation in 2026/27, while Treasury's BEFU 2026 NZSF contribution-rate model gives $22.020 billion net of tax for the same fiscal year; Veteran's Pension is a separate $131.683 million appropriation in Vote Social Development and should not be silently merged into the NZS line. [Vote Social Development Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v9/est26-v9-socdev.pdf); [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx)
- **The clean BEFU 2026 current baseline is $26.5b gross, $22.0b net, 5.6% of GDP gross, and 4.6% of GDP net in 2026/27.** Those shares use Treasury's BEFU 2026 nominal GDP forecast of $474.922 billion for the June 2027 year. [Treasury BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/befu26.pdf); [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx)
- **On current recipients, the 2026/27 gross NZS appropriation is about $4,940 per resident and $27,380 per March 2026 NZS recipient; net-of-tax it is about $4,107 per resident and $22,767 per March 2026 NZS recipient.** The denominators are Stats NZ's provisional resident population of 5,361,300 at 31 March 2026 and MSD's March 2026 count of 967,176 NZS recipients, so these are transparent cross-sectional ratios, not payment entitlements. [Stats NZ population indicator](https://www.stats.govt.nz/indicators/population-of-nz/); [MSD March 2026 NZS/VP workbook](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/2026/quarterly-benefit-fact-sheets-nzs-and-vp-tables-march-2026.xlsx); [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx)
- **The BEFU 2026 NZSF model projects NZS at $161.949b gross and $134.325b net in 2064/65, or 8.0% and 6.6% of nominal GDP respectively.** With the model's 1.983 million average NZS recipients and Stats NZ's 2065 median national population projection of 7.287 million, that is about $81,681 gross per modelled recipient, $67,748 net per modelled recipient, $22,223 gross per resident, and $18,433 net per resident, all in nominal 2064/65 dollars. [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx); [Stats NZ 2024-base projections](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/national-population-projections-2024base2078/)
- **Do not mix the OECD pension-expenditure series into the Budget baseline.** OECD Pensions at a Glance 2025 reports New Zealand public pension expenditure at 5.3% of GDP in 2025, 6.5% in 2050, and 7.2% in 2060, but its table note says the New Zealand series comes from the NZSF contribution-rate model at BEFU 2025, not the BEFU 2026 model used for the current Budget baseline. [OECD Pensions at a Glance 2025, Table 8.4](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/11/pensions-at-a-glance-2025_76510fe4/full-report/long-term-projections-of-public-pension-expenditure_af4ed734.html)

**Overall confidence:** High for the reconciled baseline and ratios, because the inputs are official Budget, Treasury, MSD, Stats NZ, and OECD sources. Medium for strict comparability across sources, because Treasury's 2025 LTFM uses BEFU 2025 while the NZSF contribution-rate model has already been updated to BEFU 2026. [Treasury LTFM page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/treasurys-stewardship-reports/long-term-fiscal-position/long-term-fiscal-model); [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026 page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/new-zealand-superannuation-fund-contribution-rate-model-befu-2026)

### Current-denominator ratios

The current recipient denominator should come from MSD's March 2026 NZS/VP workbook rather than BEFU's fiscal-year average forecast when the question is "current recipient count": MSD counted 967,176 NZS recipients, 4,689 Veteran's Pension recipients, and 971,865 NZS/VP recipients in March 2026. [MSD March 2026 NZS/VP workbook](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/2026/quarterly-benefit-fact-sheets-nzs-and-vp-tables-march-2026.xlsx)

Using the Vote/Treasury $26.481340 billion gross NZS appropriation, the Treasury NZSF $22.020 billion net-of-tax NZS figure, Stats NZ's 5,361,300 provisional resident population at 31 March 2026, and MSD's 967,176 March 2026 NZS recipients gives these transparent ratios: $4,939 gross and $4,107 net per resident, and $27,380 gross and $22,767 net per current NZS recipient. [Vote Social Development Estimates 2026/27](https://budget.govt.nz/budget/pdfs/estimates/v9/est26-v9-socdev.pdf); [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx); [Stats NZ population indicator](https://www.stats.govt.nz/indicators/population-of-nz/); [MSD March 2026 NZS/VP workbook](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/2026/quarterly-benefit-fact-sheets-nzs-and-vp-tables-march-2026.xlsx)

For 2064/65, pairing the BEFU 2026 NZSF model's $161.949 billion gross NZS, $134.325 billion net NZS, and 1.9827 million average NZS recipients with Stats NZ's 2065 median projected national population of 7,287,300 gives about $22,223 gross and $18,433 net per resident, and $81,681 gross and $67,748 net per average NZS recipient, all in nominal 2064/65 dollars. [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx); [Stats NZ 2024-base projections](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/national-population-projections-2024base2078/)

### How the sources fit together

Budget 2026 and BEFU 2026 are the right sources for the near-term gross fiscal appropriation: BEFU Table 2.3 says NZS adds $1.8 billion to core Crown expenses between 2025/26 and 2026/27, and BEFU Table 5.2 gives NZS expenses of $24.730 billion in 2025/26, $26.481 billion in 2026/27, and $31.207 billion in 2029/30. [Treasury BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/befu26.pdf)

The 2025 LTFS is the right source for the official sustainability framing, not the most current BEFU 2026 baseline: Treasury says current policies are not sustainable, says the projection is not its prediction, and shows NZS real per-capita spending rising from $2,980 in 2024/25 to $6,412 in 2064/65 in 2009/10 dollars. [Treasury LTFS 2025](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2025-09/ltfs-2025.pdf)

The LTFM should be cited as the model behind the 2025 LTFS, but not treated as a BEFU 2026 update: Treasury says the latest LTFM uses 2025 BEFU fiscal and macroeconomic forecasts, Stats NZ 2024-base population projections, and produces the LTFS bottom-up projection scenario. [Treasury LTFM page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/treasurys-stewardship-reports/long-term-fiscal-position/long-term-fiscal-model)

OECD's long-term public-pension-expenditure table is useful for international comparisons but has a wider and different frame than a Vote Social Development NZS appropriation: OECD says country pension projections differ in programme coverage and may include or exclude resource-tested retiree benefits, and its note identifies the New Zealand source as the BEFU 2025 NZSF contribution-rate model. [OECD Pensions at a Glance 2025, Table 8.4](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/11/pensions-at-a-glance-2025_76510fe4/full-report/long-term-projections-of-public-pension-expenditure_af4ed734.html)

### Practical denominator rules

Use **gross NZS appropriation/expense** when asking how much Budget money is authorised or how NZS appears in the Financial Statements of Government, because Treasury says projected gross NZS tracks are gross of tax and that this is how expenses are recorded in the Financial Statements of Government. [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026 page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/new-zealand-superannuation-fund-contribution-rate-model-befu-2026)

Use **net-of-tax NZS** when asking how much NZS ultimately costs the Crown after tax paid back on NZS, because the NZSF model explicitly gives aggregate NZS expenditure exclusive of tax and uses net after-tax NZS expenditure as a core input for the contribution formula. [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026 page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/new-zealand-superannuation-fund-contribution-rate-model-befu-2026)

Use **per-resident cost** only as a public-scale denominator, not as a tax-incidence claim, because Stats NZ's resident population includes children, retirees, migrants, and non-taxpayers, and the Stats NZ population indicator is an estimated resident population measure rather than a taxpayer count. [Stats NZ population indicator](https://www.stats.govt.nz/indicators/population-of-nz/)

Use **per-recipient cost** only as a programme-average fiscal denominator, not as an individual entitlement, because NZS rates vary by relationship and living situation and because fiscal-year average recipients in the NZSF model do not exactly match MSD's point-in-time March 2026 recipient count. [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx); [MSD March 2026 NZS/VP workbook](https://www.msd.govt.nz/documents/about-msd-and-our-work/publications-resources/statistics/benefit/2026/quarterly-benefit-fact-sheets-nzs-and-vp-tables-march-2026.xlsx)

Use **percentage of GDP** for long-run fiscal sustainability and OECD comparison, because the NZSF model, LTFS, and OECD all express long-run pension cost relative to nominal GDP or GDP. [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx); [Treasury LTFS 2025](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2025-09/ltfs-2025.pdf); [OECD Pensions at a Glance 2025, Table 8.4](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/11/pensions-at-a-glance-2025_76510fe4/full-report/long-term-projections-of-public-pension-expenditure_af4ed734.html)

## What would change this conclusion

- A later Treasury forecast, especially PREFU 2026 or HYEFU 2026, would replace the BEFU 2026 NZSF contribution-rate model as the current baseline. [Treasury forecasts page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/budgets/forecasts)
- A revised Stats NZ population projection would change the 2065 per-resident denominator, but would not change the BEFU 2026 NZSF model's own nominal-dollar or GDP-share path unless Treasury also updated its model inputs. [Stats NZ 2024-base projections](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/national-population-projections-2024base2078/); [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026 page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/new-zealand-superannuation-fund-contribution-rate-model-befu-2026)
- A policy change to NZS eligibility, indexation, taxation, or NZSF contributions would make this a current-policy baseline rather than a reform forecast. [Treasury LTFS 2025](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2025-09/ltfs-2025.pdf)
- I did not independently rebuild Treasury's NZSF contribution-rate formulas; I extracted the published `NZS data` worksheet and checked the headline direction against LTFS and OECD tables. [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2026-05/nzsf-model-befu26.xlsx); [OECD Pensions at a Glance 2025, Table 8.4](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/11/pensions-at-a-glance-2025_76510fe4/full-report/long-term-projections-of-public-pension-expenditure_af4ed734.html)
- I could verify the latest LTFM page and LTFS figures, but I did not need to extract a separate LTFM workbook because the LTFM is a BEFU 2025 LTFS tool and the issue's reconciliation question is better answered by the newer BEFU 2026 NZSF model for the NZS path. [Treasury LTFM page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/treasurys-stewardship-reports/long-term-fiscal-position/long-term-fiscal-model); [Treasury NZSF model BEFU 2026 page](https://www.treasury.govt.nz/publications/new-zealand-superannuation-fund-contribution-rate-model-befu-2026)
