# NZ's freshwater consent-and-plan approach is less entitlement-based than Australia, shorter-transitioning than California, and less nationally prescriptive than the Netherlands

## Executive answer

- **New Zealand gives water and nutrient users conditional consent security, not a basin-wide property entitlement.** Freshwater allocation sits mainly in regional plans and resource consents under national direction, councils have until December 2027 to notify freshwater plans, and many expiring consents are extended only to 31 December 2027 while water-related consents are excluded from extension beyond a 35-year total duration [MfE, NPS-FM](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/); [MfE, Duration of Consents Amendment Act 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/resource-management-duration-of-consents-amendment-act/).
- **Australia's Murray-Darling Basin is the closest comparator for formal allocation security, trading and buy-backs.** The Basin Plan sets sustainable diversion limits for consumptive use, water rights can be privately or publicly owned and traded in regulated markets, and the Australian Government purchases entitlements for environmental recovery [MDBA, sustainable diversion limits](https://www.mdba.gov.au/water-use/water-limits/sustainable-diversion-limits); [DCCEEW, water purchasing](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/water/policy/water-recovery/government-water-purchasing).
- **California's SGMA is a long-transition groundwater correction rather than a consent-renewal system.** Local groundwater sustainability agencies must reach sustainability within 20 years of plan adoption, with critically overdrafted basins due by 2040 and other high- and medium-priority basins by 2042, and local tools include pumping allocations, extraction fees, groundwater trading and fallowing credits [State Water Board, SGMA](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/about_sgma.html); [PPIC, San Joaquin Valley transitions](https://www.ppic.org/publication/managing-water-and-farmland-transitions-in-the-san-joaquin-valley/).
- **The Netherlands is a nutrient/manure limit comparator, not a water-allocation comparator.** Its EU nitrates derogation is being phased down to the normal 170 kg nitrogen per hectare limit by 2026, manure production caps are nationally specified, and livestock buy-out or relocation support sits beside the environmental limit [EUR-Lex, Decision (EU) 2022/2069](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX%3A32022D2069); [USDA FAS, Dutch Loss of Manure Derogation](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dutch+Loss+of+Manure+Derogation_The+Hague_Netherlands_NL2024-0007.pdf); [European Commission, relocation scheme](https://knowledge4policy.ec.europa.eu/news/commission-approves-%E2%82%AC105-million-dutch-state-aid-scheme-reduce-nitrogen-deposition-nature_en).
- **Documented production effects are strongest for Australia and California modelling, weaker for NZ national dairy, and mostly cost/stock-pressure evidence for the Netherlands.** ABARES estimates a 225 GL buy-back scenario would reduce southern MDB irrigated production value by about $111 million/year or 2 percent, PPIC estimates close to half a million acres of San Joaquin Valley irrigated land could leave production even with optimistic new supplies, NZ has not yet published a national dairy production-at-risk estimate, and USDA FAS reports Dutch derogation loss raises manure disposal costs and could affect productivity and farm exit [ABARES, further water recovery](https://www.agriculture.gov.au/abares/research-topics/water/the-impacts-of-further-water-recovery); [PPIC, future agriculture](https://www.ppic.org/publication/policy-brief-the-future-of-agriculture-in-the-san-joaquin-valley/); [analysis for stream #436](../../../analysis/nz-dairy-sector-water-use-and-consent-renewal-how-framing.md); [USDA FAS, Dutch Loss of Manure Derogation](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dutch+Loss+of+Manure+Derogation_The+Hague_Netherlands_NL2024-0007.pdf).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - high for the legal and policy architecture because official sources agree; medium for production effects because Australia and California rely on modelled scenarios, Dutch evidence is recent and partly prospective, and NZ lacks a national public dairy exposure dataset.

### Interpretation for NZ policy transfer

Australia shows that stronger allocation security can be paired with environmental reallocation through markets and buy-backs, but ABARES also shows that water recovery raises allocation prices and reduces irrigated production value in modelled scenarios [ABARES, economic effects](https://www.agriculture.gov.au/abares/products/insights/economic-effects-of-water-recovery-in-murray-darling-basin); [ABARES, further water recovery](https://www.agriculture.gov.au/abares/research-topics/water/the-impacts-of-further-water-recovery).

California shows that a long transition can reduce legal shock but still create large land-use effects when the hydrological deficit is large, because SGMA plans must ultimately reduce overdraft and PPIC expects substantial San Joaquin Valley fallowing under plausible futures [State Water Board, SGMA](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/about_sgma.html); [PPIC, future agriculture](https://www.ppic.org/publication/policy-brief-the-future-of-agriculture-in-the-san-joaquin-valley/).

The Netherlands shows that nationally prescriptive nutrient limits can move faster than farm systems can absorb without support, because the derogation phase-down reaches the normal EU limit by 2026 and USDA FAS reports an immediate manure surplus, higher disposal costs and possible pressure toward business exit [EUR-Lex, Decision (EU) 2022/2069](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX%3A32022D2069); [USDA FAS, Dutch Loss of Manure Derogation](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dutch+Loss+of+Manure+Derogation_The+Hague_Netherlands_NL2024-0007.pdf).

The immediate NZ lesson is not that one overseas model should be copied; it is that NZ's consent-and-plan model currently has weaker public visibility of who bears adjustment costs than Australia's buy-back accounting, California's land-transition modelling, or the Netherlands' nationally specified manure phase-down [data.govt.nz CKAN search](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/api/3/action/package_search?q=water+consent+allocation&rows=5); [ABARES, further water recovery](https://www.agriculture.gov.au/abares/research-topics/water/the-impacts-of-further-water-recovery); [PPIC, future agriculture](https://www.ppic.org/publication/policy-brief-the-future-of-agriculture-in-the-san-joaquin-valley/); [EUR-Lex, Decision (EU) 2022/2069](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX%3A32022D2069).

## What would change this conclusion

- A national NZ dataset joining dairy-related water take, discharge consents, nutrient limits, consent expiry, actual use and milk production would change the conclusion that NZ has weaker visibility of production effects [analysis for stream #436](../../../analysis/nz-dairy-sector-water-use-and-consent-renewal-how-framing.md).
- Final replacement resource-management legislation, a replacement NPS-FM, or new national freshwater-allocation rules could change the comparison because NZ's current transition settings are explicitly tied to RMA replacement and freshwater-plan timing [MfE, Duration of Consents Amendment Act 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/resource-management-duration-of-consents-amendment-act/); [MfE, NPS-FM](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/).
- Observed ex-post evidence from SGMA implementation after major pumping cuts, not just PPIC modelling and early local measures, could strengthen or weaken the California production-effects comparison [PPIC, San Joaquin Valley transitions](https://www.ppic.org/publication/managing-water-and-farmland-transitions-in-the-san-joaquin-valley/).
- Verified Dutch farm-level production, herd-size and exit data after the full 2026 derogation phase-out could change the Netherlands conclusion from "cost and productivity pressure" to a quantified production-loss estimate [USDA FAS, Dutch Loss of Manure Derogation](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dutch+Loss+of+Manure+Derogation_The+Hague_Netherlands_NL2024-0007.pdf); [RIVM, derogation farms](https://www.rivm.nl/en/news/derogation-farms-nitrate-concentrations-in-groundwater-decreased-in-2022-but-levels-are-still).
- I could not verify a single public NZ source that compares consent renewal outcomes across all regional councils, nor a single current public source that prices adjustment support for NZ dairy farmers under tighter freshwater limits.
- A resource-management lawyer, regional-council consent planner, mana whenua freshwater governance expert, dairy-sector adviser and affected farmer representatives should review any policy decision that uses this comparison.

## Open follow-up questions

- Which NZ regional plans or replacement-consent cohorts most resemble Australia's SDL water recovery, California SGMA groundwater balancing, or the Netherlands' nutrient-limit phase-down?
- What would an NZ adjustment-support package cost if it compensated only verified production loss from reduced water or nutrient headroom?
- Could NZ publish a national consent-and-production exposure dashboard without disclosing individual farm or personal data?
