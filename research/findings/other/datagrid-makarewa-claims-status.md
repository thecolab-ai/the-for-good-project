# DataGrid Makarewa claims catalogue: several project facts are confirmed, but the debate mixes consented facts, impact interpretations, and unsupported assertions

## Executive answer

- The strongest confirmed facts are that Environment Southland granted Datagrid NZ Partnership Limited consents for a datacentre near Makarewa, including groundwater take, wetland removal, and coastal marine area cable activities, and that DataGrid states the wider approval package covers Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council. [Environment Southland, 2026](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z) [DataGrid, 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent)
- The public opposition petition is real and closed with 26,027 supporters, but several claims in its text are overbroad or unsupported by the sources checked here, especially projected water-price rises, power-bill rises, and government spending on the build. [Change.org, 2026](https://www.change.org/p/stop-the-building-of-an-ai-centre-in-invercargill)
- The clearest example of a contested statement is the wetland claim: The Spinoff says the project will destroy a nearby wetland, and Environment Southland confirms consent "To remove a wetland", but the significance is contested because the consent record describes the affected wetland as low-value and the claim text does not carry that qualification. [The Spinoff, 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project) [Environment Southland, 2026](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z)
- DataGrid's power and consent claims are better evidenced than its broader climate/economic claims: the 15-year, 140MW Mercury PPOA is confirmed by DataGrid and DCD, while broader low-carbon, PUE, emissions-savings, and jobs-benefit statements still need independent modelling or the underlying economic/technical assessments. [DataGrid Mercury release, 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/mercury) [DCD, 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/)

### Synthesis of the table

The debate is not simply "critics wrong" or "DataGrid wrong"; it is a mix of confirmed base facts, contested interpretations, and a few unsupported assertions. The confirmed base facts are that Environment Southland granted regional resource consents for a Makarewa datacentre and related cable works, including groundwater take, wetland removal, and coastal cable activities; DataGrid separately states the full package includes district/city approvals and a 78,000 sqm, 280MW facility. [Environment Southland, 2026](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z) [DataGrid, 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent)

The petition's strongest claims map to real resource issues: water take, power demand, wetland removal, and cable landing are all in the consent/public-source record. [Environment Southland, 2026](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z) Its weakest claims are causal forecasts not shown by the checked evidence: household water-price rises, household power-bill rises, "destroy wildlife" at broad scale, and government spending "$5.1 billion" to build the project. [Change.org, 2026](https://www.change.org/p/stop-the-building-of-an-ai-centre-in-invercargill)

DataGrid's strongest claims are those tied to a public release and corroborated by trade reporting: the resource-consent announcement, the 280MW/78,000 sqm project description, and the Mercury 140MW/15-year PPOA. [DataGrid, 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent) [DCD, 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/) [DCD, 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/) Its weakest claims are broader benefit claims that require independent assessment: low-carbon operation under marginal-grid conditions, jobs/economic benefit, latency reduction, and emissions savings versus overseas compute. [DataGrid Mercury release, 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/mercury) [The Spinoff, 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project)

## What would change this conclusion

- The full Press article text could materially change the row on DataGrid's "different projects" response by showing which critic claims DataGrid was answering and the exact wording of the company quote.
- The Southland District Council and Invercargill City Council decisions could raise the "full resource consent" claim from partly confirmed to confirmed across all authorities, or reveal scope/condition differences not visible in the Environment Southland summary.
- The Environment Southland decision PDF and technical attachments would sharpen the water, wetland, and coastal-disturbance rows; direct curl to several PDF URLs returned a 118-byte HTML response in this environment, while WebSearch could see snippets from the PDFs.
- A transparent energy model could move the power-bill, low-carbon, and "half a million cars" claims from contested/unverifiable to confirmed or refuted.
- A funding and ownership review could settle whether any public money is involved and whether the "$5.1b" figure refers to the consented 280MW project, a larger ambition, or a headline estimate.
