# Who owns Datagrid NZ and what is defensible about capex and jobs for the Makarewa project

## Executive answer

- Datagrid’s New Zealand legal structure is a layered corporate chain registered with NZ Companies Office that includes `Datagrid NZ Limited` (9410784), `Datagrid Holding Group NZ Limited` (9410701), and `Datagrid NZ Services Limited` (9410788), with shared directorship of Perrine Dhalluín and Rémi Galasso in the public registry snapshots used. [Companies Office](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410784), [Companies Office](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410701), [Companies Office](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410788), [Companies Office](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/8160662)  (High)
- On ownership concentration, Datagrid’s own project filing indicates Rémi Galasso is the founder and major shareholder/final beneficial owner, while MBIE backgrounding identifies founders as Rémi Galasso and Malcolm Dick. Those two points are consistent across independent official documents but not all public ownership percentages are fully harmonised in one place publicly. [Environment NZ Fast-Track submission](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf), [MBIE ministerial background note](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025) (Medium)
- OIO approval status is verifiable at least for land-acquisition and ownership context (`case 202400315`) and one follow-up decision; these establish regulatory permission and foreign-ownership composition context, but not a final audited NZ-wide economic projection. [LINZ OIO decision](https://www.linz.govt.nz/our-work/overseas-investment-regulation/decisions/2024-07/202400315), [LINZ 202400315 letter](https://web.archive.org/web/20260704050540/https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/202400315%20Notice%20of%20Decision.pdf), [LINZ 202500359 exemption](https://web.archive.org/web/20260704052437/https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/doc/Notice%20of%20Decision-%20202500359-%20Exemption.pdf) (Medium)
- Environment Southland confirms Makarewa consents were granted for a datacentre with up to 240 MW of IT capacity, plus utility/cable consents; this is independent of private-company promotional capex claims. [Environment Southland resource consent page](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z)
- Public capex/job numbers are not consistent: RNZ uses “about $3b” and “up to 550” construction jobs; Spinoff reports $3.5b and 1,200 construction jobs plus 50 permanent. ODT repeats 1,200 construction jobs and adds around $4b economic injection and $60m/yr GDP claim. A paywalled Herald piece is widely circulated as “$5.1b” but could not be verified from directly readable content here; this is likely a scope/version variant or secondary framing rather than a standalone audited baseline. [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-countrys-second-largest-drain-on-power), [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project?brid=YWdncwHl8lKrRLV4WkBzkH3ImNZj), [ODT](https://www.odt.co.nz/southland/who-gains-southlands-massive-new-ai-data-centre), [NZ Herald archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260510173938/https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/) (Low–Medium)
- Anchor-tenant point is directly documented in the University’s release: University of Otago is the named future anchor-tenant for the Makarewa hyperscale park. [University of Otago press release](https://www.otago.ac.nz/news/newsroom/datagrid-welcomes-university-of-otago-as-future-anchor-tenant), [MBIE background note](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025)

**Overall confidence:** Medium — ownership/founders and approvals are supported by multiple official filings, while investment/jobs figures remain contested and mostly media-reported with different scopes.

## Evidence

### Corporate registration and structure
- Companies Office record pages for Datagrid entities show separate NZ-registered entities including Datagrid NZ Limited (9410784), Datagrid Holding Group NZ Limited (9410701), Datagrid NZ Services Limited (9410788), and Datagrid New Zealand General Partner Limited (8160662), with the expected holding relationships and directors identified in registry output. [Datagrid NZ Limited](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410784), [Datagrid Holding Group NZ Limited](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410701), [Datagrid NZ Services Limited](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/9410788), [Datagrid New Zealand General Partner Limited](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/8160662)  (High)
- The Environment NZ Fast-Track consultation response states the project is the Datagrid Sustainable Data Centre Park and records `Mr. Galasso ... the major shareholder` in ownership narrative for the applicant; it also names him as founder of Hawaiki and links associated principals on the project’s history. [Environment NZ response](https://environment.govt.nz/assets/what-government-is-doing/Fast-Track-Unlisted/Datagrid-Sustainable-Data-Centre-Park/104.01-response-ANON-URZ4-5FRF-8_Redacted.pdf)
- MBIE's ministerial background note says Datagrid New Zealand was founded by technology entrepreneur Rémi Galasso and CallPlus founder Malcolm Dick. This supports the “Rémi majority/co-founder Malcolm” reporting chain while not replacing Companies Office legal share-structure records. [MBIE meeting background note](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025) (Medium)

### OIO and local approvals
- LINZ OIO `case 202400315` shows the key overseas investment decision: Decision `Consent granted` dated 16 July 2024 for acquisition of 6 hectares at 342 Flora Road East, Makarewa, with ownership split shown as Austria 38%, New Zealand 31%, France 31%. It is published as a decision summary on the LINZ decisions page and is linked to an OIO notice PDF in release form. [LINZ case decision](https://www.linz.govt.nz/our-work/overseas-investment-regulation/decisions/2024-07/202400315), [LINZ notice PDF snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260704050540/https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/release/202400315%20Notice%20of%20Decision.pdf) (Medium)
- LINZ 202500359 exemption documentation indicates an exemption relating to acquisition of up to 100% of Datagrid Holding Group General Partner Pte Ltd and references the same Makarewa land parcels; this supports ongoing OIO-related transaction structure beyond the first consent case. [LINZ 202500359 exemption (archived)](https://web.archive.org/web/20260704052437/https://www.linz.govt.nz/sites/default/files/doc/Notice%20of%20Decision-%20202500359-%20Exemption.pdf)
- Environment Southland public notice states resource consents were granted for Makarewa and identifies 240 MW IT capacity, along with wastewater and air-discharge permits and shoreline/cable related consents. [Environment Southland consents](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z)

### Capex and jobs: reconciling public figures
- RNZ’s coverage quotes the project as “about $3 billion,” with a 550-job construction count and around 50 ongoing jobs. This is frequently cited and appears in RNZ and secondary republishing. [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-countrys-second-largest-drain-on-power)
- The Spinoff piece reports March 2026 context of a “span 49 hectares … cost $3.5 billion” with 1,200 short-term jobs and 50 ongoing staff. [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project?brid=YWdncwHl8lKrRLV4WkBzkH3ImNZj)
- ODT repeats 1,200 construction jobs and adds ~1200 jobs and `$4b` injection claim along with ~$60m annual GDP claim, framing who benefits from the output pipeline. [ODT](https://www.odt.co.nz/southland/who-gains-southlands-massive-new-ai-data-centre)
- Publicly available approvals and filings show technical scope details (land, MW, consents, anchor tenant, timelines), but do not by themselves provide a single audited capex/ jobs baseline for whole park versus phase staging; therefore the dollar/job figures remain media-reported and likely scope-dependent. [Environment Southland](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z), [LINZ OIO case](https://www.linz.govt.nz/our-work/overseas-investment-regulation/decisions/2024-07/202400315)

### Anchor tenant / who benefits
- University of Otago press release explicitly states a strategic agreement naming the University as future anchor tenant of New Zealand’s first fully carbon-neutral hyperscale centre and 43 hectares in Makarewa. [University of Otago](https://www.otago.ac.nz/news/newsroom/datagrid-welcomes-university-of-otago-as-future-anchor-tenant)
- MBIE background note similarly notes University of Otago is set to be an anchor tenant. [MBIE](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/dmsdocument/31215-meeting-with-representatives-from-datagrid-new-zealand-limited-5-june-2025)

## What would change this conclusion

- If LINZ or another regulator publishes a single, consolidated, phase-by-phase financial disclosure for the full Makarewa programme (including stage definitions), the present “conflicting capex/jobs” section could be replaced by one audited baseline.
- If Datagrid publicly publishes a technical planning document showing which figures are “park-level,” “first-module,” and “full build” capex and each stage’s workforce demand, the split between 550 and 1,200 jobs claims can be reconciled more confidently.
- If we obtain direct, non-blocked access to the NZ Herald `5.1b` article text, this could be verified as either a later-phase cost revision or a different project scope.

## Open follow-up questions

- Does the `$5.1b` claim refer to a different project boundary (full park, all phases, and cable/equipment supply chain) than the `$3b/$3.5b` figures?
- What independent estimate exists for permanent NZ employment in the operating phase by module/tenant, and is there any audited local economic impact evaluation?
