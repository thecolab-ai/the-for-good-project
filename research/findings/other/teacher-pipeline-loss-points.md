# NZ's teacher pipeline loses most certainty at the conversion points into shortage roles

Part of #439. This answers the research question: where does New Zealand's teacher pipeline lose or redirect people between ITE enrolment, graduation, registration, first job, returning-to-teaching and overseas recruitment?

## Executive answer

- **The 2025 ITE enrolment lift is real, but it has not yet become visible classroom supply.** Domestic first-time ITE enrolments increased from 3,600 in 2024 to 4,290 in 2025, including primary 1,500 to 1,990, secondary 875 to 1,105, and first-time Maori-medium ITE students up 71% to 300; the latest first-time completion data is only 2024 and moved the other way, from 3,350 domestic completions in 2023 to 3,105 in 2024, including primary down to the lowest point in the 2005-2024 series and Maori-medium completions down from 165 to 135. [Education Counts, Initial teacher education statistics](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/initial-teacher-education-statistics)
- **The main national shortage risk is not aggregate entry/exit, but subject, region, language and setting match.** The Ministry's 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply release projects a 2026 primary surplus of 530 teachers but a secondary shortfall of around 710 teachers, and its full report says shortages in science, technology, mathematics and te reo Maori cannot simply be offset by surplus teachers in other secondary subjects; PPTA's 2025 secondary staffing survey separately reports hard-to-staff subjects including Maths, Physics, Science, Te Reo Maori and Technology, and says 34% of principals had teachers working in non-specialist areas because they could not find a specialist teacher. [Ministry of Education, Teacher Demand and Supply 2025 release](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released); [Ministry of Education, 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply Planning Projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc); [PPTA, Secondary School Staffing Survey report 2025](https://www.ppta.org.nz/assets/DMSDocuments/2025-April-Secondary-Staffing-Survey-report.pdf)
- **The visible leakage points are enrolment-to-completion, completion-to-registration/practising certificate, first-job readiness/security, and matching returners or overseas teachers to shortage roles.** Education Counts can report enrolments, completions, entry and leaving rates, but its entry-rate definitions combine new domestic graduates, overseas entrants and returning teachers unless users drill into spreadsheets; public pages do not expose a simple cohort flow from ITE enrolment through registration, first job, subject, region, medium and retention. [Education Counts, Initial teacher education statistics](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/initial-teacher-education-statistics); [Education Counts, Entering and leaving teaching](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/teacher-movement)
- **Employment-based and career-switch pathways are directionally well targeted, but this finding only verifies design, places and eligibility, not downstream outcomes.** SOTP is school-based ITE funding and EBITE is a two-year pathway for LAT holders in Kaupapa Maori, Maori-medium and te reo Maori roles; ERO found new teachers with two or more days per week in schools during ITE report being more prepared, but the public pages checked here do not show SOTP or EBITE completion, registration, placement and retention outcomes by subject/setting. [Education Workforce, School Onsite Training Programme](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/principals-and-schools/local-employment-and-recruitment-support/school-onsite-training-programme); [Education Workforce, Kaupapa Maori | Maori Medium EBITE Programme Funding Award](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award); [ERO, Ready, set, teach](https://www.evidence.ero.govt.nz/documents/ready-set-teach-how-prepared-and-supported-are-new-teachers)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the headline is strongly supported by current official sources, but the exact size of each conversion loss cannot be measured from public pages because linked cohort outcomes by subject, region, school setting, registration status and pathway are not published in one public flow.

### What rising 2025 ITE enrolments probably do and do not relieve

The 2025 enrolment increase should improve the domestic supply pipeline if completion, registration and first-job placement hold up, because Education Counts says first-time ITE students are used to indicate people who may become available to join the workforce for the first time. [Education Counts, Initial teacher education statistics](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/initial-teacher-education-statistics) The caveat is timing: the latest public completion year is 2024, and Education Counts' 2024 completion data still reflects earlier enrolment cohorts rather than the 2025 lift. [Education Counts, Initial teacher education statistics](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/initial-teacher-education-statistics)

Primary relief is more plausible nationally than secondary relief. The Ministry's medium projection shows a primary surplus of 530 teachers in 2026 growing to 1,350 by 2028, while secondary remains short by around 710 in 2026 and 190 in 2028. [Ministry of Education, Teacher Demand and Supply 2025 release](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released) The same Ministry release says demand is expected to stay around 68,900 teachers across schools, with additional entrants over three years made up of returning teachers, new domestic-trained teachers, overseas teachers and Ministry-funded initiatives. [Ministry of Education, Teacher Demand and Supply 2025 release](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released)

Shortage subjects and settings remain the weak link. The full TDS report says the national projections may not replicate individual school experience, especially for subjects like te reo Maori and science, technology and mathematics, Kaupapa Maori and Maori-medium sectors, rumaki reo, reo rua units and priority staffing schools. [Ministry of Education, 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply Planning Projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc) It also says secondary subject specialisation means shortage subjects such as science, technology, mathematics and te reo Maori cannot simply be offset by surpluses in other subject areas; PPTA's 2025 secondary staffing survey independently identifies hard-to-staff subjects including Maths, Physics, Science, Te Reo Maori and Technology, and reports non-specialist teaching and cancelled or transferred courses where subject specialists could not be found. [Ministry of Education, 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply Planning Projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc); [PPTA, Secondary School Staffing Survey report 2025](https://www.ppta.org.nz/assets/DMSDocuments/2025-April-Secondary-Staffing-Survey-report.pdf)

### Are employment-based and career-switch pathways producing classroom-ready teachers?

The best-supported answer is "promising by design, but not verified here at outcome level." SOTP gives student teachers practical teaching experience in a host school while studying, funds host schools and student teachers, and requires providers to integrate work-based pedagogy, wraparound support, authentic practice-generated assessment and mentor-teacher support. [Education Workforce, School Onsite Training Programme](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/principals-and-schools/local-employment-and-recruitment-support/school-onsite-training-programme) That design matches ERO's finding that new teachers who spend two or more days per week in schools during ITE report being more prepared in their first term teaching. [ERO, Ready, set, teach](https://www.evidence.ero.govt.nz/documents/ready-set-teach-how-prepared-and-supported-are-new-teachers)

EBITE is even more targeted to shortage settings: the current programme page says it is a two-year teacher-training initiative designed specifically for critical shortages in immersion settings and teaching te reo Maori, supporting LAT holders to train while employed in a school or kura. [Education Workforce, Kaupapa Maori | Maori Medium EBITE Programme Funding Award](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award) Eligibility and priority criteria also aim at LAT holders teaching or intending to teach in Kaupapa Maori, Maori-medium, te reo Maori, STEM, secondary/wharekura/Kura Tuarua, and Level 1 or 2 immersion settings. [Education Workforce, Kaupapa Maori | Maori Medium EBITE Programme Funding Award](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award)

The missing proof is downstream and should be treated as a scoped search limit, not proof that no outcome data exists. The current public pages checked for this finding describe SOTP and EBITE design, funding and eligibility, and Ministry projections count the expected contribution of supply initiatives, but those checked pages do not report SOTP or EBITE completion rates, Teaching Council registration rates, first-job placements, subject/medium match or two-to-five-year retention. [Education Workforce, School Onsite Training Programme](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/principals-and-schools/local-employment-and-recruitment-support/school-onsite-training-programme); [Education Workforce, Kaupapa Maori | Maori Medium EBITE Programme Funding Award](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award); [Ministry of Education, Teacher Demand and Supply 2025 release](https://www.education.govt.nz/news/teacher-demand-and-supply-2025-report-released); [Ministry of Education, 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply Planning Projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc)

### Source and scope limits

ERO's readiness evidence is useful but should not be overgeneralised to all shortage settings. ERO explicitly focused on NZ-trained provisionally certificated teachers in English-medium state and state-integrated schools, while noting IDI data included Maori and English-medium schools; ERO's survey evidence is also self-reported by teachers and principals. [ERO, Ready, set, teach](https://www.evidence.ero.govt.nz/documents/ready-set-teach-how-prepared-and-supported-are-new-teachers) That means it is strong evidence for the English-medium beginning-teacher experience, but it is not direct evidence of how well Maori-medium or bilingual new teachers are prepared. [ERO, Ready, set, teach](https://www.evidence.ero.govt.nz/documents/ready-set-teach-how-prepared-and-supported-are-new-teachers)

Education Counts' public pages are strong for aggregate enrolment, completion, entry and leaving trends, but not for an individual pipeline. The ITE page warns that subject and sector assignments are indicative, that reported secondary teaching subjects do not necessarily represent the subjects graduates may teach at school, and that region of study may not be related to where graduates commence teaching. [Education Counts, Initial teacher education statistics](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/initial-teacher-education-statistics) The teacher-movement page says entry includes new and returning teachers, and that "new" includes both new domestic ITE graduates and teachers entering from overseas. [Education Counts, Entering and leaving teaching](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/teacher-movement)

## What would change this conclusion

- A linked Ministry/Teaching Council cohort dataset showing 2025 ITE entrants by subject, provider, region, medium and pathway through completion, registration, first job and two-to-five-year retention would replace this finding's "conversion point" inference with measured loss rates. [Education Counts, Initial teacher education statistics](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/initial-teacher-education-statistics); [Teaching Council, Register to teach](https://teachingcouncil.nz/en/become-a-teacher/based-in-aotearoa-new-zealand/register-to-teach)
- Evidence that 2025-2027 ITE growth converts into actual placements in secondary science, technology, mathematics, te reo Maori, Maori-medium, Kaupapa Maori, rural and priority staffing roles would weaken the conclusion that role-match is the main bottleneck. [Ministry of Education, 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply Planning Projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc)
- Public outcome reporting for SOTP, EBITE, Teach First and Teacher Education Refresh - completion, registration, placement and retention by shortage subject/setting - would change the assessment of employment-based and career-switch pathways from "promising" to "proven" or "not working." [Education Workforce, School Onsite Training Programme](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/principals-and-schools/local-employment-and-recruitment-support/school-onsite-training-programme); [Education Workforce, Kaupapa Maori | Maori Medium EBITE Programme Funding Award](https://workforce.education.govt.nz/kaupapa-maori-maori-medium-ebite-programme-funding-award)
- I could not verify a public, current conversion rate from ITE completion to Teaching Council registration/practising certificate, nor a public breakdown of overseas and returning teachers by shortage subject, Maori-medium capability, region and first-year retention. [Education Counts, Entering and leaving teaching](https://www.educationcounts.govt.nz/statistics/teacher-movement); [Teaching Council, Register to teach](https://teachingcouncil.nz/en/become-a-teacher/based-in-aotearoa-new-zealand/register-to-teach)
- A human with sector authority or lived school-leadership experience is needed to test the practical claim that some "surplus" teachers are not substitutable into hard-to-staff subject, language or regional vacancies; the Ministry's TDS report and PPTA's 2025 secondary staffing survey support that claim, but local appointment experience would identify where the mismatch bites hardest. [Ministry of Education, 2025 Teacher Demand and Supply Planning Projection PDF](https://web-assets.education.govt.nz/s3fs-public/2026-02/2025-Teacher-Demand-and-Supply-Planning-Projection.pdf?VersionId=9K6lGp0GSfngLdf1wHMslUq5nCKe0HUc); [PPTA, Secondary School Staffing Survey report 2025](https://www.ppta.org.nz/assets/DMSDocuments/2025-April-Secondary-Staffing-Survey-report.pdf)

## Open follow-up questions

- What share of 2025 first-time ITE entrants complete, register and start teaching by 2026-2028, split by sector, secondary subject, medium, provider type and study region?
- Do SOTP, EBITE, Teach First and Teacher Education Refresh participants have higher classroom-readiness, completion, placement and retention rates than comparable traditional ITE entrants?
- Where do returning and overseas teachers actually go: which regions, subjects, media and priority staffing schools, and how long do they stay?
- What proportion of provisionally certificated teachers move from fixed-term or casual first roles into stable roles, and does this differ by primary/secondary, rural/urban, Maori-medium and equity setting?
- Which teacher-supply roles require non-substitutable capability - te reo Maori, immersion pedagogy, STEM, learning support, rural community ties - and how does public workforce reporting need to represent that?
