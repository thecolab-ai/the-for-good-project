# NZ wholesale electricity is concentrated, and 2024 gentailer energy margins were large but not the same as profit

## Executive answer

- The wholesale electricity generation side is concentrated: the Electricity Authority says most generation is produced by Contact, Genesis, Meridian, and Mercury, and the OECD's 2026 New Zealand survey puts 2024 generation shares at Contact 28%, Genesis 14%, Mercury 21%, Meridian 29%, and independent generators 8% [Electricity Authority sector overview](https://www.ea.govt.nz/your-power/new-zealands-electricity-sector/); [OECD New Zealand 2026, Table 2.6](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-economic-surveys-new-zealand-2026_3ec5de98-en/full-report/towards-a-more-affordable-secure-and-sustainable-electricity-system_8422a0e1.html).
- Concentration is not just a market-share story: the Electricity Authority's 2021 structure-conduct-performance review said New Zealand generation HHI had hovered around 2,000 since 2014, and Meridian's South Island generation was gross pivotal around 90% to 95% of the time in 2019 to June 2021 [Electricity Authority SCP information paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6673/Information_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_-_Wholesale_el_vHrNwSE.pdf).
- The best public energy-margin evidence is a special Electricity Authority collection for July to December 2024: the Authority required Contact, Genesis, Mercury, Meridian, Manawa, and Nova to submit weekly margin data after the winter 2024 wholesale price spike [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/).
- Across the six reporting gentailers, the Authority says weekly energy margin ranged from $60 million to $95 million and averaged $76 million from July to December 2024; energy margin excludes operating costs such as staffing, overheads, maintenance, repairs, and levies, so it is a profitability indicator rather than net profit [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/).
- The visible per-company dashboard split for the four main gentailers is approximately Mercury $554m, Contact $509m, Meridian $429m, and Genesis $354m over the July to December 2024 collection period, but I rate this split Medium rather than High confidence because the live Sigma Computing dashboard could not be exported by automated fetches and the values come from a third-party copy of the Electricity Authority chart [Labourcartel reproduction of EA dashboard chart, 2025](https://labourcartel.substack.com/p/heat-but-no-light-the-short-lived); [EA dashboard shell](https://www.ea.govt.nz/data-and-insights/charts-and-dashboards/energy-margin/).
- The Authority separately reports average weekly net profits for July to December 2024 of Contact $5.46m, Genesis $3.6m, Mercury -$2.58m, and Meridian -$4.65m, which reinforces that energy margin and accounting profit are different measures [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - concentration is well supported by official sources, but the company-level July-December 2024 energy-margin split relies on a preserved copy of an EA dashboard chart that I could not directly export in this environment.

## Evidence

### The four main gentailers dominate generation, but the wholesale market still has many registered participants

The Electricity Authority's current sector overview says "lots of companies" generate power but most electricity is generated by Contact Energy, Genesis Energy, Meridian Energy, and Mercury Energy, and it says New Zealand's wholesale market includes about 80 generation companies, 62 retailers, and four gentailers [Electricity Authority sector overview](https://www.ea.govt.nz/your-power/new-zealands-electricity-sector/).

The OECD's 2026 New Zealand survey gives a current quantified generation split for 2024: Contact Energy 28%, Genesis Energy 14%, Mercury NZ 21%, Meridian Energy 29%, and independent generators 8%, so the four named gentailers account for 92% of the table's generation share [OECD New Zealand 2026, Table 2.6](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-economic-surveys-new-zealand-2026_3ec5de98-en/full-report/towards-a-more-affordable-secure-and-sustainable-electricity-system_8422a0e1.html). I treat the OECD table as corroborating the Authority's qualitative statement, but not as a pure primary generation dataset, because the OECD cites MBIE's *Energy in New Zealand 2024* and OECD Secretariat work for the table [OECD New Zealand 2026, Table 2.6](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-economic-surveys-new-zealand-2026_3ec5de98-en/full-report/towards-a-more-affordable-secure-and-sustainable-electricity-system_8422a0e1.html).

The Authority's 2021 structure-conduct-performance review used a narrower review period, January 2019 to June 2021, and said four large vertically integrated generator-retailers supplied about 80% of generation to the market in that review frame [Electricity Authority SCP information paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6673/Information_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_-_Wholesale_el_vHrNwSE.pdf). The different percentages are not necessarily inconsistent, because they come from different years and definitions: the OECD table reports 2024 generation market share, while the EA review reports generation supplied to market during the 2019-2021 competition review [OECD New Zealand 2026, Table 2.6](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-economic-surveys-new-zealand-2026_3ec5de98-en/full-report/towards-a-more-affordable-secure-and-sustainable-electricity-system_8422a0e1.html); [Electricity Authority SCP information paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6673/Information_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_-_Wholesale_el_vHrNwSE.pdf).

### Concentration indicators show market-power risk, not proof of unlawful conduct

The Authority's 2021 review said a market dominated by a few large firms is more susceptible to the exercise of market power, and it used HHI and pivotal-supplier indicators to assess generation concentration [Electricity Authority SCP information paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6673/Information_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_-_Wholesale_el_vHrNwSE.pdf). The same review said New Zealand generation HHI had hovered around 2,000 since 2014, while noting HHI has limited usefulness for electricity markets because pivotal supply, transmission constraints, and hydro storage can matter more than national market shares [Electricity Authority SCP information paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6673/Information_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_-_Wholesale_el_vHrNwSE.pdf).

The Authority's pivotal-supplier analysis found Meridian was the only generator-retailer gross pivotal in a higher percentage of time in all three review years, rising from around 77% of the time in 2016-2018 to around 90% to 95% in 2019 to June 2021 [Electricity Authority SCP information paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6673/Information_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_-_Wholesale_el_vHrNwSE.pdf). The same paper said Meridian had about 30% of market generating capacity but was needed to meet demand over 90% of the time, while Contact had about 22% of market generating capacity and was needed less than 40% of the time [Electricity Authority SCP information paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6673/Information_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_-_Wholesale_el_vHrNwSE.pdf).

The Authority's summary paper concluded that no single indicator was concrete proof, but the full picture showed some evidence that spot prices may not have been determined in a competitive environment during January 2019 to June 2021 [Electricity Authority SCP summary paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6674/Summary_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_in_the_wholesale_e_jLClK9L.pdf). Its observations included dominance by a few firms, Meridian being needed to meet demand over 90% of the time, offer prices sometimes above estimated cost, some offers not reflecting underlying conditions, steeper supply curves suggesting more incentive and ability to economically withhold, and sometimes-high Lerner Index results [Electricity Authority SCP summary paper, 2021](https://www.ea.govt.nz/documents/6674/Summary_paper_-_Review_of_structure_conduct_and_performance_in_the_wholesale_e_jLClK9L.pdf).

The current Energy Competition Task Force programme shows the same competition concern remains live: the task force says its work programme is focused on enabling new generators and independent retailers to enter and better compete, and it has considered level-playing-field measures so independent retailers and generators can access hedge contracts on effectively the same terms as gentailers' internal retail arms [Energy Competition Task Force](https://www.ea.govt.nz/projects/all/energy-competition-task-force/); [Level playing field measures](https://www.ea.govt.nz/projects/all/energy-competition-task-force/consultation/level-playing-field-measures/).

### Energy margin is a gross energy measure, not net profit

The Electricity Authority defines energy margin as the difference between total revenue earned from generating electricity and total costs incurred in generating electricity, and it explicitly says this is not generator profit because operating costs such as staffing, office overheads, maintenance, and repairs are excluded [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/). The Authority also says energy margin is a measure of profitability because unusually high or low short-period energy margins can indicate unusually high or low profits in that period [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/).

For the July to December 2024 collection, the Authority required Contact, Genesis, Mercury, Meridian, Manawa, and Nova to provide weekly data using its information-gathering powers, and the components included generation revenue, ancillary market revenue, fixed-price contract revenue, spot electricity costs, and direct generation costs such as fuel and emissions charges [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/). The Authority says total weekly energy margin across all reporting gentailers ranged from $60m to $95m and averaged $76m during that period [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/).

The Authority also explains why high wholesale spot prices did not automatically produce higher total energy margins in winter 2024: generation revenue and spot electricity costs both rose with spot prices, and gentailers are also main buyers when prices are high, so the net effect of high spot prices can be small at aggregate level [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/).

### Net profit tells a different story from energy margin

For the same July to December 2024 window, the Authority reports average weekly net profits for the four gentailers as Contact $5.46m, Genesis $3.6m, Mercury -$2.58m, and Meridian -$4.65m, citing figures published on the companies' own websites [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/). Annualising or comparing those weekly net-profit figures mechanically would be misleading, because the Authority reports them for a specific six-month period that included winter 2024 wholesale stress and dry-year effects [Electricity Authority, 2025](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/gentailer-energy-margins-in-2024/).

Company reports also show that ordinary accounting measures can diverge sharply from energy margins: Meridian's FY2025 annual-results media announcement says EBITDAF fell from $905m to $611m, underlying net profit fell from $359m to $56m, and energy margin fell 23% from $1.276b to $982m after severe droughts and $300m spent on hedge and demand-response contracts [Meridian FY2025 media announcement](https://www.meridianenergy.co.nz/public/Investors/Reports-and-presentations/Annual-results-and-reports/2025/Media-Announcement.pdf). Mercury's FY2025 integrated report says EBITDAF was $786m, down $91m, while trading margin was $1.153b, down $75m [Mercury FY2025 integrated report](https://www.mercury.co.nz/media/Mercury%202025%20Integrated%20Report_Final%20%281%29.pdf). Contact's FY2025 financial statements define EBITDAF as a non-GAAP performance measure and describe wholesale electricity revenue net of hedging as one revenue category, which supports treating each company's reported margin measures cautiously rather than as interchangeable with the EA energy-margin dashboard [Contact FY2025 financial statements](https://contact.co.nz/getContentAsset/8e9475cb-0baf-45f3-98d9-8e43de208f69/a677e4b4-b3c2-492c-ae74-9399720288b8/2025-financial-statements.pdf?language=en).

## What would change this conclusion

- A direct export of the EA energy-margin dashboard data, or an official CSV release, would upgrade the company-level July-December 2024 margin split from Medium to High confidence.
- Updated EA or MBIE generation-share data for 2025 or 2026 could change the exact concentration ratio, especially after new generation investments, asset acquisitions, or changes in hydro conditions.
- A public, audited separation of wholesale generation, hedge, retail, and transfer-pricing profitability by gentailer would be stronger than the current mix of EA energy-margin collection, internal-transfer-price disclosures, and company-level financial statements.
- Evidence that independent generators or retailers can consistently obtain firming and hedge products on equivalent terms to gentailer retail arms would weaken the conclusion that vertical integration is a live competition problem.
- I could not verify the exact per-company EA dashboard data directly because the current Sigma Computing embed is an interactive dashboard; I used the EA article for official methodology and aggregate totals, and a third-party preserved chart only for the company-level split. **A 2026-07-08 re-attempt through the upgraded ADR-0006 fetch ladder confirmed the source is still unrecovered by automation: the EA page URL resolved on the plain-HTTP rung and returned only the static HTML shell, while the embedded Sigma URL escalated to Jina and returned UI labels but not the per-company figures.** Recovering it needs a source or tool the automated ladder lacks, such as an official EA CSV release, an interactive export from the live dashboard if available, or data supplied by the Authority. This limitation touches only the already-caveated per-company split, not the finding's core conclusions.
- This finding does not determine whether any company breached the Commerce Act, the Electricity Industry Participation Code, or trading-conduct rules; that would require legal authority and regulator-level evidence.
