# Public council records do not yet support a national dairy renewal rate, but sampled dairy renewal decisions were grants with conditions

## Executive answer

- I did not find a public national dataset that classifies dairy water-take or dairy-discharge renewals as "renewed as sought", "renewed with reduced volume/load", "renewed with shorter duration", or "declined"; the public layers found in the stream expose granted/current consent fields, while ECan and Environment Southland decision pages expose individual decision documents rather than a structured outcome table [data.govt.nz ECan dairy-effluent layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/effluent-dairy-discharge-active-consented-activities3), [data.govt.nz Waikato allocated water-take layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/allocated-water-take-seasonal-water-allocation), [ECan notified decisions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/do-it-online/resource-consents/notifications-and-submissions/notified-decisions), [Environment Southland notified consents](https://www.es.govt.nz/environment/consents/notified-consents).
- In the three public decisions I counted as dairy water-take or discharge renewal/replacement outcomes, all three were granted with conditions; none showed a simple "renewed with a lower granted volume/load than the applicant's final request", but one Southland grant shortened the term relative to what the applicant initially sought [ECan CRC202303 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5806275), [ECan CRC223604/CRC223608/CRC233077/CRC233419 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5938455), [Environment Southland APP-20211740 decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/notified-consents/2022/Platinum%20Dairies%20Limited/Decision%20Documents/01%20Hearing%20Decision%20Platinum%20Dairies%20Limited%20APP-20211740.docx.pdf).
- I reviewed two other ECan farming/water decisions but excluded them from the dairy-renewal denominator: CRC110568 was a new groundwater permit, not a renewal/replacement, and CRC250022/CRC250197 concerned a sheep-and-beef farming activity, not a dairy activity [ECan CRC110568 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5682187), [ECan CRC250022/CRC250197 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5687421).
- The best answer for the stream is therefore: observed public dairy renewal decisions show tightening mostly through conditions, monitoring, flow restrictions and common-expiry dates, not through an easily countable national pattern of councils granting dairy renewals at lower volumes or loads; a separate new-consent groundwater refusal shows refusal can occur in over-allocated systems, but it should not be counted as a dairy renewal outcome [ECan notified decisions 2025](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/do-it-online/resource-consents/notifications-and-submissions/notified-decisions/annual/2025), [Environment Southland notified consents](https://www.es.govt.nz/environment/consents/notified-consents), [ECan CRC110568 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5682187), [ORC replacement groundwater practice note](https://www.orc.govt.nz/media/jknfixzt/new-replacement-groundwater-take-and-use-applications-april-2026.pdf).

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the counted decision documents are official and directly answer "what happens at renewal" for those cases, but they are not a statistically valid national sample and public registers do not encode the requested outcome categories.

## Evidence

### Scope and method

This finding answers issue #889 by checking whether public regional-council records can classify dairy-related water-take and discharge renewals into four outcome buckets: renewed as sought, renewed with reduced volume/load or shorter duration, declined, or not classifiable [ECan notified decisions](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/do-it-online/resource-consents/notifications-and-submissions/notified-decisions), [Environment Southland notified consents](https://www.es.govt.nz/environment/consents/notified-consents). I first checked the vendored `data-govt-nz` skill for official consent datasets and confirmed that the main open-data records found are consent/register layers, not decision-outcome datasets; for example, ECan dairy-effluent data describes current consented activities and Waikato's allocation layer describes allocated water-take records, but neither is a structured table of applied-versus-granted renewal outcomes [data.govt.nz ECan dairy-effluent layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/effluent-dairy-discharge-active-consented-activities3), [data.govt.nz Waikato allocated water-take layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/allocated-water-take-seasonal-water-allocation).

The decision-document review is purposive, not random: it includes public decisions I could identify from ECan's 2025-2026 notified-decision archive and Environment Southland's notified-consent pages where the decision appeared likely to involve dairy or farming water-take, farm dairy effluent, land-use farming, or dairy-supporting discharge/water permits [ECan notified decisions 2025](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/do-it-online/resource-consents/notifications-and-submissions/notified-decisions/annual/2025), [ECan notified decisions 2026](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/do-it-online/resource-consents/notifications-and-submissions/notified-decisions), [Environment Southland notified consents](https://www.es.govt.nz/environment/consents/notified-consents). After reading the decision documents, I counted only three as dairy water-take or discharge renewal/replacement outcomes and treated two as contextual exclusions: one new groundwater permit and one sheep-and-beef renewal [ECan CRC110568 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5682187), [ECan CRC250022/CRC250197 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5687421). I avoided publishing private individuals' names in the analysis table; consent IDs and company/applicant names remain visible in the linked public council documents [ECan notified decisions 2025](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/do-it-online/resource-consents/notifications-and-submissions/notified-decisions/annual/2025), [Environment Southland notified consents](https://www.es.govt.nz/environment/consents/notified-consents).

### Why "as sought" is hard to classify

The public decision record often changes during the process, so "as sought" can mean the originally lodged application, the notified proposal, the applicant's amended proposal at hearing, or the final conditions accepted by the applicant [ECan CRC223604/CRC223608/CRC233077/CRC233419 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5938455). The ECan dairy-effluent/water-take case is the clearest example: the public notice described lower water-take and nitrogen-loading figures, but the decision records later amendments increasing the rate, annual volumes, nitrogen-loading figures and effluent-consent duration, then grants the applications with conditions [ECan CRC223604/CRC223608/CRC233077/CRC233419 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5938455).

Conditions also matter more than a headline grant/refusal classification: the Pahau dairy-named water-take decision grants the application but imposes a residual-flow condition and cease/reduction rules at specified river-flow thresholds, so production exposure would depend on actual low-flow frequency rather than on annual authorised volume alone [ECan CRC202303 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5806275). The Environment Southland APP-20211740 decision grants a dairy-farm package but changes an initially sought 10-year duration to a 30 December 2030 common expiry date, which is a shorter-duration tightening even though the decision is a "grant" [Environment Southland APP-20211740 decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/notified-consents/2022/Platinum%20Dairies%20Limited/Decision%20Documents/01%20Hearing%20Decision%20Platinum%20Dairies%20Limited%20APP-20211740.docx.pdf).

### What other council records add

Waikato's public farmer guidance confirms that dairy-shed water takes commonly need resource consent under Variation 6 and explains permitted-activity thresholds, but I did not find a public Waikato decision table that classifies dairy shed water-take renewals by applied-versus-granted volume and term [Waikato Regional Council, dairy shed water takes](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/services/consents-and-compliance/resource-consents/dairy-shed-water-takes/). Otago's public guidance is useful for duration exposure rather than outcome rates: ORC's replacement-groundwater practice note says water-take applications in Otago are limited to a maximum six-year consent term and that some short-term permits receive statutory extensions, but the practice note is not a retrospective table of renewal decisions [ORC replacement groundwater practice note](https://www.orc.govt.nz/media/jknfixzt/new-replacement-groundwater-take-and-use-applications-april-2026.pdf), [ORC Plan Change 7 page](https://www.orc.govt.nz/your-council/plans-and-strategies/water-plans-and-policies/regional-plan-water-for-otago/changes-made-operative-and-archives/plan-change-7-water-permits-to-the-regional-plan-water/).

## What would change this conclusion

- A council or national EMAR/LAWA-style decision dataset joining consent ID, application type, previous authorised amount, amount sought, amount granted, term sought, term granted, decision status, sector/purpose and whether it was a replacement would change the conclusion from a manual sample to a quantitative rate [data.govt.nz Waikato allocated water-take layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/allocated-water-take-seasonal-water-allocation), [data.govt.nz ECan dairy-effluent layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/effluent-dairy-discharge-active-consented-activities3).
- A LGOIMA response from ECan, Waikato, Environment Southland, ORC and BOPRC that exports all dairy-related replacement decisions since 2020 with applied-versus-granted volumes/loads and terms would materially improve confidence; I did not make LGOIMA requests because this issue was answerable only to a public-record standard within one finding.
- A regional-council consent planner or resource-management lawyer should review the classification rules before anyone uses this finding as legal or operational advice, because the distinction between "same activity", "amended proposal", "replacement", "variation", "reduced effect", "reduced authorised amount" and "shorter duration" is legal-context dependent [Environment Southland APP-20211740 decision](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/notified-consents/2022/Platinum%20Dairies%20Limited/Decision%20Documents/01%20Hearing%20Decision%20Platinum%20Dairies%20Limited%20APP-20211740.docx.pdf), [ECan CRC223604/CRC223608/CRC233077/CRC233419 decision](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/document/download/?uri=5938455).
- I could not verify a national denominator of all dairy water-take and discharge renewal applications, the share processed non-notified, the share withdrawn before decision, production changes after grant/refusal, or whether the three counted public dairy renewal/replacement decisions are representative of routine renewals.
