# NZ should borrow staged road-pricing design from overseas, not copy any one EV market

## Executive answer

- **No comparator has a clean, ready-made replacement for fuel-tax revenue.** Norway has mostly reduced EV purchase and use incentives after very high EV uptake, the UK is layering annual Vehicle Excise Duty with a proposed EV mileage charge from April 2028, the Netherlands is phasing EV ownership-tax discounts and introducing heavy-truck kilometre charging, and California is still piloting road charge while using an annual EV road-improvement fee [Norway Government](https://www.regjeringen.no/en/topics/transport-and-communications/veg/faktaartikler-vei-og-ts/norway-is-electric/id2677481/); [UK HM Treasury/HMRC](https://www.gov.uk/government/consultations/consultation-on-the-introduction-of-electric-vehicle-excise-duty-eved/consultation-on-the-introduction-of-electric-vehicle-excise-duty-eved); [Rijksoverheid](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/themas/belastingen-uitkeringen-en-toeslagen/belastingen-op-auto-en-motor/belastingvoordelen-klimaat); [Caltrans](https://dot.ca.gov/programs/road-charge).
- **The most transferable lesson for New Zealand is staged, low-location distance charging with manual/non-telematics options.** OECD analysis supports phasing from EV/PHEV distance charges to broader distance charging, while California and the UK both treat mileage reporting and privacy as design questions rather than assuming full GPS tracking is politically acceptable [OECD, 2023](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2023/06/decarbonisation-and-the-pricing-of-road-transport_86ac7590/54809337-en.pdf); [California TAC, 2023](https://catc.ca.gov/-/media/ctc-media/documents/ctc-committees/road-charge/sb-339-road-charge-pilot-design-recommendations-report-a11y.pdf); [UK HM Treasury/HMRC](https://www.gov.uk/government/consultations/consultation-on-the-introduction-of-electric-vehicle-excise-duty-eved/consultation-on-the-introduction-of-electric-vehicle-excise-duty-eved).
- **Annual registration or ownership taxes are administratively easy but incomplete.** The UK now applies VED to zero-emission vehicles, the Netherlands is stepping EV motor-vehicle tax back toward the normal rate, and California charges an EV Road Improvement Fee, but fixed annual charges do not vary with kilometres and therefore cannot fully replace usage-based petrol or diesel tax fairly [DVLA](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/vehicle-tax-for-electric-and-low-emissions-vehicles); [Rijksoverheid](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/themas/belastingen-uitkeringen-en-toeslagen/belastingen-op-auto-en-motor/belastingvoordelen-klimaat); [California DMV](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/handbook/vehicle-industry-registration-procedures-manual-2/appendix-1f-fees/); [OECD, 2023](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2023/06/decarbonisation-and-the-pricing-of-road-transport_86ac7590/54809337-en.pdf).
- **Rural equity is more complicated than "rural drivers drive farther".** California's road-charge work explicitly found that distance, fuel economy, income, transport alternatives and policy structure all affect geographic equity, which is directly relevant to New Zealand's rural and inter-regional travel context [Caltrans rural-equity appendix](https://dot.ca.gov/-/media/dot-media/programs/road-charge/documents/appendix-18-5-a11y.pdf).
- **Administrative cost is the main practical caution.** California's pilots show that account managers, privacy policies, audits, invoicing and fund flows are all manageable at pilot scale, but they also show why a national RUC system costs more to administer than fuel duty unless reporting, enforcement and payment are very simple [Caltrans 2017 pilot](https://dot.ca.gov/programs/road-charge/final-report); [Caltrans audit appendix](https://dot.ca.gov/-/media/dot-media/programs/road-charge/documents/appendix-14-a11y.pdf); [California TAC, 2023](https://catc.ca.gov/-/media/ctc-media/documents/ctc-committees/road-charge/sb-339-road-charge-pilot-design-recommendations-report-a11y.pdf).

**Overall confidence: Medium** - the instrument descriptions are high confidence because they come from official tax and transport sources, but transferability is medium confidence because actual revenue performance, privacy acceptance and rural impacts depend on behaviour, enforcement and final policy design that differ across jurisdictions.

### What transfers best to New Zealand

**A staged EV/PHEV-first distance charge transfers better than a sudden all-fleet switch.** OECD describes a phased path that can begin with EVs and PHEVs, then apply distance charging to all cars for non-climate externalities, and only later consider regional or congestion differentiation [OECD, 2023](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2023/06/decarbonisation-and-the-pricing-of-road-transport_86ac7590/54809337-en.pdf). That sequence fits New Zealand better than Norway's model because New Zealand already moved light EVs and PHEVs into RUC from 1 April 2024 and is preparing broader light-vehicle RUC reform rather than using very large purchase-tax exemptions as the central instrument [NZTA](https://www.nzta.govt.nz/vehicles/road-user-charges/ruc-for-electric-vehicles); [Ministry of Transport](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/RUC-RIS-updated.pdf).

**Privacy-minimising distance charging is more transferable than GPS-first congestion pricing.** The Dutch 2022 passenger-car plan proposed no time/place differentiation and only annual kilometre registration, while California's 2023 pilot design recommended multiple reporting methods including odometer readings, plug-in devices and telematics [Rijksoverheid, 2022](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/actueel/nieuws/2022/07/01/vanaf-2030-betalen-automobilisten-naar-gebruik); [California TAC, 2023](https://catc.ca.gov/-/media/ctc-media/documents/ctc-committees/road-charge/sb-339-road-charge-pilot-design-recommendations-report-a11y.pdf). That matters for New Zealand because the domestic RUC reform is already sensitive to privacy, provider oversight and user burden, and a low-location design would preserve more trust than a system that appears to track trips by default [Ministry of Transport regulations consultation](https://consult.transport.govt.nz/policy/proposed-regulations-for-road-user-charges/user_uploads/road-user-charges-regulations-consultation-document.pdf).

**Annual EV ownership charges are a useful backstop, not a complete replacement.** The UK, Netherlands and California all use or are restoring fixed ownership/registration charges on EVs, but OECD's road-pricing analysis treats distance charging as the instrument that can replace the non-carbon part of fuel taxes as fuel use falls [DVLA](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/vehicle-tax-for-electric-and-low-emissions-vehicles); [Rijksoverheid](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/themas/belastingen-uitkeringen-en-toeslagen/belastingen-op-auto-en-motor/belastingvoordelen-klimaat); [California DMV](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/handbook/vehicle-industry-registration-procedures-manual-2/appendix-1f-fees/); [OECD, 2023](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2023/06/decarbonisation-and-the-pricing-of-road-transport_86ac7590/54809337-en.pdf). For New Zealand, that means motor vehicle registration/licensing fees can share administration costs or smooth transition politics, but they should not be treated as a like-for-like substitute for petrol excise or RUC [Ministry of Transport RUC RIS](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/RUC-RIS-updated.pdf).

**Tolls and congestion charging transfer only where the problem is local congestion or a fundable asset.** London shows that urban road-user charging can be mature and administrable, and the Netherlands shows heavy-truck kilometre charging can be integrated with toll-service providers, but neither is a national light-vehicle petrol-tax replacement that maps cleanly onto New Zealand's dispersed inter-regional road network [TfL](https://tfl.gov.uk/modes/driving/congestion-charge); [Rijksoverheid truck charge](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/themas/verkeer-en-vervoer/goederenvervoer/vrachtwagenheffing). New Zealand should therefore keep tolling and congestion charging as local or corridor tools, while using RUC-style distance charging for national revenue replacement [Ministry of Transport RUC RIS](https://www.transport.govt.nz/assets/Uploads/RUC-RIS-updated.pdf).

**Rural equity should be tested empirically, not assumed.** California's rural-equity appendix says rural and long-distance concerns depend on distance, fuel economy, income, alternatives and charge structure, and cites prior work suggesting some rural drivers may not be worse off under a flat road charge than under fuel tax because rural vehicles can be less fuel-efficient [Caltrans rural-equity appendix](https://dot.ca.gov/-/media/dot-media/programs/road-charge/documents/appendix-18-5-a11y.pdf). That conclusion is not automatically transferable to New Zealand because rural access to health care, food, work and public transport differs by region, so NZ needs local household and vehicle-fleet modelling before setting discounts, rebates or exemptions [Caltrans rural-equity appendix](https://dot.ca.gov/-/media/dot-media/programs/road-charge/documents/appendix-18-5-a11y.pdf).

**Administrative cost should be a go/no-go criterion, not an afterthought.** California's 2017 pilot covered more than 5,000 vehicles and 37 million miles, and its audit appendix concluded the pilot was auditable but that real-money systems would need financial-grade records [Caltrans 2017 pilot](https://dot.ca.gov/programs/road-charge/final-report); [Caltrans audit appendix](https://dot.ca.gov/-/media/dot-media/programs/road-charge/documents/appendix-14-a11y.pdf). California's 2023 TAC also made organisational readiness, collection cost, fund handling and compliance explicit evaluation criteria for the SB 339 pilot, which is a useful checklist for New Zealand's provider-market RUC reform [California TAC, 2023](https://catc.ca.gov/-/media/ctc-media/documents/ctc-committees/road-charge/sb-339-road-charge-pilot-design-recommendations-report-a11y.pdf).

## What would change this conclusion

- **A passed and implemented UK eVED regime with published compliance costs, evasion rates and privacy safeguards** would materially strengthen the UK as the closest comparator for New Zealand's light-vehicle RUC transition [UK HM Treasury/HMRC](https://www.gov.uk/government/consultations/consultation-on-the-introduction-of-electric-vehicle-excise-duty-eved/consultation-on-the-introduction-of-electric-vehicle-excise-duty-eved).
- **A current Dutch government decision confirming, cancelling or redesigning passenger-car "paying by use" after the 2022 plan** would change the Netherlands rating, because I found an official 2022 plan and current EV/truck-tax material but did not verify a current committed passenger-car implementation path [Rijksoverheid, 2022](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/actueel/nieuws/2022/07/01/vanaf-2030-betalen-automobilisten-naar-gebruik).
- **A completed SB 339 California revenue-collection pilot final report** would strengthen or weaken California's transferability, because the sources reviewed include the design, 2017 pilot and 2025 programme material but not a final SB 339 evaluation with observed real-payment outcomes [California TAC, 2023](https://catc.ca.gov/-/media/ctc-media/documents/ctc-committees/road-charge/sb-339-road-charge-pilot-design-recommendations-report-a11y.pdf).
- **New Zealand-specific rural household, income, vehicle-efficiency and trip-purpose data** could overturn the rural-equity caution, because California's finding that rural drivers may not be worse off under road charge cannot be assumed for NZ without local modelling [Caltrans rural-equity appendix](https://dot.ca.gov/-/media/dot-media/programs/road-charge/documents/appendix-18-5-a11y.pdf).
- **Human policy judgement is needed** on acceptable privacy trade-offs, exemptions and hardship design, because official sources can describe instruments but cannot decide how much surveillance risk or rural cost burden New Zealand communities will accept.

## Open follow-up questions

- What would the NZ revenue, equity and compliance results look like under three designs: annual odometer-declared RUC, account-based electronic RUC, and GPS-capable but location-disabled RUC?
- Which NZ user groups would be worse off if fixed annual registration fees were raised instead of expanding distance charging?
- What minimum privacy standard should NZ require for private RUC providers before petrol and non-plug-in hybrid vehicles enter the system?
