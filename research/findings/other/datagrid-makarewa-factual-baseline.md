# DataGrid's Makarewa data centre: the announced campus facts are consistently reported (consent records still unchecked), the public debate conflates the 280MW project with a stated up-to-1GW ambition, and the capex and jobs numbers diverge across outlets

## Executive answer

- The consistently reported project shape: a **280MW, 78,000 sqm** hyperscale campus on **49ha** at **Makarewa** (~7km north of Invercargill). In March 2026 **DataGrid announced** resource consent granted by **Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council** [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), and outlets reported the same [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [Telecompaper](https://www.telecompaper.com/news/datagrid-secures-approvals-for-280-mw-ai-data-centre-in-southland--1564871) — but these all trace to the company's announcement; **the council decision records themselves have not yet been checked** (child #214), so the consent baseline is Medium, not High. First phase targets **2028** [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project), connected via the **6,000km Tasman Ring** subsea cable landing at Ōreti Beach, reported go-live 2027 [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power).
- The power deal is confirmed: a **15-year, 140MW Power Purchase Option Agreement with Mercury** (~1.2TWh/yr, described by the parties as ~3% of national electricity demand) underpins phase one [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/); [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/datagrid-signs-140mw-deal-to-power-southland-ai-campus) ([BusinessDesk](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/datagrid-signs-electricity-deal-with-mercury-for-southland-ai-data-centre) corroborates the 140MW option contract), with a dedicated 220kV Transpower grid connection planned [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).
- **The "twice the power of Tiwai" claim conflates two different things — and both are real.** The *consented campus* is 280MW, which RNZ reports would make it the **second-largest electricity user *after* Tiwai** [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power). Tiwai's contracted load is **572MW** [Electricity Authority](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/the-tiwai-point-smelter-demand-response-in-winter-2024/); [Rio Tinto](https://www.riotinto.com/en/news/releases/2024/long-term-future-for-new-zealands-tiwai-point-aluminium-smelter-secured-with-new-power-deals), so 280MW is ~49% of Tiwai — roughly half. Separately, DataGrid's CEO has stated a long-term *ambition*: "Our objective is to secure 240MW in phase 1 and grow over time up to 1GW" [w.media, Jul 2024](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/) — and 1GW would be ~1.75× Tiwai's 572MW contracted load. Neither the 1GW end-state nor its power supply is consented or contracted. Keeping the consented figure and the stated ambition distinct is essential to a fair debate — the gap between them is one concrete way public descriptions of "the project" can legitimately diverge. (Whether this is what DataGrid's reported "describing quite different projects" response refers to cannot be assessed yet — that article is paywalled and was not accessed; it is a lead only [The Press](https://www.thepress.co.nz/nz-news/361035235/datagrid-says-critics-describing-quite-different-projects-southlands-data-centre).)
- **The headline capex figure is contested across outlets**: NZ$3.5b [Newsroom](https://newsroom.co.nz/2026/03/16/from-dairy-to-data-the-3-5-billion-bet-on-southland/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project), "$3 billion" [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power), and **$5.1b** [NZ Herald (premium)](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/). Jobs figures also need care: "over 1,200" construction jobs is DataGrid's own claim [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), RNZ reports up to 550 workers on-site [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power), and both agree on only ~**50 permanent** operational staff.
- Water: consents allow a groundwater take of **up to 220 million litres annually** [BusinessDesk](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/southland-data-centre-to-draw-220m-litres-of-water-a-year-consents-show); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project) — but discharge volumes and treatment design still need the consent record itself.
- There are **two consenting tracks in the public record**: the announced council RMA consents [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), *and* a separate Fast-track application (FTA104) page on MfE's site — last updated 14 January 2025, listing heavily redacted application documents, and **stating no outcome or current status** [MfE FTA104 page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/). The page does not explain how the fast-track application relates to the March 2026 council consents. Untangling exactly what was approved, by which pathway, with what conditions, is the highest-value research child.

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the project shape and the Mercury deal are consistently reported and the water take has consent-based reporting behind it; but the consent claim itself currently traces to DataGrid's own announcement (council records unchecked), capex and jobs diverge between outlets, and the 1GW ambition has no consenting or supply arrangements behind it.

## Evidence

### Scale, site, and consents

The campus is planned at **280MW** capacity and **78,000 sqm** at Makarewa, ~7km north of Invercargill. On 11 March 2026 **DataGrid announced** that resource consent had been granted by **Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council** [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent); [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/) and [Telecompaper](https://www.telecompaper.com/news/datagrid-secures-approvals-for-280-mw-ai-data-centre-in-southland--1564871) reported the approvals the same day, apparently from that announcement. **No council decision document has yet been sighted** — the three-council consent claim is company-announced and outlet-repeated, not independently verified against the public authority records (child #214). The site is **49 hectares** of farmland (43ha acquired in early 2022, extended by ~6ha with Overseas Investment Office approval) [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [w.media](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/); [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). RNZ describes six data halls, 12-metre-high noise-control barriers over 9.5ha, and **84 emergency generators, each with a 10,000-litre diesel tank** — though it gives the property as **48ha**, a minor divergence from the 49ha reported elsewhere [RNZ The Detail, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power).

### The fast-track record (primary-source gold, but stale and redacted)

MfE lists a **"Datagrid Sustainable Data Centre Park" Fast-track application (FTA104)** with the application, location/cable plans, records of title, economic-benefit material, national-significance assessment, highly-productive-land analysis, and MPI advice — substantial portions **redacted** under OIA withholding grounds for commercial sensitivity [MfE fast-track project page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/) (accessed 3 Jul 2026). The page is **last updated 14 January 2025** and **states no outcome or current status** for the application. Meanwhile DataGrid announced the council consents in March 2026 [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent). How the fast-track application and the council RMA consents relate — superseded, parallel, or partial — is **not established** in any source found, and the issue's original link to the MfE assets folder now 404s from this path. Note: the issue text says "submissions exist" on the MfE page; the documents visible on the current page are application-side (plus MPI advice) — whether public submissions are posted needs the child investigation to confirm.

### Energy: the consented 280MW vs the stated 1GW ambition

DataGrid signed a **15-year, 140MW Power Purchase Option Agreement with Mercury**, ~**1.2TWh/year**, described by the parties as one of NZ's largest single commercial power agreements and equivalent to ~**3% of national electricity demand**, underpinning phase one of the 280MW build [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/); [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/datagrid-signs-140mw-deal-to-power-southland-ai-campus) — the 15-year term and ~1.2TWh volume are supported by DCD and itbrief; [BusinessDesk](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/datagrid-signs-electricity-deal-with-mercury-for-southland-ai-data-centre) corroborates the 140MW option contract itself. A dedicated **220kV grid connection to Transpower's network** is planned [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent). Power is expected to draw on Manapōuri hydro (which also supplies Tiwai) with a fast-tracked Contact Energy wind farm ~50km away as potential additional supply [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power). The Spinoff reports annual consumption would exceed Wellington city's total usage [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project).

At 280MW the campus would be the **second-largest single electricity user after the Tiwai Point smelter** [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power). Tiwai's benchmark load is **572MW**: the Electricity Authority describes the smelter as "New Zealand's single largest electricity consumer at 572MW" [EA, 2024](https://www.ea.govt.nz/news/eye-on-electricity/the-tiwai-point-smelter-demand-response-in-winter-2024/), and Rio Tinto's 2024 supply agreements price "an aggregate of 572 megawatts (MW) of electricity to meet the smelter's full electricity needs" [Rio Tinto, 2024](https://www.riotinto.com/en/news/releases/2024/long-term-future-for-new-zealands-tiwai-point-aluminium-smelter-secured-with-new-power-deals). So the consented 280MW is **~49% of Tiwai — roughly half, not double**. However, Perrine Dhalluin, described as DataGrid's CEO at the time (July 2024), publicly stated a larger end-state ambition: **"Our objective is to secure 240MW in phase 1 and grow over time up to 1GW"** [w.media, 23 Jul 2024](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/). A 1GW campus would draw **~1.75× Tiwai's contracted load** — near, though short of, "twice Tiwai". So the "twice Tiwai" figure circulating publicly is not a description of the consented project, but it is not invented either — it tracks the stated long-term ambition, for which **no consent, PPA, or grid arrangement has been identified**. Keeping these two numbers distinct is essential to a fair debate. Whether this consent-vs-ambition gap is what DataGrid's reported "describing quite different projects" response refers to is **unverified** — that article is paywalled and its text was not retrieved by the fetch ladder; it remains a lead for the claims-vs-confirmed child (#218).

### Water

Consents allow a groundwater take of **up to 220 million litres annually** — reported from the consent record by [BusinessDesk — "Southland data centre to draw 220m litres of water a year, consents show"](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/southland-data-centre-to-draw-220m-litres-of-water-a-year-consents-show) and independently by [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). The Spinoff also reports destruction of a nearby wetland Environment Southland assessed as "very low ecological value", and likely disturbance to species at Ōreti Beach during cable landing [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). Discharge volumes, treatment/swale design, and cooling architecture still need the consent documents themselves; that is a research child.

### Connectivity

The **Tasman Ring Network** is a 6,000km subsea cable system connecting NZ to Australia — a direct connection rather than routing through Auckland — with reported go-live 2027 and 540Tbps design capacity [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). The cable lands at Ōreti Beach near Invercargill [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project).

### Ownership, economics, and tenants

DataGrid was founded by **Rémi Galasso** (founder of Hawaiki Cable; reported majority stake) and **Malcolm Dick** (CallPlus co-founder) [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). Leadership reporting has shifted: **Perrine Dhalluin was described as CEO in July 2024** [w.media](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/), while DataGrid's **March 2026 release names Rémi Galasso as "Founder and CEO"** [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent) — the current executive structure should be confirmed from the Companies Office record (child #217). The University of Otago is reported as an anchor tenant [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/). Capex is reported as **$3.5b** [Newsroom, 16 Mar 2026](https://newsroom.co.nz/2026/03/16/from-dairy-to-data-the-3-5-billion-bet-on-southland/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project), **$3b** [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power), and **$5.1b** [NZ Herald (premium)](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/) — the divergence itself is a finding (the Herald figure may price the larger ambition; not yet accessible). Jobs: **"over 1,200 skilled and technical jobs"** during construction is DataGrid's own claim [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), repeated in reporting [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project); RNZ reports **up to 550 construction workers on-site** [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power) — these may measure different things (total roles vs peak on-site) and need reconciling from the economic assessment. Both report ~**50 permanent** operational staff.

### Public contest

A public petition calling to stop the project has topped **26,000 signatures** (The Spinoff describes it as hosted on "Change.com"), and DataGrid/supporters make strong environmental claims (near-zero-emissions hydro/wind supply; running AI workloads in NZ vs China saving emissions "equivalent to almost half a million cars") that are so far **claims, not independently verified analysis** [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). A Press headline indicates DataGrid has responded that critics are "describing quite different projects" [The Press](https://www.thepress.co.nz/nz-news/361035235/datagrid-says-critics-describing-quite-different-projects-southlands-data-centre) — **headline-only lead: the article is paywalled and its text was not retrieved**, so what DataGrid actually said and meant is unverified. The claims-vs-confirmed catalogue is a research child (#218).

## What would change this conclusion

- **The primary consent documents.** The three-council consent claim currently rests on DataGrid's own release plus trade press repeating it; the council decision documents (conditions, lapse dates, take *and* discharge volumes) would settle it independently — child #214.
- **The NZ Herald's $5.1b basis.** It's premium-paywalled; it may price the up-to-1GW ambition rather than the consented 280MW campus, which would reconcile the capex divergence — child #217.
- **Any consenting, PPA, or grid work supporting the 1GW ambition.** If expansion beyond 280MW turns out to have live applications or agreements, the consent-vs-ambition distinction drawn above weakens and the "twice Tiwai" framing gains substance — children #214/#215.
- **The jobs basis.** DataGrid's "over 1,200" vs RNZ's "up to 550 on-site" likely measure different things; the economic assessment behind the consent application would resolve it — child #217.
- **Not yet accessed:** The Press article (paywalled), the ODT "who gains" analysis, and the FTA104 redacted documents in full. Fetched via WebFetch and the repo's `fetch.mjs` ladder; Newsroom was bot-blocked (403) on direct fetch but retrieved via the plain-HTTP rung — a paywall limits body text, so its capex figure is cited from its own headline/metadata.
