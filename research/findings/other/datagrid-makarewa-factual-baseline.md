# DataGrid's Makarewa data centre: the consented campus facts hold up, but the public debate conflates the 280MW consent with a stated up-to-1GW ambition, and the capex and jobs numbers diverge across outlets

## Executive answer

- The core project facts are solid and multiply sourced, including a primary source: a **280MW, 78,000 sqm** hyperscale campus on **49ha** at **Makarewa** (~7km north of Invercargill), with resource consents granted by **Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council** in March 2026 [DataGrid press release, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent); [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [Telecompaper](https://www.telecompaper.com/news/datagrid-secures-approvals-for-280-mw-ai-data-centre-in-southland--1564871). First phase targets **2028** [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project), connected via the **6,000km Tasman Ring** subsea cable landing at Ōreti Beach, reported go-live 2027 [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/nz-s-first-hyperscale-data-centre-to-be-built-in-invercargill).
- The power deal is confirmed: a **15-year, 140MW Power Purchase Option Agreement with Mercury** (~1.2TWh/yr, described by the parties as ~3% of national electricity demand) underpins phase one [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/); [BusinessDesk](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/datagrid-signs-electricity-deal-with-mercury-for-southland-ai-data-centre), with a dedicated 220kV Transpower grid connection planned [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent).
- **The "twice the power of Tiwai" claim conflates two different things — and both are real.** The *consented campus* is 280MW, which RNZ reports would make it the **second-largest electricity user *after* Tiwai** (roughly half the smelter's draw) [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power). Separately, DataGrid's CEO has stated a long-term *ambition*: "Our objective is to secure 240MW in phase 1 and grow over time up to 1GW" [w.media, Jul 2024](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/) — and 1GW *would* be nearly twice Tiwai's draw. Neither the 1GW end-state nor its power supply is consented or contracted. This consent-vs-ambition gap is plausibly what DataGrid means by critics "describing quite different projects" [The Press (paywalled)](https://www.thepress.co.nz/nz-news/361035235/datagrid-says-critics-describing-quite-different-projects-southlands-data-centre).
- **The headline capex figure is contested across outlets**: NZ$3.5b [Newsroom](https://newsroom.co.nz/2026/03/16/from-dairy-to-data-the-3-5-billion-bet-on-southland/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project), "$3 billion" [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power), and **$5.1b** [NZ Herald (premium)](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/). Jobs figures also need care: "over 1,200" construction jobs is DataGrid's own claim [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), RNZ reports up to 550 workers on-site [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power), and both agree on only ~**50 permanent** operational staff.
- Water: consents allow a groundwater take of **up to 220 million litres annually** [BusinessDesk](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/southland-data-centre-to-draw-220m-litres-of-water-a-year-consents-show); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project) — but discharge volumes and treatment design still need the consent record itself.
- There are **two consenting tracks in the public record**: the granted council RMA consents [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), *and* a separate Fast-track application (FTA104) on MfE's site whose listed status is stale/unclear and whose documents are heavily redacted [MfE FTA104 page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/). Untangling exactly what was approved, by which pathway, with what conditions, is the highest-value research child.

**Overall confidence:** Medium — scale, location, the three consenting councils, the Mercury deal, and the water take are multiply sourced (including DataGrid's own release and consent-based reporting); but capex and jobs diverge between outlets, the 1GW ambition has no consenting or supply arrangements behind it, and the primary consent documents have not yet been pulled.

## Evidence

### Scale, site, and consents

The campus is planned at **280MW** capacity and **78,000 sqm** at Makarewa, ~7km north of Invercargill, with resource consent granted by **Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council** — confirmed by DataGrid's own release of 11 March 2026 [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), and reported by [DataCenterDynamics, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/) and [Telecompaper, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.telecompaper.com/news/datagrid-secures-approvals-for-280-mw-ai-data-centre-in-southland--1564871). The site is **49 hectares** of farmland (43ha acquired in early 2022, extended by ~6ha with Overseas Investment Office approval) [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [w.media](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/); [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). RNZ describes six data halls, 9.5ha of noise-control barriers, and **84 emergency diesel generators each with a 10,000-litre tank** [RNZ The Detail, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power).

### The fast-track record (primary-source gold, but stale and redacted)

MfE lists a **"Datagrid Sustainable Data Centre Park" Fast-track application (FTA104)** with the application, location/cable plans, records of title, economic-benefit material, national-significance assessment, highly-productive-land analysis, and MPI advice — substantial portions **redacted** for commercial sensitivity [MfE fast-track project page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/) (accessed 3 Jul 2026; page last updated Jan 2025, status listed as under consideration). Meanwhile the March 2026 council consents were granted [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent). How the fast-track application and the council RMA consents relate — superseded, parallel, or partial — is **not established** in any source found, and the issue's original link to the MfE assets folder now 404s from this path. Note: the issue text says "submissions exist" on the MfE page; the documents visible on the current page are application-side (plus MPI advice) — whether public submissions are posted needs the child investigation to confirm.

### Energy: the consented 280MW vs the stated 1GW ambition

DataGrid signed a **15-year, 140MW Power Purchase Option Agreement with Mercury**, ~**1.2TWh/year**, described by the parties as one of NZ's largest single commercial power agreements and equivalent to ~**3% of national electricity demand**, underpinning phase one of the 280MW build [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/); [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/datagrid-signs-140mw-deal-to-power-southland-ai-campus); [BusinessDesk](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/datagrid-signs-electricity-deal-with-mercury-for-southland-ai-data-centre). A dedicated **220kV grid connection to Transpower's network** is planned [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent). Power is expected to draw on Manapōuri hydro (which also supplies Tiwai) with a fast-tracked Contact Energy wind farm ~50km away as potential additional supply [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power). The Spinoff reports annual consumption would exceed Wellington city's total usage [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project).

At 280MW the campus would be the **second-largest single electricity user after the Tiwai Point smelter** [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power) — roughly *half* Tiwai's ~570MW draw, not double it. However, DataGrid's CEO Perrine Dhalluin has publicly stated a larger end-state ambition: **"Our objective is to secure 240MW in phase 1 and grow over time up to 1GW"** [w.media, 23 Jul 2024](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/). A 1GW campus *would* draw nearly twice Tiwai. So the "twice Tiwai" figure circulating publicly is not a description of the consented project, but it is not invented either — it tracks the stated long-term ambition, for which **no consent, PPA, or grid arrangement has been identified**. Keeping these two numbers distinct is essential to a fair debate; conflating them is plausibly what DataGrid's "describing quite different projects" response refers to [The Press (paywalled)](https://www.thepress.co.nz/nz-news/361035235/datagrid-says-critics-describing-quite-different-projects-southlands-data-centre).

### Water

Consents allow a groundwater take of **up to 220 million litres annually** — reported from the consent record by [BusinessDesk — "Southland data centre to draw 220m litres of water a year, consents show"](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/southland-data-centre-to-draw-220m-litres-of-water-a-year-consents-show) and independently by [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). The Spinoff also reports destruction of a nearby wetland Environment Southland assessed as "very low ecological value", and likely disturbance to species at Ōreti Beach during cable landing [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). Discharge volumes, treatment/swale design, and cooling architecture still need the consent documents themselves; that is a research child.

### Connectivity

The **Tasman Ring Network** is a 6,000km subsea cable system connecting NZ to Australia, landing at Ōreti Beach — the South Island's first international subsea link — with reported go-live 2027 and 540Tbps design capacity [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/nz-s-first-hyperscale-data-centre-to-be-built-in-invercargill); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project).

### Ownership, economics, and tenants

DataGrid was founded by **Rémi Galasso** (founder of Hawaiki Cable; reported majority stake) and **Malcolm Dick** (CallPlus co-founder) [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project); current CEO is Perrine Dhalluin [w.media](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/). The University of Otago is reported as an anchor tenant [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/). Capex is reported as **$3.5b** [Newsroom, 16 Mar 2026](https://newsroom.co.nz/2026/03/16/from-dairy-to-data-the-3-5-billion-bet-on-southland/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project), **$3b** [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power), and **$5.1b** [NZ Herald (premium)](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/) — the divergence itself is a finding (the Herald figure may price the larger ambition; not yet accessible). Jobs: **"over 1,200 skilled and technical jobs"** during construction is DataGrid's own claim [DataGrid press release](https://www.datagrid.nz/pr1-rc/resourceconsent), repeated in reporting [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project); RNZ reports **up to 550 construction workers on-site** [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power) — these may measure different things (total roles vs peak on-site) and need reconciling from the economic assessment. Both report ~**50 permanent** operational staff.

### Public contest

A Change.org petition opposing the project has **26,000+ signatures**, and DataGrid/supporters make strong environmental claims (near-zero-emissions hydro/wind supply; running AI workloads in NZ vs China saving emissions "equivalent to almost half a million cars") that are so far **claims, not independently verified analysis** [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). DataGrid says critics are "describing quite different projects" [The Press (paywalled)](https://www.thepress.co.nz/nz-news/361035235/datagrid-says-critics-describing-quite-different-projects-southlands-data-centre) — not yet accessed; the claims-vs-confirmed catalogue is a research child.

## What would change this conclusion

- **The primary consent documents.** The three-council consent claim currently rests on DataGrid's own release plus trade press repeating it; the council decision documents (conditions, lapse dates, take *and* discharge volumes) would settle it independently — child #214.
- **The NZ Herald's $5.1b basis.** It's premium-paywalled; it may price the up-to-1GW ambition rather than the consented 280MW campus, which would reconcile the capex divergence — child #217.
- **Any consenting, PPA, or grid work supporting the 1GW ambition.** If expansion beyond 280MW turns out to have live applications or agreements, the consent-vs-ambition distinction drawn above weakens and the "twice Tiwai" framing gains substance — children #214/#215.
- **The jobs basis.** DataGrid's "over 1,200" vs RNZ's "up to 550 on-site" likely measure different things; the economic assessment behind the consent application would resolve it — child #217.
- **Not yet accessed:** The Press article (paywalled), the ODT "who gains" analysis, and the FTA104 redacted documents in full. Fetched via WebFetch and the repo's `fetch.mjs` ladder; Newsroom was bot-blocked (403) on direct fetch but retrieved via the plain-HTTP rung — a paywall limits body text, so its capex figure is cited from its own headline/metadata.
