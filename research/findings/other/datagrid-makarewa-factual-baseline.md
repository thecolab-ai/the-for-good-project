# DataGrid's Makarewa data centre: the verifiable facts hold up on scale and consents, but the power comparison, capex figure, and jobs numbers circulating publicly are contested

## Executive answer

- The core project facts are solid and multiply sourced: a **280MW, 78,000 sqm** hyperscale campus on **49ha** at **Makarewa** (~7km north of Invercargill), resource consents granted by Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council, first phase targeting **2028**, connected via the **6,000km Tasman Ring** subsea cable landing at Ōreti Beach (go-live 2027).
- The power deal is confirmed: a **15-year, 140MW Power Purchase Option Agreement with Mercury** (~1.2TWh/yr, described by the parties as ~3% of national electricity demand) underpins phase one.
- **The issue's starting claim that full build needs "~twice the power of Tiwai" is not supported.** RNZ's own report says the site would be the country's **second-largest electricity user *after* Tiwai** — 280MW is roughly *half* the smelter's ~570MW draw, not double it.
- **The headline capex figure is contested across outlets**: NZ$3.5b (Newsroom, The Spinoff), "$3 billion" (RNZ), and **$5.1b** (NZ Herald). Construction jobs also diverge (550 on-site per RNZ vs 1,200 per The Spinoff); both agree on only ~50 permanent operational staff.
- There are **two consenting tracks in the public record**: the granted council RMA consents, *and* a separate Fast-track application (FTA104) on MfE's site whose listed status is stale/unclear and whose documents are heavily redacted. Untangling exactly what was approved, by which pathway, with what conditions, is the highest-value research child.

**Overall confidence:** Medium — scale, location, consents, and the Mercury deal are multiply sourced from current reporting; but key public-debate numbers (capex, jobs, power comparison, water volumes) are single-origin or diverge between outlets, and the primary consent documents have not yet been pulled.

## Evidence

### Scale, site, and consents

The campus is planned at **280MW** capacity and **78,000 sqm (840,000 sq ft)** at Makarewa, ~7km north of Invercargill, with "full resource consent" reported as granted by Southland District Council, Environment Southland, and Invercargill City Council [DataCenterDynamics, 11 Mar 2026](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/). The site is **49 hectares** of farmland (43ha acquired in early 2022, later extended by ~6ha with Overseas Investment Office approval) [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [The Spinoff, 15 May 2026](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project); [w.media](https://w.media/datagrid-new-zealand-gets-oio-approval-for-data-center-park-extension/). RNZ describes six data halls, 9.5ha of noise-control barriers, and **84 emergency diesel generators each with a 10,000-litre tank** [RNZ The Detail, 17 Mar 2026](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power).

### The fast-track record (primary-source gold, but stale and redacted)

MfE lists a **"Datagrid Sustainable Data Centre Park" Fast-track application (FTA104)** with the application, location/cable plans, records of title, economic-benefit material, national-significance assessment, highly-productive-land analysis, and MPI advice — substantial portions **redacted** for commercial sensitivity [MfE fast-track project page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/fast-track-approvals/fast-track-projects/datagrid-sustainable-data-centre-park/) (accessed 3 Jul 2026; page last updated Jan 2025, status listed as under consideration). Meanwhile 2026 reporting says council resource consents were **granted** [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/). How the fast-track application and the council RMA consents relate — superseded, parallel, or partial — is **not established** in any source found, and the issue's original link to the MfE assets folder now 404s from this path. Note: the issue text says "submissions exist" on the MfE page; the documents visible on the current page are application-side (plus MPI advice) — whether public submissions are posted needs the child investigation to confirm.

### Energy

DataGrid signed a **15-year, 140MW Power Purchase Option Agreement with Mercury**, ~**1.2TWh/year**, described by the parties as one of NZ's largest single commercial power agreements and equivalent to ~**3% of national electricity demand**, underpinning phase one of the 280MW build [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datagrid-signs-power-supply-deal-with-mercury-for-planned-280mw-data-center-campus-in-invercargill-new-zealand/); [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/datagrid-signs-140mw-deal-to-power-southland-ai-campus); [BusinessDesk](https://businessdesk.co.nz/article/infrastructure/datagrid-signs-electricity-deal-with-mercury-for-southland-ai-data-centre). Power is expected to draw on Manapōuri hydro (which also supplies Tiwai) with a fast-tracked Contact Energy wind farm ~50km away as potential additional supply [RNZ The Detail](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power). The Spinoff reports annual consumption would exceed Wellington city's total usage [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project).

### Water

The Spinoff reports a consented groundwater allocation of **up to 220 million litres annually**, plus destruction of a nearby wetland Environment Southland assessed as "very low ecological value", and likely disturbance to species at Ōreti Beach during cable landing [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). **Single source** — the consent documents themselves (take *and* discharge volumes, treatment/swale design) have not been pulled; that is a research child.

### Connectivity

The **Tasman Ring Network** is a 6,000km subsea cable system connecting NZ to Australia, landing at Ōreti Beach — the South Island's first international subsea link — with reported go-live 2027 and 540Tbps design capacity [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [itbrief](https://itbrief.co.nz/story/nz-s-first-hyperscale-data-centre-to-be-built-in-invercargill); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project).

### Ownership, economics, and tenants

DataGrid was founded by **Rémi Galasso** (founder of Hawaiki Cable; reported majority stake) and **Malcolm Dick** (CallPlus co-founder) [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). The University of Otago is reported as an anchor tenant [DataCenterDynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/new-zealands-datagrid-gets-approval-for-280mw-campus-near-invercargill/). Capex is reported as **$3.5b** [Newsroom, 16 Mar 2026](https://newsroom.co.nz/2026/03/16/from-dairy-to-data-the-3-5-billion-bet-on-southland/); [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project), **$3b** [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power), and **$5.1b** [NZ Herald (premium)](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/datagrids-51b-southland-data-centre-the-three-major-hurdles-facing-the-plan-to-build-nzs-largest-ai-factory/premium/CWUEVJCRZJDQHFPABSJOZOGOUY/) — the divergence itself is a finding. Jobs: **up to 550 construction workers on-site** [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/thedetail/589770/a-new-southland-datacentre-would-be-the-country-s-second-largest-drain-on-power) vs **1,200 construction jobs** [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project); both report ~**50 permanent** operational staff.

### Public contest

A Change.org petition opposing the project has **26,000+ signatures**, and DataGrid/supporters make strong environmental claims (near-zero-emissions hydro/wind supply; running AI workloads in NZ vs China saving emissions "equivalent to almost half a million cars") that are so far **claims, not independently verified analysis** [The Spinoff](https://thespinoff.co.nz/business/15-05-2026/why-this-ai-data-centre-might-be-new-zealands-most-important-climate-project). DataGrid says critics are "describing quite different projects" [The Press (paywalled)](https://www.thepress.co.nz/nz-news/361035235/datagrid-says-critics-describing-quite-different-projects-southlands-data-centre) — not yet accessed; the claims-vs-confirmed catalogue is a research child.

## What would change this conclusion

- **The primary consent documents.** Everything above is reporting *about* the consents; the council decision documents and the FTA104 record would settle scale, conditions, water volumes, and which consenting pathway actually governs. If they contradict the reported 280MW/220ML figures, the baseline changes.
- **The NZ Herald's $5.1b basis.** It's premium-paywalled; it may describe a larger full-build scope (which would also explain "twice Tiwai" claims circulating). Accessing it could reconcile the capex divergence rather than leaving it contested.
- **Whether a later expansion phase beyond 280MW exists.** If DataGrid has stated a >280MW end-state anywhere primary, the Tiwai comparison correction above would need revisiting.
- **Not yet accessed:** The Press article (paywalled), the ODT "who gains" analysis, and the FTA104 redacted documents in full. Fetched via WebFetch and the repo's `fetch.mjs` ladder; Newsroom was bot-blocked (403) on direct fetch but retrieved via plain-HTTP rung — a paywall limits body text, so its capex figure is cited from its own headline/metadata.

## Open follow-up questions

These are the stream's research children (to be opened as issues, `Part of #212`):

1. **Consent & regulatory record** — What exactly is approved, by whom, under which pathway (council RMA consents vs Fast-track FTA104), with what conditions? Pull the council decisions and the MfE record as primary sources; establish whether public submissions exist and what they say.
2. **Energy** — Real MW by phase, the Mercury PPOA terms, grid impact, and an honest Tiwai/national-demand comparison replacing the garbled "twice Tiwai" meme.
3. **Water** — Consented take and discharge volumes, source aquifer, treatment/swale design, verified against the Environment Southland consent record (not reporting).
4. **Economics & ownership** — Who DataGrid is (Companies Office record), the backers, reconciling the $3b/$3.5b/$5.1b capex spread and the 550-vs-1,200 jobs spread, claimed vs independent GDP/jobs estimates.
5. **Claims vs confirmed** — Catalogue what critics allege (petition, wetland, Ōreti Beach disturbance, power/water fears) and what DataGrid states, mapping each to confirmed / contested / unverifiable.
