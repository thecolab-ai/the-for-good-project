# Post-settlement iwi ventures show success when matched to durable assets and partners, and struggle when concentrated in shock-exposed sectors

## Executive answer

- Public evidence supports venture-level case studies, not a clean success-rate denominator: annual reports and registers show individual exits, closures, losses, recoveries and partnerships, but they do not disclose every investment on a comparable return basis. [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2024/25](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/TRONT-AR2025-new.pdf); [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf)
- The clearest success pattern is patient control of scarce whenua or strategic assets, then partnering to add capital or capability while retaining land ownership: Waikato-Tainui says Ruakura converted a dairy farm into an inland port/logistics hub whose value grew 100-fold over 12 years, and its Brookfield model lets TGH contribute whenua on long-term ground leases while Brookfield funds new buildings and Waikato-Tainui retains whenua ownership. [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf)
- The clearest failure pattern is concentrated exposure to volatile operating sectors: Ngāi Tahu wound down Oha Honey after sector challenges, previous losses and a 2019 write-down, while Ngāti Awa-owned White Island Tours ceased trading after the 2019 Whakaari eruption, was fined and ordered to pay reparation, entered liquidation in 2024, and was removed from the Companies Register in 2026. [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2024/25](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/TRONT-AR2025-new.pdf); [Ngāi Tahu Hui-ā-Tau 2025 Q&A](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/hui-a-tau-2025-ngai-tahu-holdings-panel-discussion/); [District Court sentencing judgment, 2024](https://thelawassociation.nz/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/WorkSafe-New-Zealand-v-Whakaari-Management-Limited-2024-NZDC-4119.pdf); [White Island Tours liquidators' final report, 2026](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/service/services/documents/379DE536B5FF15D13226B80F8B9445C3); [Companies Office record, accessed 2026](https://app.companiesoffice.govt.nz/companies/app/ui/pages/companies/1609547)
- Tourism and property were not simply "good" or "bad": Ngāi Tahu Tourism was severely hit by COVID-19 but by FY2025 hosted more than 540,000 visitors and had several experiences back to pre-COVID visitor numbers, while Waikato-Tainui hotels and residential assets remained below expectations in FY2025 and hotel values fell 9%. [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2019/20](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/Te-Runanga-o-Ngai-Tahu-Annual-Report-2020.pdf); [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2024/25](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/TRONT-AR2025-new.pdf); [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf)
- Smaller iwi evidence points to the same risk discipline rather than a different rule: Te Rarawa reports commercial asset growth from $32.6m in 2016 to $115.2m in 2025, but also slowed new investments, protected liquidity and reviewed Bells Produce after weather, inflation, supply-chain and road-closure pressures hit performance. [Te Rarawa annual report 2024/25](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/files/annual-reports/annual-report-2024-25.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium -- the named venture facts are well supported by primary annual reports, court records and Companies Office documents, but the pattern explanation is a case-study synthesis from uneven public disclosure rather than a statistical outcome study.

## Evidence

### Scope and method

This finding answers issue #493 by sampling ventures with enough public evidence to classify outcomes at venture level: Ngāi Tahu's Oha Honey, tourism recovery, GoBus exit, forestry-rights sale and Mint Aotearoa; Waikato-Tainui's Ruakura/Brookfield logistics partnership, hotels, residential property, Ruakura Energy and fisheries exposure; Ngāti Awa's White Island Tours; Te Rarawa's Bells Produce, farming and Māhuru housing development; and Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei's property-heavy losses and planned Eastcliffe divestment. [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2024/25](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/TRONT-AR2025-new.pdf); [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf); [Te Rarawa annual report 2024/25](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/files/annual-reports/annual-report-2024-25.pdf); [Ngāti Whātua Ōrākei financial statements 2024/25](https://ngatiwhatuaorakei.com/media/c51kskad/nwo-trust-nga-taua-ki-pu-tea-202425_digi.pdf)

I used public sources only, so "succeeded", "struggled" and "failed" mean publicly observable results: profitable or expanding operations, asset sales/exits, write-downs, wind-downs, liquidation/removal, reported underperformance, or management statements about consolidation and liquidity. [Companies Register liquidation guidance](https://companies-register.companiesoffice.govt.nz/help-centre/when-your-company-fails/what-happens-during-liquidation/)

### Patterns across cases

**Asset control plus partnership looks more resilient than outright operating exposure.** Ruakura and Mint Aotearoa both use partnership structures, but Ruakura already has land-use conversion, tenants, port activity and debt reduction in the public record, while Mint Aotearoa is still pipeline-stage. [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf); [TGH FY2026 half-year report](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/agriy1zw/tgh-half-year-report-fy2026.pdf); [Ngāi Tahu Hui-ā-Tau 2025 Q&A](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/hui-a-tau-2025-ngai-tahu-holdings-panel-discussion/)

**Diversification is not an abstract governance slogan; it shows up directly in downturns.** Waikato-Tainui says the WRLT fund's FY2025 -2% loss would have been roughly double without global equities and that the fund holds 80% of value in local real-estate-related assets, while Ngāi Tahu says its balanced portfolio helped it recover from COVID-19, Oha Honey and accounting/market shocks. [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf); [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2024/25](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/TRONT-AR2025-new.pdf)

**Tourism exposure produced both the sharpest shock and some of the clearest recovery evidence.** Ngāi Tahu's COVID-19 tourism pause and FY2025 recovery show that tourism ventures can rebound, while White Island Tours shows that some tourism risks are catastrophic rather than cyclical. [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2019/20](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/Te-Runanga-o-Ngai-Tahu-Annual-Report-2020.pdf); [Ngāi Tahu annual report 2024/25](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/assets/Documents/TRONT-AR2025-new.pdf); [District Court sentencing judgment, 2024](https://thelawassociation.nz/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/WorkSafe-New-Zealand-v-Whakaari-Management-Limited-2024-NZDC-4119.pdf)

**Primary-sector and food ventures are exposed to climate, commodity and logistics shocks.** Oha Honey's losses and wind-down, Te Rarawa's Bells Produce cashflow review, Ngāi Tahu's dry-weather farming constraints and Waikato-Tainui's inconsistent Kawaroa Forest returns all point to operating risk that is not solved by iwi ownership alone. [Ngāi Tahu Hui-ā-Tau 2025 Q&A](https://ngaitahu.iwi.nz/hui-a-tau-2025-ngai-tahu-holdings-panel-discussion/); [Otago Daily Times, 2019](https://www.odt.co.nz/business/honey-business-not-so-sweet-ngai-tahu-holdings); [Te Rarawa annual report 2024/25](https://www.terarawa.iwi.nz/files/annual-reports/annual-report-2024-25.pdf); [Waikato-Tainui annual report 2025](https://www.tgh.co.nz/media/ptud0oru/wt_annual-report_final-pop-ups.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A full iwi-approved investment register showing acquisition cost, capital injected, exit proceeds, write-downs, dividends, debt and social outcomes for each venture would replace this case-study synthesis with a more defensible success/failure rate.
- Non-public board papers or investment reviews could change the classification of individual ventures, especially where annual reports disclose a wind-down or loss but not lifetime returns.
- Completion evidence for still-open ventures would change the ranking: Ruakura/Brookfield, Ruakura Energy, Mint Aotearoa, Māhuru housing and Eastcliffe divestment are current or partly forward-looking cases.
- I could not verify a full lifetime return for GoBus, Oha Honey, White Island Tours, Bells Produce, Ruakura or Eastcliffe from public sources alone.
- This finding does not assess whether commercial outcomes met iwi-defined tikanga, mana motuhake or whānau wellbeing goals; that requires iwi mandate and Māori data-governance review, not just public financial documents.

## Open follow-up questions

- What would an iwi-controlled venture reporting schema look like if it protected commercial confidentiality but still disclosed acquisition, capital-at-risk, distributions, write-downs and exits consistently?
- Which governance controls changed after failed ventures such as Oha Honey and White Island Tours, and are those controls visible to members?
- How should reviewers classify ventures that deliver social or whenua-retention outcomes before they deliver cash returns?
