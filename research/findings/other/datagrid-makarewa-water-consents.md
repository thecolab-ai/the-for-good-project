# DataGrid Makarewa's Environment Southland consents allow a 7 L/s Waihopai-zone groundwater take for evaporative cooling and potable supply, plus 5 m3/day treated wastewater discharge to land

## Executive answer

- Environment Southland granted DataGrid NZ Limited Partnership's Makarewa regional consents on 11 March 2026; the package includes permits to discharge treated wastewater to land, take groundwater for operation, take and discharge groundwater for construction dewatering, remove one natural inland wetland, and install/occupy the coastal marine area with the data cable [Environment Southland public notice, 13 Mar 2026](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z); [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).
- The operational water permit is **AUTH-20252550-03**: it allows groundwater from four listed Waihopai groundwater-zone bores at **7 L/s**, **604,800 L/day**, and **220,752,000 L/year** for **cooling water and potable supply** at 342, 350, 360 and 370 Flora Road East [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf). The consent itself has no seasonal monthly cap; instead it requires metering, 15-minute records, daily telemetry, a pre-abstraction borefield pump test, wetland monitoring, and allows review to reduce the rate/volume if monitoring shows the take cannot be fully used [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf).
- The cooling design is **evaporative**, not purely closed-loop dry cooling: Environment Southland's independent groundwater reviewer describes the application as seeking groundwater abstraction for "evaporative cooling purposes" [LWP technical review, 13 Aug 2025](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Datagrid_Makarewa_Review%20of%20Groundwater%20Assessment.pdf). The final water consent's annual volume is lower than the application volume described in that review, which was 10 L/s and 315,360 m3/year [LWP technical review, 13 Aug 2025](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Datagrid_Makarewa_Review%20of%20Groundwater%20Assessment.pdf); [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf).
- The operational wastewater discharge permit is **AUTH-20252550-01**: it allows up to **5 m3/day** of treated wastewater to land from an onsite wastewater system, requiring secondary treatment through an approved proprietary device and disposal to an evapotranspiration-seepage system or equivalent disposal field under AS/NZS 1547:2012 [Discharge Permit AUTH-20252550-01](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Discharge%20Permit%20AUTH-20252550-01.pdf). The commissioner's decision describes this as amenities-block wastewater from sinks, emergency showers and toilets, equivalent in nature and scale to septic effluent from 2-3 large households [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).
- The wetland issue is two-part: the consent authorises removal of the **0.24 ha Northern Upper Wetland** and a pond/fringing wetland, but the higher-value **Taylor Road Wetland** is to be protected by monitoring and stormwater recharge through an infiltration trench if required [Land Use Consent AUTH-20252550-05](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Land%20Use%20Consent%20AUTH-20252550-05.pdf); [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf). The "very low ecological value" phrase applies to the removed Northern Upper Wetland, while Environment Southland's wetland ecology review says the Taylor Road Wetland has high ecological values and could, without mitigation, lose wetland values from the large groundwater take [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf); [Environment Southland wetland ecology review](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Assessment%20of%20Data%20Grid%20Wetland%20Ecology%20report%20-%20Darin.pdf).

**Overall confidence:** High for the consented rates, source zone, wastewater volume, and consent conditions because they come from the final Environment Southland consent instruments; Medium for ecological sufficiency and long-term hydrological effects because the documents themselves require pre-abstraction testing, wetland monitoring, and adaptive mitigation before operation.

## Evidence

### What was granted

Environment Southland's public notice says the resource consents sought by DataGrid NZ Partnership Limited were granted for a datacentre near Makarewa, and lists nine regional consents: wastewater-to-land discharge, air discharge from diesel generators, operational groundwater take, construction dewatering take, wetland removal, coastal occupation and cable installation, dewatering discharge, and earthworks near a wetland [Environment Southland public notice, 13 Mar 2026](https://www.es.govt.nz/about-us/news?item=id%3A2xhpahbzw17q9s3f3x4z). The commissioner's decision grants the same package for 35 years and describes the datacentre as providing up to 240 MW of IT capacity [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).

### Operational groundwater take

The final operational water permit, **AUTH-20252550-03**, grants DataGrid the right to take and use groundwater for cooling water and potable supply at the datacentre from listed bores **CG10/0238, CG10/0239, CG10/0240 and CG10/0241** at 342, 350, 360 and 370 Flora Road East, Makarewa, in the **Waihopai groundwater zone**, Waikiwi catchment, Oreti physiographic zone [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf). The consent's Condition 2 caps total abstraction at **7 L/s**, **604,800 L/day**, and **220,752,000 L/year** [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf). The permit requires abstraction metering and datalogging at 15-minute intervals, daily telemetry to Environment Southland, meter verification, a full borefield constant-rate pumping test before abstraction, a Wetland Monitoring Plan, a Ground Settlement Monitoring Plan, and review powers that include adjusting the rate or volume if monitoring shows the consented volume cannot be fully used [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf).

Environment Southland's groundwater-zone page describes the Waihopai Groundwater Management Zone as about **42,000 ha**, covering the Waihopai River and Waikiwi Stream catchments, with a spatially extensive unconfined aquifer system in weathered alluvial deposits whose bore yields are typically low to moderate [Environment Southland Waihopai GMZ page](https://www.es.govt.nz/environment/water/groundwater/groundwater-management-zones/waihopai). The LWP review, prepared for Environment Southland, describes the local Makarewa water-bearing unit as silty, sandy gravels under 3-7 m of clay or silt, over Gore Lignite Measures, with static water levels varying by location [LWP technical review, 13 Aug 2025](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Datagrid_Makarewa_Review%20of%20Groundwater%20Assessment.pdf).

The final consent has no explicit seasonal monthly cap; its operational control is an annual cap plus metering/telemetry, pre-operation pump testing, wetland monitoring, and review conditions [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf). The commissioner's decision says allocation in the groundwater zone, including DataGrid's proposed take, is below 9% of the allocation limit; even if construction dewatering and operational cooling occurred in the same water year, the total would remain below 10% of the limit [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).

### Cooling design

The primary consent does not use the phrase "closed-loop" or "dry cooling"; it authorises groundwater for "cooling water and potable supply" [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf). The independent groundwater review commissioned by Environment Southland is more specific: it says the application sought authorisation to enable groundwater abstraction for **evaporative cooling purposes** at the proposed Makarewa data centre [LWP technical review, 13 Aug 2025](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Datagrid_Makarewa_Review%20of%20Groundwater%20Assessment.pdf). The same review states that the application proposed five production bores, 10 L/s, and 315,360 m3/year, but the final consent caps the operational take at four listed bores, 7 L/s, and 220,752 m3/year [LWP technical review, 13 Aug 2025](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Datagrid_Makarewa_Review%20of%20Groundwater%20Assessment.pdf); [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf).

### Wastewater and other water discharges

The operational wastewater consent, **AUTH-20252550-01**, authorises up to **5 m3/day** of treated wastewater to land from an onsite wastewater treatment and disposal system at 370 Flora Road East [Discharge Permit AUTH-20252550-01](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Discharge%20Permit%20AUTH-20252550-01.pdf). The system must include secondary treatment from an approved proprietary device and discharge to an evapotranspiration-seepage system or alternative disposal field in accordance with AS/NZS 1547:2012, with final design submitted to Environment Southland for certification before the consent is exercised [Discharge Permit AUTH-20252550-01](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Discharge%20Permit%20AUTH-20252550-01.pdf). The permit also requires six-monthly groundwater sampling from well CG10/0224 for conductivity, nitrate nitrogen, total nitrogen, dissolved oxygen, E. coli, bromine, chloride and dissolved iron [Discharge Permit AUTH-20252550-01](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Discharge%20Permit%20AUTH-20252550-01.pdf).

Construction dewatering is separate from the operational cooling-water take. **AUTH-20252550-04** allows construction dewatering groundwater take of up to **60 L/s**, **5,184,000 L/day**, and **321,408,000 L total** [Water Permit Dewatering Take AUTH-20252550-04](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Dewatering%20Take%20AUTH-20252550-04.pdf). **AUTH-20252550-08** authorises discharge of dewatering water to land; the commissioner's decision says the dewatering water will pass through treatment tanks to remove sediment and ultimately flow to wetlands south of the site [Discharge Permit Dewatering AUTH-20252550-08](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Discharge%20Permit%20Dewatering%20AUTH-20252550-08.pdf); [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf). The stormwater recharge used to maintain Taylor Road Wetland water levels is treated as a permitted stormwater discharge rather than a separate discharge consent in the decision [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf).

### Wetlands and hydrology

The land-use consent **AUTH-20252550-05** authorises excavation and removal of the **Northern Upper Wetland** and a pond with fringing wetland at 370 Flora Road East [Land Use Consent AUTH-20252550-05](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Land%20Use%20Consent%20AUTH-20252550-05.pdf). It requires a Wetland Management Plan before removal, wetland extension/enhancement at least equal to the **2,400 m2** Northern Upper Wetland area being removed, annual progress reporting until completion, three-yearly ecological value assessments after completion, and bird-nesting checks if works occur between 1 July and 31 March [Land Use Consent AUTH-20252550-05](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Land%20Use%20Consent%20AUTH-20252550-05.pdf).

The commissioner's decision says the Northern Upper Wetland is approximately **0.24 ha**, has **very low ecological value**, resembles a low wet area of exotic pasture, and had no indigenous species present during site surveys [commissioner's decision APP-20252550](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Application%20documents/Resource%20Consent%20Decision%20APP-20252550.pdf). That should not be read as applying to all wetlands on the site: Environment Southland's wetland ecology review says Taylor Road Wetland has **high ecological values**, and that without mitigation it would likely transform into dryland with loss of wetland values due to the groundwater take [Environment Southland wetland ecology review](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Assessment%20of%20Data%20Grid%20Wetland%20Ecology%20report%20-%20Darin.pdf).

The operational water permit requires an infiltration trench along the northern edge of Taylor Road Wetland, outside the wetland itself, to discharge treated stormwater to the aquifer to replicate natural groundwater inflows and maintain the wetland hydrological regime [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf). The groundwater review considered this mitigation technically plausible but said the assessment gave limited practical detail, and recommended hydrologist and ecologist sign-off, water-level triggers, and ongoing monitoring because drawdown effects would develop slowly over time [LWP technical review, 13 Aug 2025](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Datagrid_Makarewa_Review%20of%20Groundwater%20Assessment.pdf).

## What would change this conclusion

- A final certified cooling-system design showing materially different water recirculation, dry-cooling, or wastewater/cooling-bleed handling would change the cooling-design conclusion; the consent package confirms evaporative cooling through the technical review, but it does not publish a final engineering design.
- The pre-abstraction borefield pump test could change the usable take: the water permit requires the test before abstraction and allows review of the consented rate/volume if monitoring shows the consented volume cannot be fully utilised [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf).
- A hydrologist/ecologist should review whether the Taylor Road Wetland mitigation is likely to work over decades. The consent and review documents identify uncertainty, require monitoring and adaptive management, and depend on a stormwater-fed infiltration trench to replicate groundwater inflows [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf); [LWP technical review](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Technical%20comments/Datagrid_Makarewa_Review%20of%20Groundwater%20Assessment.pdf).
- I could not verify actual future water use, only consented maximums and conditions; no operational abstraction data exists yet because the consent starts from 11 March 2026 and pre-abstraction steps are still required [Water Permit AUTH-20252550-03](https://www.es.govt.nz/repository/libraries/id%3A26gi9ayo517q9stt81sd/hierarchy/environment/consents/documents/Datagrid%20resource%20consents/Resource%20consents/Water%20Permit%20Groundwater%20take%20AUTH-20252550-03.pdf).
- The public consent-layer comparisons are approximate because the ArcGIS layer stores details as text, can duplicate a consent across multiple points/bores, and does not by itself provide a clean deduplicated hydrological-allocation table [Environment Southland Current Resource Consents layer](https://maps.es.govt.nz/server/rest/services/Public/Consents/MapServer/2). They are useful context, not a substitute for Environment Southland's internal allocation accounting.

## Open follow-up questions

- No new issue opened. The key remaining question is technical rather than a separate research task: a hydrologist/ecologist should assess whether the Taylor Road Wetland recharge and trigger framework is adequate once the certified plans and pre-abstraction pump-test results exist.
