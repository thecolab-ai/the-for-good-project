# New Zealand's public methamphetamine data show a sustained 2024-2026 consumption shock, but not proof that meth caused crime harms

## Executive answer

- **The current public signal is more than a one-quarter spike.** Police wastewater reporting shows tested-site methamphetamine consumption rising from a minimum 732 kg in 2023 to 1,434 kg in 2024, then 1,751 kg in 2025, and the Q1 2026 average was 36.6 kg per week, 15% above the previous four-quarter average. [NZ Police 2024 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2024-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf)
- **The best wording is "sustained 2024-2026 market shock", not yet "proven permanent step-change".** The public wastewater series supports a sustained elevated consumption level after 2023, but proving a structural step-change would require full-year 2026 and 2027 wastewater results on stable sites, alongside public price/purity, seizure, treatment, justice, and harm indicators that stay elevated after the current supply shock. [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf); [Ministry of Justice, actions to combat methamphetamine](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/key-initiatives/actions-to-combat-meth/)
- **Survey data do not show the same sharp expansion in the number of people using.** A 2026 Ministry of Health briefing says 1.3% of adults, about 56,000 people, reported amphetamine-type stimulant use in 2023/24 and that this was not a statistically significant increase over several years; the same briefing says the 2024 wastewater rise appears more likely to be existing users using more rather than many new users. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)
- **Public data support association with organised crime and community harm, not simple causal claims.** Customs reports transnational organised-crime smuggling pressure and over 6 tonnes of methamphetamine or methamphetamine-yield seized at or before the border in 2024/25, while Ministry of Justice court data show 7,963 finalised methamphetamine charges and 3,693 people with finalised methamphetamine charges in 2025; those are supply, enforcement, and co-offending indicators, not proof that methamphetamine caused specific violent offending or community harm. [New Zealand Customs Service Annual Report 2025](https://www.customs.govt.nz/media/squnnrax/new-zealand-customs-service-annual-report-2025.pdf); [Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/4JcAKF_Methamphetamine-offences_dec2025_v1.0.xlsx)
- **Harm and inequity are visible, but the public treatment data are blunt.** The Drug Harm Index 2023 put 2021 methamphetamine harm at $864.9m and Police wastewater-derived estimates put 2025 tested-site methamphetamine social harm at $1.8b; Northland's Q1 2026 per-capita wastewater rate was about four times Southern's rate, and specialist addiction services served 44,850 people in 2023/24, more than 5,000 fewer than five years earlier, but the public service series is for addiction services overall rather than methamphetamine-only treatment demand. [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf); [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the wastewater, Customs, Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Health, and Te Hiringa Mahara sources are official or public-sector sources, but public data still cannot isolate methamphetamine-only treatment demand, prove individual-level crime causation, or distinguish a permanent structural step-change from a multi-year supply shock without more post-2025 observations.

## Evidence

### Wastewater: the volume signal is large and sustained

Police's National Drugs in Wastewater Programme tests wastewater for one week each month, and the Q1 2026 report says current sites cover almost 80% of the New Zealand population, while warning that data are representative of tested sites and should not be extrapolated to nearby untested communities. [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf)

The tested-site methamphetamine series was flat-to-lower before 2024, with minimum annual consumption of 833 kg in 2019, 778 kg in 2020, 826 kg in 2021, 832 kg in 2022, and 732 kg in 2023; it then rose to 1,434 kg in 2024 and 1,751 kg in 2025. [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf)

Police described 2024 as an "unprecedented" 96% increase from 2023, and the 2024 overview says newly added testing sites contributed about 38 kg of methamphetamine, so the increase was not mainly a site-coverage artefact. [NZ Police 2024 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2024-annual-overview.pdf)

Police described 2025 as a year in which methamphetamine consumption "continued the markedly elevated levels first observed in 2024", with the higher rate maintained throughout 2025 and lower prices likely contributing to increased demand. [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf)

Q1 2026 then averaged 36.6 kg per week across tested sites, which Police reported as 4.7 kg, or 15%, above the previous four-quarter average, with a weekly social-harm estimate of $38.4m using the Drug Harm Index method. [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf)

**Interpretation:** public data already refute the idea that 2024 was just a short-lived one-quarter spike, because the elevated level persisted across 2025 and into Q1 2026. [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf)

**Limit:** public data do not yet prove a permanent structural step-change, because the public series has only two complete elevated years, Q1 2026 is one quarter, and Police cautions that wastewater estimates are tested-site estimates rather than whole-country extrapolations. [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf)

### Survey prevalence: more wastewater volume does not mean many more users

The Ministry of Health's 2026 overview briefing reports that 1.3% of adults, or about 56,000 people, used amphetamine-type stimulants in the previous 12 months in the 2023/24 New Zealand Health Survey, and says this was not a statistically significant change over recent years. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

The same briefing says the 2024 wastewater increase appears to reflect current users consuming larger quantities rather than a large increase in the number of people using methamphetamine. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

The Ministry of Health's mental health and problematic substance-use report gives a wider screening signal: moderate or high-risk problematic amphetamine-type stimulant use increased from 1.2% in 2016/17 to 1.8% in pooled 2021/22-2022/23, but the report says its screening tools are not diagnostic prevalence estimates. [Ministry of Health mental health and problematic substance-use report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/mental_health_and_problematic_substance_use_report_-_final.pdf)

The 2023/24 Health Survey inequity pattern reported in the Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing was higher among men, people aged 25-34, Māori, disabled people, and people in the most deprived neighbourhoods, with women in the most deprived neighbourhoods reported as 10.33 times as likely as women in the least deprived neighbourhoods to report recent amphetamine-type stimulant use. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

**Interpretation:** the best public reading is a consumption-intensity and availability shock, not a proven population-wide prevalence explosion. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf)

**Limit:** the Health Survey item is amphetamine-type stimulants rather than methamphetamine-only prevalence, and self-report surveys can miss high-risk groups such as people in prison, unstable housing, or acute crisis. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [Ministry of Health mental health and problematic substance-use report](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-06/mental_health_and_problematic_substance_use_report_-_final.pdf)

### Harm estimates: DHI gives a conservative model, wastewater moves the current estimate

The Drug Harm Index 2023 estimated 2021 methamphetamine harm at $864.9m, split into $451.26m personal harm and $413.65m community harm. [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf)

The Drug Harm Index 2023 estimated methamphetamine harm at $1,048,211 per kg, while warning that the index captures only harms with robust available data and excludes some harms, including costs borne by children of people who use drugs. [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf)

Police's 2025 wastewater overview applied the Drug Harm Index method to the 2025 tested-site wastewater estimate and reported 1,751 kg of methamphetamine, 88 million doses, and $1.8b in social harm. [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf)

The Ministry of Justice public methamphetamine page reports a conservative 2024 methamphetamine social-harm estimate of $1.5b and links it to the 2024 wastewater doubling. [Ministry of Justice, actions to combat methamphetamine](https://www.justice.govt.nz/justice-sector-policy/key-initiatives/actions-to-combat-meth/)

**Interpretation:** the harm estimate has increased because the public wastewater volume estimate increased, not because a new public source has directly measured all downstream health, family, crime, and service harms in real time. [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf); [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf)

**Limit:** the public harm-cost numbers are modelled estimates and should not be read as audited expenditure or proof that each modelled harm was caused by methamphetamine in a specific community. [Drug Harm Index 2023](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/2023-illicit-drug-harm-index.pdf)

### Regional inequity: wastewater, survey, charges, and treatment all point to uneven burden

Northland's Q1 2026 wastewater rate was 2,282 mg/day/1,000 people, compared with an all-sites rate of 1,352 mg/day/1,000 people and Southern's 574 mg/day/1,000 people. [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf)

The 2025 annual wastewater overview says high per-capita methamphetamine locations were predominantly in the North Island, while the 2024 annual overview says high per-capita locations were largely regional North Island towns with high socioeconomic deprivation. [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police 2024 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2024-annual-overview.pdf)

The Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing reports higher 2023/24 amphetamine-type stimulant use among Māori, disabled people, and people in the most deprived neighbourhoods. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

The Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook reports 3,693 people with finalised methamphetamine charges in 2025, with Māori recorded in 1,823 of those people charged and 1,407 of the 2,801 people convicted, while warning that ethnicity is a multiple-response field and that criminal justice data are affected by recording, enforcement, and court processes. [Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/4JcAKF_Methamphetamine-offences_dec2025_v1.0.xlsx)

Te Hiringa Mahara reports that 44,850 people accessed specialist addiction services in 2023/24, including 17,579 Māori and 4,556 Pacific peoples, and says total-response ethnicity means people can be counted in more than one ethnic group. [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf)

**Interpretation:** multiple public systems point to higher burden in already disadvantaged communities, but no single public dataset can allocate cause across drug availability, poverty, policing, service access, trauma, housing instability, and local market dynamics. [NZ Police 2024 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2024-annual-overview.pdf); [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/4JcAKF_Methamphetamine-offences_dec2025_v1.0.xlsx)

### Supply and organised crime: public indicators are strong, but they are not causal harm proof

Customs says transnational organised-crime groups continue to smuggle drugs and illicit goods, and its 2025 annual report describes a sharp increase in drug smuggling via passengers, unaccompanied baggage, and "rip-on/rip-off" concealment methods. [New Zealand Customs Service Annual Report 2025](https://www.customs.govt.nz/media/squnnrax/new-zealand-customs-service-annual-report-2025.pdf)

Customs reports that over 6 tonnes of methamphetamine or methamphetamine-yield from precursors was seized at the border or offshore on the way to New Zealand in 2024/25, and that methamphetamine made up about 83% of its $7.6b potential social harm prevented in that year. [New Zealand Customs Service Annual Report 2025](https://www.customs.govt.nz/media/squnnrax/new-zealand-customs-service-annual-report-2025.pdf)

The Ministry of Justice Cabinet paper on methamphetamine-related harm says a global glut, lower prices, and transnational organised-crime sophistication are contributing to greater availability in New Zealand; the same paper says the wholesale methamphetamine price fell from about NZ$160,000 per kg in 2019 to about NZ$100,000 per kg in 2024. [Ministry of Justice Cabinet paper on methamphetamine-related harm](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/Action-to-Address-Methamphetamine-Related-Harm_FINAL.pdf)

The Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook reports 7,963 finalised methamphetamine charges in 2025, including 4,303 possession/use charges, 3,331 dealing/distribution charges, 249 international trafficking charges, and 76 manufacture/cultivation charges. [Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/4JcAKF_Methamphetamine-offences_dec2025_v1.0.xlsx)

The same workbook reports 2,872 conviction instances in 2025, of which 324 were methamphetamine-only and 2,548 included methamphetamine and other offences. [Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/4JcAKF_Methamphetamine-offences_dec2025_v1.0.xlsx)

**What public data can say:** supply pressure, importation, dealing/distribution charges, co-offending, and organised-crime involvement are all visible in public Customs, Justice, and Police-linked sources. [New Zealand Customs Service Annual Report 2025](https://www.customs.govt.nz/media/squnnrax/new-zealand-customs-service-annual-report-2025.pdf); [Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/4JcAKF_Methamphetamine-offences_dec2025_v1.0.xlsx); [Ministry of Justice Cabinet paper on methamphetamine-related harm](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/Action-to-Address-Methamphetamine-Related-Harm_FINAL.pdf)

**What public data cannot say:** those public indicators do not prove that methamphetamine caused a specific offence, that a district's crime trend was caused by wastewater consumption, or that treatment recipients are the people driving violent crime. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [Ministry of Justice methamphetamine offences workbook](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/4JcAKF_Methamphetamine-offences_dec2025_v1.0.xlsx)

### Crime and community harm: association is real, causal inference is constrained

The Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing says people involved with methamphetamine are more likely to commit offences and experience victimisation, while also saying Police analysis suggests methamphetamine involvement may sometimes be a byproduct of involvement in criminal offending rather than the cause of that offending. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

The University of Otago's Christchurch Health and Development Study reported that people who used methamphetamine at least weekly were more than twice as likely to be victims of intimate partner victimisation and almost five times as likely to perpetrate violence as people who had not used methamphetamine, and PubMed's abstract for the peer-reviewed article describes methamphetamine use as an independent risk factor for violence perpetration and victimisation in the cohort. [University of Otago news release](https://www.otago.ac.nz/news/newsroom/methamphetamine-use-and-its-impact-on-violence-laid-bare-in-world-first-study); [PubMed abstract](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31927159/)

The Australian Institute of Criminology's review says methamphetamine use is associated with domestic-violence offending but also highlights developmental and dispositional factors, including prior violence and victimisation, that complicate causal interpretation. [Australian Institute of Criminology review](https://www.aic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2020-05/is_methamphetamine_use_associated_with_domestic_violence.pdf)

The Ministry of Health briefing also says people already receiving alcohol and other drug treatment for methamphetamine or other substance use are unlikely to be involved in committing violent offences, which matters because a treatment-demand dataset should not be treated as an offender dataset. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

**Interpretation:** public data justify saying methamphetamine is associated with serious community harm and organised-crime markets; they do not justify reducing community harm to "meth causes crime" without individual-level longitudinal evidence, counterfactual controls, and separation of use, supply, enforcement, and deprivation. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [Australian Institute of Criminology review](https://www.aic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2020-05/is_methamphetamine_use_associated_with_domestic_violence.pdf)

### Treatment and service demand: pressure is visible, meth-specific public data are thin

Te Hiringa Mahara reports that 44,850 people accessed specialist addiction services in 2023/24, more than 5,000 fewer than in 2019/20. [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf)

Te Hiringa Mahara reports that 60,016 referrals were made to specialist addiction services in 2023/24, 14.6% below the 2020/21 peak, and that declined referrals increased from 4.7% in 2019/20 to 8.6% in 2023/24. [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf)

Te Hiringa Mahara says the reduction in referrals is not evidence of lower demand and may reflect workforce constraints, complex needs, and service capacity, and its accompanying news release says the methamphetamine surge is likely increasing pressure on services. [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara service-pressure release](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/news-and-resources/pressure-on-addiction-treatment-services-highlighted/)

Te Hiringa Mahara reports that 75.8% of specialist addiction service users were seen within three weeks in 2023/24, below the 80% target, and that Māori service users' three-week access rate fell from 77.8% in 2022/23 to 76.1% in 2023/24. [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf)

The Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing lists methamphetamine-relevant services including Te Ara Oranga, the Meth Helpline, telehealth, primary and community services, withdrawal and residential treatment, alcohol and other drug treatment courts, drug checking, and peer-support services, but it also says methamphetamine has no established pharmacological treatment comparable to alcohol or opioid dependence. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

**Interpretation:** public treatment data show system pressure and inequity in addiction services, but they do not publish a clean methamphetamine-only demand, wait-time, outcome, or relapse series that can be lined up against wastewater by region. [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf); [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A full-year 2026 and 2027 wastewater series showing methamphetamine consumption returning near the 2019-2023 tested-site range would change the conclusion from "sustained shock" toward "temporary market spike". [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf)
- A full-year 2026 and 2027 wastewater series remaining near or above the 2024-2025 range, with stable testing-site coverage and method notes, would strengthen the conclusion to "structural step-change in public wastewater consumption". [NZ Police 2025 annual wastewater overview](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-2025-annual-overview.pdf)
- Public price, purity, importation, and seizure series showing that the 2024-2026 volume increase persisted after the current global supply glut and lower-price period ended would strengthen the step-change interpretation. [Ministry of Justice Cabinet paper on methamphetamine-related harm](https://www.justice.govt.nz/assets/Documents/Publications/Action-to-Address-Methamphetamine-Related-Harm_FINAL.pdf); [New Zealand Customs Service Annual Report 2025](https://www.customs.govt.nz/media/squnnrax/new-zealand-customs-service-annual-report-2025.pdf)
- A methamphetamine-only treatment-demand dataset by region, referral source, wait time, completion, relapse, and unmet need would change the treatment-demand confidence level, because current public service data are addiction-wide. [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf); [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf)
- Individual-level or small-area longitudinal evidence linking changes in methamphetamine exposure to changes in victimisation, offending, health presentations, and service need after controlling for deprivation, policing intensity, prior offending, and market supply would change the causal-crime confidence level. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [Australian Institute of Criminology review](https://www.aic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2020-05/is_methamphetamine_use_associated_with_domestic_violence.pdf)
- I could not verify a public methamphetamine-only adult prevalence series for 2024-2026, a public purity-adjusted consumption series, a public methamphetamine-only treatment outcomes series, or a public district-level causal estimate of methamphetamine's contribution to violent crime. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [NZ Police Q1 2026 wastewater results](https://www.police.govt.nz/sites/default/files/publications/wastewater-results-quarter-1-2026.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara addiction services data summary](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/assets/Reports/SPE3-infographics/MHWC-addiction-services-data-summary-May-2025.pdf)
- Lived-experience and provider review would be needed before using this finding to design services, because public aggregate data do not show what affected people and communities would find safe, accessible, or useful. [Ministry of Health methamphetamine briefing](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-05/H2024057684-Briefing-Overview-of-methamphetamine-use-in-New-Zealand.pdf); [Te Hiringa Mahara service-pressure release](https://www.mhwc.govt.nz/news-and-resources/pressure-on-addiction-treatment-services-highlighted/)
