# How ready is NZ's health system for the zoonotic and pandemic tail-risk of H5N1?

## Executive answer

- **NZ has the pieces of a human-health response in place, but most are modest, and the load-bearing quantities are not public.** The Ministry of Health (MoH) and Health NZ hold influenza antivirals (including oseltamivir/Tamiflu) and a pre-pandemic vaccine in the National Reserve Supply (NRS), have wired ICU influenza-A subtyping into surveillance to catch severe human cases, and treat the 2024 New Zealand Pandemic Plan as the operative document if sustained human-to-human (H2H) transmission emerges ([MoH briefing H2025068297, 17 Jun 2025, paras 32–35, 28](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). But the antiviral stockpile figure was redacted under s9(2)(c) in that briefing (para 36), and vaccine quantities are withheld "for security reasons" ([Pre-Pandemic Influenza Vaccine Framework, Feb 2026, p1](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)), so an outsider cannot verify sufficiency.

- **The pre-pandemic vaccine NZ actually holds is PANVAX H5N8 — an H5N8 vaccine, not an H5N1 one.** It is egg-based, adjuvanted, made by CSL Seqirus, two doses 21 days apart, 24-month shelf life ([Vaccine Framework, Appendix 2, p24](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf); [Medsafe PANVAX H5N8 data sheet](https://www.medsafe.govt.nz/Profs/Datasheet/p/PanvaxH5N8inj.pdf)). MoH's own framework describes it as a "very limited quantity," enough to vaccinate only "some, not all" priority groups, and only *assumes* it is "moderately effective," relying on possible cross-protection against the circulating H5N1 clade 2.3.4.4b — it is not a matched vaccine.

- **Procurement of the pre-pandemic vaccine sits with Health NZ, not Pharmac** — a change from the usual medicines pathway, made in the 2022 health reforms. Pharmac's role here is limited to the antiviral treatments (which it has agreed to supply through hospital pharmacies) and the seasonal flu vaccine (which does not protect against H5N1) ([Vaccine Framework, pp2, 12](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf); [MoH briefing, para 34](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). A **pandemic-specific** (matched) vaccine "may take months to arrive."

- **Occupationally exposed workers are the group at real risk, and the system for them is guidance-and-PPE-based.** The Communicable Disease Control Manual requires high-risk contacts and exposed people to be assessed, offered post-exposure oseltamivir prophylaxis where appropriate, and put under active monitoring "within one day," with PPE for anyone working with potentially infected birds or animals ([Health NZ, Avian influenza — CDC Manual](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/health-professionals/guidance-standards/topic/conditions/communicable-disease-control-manual/avian-influenza)). This was exercised in the December 2024 Otago H7N6 poultry outbreak: 100+ exposed workers monitored, **zero human cases** ([MoH briefing, para 23](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)).

- **The stated risk is genuinely low today, and the whole edifice rests on a mutation that has not happened.** MoH/ESR assess the current public-health risk as low because H5N1 has never been detected in NZ and there is no sustained H2H transmission anywhere ([MoH briefing, paras 5, 20–21](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). Globally, WHO has recorded ~1,000 human H5N1 cases and ~479 deaths since 2003 (~48% case-fatality), overwhelmingly from animal contact ([WHO cumulative table, to May 2026](https://cdn.who.int/media/docs/default-source/influenza/h5n1-human-case-cumulative-table/2026_table_h5n1_may.pdf)). Independent NZ experts argued in 2024 that NZ should "ramp up" — review timely supply of testing, vaccine, antivirals and IPC equipment, and update the pandemic plan ([Baker, French, Geoghegan et al., PHCC briefing, 15 May 2024](https://www.phcc.org.nz/briefing/potential-avian-influenza-pandemic-time-nz-ramp-preparedness)).

**Overall confidence:** Medium — the *structure* of NZ's readiness is well-documented in current official sources (High), but the *adequacy* of it cannot be assessed because the two most load-bearing numbers (antiviral and vaccine stockpile quantities) are deliberately withheld, and the vaccine's real-world effectiveness against H5N1 is unmeasurable in advance.

## Evidence

### The current situation: confirmed status vs. scenario

Keep these separate. **Confirmed:** HPAI H5N1 clade 2.3.4.4b has never been detected in New Zealand; NZ's only HPAI event was a contained H7N6 outbreak on an Otago free-range poultry farm in December 2024 (culling and decontamination finished 27 December 2024; no human cases among 100+ monitored workers; no human H7N6 cases have ever been reported globally) ([MoH briefing, para 23](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). MPI assesses it is "possible for H5N1 to arrive via migratory birds in the next 12 months," and it has reached the Antarctic Peninsula and subantarctic territories but not yet Australia or the South Pacific ([MoH briefing, para 16](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)).

**Scenario (not current):** the load-bearing risk is a mutation or reassortment that gives H5N1 sustained, efficient H2H transmission — "well recognised by international bodies and experts" as a pandemic threat, but which has not occurred ([MoH briefing, paras 19–20](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). MoH and ESR (now the NZ Institute for Public Health and Forensic Science) rate the overall human public-health risk to NZ as **low**, citing very low likelihood of importing a human case and no sustained H2H spread ([MoH briefing, paras 5, 21](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)).

The global human toll to date is dominated by direct animal exposure. WHO's cumulative table records ~1,000 confirmed human H5N1 cases and ~479 deaths across ~24 countries from 2003 to May 2026 (a ~48% crude case-fatality rate), "almost all… linked to close contact with infected live or dead birds or mammals, or contaminated environments" ([WHO cumulative table, to May 2026](https://cdn.who.int/media/docs/default-source/influenza/h5n1-human-case-cumulative-table/2026_table_h5n1_may.pdf); [MoH briefing, para 18](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). The recent US clade-2.3.4.4b cases have been notably milder than that historic average (see US comparator below).

### Antivirals — a real reserve, but the figure is redacted

Oseltamivir (Tamiflu) is the recommended treatment for avian-influenza cases and for some exposed people, "along with two alternative antivirals" ([MoH briefing, para 33](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). MoH and Health NZ have engaged Pharmac to make these treatments available to cases and exposed people through hospital pharmacies ([para 34](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). NZ "has a supply of influenza antivirals, including Tamiflu," held in the NRS for treatment or prophylaxis in a pandemic, and "according to the WHO and infectious diseases experts, Tamiflu is expected to be effective against the H5N1 strains… currently circulating globally" ([para 35](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). The National Reserve Supply is confirmed to include influenza antiviral medicines and PPE ([Vaccine Framework, p2](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf); [MoH, National Reserve Supplies](https://www.health.govt.nz/strategies-initiatives/programmes-and-initiatives/emergency-management/national-reserve-supplies)).

**The stockpile quantity is withheld.** The very next paragraph of the briefing (para 36) is redacted in full under s9(2)(c) of the Official Information Act — the ground for information whose release "would be likely to prejudice measures protecting the health or safety of members of the public" ([MoH briefing, para 36, redacted](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). So the public record confirms a reserve *exists* and is expected to work, but not that it is *sufficient* for a pandemic-scale event. (Note that an earlier peer-reviewed analysis questioned whether NZ's antiviral stockpile was adequately maintained for pandemic preparedness — [Wilson & Baker, 2014, NZMJ/PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24997469/) — an old source, flagged only as historical context, not current fact.)

### Vaccine — modest, and it is an H5N8 vaccine, not H5N1

NZ's pre-pandemic influenza vaccine is part of the NRS and is described repeatedly as a "very limited quantity" and "highly constrained emergency resource" — enough to vaccinate "some, not all" of the priority groups the framework identifies ([Vaccine Framework, Executive summary and pp1, 5](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)). Quantities and locations are not published for security reasons ([p1](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)).

Crucially, the actual product in the stockpile is **PANVAX H5N8** (CSL Seqirus): an egg-based, adjuvanted vaccine containing A/Astrakhan/3212/2020 (H5N8)-like antigen at 30 µg HA per 0.5 mL, given as two 0.5 mL doses 21 days apart, with a 24-month shelf life ([Vaccine Framework, Appendix 2, p24](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf); [Medsafe PANVAX H5N8 data sheet](https://www.medsafe.govt.nz/Profs/Datasheet/p/PanvaxH5N8inj.pdf)). It is an H5N8 vaccine held against an H5N1 threat: the framework only *assumes* it is "moderately effective," and its usefulness against circulating H5N1 clade 2.3.4.4b depends on cross-protection, which the framework says applies only "in certain circumstances" and on expert advice ([Vaccine Framework, pp3, vii](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)). The underlying immunology is plausible — a peer-reviewed study found licensed H5N1 vaccines generate cross-neutralising antibodies against 2.3.4.4b ([Khurana et al., Nature Medicine, 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-024-03189-y)) — but effectiveness cannot be measured until the virus circulates through a vaccinated population, so only immunogenicity data exist ([Vaccine Framework, p2](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)).

**Who procures it is unusual.** Since the 2022 health reforms, Health NZ — not Pharmac — procures, funds and stores the pre-pandemic vaccine, while Medsafe historically approves it for use in line with the Pandemic Plan ([Vaccine Framework, p2](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)). Release is authorised by the Director-General of Health on the advice of the Director of Public Health, with the release threshold being "a significant public health threat posed by a novel avian influenza virus" ([Vaccine Framework, p3](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)). A matched, **pandemic-specific** vaccine would be made only after a pandemic is declared and "may take months to arrive" ([pp vii, 5](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)). The seasonal flu vaccine that Pharmac funds does **not** protect against H5N1 ([Vaccine Framework, p12](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)).

Priority-group logic, if the vaccine is released: in an *animal-disease* scenario, first to people with direct animal contact (poultry workers, vets, dairy farmers, DOC workers, cullers/decontaminators) then lab workers; in *pandemic* scenarios, border staff, then frontline health and lab workers, then critical-service workers (police, fire, corrections) ([Vaccine Framework, pp9, 17–18, 18](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)). NZ also reserves a number of vaccine courses for its Pacific Realm obligations (Cook Islands, Niue, Tokelau) ([p11](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)).

### Protecting exposed workers — the operational front line

Because human risk concentrates in occupational exposure, worker protection is the part of readiness that would actually be exercised first. NZ's approach is guidance-, PPE- and prophylaxis-based rather than mass vaccination:

- The Communicable Disease Control Manual's Avian influenza chapter requires that, on notification of a case, high-risk contacts and exposed people be identified and, "within one day," assessed, offered post-exposure prophylaxis where appropriate, and placed under active monitoring; the medical officer of health can prescribe oseltamivir after consulting infectious-disease specialists ([Health NZ, Avian influenza — CDC Manual](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/health-professionals/guidance-standards/topic/conditions/communicable-disease-control-manual/avian-influenza)).
- Public-health preventive measures include promoting PPE and hygiene for "anyone working with potentially infected birds," with workplace health-and-safety guidance provided ([Health NZ, CDC Manual](https://www.healthnz.govt.nz/health-professionals/guidance-standards/topic/conditions/communicable-disease-control-manual/avian-influenza)); MPI issues staying-safe guidance for people outdoors and workers ([MPI, Avian influenza: staying safe and alert when outdoors](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/biosecurity/pest-and-disease-threats-to-new-zealand/animal-disease-threats-to-new-zealand/high-pathogenicity-avian-influenza/)).
- MoH's own assessment is that "adequate PPE and other occupational health measures are likely to mitigate most animal-to-human transmission risk" ([MoH briefing, para 22](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)).
- This was tested in the December 2024 Otago H7N6 response — 100+ exposed workers monitored, no human infections ([MoH briefing, para 23](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)) — but H7N6 has no human-transmission track record, so this is weak evidence for how the system would cope against H5N1.

What the public record does **not** establish: the size of any PPE reserve earmarked for farm/wildlife workers, real-world PPE uptake and fit-testing among dispersed rural workforces, WorkSafe's enforcement role, or ACC coverage for an occupationally acquired zoonosis. That gap is why the US dairy experience matters as a comparator, and it is the main thing I have split off (see follow-ups).

### The US dairy comparator — why worker protection is the weak point everywhere

The ongoing US H5N1 clade-2.3.4.4b outbreak is the closest real-world stress test. The US has recorded **71 human A(H5) cases (one death) since February 2024**, with **no** confirmed human-to-human transmission — CDC reports 71 (64 detected through human monitoring, 7 through flu surveillance), and WHO's May-2026 cumulative table independently records the US at 71 cases / 1 death ([CDC, Current Situation](https://www.cdc.gov/bird-flu/situation-summary/index.html); [WHO cumulative table, to May 2026](https://cdn.who.int/media/docs/default-source/influenza/h5n1-human-case-cumulative-table/2026_table_h5n1_may.pdf)). A peer-reviewed case series of the first **70** of those infections (March 2024–May 2025) found 41 linked to dairy cows, 26 to poultry (24 commercial, 2 backyard) and 3 to an unidentified source, with no human-to-human transmission and no neuraminidase-inhibitor resistance ([Nature Medicine, US H5N1 human infections Mar 2024–May 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-025-03905-2)). Two lessons transfer to NZ: (1) most infections were mild and occupational, contracted despite available guidance, exposing how hard PPE compliance is in real dairy/poultry work; and (2) infection via "contaminated milking equipment" shows raw-milk and equipment pathways matter, not just direct bird contact ([MoH briefing, para 17](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). NZ's dairy sector is large and predominantly pasture-based — cow numbers reached 4.7 million in 2023/24 ([DairyNZ, New Zealand Dairy Statistics 2023/24, §1.2.2](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/bywm13d4/dairy-statistics-2023-24.pdf)) — so *if* H5N1 were ever to establish in NZ cattle, the same contaminated-equipment and occupational pathways could plausibly apply here. (That last clause is an inference transferred from the US experience, not an observed NZ risk: H5N1 has never been detected in NZ.)

### Surveillance and the pandemic-plan trigger — how fast NZ could stand up a response

Detecting the first human case relies on: HPAI being a notifiable disease under the Health Act 1956; laboratory influenza-A subtyping requested when clinicians suspect avian exposure; and — the notable enhancement — **all ICU patients nationwide who test positive for influenza A now undergoing subtyping to detect H5N1**, to catch severe zoonotic cases early ([MoH briefing, paras 29–32](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). Routine acute-respiratory surveillance runs through general practice, hospital surveillance (Auckland only) and syndromic systems ([Vaccine Framework, p3 footnote 4](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2026-02/pre-pandemic-influenza-vaccine-framework.pdf)) — a footnote worth flagging, because hospital surveillance being Auckland-only is a genuine coverage limitation. Genomic capability sits with ESR/PHF Science and a Te Niwha-funded project (led by Prof Jemma Geoghegan, University of Otago, and Dr David Winter, ESR): 2023–24 wild-bird surveillance of 700 individuals across 33 species found no H5N1 but did detect H1N9 in migratory shorebirds, demonstrating a working sequencing pipeline ([Waller et al., Influenza and Other Respiratory Viruses, 2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/irv.70099); [ESR, Genomics research to boost NZ's defences against bird flu](https://www.esr.cri.nz/news-publications/genomics-research-to-boost-aotearoa-new-zealand-s-defences-against-highly-pathogenic-bird-flu/)).

**The plan and its trigger.** Two documents govern the human response. The *HPAI Incursion Health Sector Framework* (developed 2024) covers surveillance, communications, at-risk-group engagement, case/outbreak response and use of the NRS for an animal-side incursion ([MoH briefing, paras 26–27](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)). The **New Zealand Pandemic Plan (interim update, July 2024)** is "the operative document in the event of sustained human-to-human transmission of HPAI detected in New Zealand or globally" ([MoH briefing, para 28](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf); [NZ Pandemic Plan, 2024](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/interim_nz_pandemic_plan_v2.pdf)). Its phases and the trigger that would move NZ up them are explicit ([NZ Pandemic Plan, Table 7, p58](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/interim_nz_pandemic_plan_v2.pdf)):

| Phase | NZ situation / trigger |
|---|---|
| **Plan For It** | No human cases in NZ; an animal pathogen is a specific pandemic threat but has caused only sporadic/small human clusters (**H5N1 sits here now**) |
| **Keep It Out** | Escalation trigger: verified H2H transmission able to sustain community-level outbreaks (globally / in two+ countries in one WHO region) |
| **Stamp It Out** | First case / clusters in NZ |
| **Manage It** (+ Post-Peak) | Substantial community transmission; containment no longer feasible |
| **Recover From It** | Pandemic over / population largely protected |

So the answer to "how fast could NZ stand up a response" is structurally clear: the escalation is triggered by **verified sustained H2H transmission**, at which point border, containment and vaccine-release levers activate. What is *not* verifiable from public sources is whether the antiviral and vaccine reserves are deep enough, and whether the H5N8 vaccine would work against the pandemic strain — the two things that determine whether "standing up a response" translates into protection.

### The independent expert view

In May 2024, eight NZ public-health and virology researchers (Michael Baker, John Potter, Nigel French, Jemma Geoghegan, Nick Wilson, Oz Mansoor, Sue Huang, Richard Webby) argued NZ should "ramp up preparedness": review systems to ensure "timely supply of A(H5N1) testing, vaccine, antivirals, and infection prevention and control (IPC) equipment," strengthen One Health surveillance across poultry/livestock/wildlife/humans, and update the national pandemic plan — noting the then-public plan was still the pre-COVID 2017 version ([Baker et al., PHCC briefing, 15 May 2024](https://www.phcc.org.nz/briefing/potential-avian-influenza-pandemic-time-nz-ramp-preparedness)). Since then, MoH has published the July-2024 interim Pandemic Plan and the Feb-2026 Vaccine Framework, which address the plan-update and vaccine-policy gaps at least on paper; the "timely supply" and IPC-equipment concerns remain unverifiable from public sources.

## What would change this conclusion

- **The redacted numbers.** An OIA release (or ministerial disclosure) of the antiviral stockpile quantity (para 36) and the vaccine course count would move the adequacy question from "unverifiable" to answerable. Right now the "low readiness base" characterisation rests on qualitative descriptors ("very limited," "highly constrained") — if the withheld quantities are in fact large, my Medium confidence on *adequacy* would rise.
- **Vaccine match.** If NZ swaps the H5N8 PANVAX stock for a matched H5N1 clade-2.3.4.4b product (the framework says the stockpile is updated periodically on expert advice), the cross-protection caveat weakens and readiness improves. Conversely, published immunogenicity data showing PANVAX H5N8 poorly neutralises 2.3.4.4b would sharpen the concern.
- **A change in the epidemiology.** Two distinct things would move the picture, and they should not be conflated:
  - *Risk-assessment escalation (not a formal phase trigger).* H5N1 spreading closer to NZ — clade 2.3.4.4b reaching Australia or the South Pacific, or expanding mammal-to-mammal transmission — would strengthen the case for lifting MoH/ESR's current "low" human-health risk rating. But by itself it would **not** move NZ up a Pandemic Plan phase: the MoH briefing already records that H5N1 has spread among cattle overseas and reached the Antarctic Peninsula while still assessing NZ's *current* risk as low ([MoH briefing, paras 5, 16–17, 21](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2025-10/H2025068297-Briefing-Introduction-to-High-Pathogenic-Avian-Influenza-HPAI.pdf)).
  - *Formal "Keep It Out" trigger.* The Pandemic Plan escalates into "Keep It Out" only when "human-to-human transmission of an animal or human–animal pathogen able to sustain community-level outbreaks has been verified," or the pathogen "has caused sustained community-level outbreaks in two or more countries in one WHO region" — **not** on mammal-to-mammal spread or geographic arrival alone (though "precautionary measures may be initiated prior to confirmation") ([NZ Pandemic Plan, Table 7](https://www.health.govt.nz/system/files/2024-07/interim_nz_pandemic_plan_v2.pdf)). That verified-sustained-H2H threshold is what activates border, containment and vaccine-release levers and changes the whole posture.
- **What I could not verify:** (1) the actual size/sufficiency of the antiviral and vaccine reserves; (2) whether NZ has secured advance-purchase agreements for a *pandemic-specific* vaccine (the framework says one would take "months" but gives no manufacturing/supply-agreement detail); (3) real-world PPE reserves, distribution and uptake for dispersed farm/wildlife workforces, and the WorkSafe/ACC dimensions of occupational protection; (4) surge lab capacity for mass H5 subtyping beyond the current ICU-sentinel approach.
- **A human with authority is needed** to confirm the withheld quantities and procurement contracts — this is an MoH/Health NZ/Pharmac disclosure question, not something an open-source researcher can close.

## Open follow-up questions

I have narrowed this finding to the **health-system readiness core** (antivirals, the pre-pandemic vaccine, the pandemic-plan trigger, and an overview of worker protection and surveillance). I split off the two parts that each deserve hours of dedicated work rather than a summary:

- **Occupational-health readiness for at-risk workers** (dairy/poultry/wildlife/lab): WorkSafe's role, PPE reserves and real-world uptake, post-exposure prophylaxis logistics for dispersed rural workforces, ACC coverage for an occupational zoonosis, and the transferable lessons from US dairy-herd worker infections. *(Fan-out sub-issue below.)*
- **Speed-of-detection capability:** whether NZ's genomic + wastewater + ICU-sentinel surveillance could detect the H2H-enabling mutation (or an imported human case) fast enough to matter, benchmarked against the "Keep It Out"/"Stamp It Out" response timelines. *(Fan-out sub-issue below.)*

Other researchable leads: NZ's advance-purchase/supply arrangements for a pandemic-specific (matched) vaccine; the adequacy of Auckland-only hospital respiratory surveillance for national coverage; and whether the H5N8→H5N1 cross-protection assumption is defensible on current immunogenicity data.
