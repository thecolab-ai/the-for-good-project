# The Charities Register can support a useful but imperfect census of NZ charitable grantmakers

## Executive answer

- The Charities Register open-data service can identify a reproducible, machine-readable set of registered charities that are likely grantmakers, because `Organisations` and `Groups` expose grantmaking activity/purpose fields and `GrpOrgLatestReturns` exposes latest-return grant/donation payment fields. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data); [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)
- A conservative "probable grantmaker" pull is the intersection of explicit grantmaking signals and positive latest-return grant/donation payments: 5,568 registered entities, with NZ$1,210,961,861 reported in latest-return grant/donation payments in a live pull at 2026-07-02 10:33 UTC. This is a single-origin register calculation, not an independently corroborated market-size estimate. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered organisations](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered groups](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Groups?$select=GroupId,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)
- A broader "any positive grants/donations paid" pull finds 10,873 registered entities with NZ$1,855,868,384 reported in latest-return grant/donation payments, but that is less reliable as a funder census because it includes charities that may mainly deliver services while also making grants or donations. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Charities Services, Charities data how-to guide](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/charities-data-how-to-guide)
- These figures exclude central and local government funders, Crown entities, iwi entities, corporate funders that are not registered charities, and other non-charity grantmakers; their aggregate grant scale is not quantified here. The JBWere/Philanthropy New Zealand Support Report estimated total New Zealand philanthropy and grantmaking at NZ$3.8 billion in 2018, so the register-derived NZ$1.21b-NZ$1.86b figures should be treated as a partial register view, not a near-complete funding-market census. [The New Zealand Support Report summary](https://primrose-ranunculus-tm9g.squarespace.com/s/PNZ_JB-Were-Fact-Sheet.pdf); [Philanthropy New Zealand media release](https://primrose-ranunculus-tm9g.squarespace.com/s/Joint-media-release-NZ-Support-Report-JBWere-PNZ.pdf)
- The Register is not enough to build a complete grant-matching database, because it does not publish grant programme eligibility, application dates, application URLs, decision cycles, or recipient-level grants in these OData fields. [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)
- The register data is self-reported by charities, and data.govt.nz says Charities Services has limited control over quality and cannot verify accuracy, so a public tool should treat this as a candidate funder list that needs validation before exposing funder profiles. [data.govt.nz, Charities Register case study](https://www.data.govt.nz/catalogue-guide/showcase/charities-register); [Charities Services Annual Review 2020/2021](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0024/15378/Annual-Review-2020-2021.pdf)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the OData fields and aggregate counts are reproducible from official sources, but the classification is imperfect because grantmaking is not a single authoritative register flag and the data is self-reported.

## Evidence

### What the register exposes

Charities Services says its open-data web service contains data about charities, officers and annual returns, includes data published to the Charities Register, excludes withheld data, is free and read-only, and can be queried in JSON, CSV or Atom. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)

Charities Services says open-data queries are restricted to 1,000 records by default and that the default can be overridden with `$returnall=true`; every aggregate in this finding used `$returnall=true`. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)

Charities Services says the database was updated on 19 August 2025 with new fields from XRB reporting standards, Charities Act 2005 changes and other Register improvements; I therefore treated older and newer grant-spend fields separately instead of assuming one stable field covers all annual-return eras. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)

The OData service exposes `Organisations`, `Groups`, `Activities`, `AnnualReturn`, `GrpOrgAllReturns` and `GrpOrgLatestReturns` entity sets, so it can join entity-level signals with latest annual-return finance by using `EntityType` plus `Id` in `GrpOrgLatestReturns`. [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata); [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf)

The activity reference data includes `ActivityId` 2 as "Makes grants / loans to individuals" and `ActivityId` 3 as "Makes grants to organisations (including schools or other charities)", which are the two strongest activity-code signals for grantmaking. [Activities OData query](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Activities?$format=json)

The entity-level fields I used as explicit grantmaking signals were `PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations`, `OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers`, and `MainActivityId in (2,3)` on both `Organisations` and `Groups`. [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)

The latest-return grant-spend fields I used were `GrantsPaidWithinNZ`, `GrantsPaidOutsideNZ`, `GrantsorDonationsPaid`, and `GrantsAndDonationsMade` on `GrpOrgLatestReturns`; the metric in this finding is their row-level sum, treating null as zero. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)

### Reproducible pull, method and counts

The latest-return pull used `GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus eq 'Registered'&$returnall=true&$format=json`, which returned 27,365 registered entities with a latest-return row in a live pull at 2026-07-02 10:33 UTC. Those rows split into 27,107 organisation rows and 258 group rows. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)

The explicit-signal pulls used `Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus eq 'Registered'&$returnall=true&$format=json` and `Groups?$select=GroupId,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus eq 'Registered'&$returnall=true&$format=json`. [Reproducible OData pull, registered organisations](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered groups](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Groups?$select=GroupId,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)

Those explicit-signal pulls found 9,677 registered organisations and 63 registered groups with at least one of `PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations=true`, `OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers=true`, or `MainActivityId` equal to 2 or 3. Of those 9,740 explicit-signal entities, 9,016 joined to a `GrpOrgLatestReturns` row and 724 did not; likely explanations include registered entities that had not filed a latest return exposed through `GrpOrgLatestReturns`, withheld or incomplete return data, or timing differences between entity registration and annual-return publication. [Reproducible OData pull, registered organisations](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered groups](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Groups?$select=GroupId,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data); [Charities Services, Annual returns](https://www.charities.govt.nz/reporting-standards/annual-return)

The join/filter calculation used this logic; the figures are point-in-time aggregates from mutable live endpoints and will drift as charities file or update returns. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered organisations](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered groups](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Groups?$select=GroupId,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)

```text
explicit_signal(entity) =
  PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations == true
  OR OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers == true
  OR MainActivityId in {2, 3}

grant_spend(return_row) =
  coalesce(GrantsPaidWithinNZ, 0)
  + coalesce(GrantsPaidOutsideNZ, 0)
  + coalesce(GrantsorDonationsPaid, 0)
  + coalesce(GrantsAndDonationsMade, 0)

signal_keys =
  Organisation:{OrganisationId} for registered Organisations with explicit_signal
  UNION Group:{GroupId} for registered Groups with explicit_signal

latest_keys =
  {EntityType}:{Id} for registered GrpOrgLatestReturns rows

conservative_set =
  latest return rows where {EntityType}:{Id} is in signal_keys
  AND grant_spend(row) > 0

broad_spend_only_set =
  latest return rows where grant_spend(row) > 0
```

### Reliability for a grant-matching tool

The Charities Register supports a candidate funder census better than a grants database, because it exposes entity-level purpose/activity and latest aggregate grant-spend fields but not programme-level eligibility, application windows, application forms, decision dates, geographic criteria, or named grant opportunities. [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)

The "spend-only" definition is over-inclusive for grant matching because a charity can report grants/donations paid while its main charitable activity is service delivery, research, religious activity, or another non-grantmaking activity. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)

The "explicit-signal only" definition is over-inclusive for live funding discovery because 3,448 registered latest-return entities had an explicit grantmaking signal but no positive latest-return grant/donation payment in the four fields used here. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered organisations](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered groups](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Groups?$select=GroupId,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)

The strongest practical route is a two-stage pipeline: use the conservative explicit-plus-spend set as the seed list of probable grantmakers, then enrich and validate each candidate against the charity's own website, annual report PDFs, gaming-trust/public-funder datasets, council grants pages, or funder directories before presenting matchable grant opportunities. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data); [data.govt.nz, Charities Register case study](https://www.data.govt.nz/catalogue-guide/showcase/charities-register)

The spend totals may overstate externally available grants because the OData fields do not distinguish contestable grants from internal transfers, related-party payments, scholarships, overseas giving, donations, or programme delivery grants. Group/member double counting is also possible because the live pull included 258 group rows as well as organisation rows, and Charities Services' own annual-review method says it excludes information included twice when charities report as both a group and an individual charity. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Charities Services Annual Review 2020/2021](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0024/15378/Annual-Review-2020-2021.pdf)

## What would change this conclusion

- A Charities Services data dictionary or future OData schema that defines a single authoritative "grantmaker" or "open to applications" field would change the classification from Medium-confidence inference to High-confidence register extraction.
- A reconciliation against audited financial statements or annual-report PDFs could change the spend totals if some charities map grantmaking into other expense fields, use grant/donation fields inconsistently, or report group totals that overlap with member charity totals.
- A manual validation sample of the 5,568 conservative candidates could materially change the recommended threshold if many explicit-plus-spend entities do not run externally accessible grant programmes.
- I could not verify from OData alone whether reported grants were contestable grants, internal transfers, scholarships, donations to related parties, programme delivery grants, overseas giving, or other kinds of payment; this is a material risk for using the NZ$1.21b figure as an externally accessible grant-market size.
- I could not independently benchmark the exact NZ$1.21b or NZ$1.86b Register-derived sums; the JBWere/Philanthropy New Zealand NZ$3.8b estimate is only a broad 2018 sector sanity check.
- I could not verify from OData alone whether a candidate grantmaker currently accepts unsolicited applications, has open rounds, or funds small charities/community groups.
- I did not publish entity names, contacts, officers or email addresses in this finding, because a grant-matching tool should avoid using Register contact data for unsolicited marketing and should validate funder contact channels from each funder's own public application material. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)
