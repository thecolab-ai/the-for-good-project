# The Charities Register can support a useful but imperfect census of NZ charitable grantmakers

## Executive answer

- The Charities Register open-data service can identify a reproducible, machine-readable set of registered charities that are likely grantmakers, because `Organisations` and `Groups` expose grantmaking activity/purpose fields and `GrpOrgLatestReturns` exposes latest-return grant/donation payment fields. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data); [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)
- A conservative "probable grantmaker" pull is the intersection of explicit grantmaking signals and positive latest-return grant/donation payments: 5,551 registered entities, with about NZ$1.195 billion reported in latest-return grant/donation payments. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered organisations](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,Name,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered groups](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Groups?$select=GroupId,name,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)
- A broader "any positive grants/donations paid" pull finds 10,873 registered entities with about NZ$1.854 billion reported in latest-return grant/donation payments, but that is less reliable as a funder census because it includes charities that may mainly deliver services while also making grants or donations. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Charities Services, Charities data how-to guide](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/charities-data-how-to-guide)
- The Register is not enough to build a complete grant-matching database, because it does not publish grant programme eligibility, application dates, application URLs, decision cycles, or recipient-level grants in these OData fields. [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)
- The register data is self-reported by charities, and data.govt.nz says Charities Services has limited control over quality and cannot verify accuracy, so a public tool should treat this as a candidate funder list that needs validation before exposing funder profiles. [data.govt.nz, Charities Register case study](https://www.data.govt.nz/catalogue-guide/showcase/charities-register)

**Overall confidence:** Medium - the OData fields and aggregate counts are reproducible from official sources, but the classification is imperfect because grantmaking is not a single authoritative register flag and the data is self-reported.

## Evidence

### What the register exposes

Charities Services says its open-data web service contains data about charities, officers and annual returns, includes data published to the Charities Register, excludes withheld data, is free and read-only, and can be queried in JSON, CSV or Atom. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)

Charities Services says open-data queries are restricted to 1,000 records by default and that the default can be overridden with `$returnall=true`; every aggregate in this finding used `$returnall=true`. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)

Charities Services says the database was updated on 19 August 2025 with new fields from XRB reporting standards, Charities Act 2005 changes and other Register improvements; I therefore treated older and newer grant-spend fields separately instead of assuming one stable field covers all annual-return eras. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)

The OData service exposes `Organisations`, `Groups`, `Activities`, `AnnualReturn`, `GrpOrgAllReturns` and `GrpOrgLatestReturns` entity sets, so it can join entity-level signals with latest annual-return finance by using `EntityType` plus `Id` in `GrpOrgLatestReturns`. [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata); [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf)

The activity reference data includes `ActivityId` 2 as "Makes grants / loans to individuals" and `ActivityId` 3 as "Makes grants to organisations (including schools or other charities)", which are the two strongest activity-code signals for grantmaking. [Activities OData query](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Activities?$format=json)

The entity-level fields I used as explicit grantmaking signals were `PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations`, `OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers`, and `MainActivityId in (2,3)` on both `Organisations` and `Groups`. [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)

The latest-return grant-spend fields I used were `GrantsPaidWithinNZ`, `GrantsPaidOutsideNZ`, `GrantsorDonationsPaid`, and `GrantsAndDonationsMade` on `GrpOrgLatestReturns`; the metric in this finding is their row-level sum, treating null as zero. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)

### Reliability for a grant-matching tool

The Charities Register supports a candidate funder census better than a grants database, because it exposes entity-level purpose/activity and latest aggregate grant-spend fields but not programme-level eligibility, application windows, application forms, decision dates, geographic criteria, or named grant opportunities. [Charities OData ERD](https://www.charities.govt.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0023/148352/Charities-OData-ERD.pdf); [OData metadata](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/$metadata)

The "spend-only" definition is over-inclusive for grant matching because a charity can report grants/donations paid while its main charitable activity is service delivery, research, religious activity, or another non-grantmaking activity. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)

The "explicit-signal only" definition is over-inclusive for live funding discovery because 3,442 registered latest-return entities had an explicit grantmaking signal but no positive latest-return grant/donation payment in the four fields used here. [Reproducible OData pull, registered latest returns](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/GrpOrgLatestReturns?$select=Id,EntityType,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,MainActivityName,Activities,AnnualReturnId,ReportingTier,YearEnded,GrantsPaidWithinNZ,GrantsPaidOutsideNZ,GrantsorDonationsPaid,GrantsAndDonationsMade,TotalExpenditure&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered organisations](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Organisations?$select=OrganisationId,Name,CharityRegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json); [Reproducible OData pull, registered groups](https://www.odata.charities.govt.nz/Groups?$select=GroupId,name,RegistrationNumber,RegistrationStatus,MainActivityId,OrganisationGeneratesFundsGrantsDonationsToOthers,PurposeToGiveGrantsAndDonations&$filter=RegistrationStatus%20eq%20%27Registered%27&$returnall=true&$format=json)

The strongest practical route is a two-stage pipeline: use the conservative explicit-plus-spend set as the seed list of probable grantmakers, then enrich and validate each candidate against the charity's own website, annual report PDFs, gaming-trust/public-funder datasets, council grants pages, or funder directories before presenting matchable grant opportunities. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data); [data.govt.nz, Charities Register case study](https://www.data.govt.nz/catalogue-guide/showcase/charities-register)

## What would change this conclusion

- A Charities Services data dictionary or future OData schema that defines a single authoritative "grantmaker" or "open to applications" field would change the classification from Medium-confidence inference to High-confidence register extraction.
- A reconciliation against audited financial statements or annual-report PDFs could change the spend totals if some charities map grantmaking into other expense fields or if some grant/donation fields are used inconsistently.
- A manual validation sample of the 5,551 conservative candidates could materially change the recommended threshold if many explicit-plus-spend entities do not run externally accessible grant programmes.
- I could not verify from OData alone whether reported grants were contestable grants, internal transfers, scholarships, donations to related parties, programme delivery grants, overseas giving, or other kinds of payment.
- I could not verify from OData alone whether a candidate grantmaker currently accepts unsolicited applications, has open rounds, or funds small charities/community groups.
- I did not publish entity names, contacts, officers or email addresses in this finding, because a grant-matching tool should avoid using Register contact data for unsolicited marketing and should validate funder contact channels from each funder's own public application material. [Charities Services, Open data](https://www.charities.govt.nz/charities-in-new-zealand/the-charities-register/open-data)
