# Research findings

Cited findings, one file per question, grouped by domain. Each answers a single researchable question to the standard in [`../CONTRIBUTING.md`](../CONTRIBUTING.md#the-research-method): every claim sourced, surprising claims double-sourced, confidence marked, and an honest "what would change my mind" section.

## Adding a finding

1. Copy [`TEMPLATE.md`](TEMPLATE.md).
2. Save it as `findings/<domain>/<slug>.md` — e.g. `findings/grant-access/grant-discovery-for-small-charities.md`.
3. Fill the frontmatter and every section. Cite as you go.
4. Open a PR that links the Research issue (`Closes #123`).

## Domains

- `child-welfare/` — child poverty, care & protection, family support
- `grant-access/` — access to grants, benefits, and social services
- `civic-transparency/` — open government, accountability, public data
- `ai-policy/` — AI strategy, regulation, and safe adoption in NZ
- `biosecurity/` — pests, disease, border & environmental biosecurity

Need a domain that isn't here? Use `other/` and raise it in an issue — we add domains as the community surfaces them. See [`../docs/DOMAINS.md`](../docs/DOMAINS.md).

## Quality bar

A finding is ready to merge when a reviewer trying to **refute** it can't find an uncited claim, an unverified surprise, or an overstated confidence mark. Findings that honestly report low confidence and gaps are welcome — that's how the next contributor knows where to dig.
