# NZ dairy freshwater-consent risk is real but not yet quantified nationally

Discovery framing for stream root #436 (Part of #436). Tests the root premise about dairy water use, consent renewal, production risk, farmer adaptation, regulatory direction, and international comparators, then proposes child research issues.

## Executive answer

- **The economic scale claim is supported, and the dollar figure should be corrected upward.** MPI's latest SOPI page forecasts dairy export revenue of **$27.4 billion** in the year to 30 June 2026, not just "$18B+" [MPI SOPI, December 2025](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/resources-and-forms/economic-intelligence/situation-and-outlook-for-primary-industries/), and DairyNZ/LIC's 2024/25 dairy statistics record **21 billion litres** of milk processed, **1.94 billion kg** of milksolids, **4.68 million** dairy cows, and **10,370** herds [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf). Stats NZ separately counted **5.7 million dairy cattle** at 30 June 2025 [Stats NZ, Agricultural production statistics: year to June 2025](https://www.stats.govt.nz/information-releases/agricultural-production-statistics-year-to-june-2025/).
- **The "water consents are expiring and tightening" premise is partially supported, but the national volume is not publicly pre-totalled.** Freshwater regulation already requires councils to set limits and bottom lines through the NPS-FM and related national direction [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/), and the 2025 Duration of Consents Act extends many expiring consents only to 31 December 2027 while excluding water-related consents from extension beyond 35 years [MfE, Duration of Consents Amendment Act 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/resource-management-duration-of-consents-amendment-act/). But the data found is regional, not national: ECan publishes active groundwater, surface-water and dairy-effluent consent layers with expiry and activity details [data.govt.nz, ECan groundwater-take active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/groundwater-take-active-consented-activities6); [data.govt.nz, ECan surface-water-take active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/surface-water-take-active-consented-activities3); [data.govt.nz, ECan dairy-effluent active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/effluent-dairy-discharge-active-consented-activities3), and Waikato publishes an allocated-water-take seasonal layer [data.govt.nz, Waikato allocated water take](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/allocated-water-take-seasonal-water-allocation).
- **The production-risk question is real, but "consent renewal" is too narrow.** A 2025 open-access water-accounting paper estimates the dairy sector is responsible for about **20 percent** of New Zealand consumptive blue-water use, with more than **90 percent** of that driven by irrigation and about **80 percent** sourced from surface water [Cameron and Peer, Agricultural Systems, 2025](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0308521X25000125). Production exposure therefore depends on a combined stack: water-take consents, irrigation-scheme access, dairy-effluent discharge conditions, nitrogen/phosphorus limits, stock-exclusion rules, freshwater farm plans, and catchment plan changes, rather than a single national class of "dairy water consent" [MfE, NES-FW](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/regulations/national-environmental-standards-for-freshwater/); [DairyNZ, irrigation management and operation](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/environment/irrigation/management-and-operation/).
- **Observed production reduction is not yet established nationally.** The best evidence found is catchment-specific: the Lake Taupo nitrogen cap used public funds and nitrogen-discharge allowances to permanently reduce manageable nitrogen entering the lake by at least **20 percent** [Waikato Regional Council, Variation 5 Lake Taupo](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/regional-plan/protecting-lake-taupo/); [Protecting Lake Taupo project](https://www.protectinglaketaupo.nz/about/the-project/), while DairyNZ/LIC reports that national milksolids production increased **2.9 percent** in 2024/25 despite a **0.5 percent** fall in cow numbers [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf). That combination supports a research question, not a national production-loss claim.
- **The regulatory trajectory is mixed: environmental limits are tightening, while the Government is also delaying and redesigning parts of the system.** The NPS-FM remains in force, requires councils to improve degraded water bodies and maintain or improve others, and gives councils until 31 December 2027 to notify freshwater plan changes [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/). At the same time, freshwater farm-plan rollout was paused and redesigned to reduce duplication and recognise industry programmes, although commercial dairy farms remain in scope and Southland still requires plans [MPI, freshwater farm plans](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/agriculture/farm-management-the-environment-and-land-use/protecting-freshwater-health/freshwater-farm-plans); [Environment Southland, Southland Farm Plans](https://www.es.govt.nz/community/farming/southland-farm-plans).
- **The international-comparator question is useful, but it should compare instruments, not just countries.** Australia's Murray-Darling Basin uses sustainable diversion limits across 29 surface-water areas and 80 groundwater areas [Murray-Darling Basin Authority, sustainable diversion limits](https://www.mdba.gov.au/water-use/water-limits/sustainable-diversion-limits), California's SGMA targets groundwater basins returning to balanced pumping and recharge [California State Water Resources Control Board, SGMA](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/about_sgma.html), and the Netherlands sits inside EU nitrate/manure controls including a phased end to the nitrates derogation [EUR-Lex, Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2022/2069](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX%3A32022D2069); [USDA FAS, Dutch Loss of Manure Derogation](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dutch+Loss+of+Manure+Derogation_The+Hague_Netherlands_NL2024-0007.pdf). NZ's distinctive comparison point is regional consent and plan-based allocation under national direction, not a basin-wide entitlement market or a nationally uniform dairy quota.

**Overall confidence:** Medium. The sector scale, current national rules, and existence of regional consent datasets are well supported by official sources; the national production-at-risk denominator remains uncomputed and should be treated as the stream's main evidence gap.

## Confidence & limits

**What is fact (High):** MPI forecasts dairy export revenue at $27.4 billion for the year to 30 June 2026 [MPI SOPI, December 2025](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/resources-and-forms/economic-intelligence/situation-and-outlook-for-primary-industries/); DairyNZ/LIC reports 21 billion litres of milk, 1.94 billion kg of milksolids, 4.68 million cows and 10,370 herds for 2024/25 [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf); the NPS-FM 2020 is in force with December 2025 amendments and requires councils to set freshwater objectives, limits and action plans [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/); ECan and Waikato publish machine-readable regional consent/allocation layers through data.govt.nz [data.govt.nz, ECan resource consents](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents19); [data.govt.nz, Waikato allocated water take](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/allocated-water-take-seasonal-water-allocation); Southland farm plans are required by 27 November 2027 under the Southland Water and Land Plan pathway, as Environment Southland's page rendered on 2026-07-05 states [Environment Southland, Southland Farm Plans](https://www.es.govt.nz/community/farming/southland-farm-plans).

**What is interpretation:** that the stream should reframe from "how many dairy water consents are expiring?" to "what production is exposed when water-take, irrigation, discharge and nutrient headroom are reduced at consent renewal or plan transition." That interpretation follows from the dairy water-accounting paper's irrigation-heavy water-use estimate [Cameron and Peer, 2025](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0308521X25000125), the regional structure of consent datasets [data.govt.nz, ECan groundwater-take active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/groundwater-take-active-consented-activities6), and the fact that national rules regulate dairy conversion, stock exclusion, nitrogen fertiliser, farm planning and water reporting through several instruments rather than one dairy consent category [MfE, NES-FW](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/regulations/national-environmental-standards-for-freshwater/); [MfE, stock exclusion regulations](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/regulations/stock-exclusion-regulations/); [MPI, freshwater farm plans](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/agriculture/farm-management-the-environment-and-land-use/protecting-freshwater-health/freshwater-farm-plans).

**Main limits:** this framing did not compute the national consent-expiry volume because the public data found is regional and needs cleaning, deduplication and classification by use, permit type, expiry date, allocation volume and land use [data.govt.nz search results for resource consents](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/api/3/action/package_search?q=resource+consents+regional+council&rows=10). It did not access private processor, irrigation-scheme, farm-production, Overseer, compliance, or individual consent-condition datasets. It is not legal advice on RMA replacement bills, consent duration, or regional-plan appeal outcomes. The Bay of Plenty Plan Change 10 live page was bot-blocked by the repo fetch ladder, so the cited version below uses a Wayback snapshot captured/reused by `scripts/archive-cite.mjs` [BOPRC PC10 live page](https://www.boprc.govt.nz/your-council/plans-and-policies/plans/regional-plans/regional-natural-resources-plan/lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10/); [Wayback snapshot, 16 March 2026](https://web.archive.org/web/20260316120315/https://www.boprc.govt.nz/your-council/plans-and-policies/plans/regional-plans/regional-natural-resources-plan/lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10/). A resource-management lawyer, regional-council consent officer, dairy-sector adviser, and mana whenua freshwater governance perspective would materially improve the stream before any G1 decision.

## Problem statement

New Zealand dairy is economically large and water-dependent: MPI forecasts dairy exports at $27.4 billion for 2025/26 [MPI SOPI, December 2025](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/resources-and-forms/economic-intelligence/situation-and-outlook-for-primary-industries/), DairyNZ/LIC records 4.68 million cows and 10,370 herds in 2024/25 [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf), and a 2025 water-accounting study estimates dairy accounts for about 20 percent of national consumptive blue-water use, mostly irrigation [Cameron and Peer, 2025](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0308521X25000125). Freshwater pressures are also real: Our Freshwater 2026 says land-use pressures, contamination, abstraction and climate change are altering water quality and movement, and it identifies excess agricultural nutrients from fertiliser and livestock as a major source of freshwater-quality pressure [MfE and Stats NZ, Our Freshwater 2026](https://environment.govt.nz/publications/our-freshwater-2026/).

The decision problem is not whether dairy uses water or whether freshwater regulation is changing; both are established [Cameron and Peer, 2025](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0308521X25000125); [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/). The decision problem is that no national public source was found that links dairy-specific water-take volumes, discharge permits, nutrient limits, expiry dates, renewal outcomes and milk production into one exposure estimate [data.govt.nz, ECan resource consents](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents19); [data.govt.nz, Waikato allocated water take](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/allocated-water-take-seasonal-water-allocation). Without that joined denominator, "how much production is at risk" is currently a hypothesis to quantify, not a fact to cite.

The stream should therefore establish the exposure stack region by region: which dairy-related water-take, irrigation, effluent and nutrient permissions expire by 2031 and 2036; which renewal rules or plan changes could materially reduce water or nutrient headroom; what production and regional employment/revenue sit behind those permissions; and what farmers are doing to adapt [DairyNZ, irrigation management and operation](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/environment/irrigation/management-and-operation/); [MPI, freshwater farm plans](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/agriculture/farm-management-the-environment-and-land-use/protecting-freshwater-health/freshwater-farm-plans).

## Who is affected

**Dairy farmers, staff and processors:** DairyNZ/LIC's 2024/25 census reports 10,370 herds, 4.68 million cows and 1.94 billion kg of milksolids [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf), while MPI forecasts dairy export revenue of $27.4 billion in the year to 30 June 2026 [MPI SOPI, December 2025](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/resources-and-forms/economic-intelligence/situation-and-outlook-for-primary-industries/). Freshwater rules can affect farm viability through water-availability, irrigation, effluent, nutrient-budgeting, stock-exclusion and farm-plan obligations [MfE, NES-FW](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/regulations/national-environmental-standards-for-freshwater/); [MPI, freshwater farm plans](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/agriculture/farm-management-the-environment-and-land-use/protecting-freshwater-health/freshwater-farm-plans).

**Regional councils and consent holders:** Regional councils administer resource consents and regional plans under national direction [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/), and the Duration of Consents Act requires councils to update affected consent records while warning consent holders to consider whether consents are water-related [MfE, Duration of Consents Amendment Act 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/resource-management-duration-of-consents-amendment-act/). ECan's public consent layers show the kind of data councils hold, including permit type, status, applicant, location, permitted period, and water-take volume/rate fields where relevant [data.govt.nz, ECan resource consents](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/resource-consents19); [data.govt.nz, ECan groundwater-take active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/groundwater-take-active-consented-activities6).

**Iwi, hapu, drinking-water users, recreation users and freshwater ecosystems:** Our Freshwater 2026 says freshwater supports health, cultural identity, recreation, communities, economy and livelihoods, and that degradation affects drinking-water security, ecosystem health, recreation and cultural practices [MfE and Stats NZ, Our Freshwater 2026](https://environment.govt.nz/publications/our-freshwater-2026/). The NPS-FM requires freshwater management to give effect to Te Mana o te Wai, including prioritising water-body health, then essential human needs, then other uses [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/).

**Catchments with intensive dairy and irrigation exposure:** Canterbury, Waikato, Southland, Bay of Plenty/Rotorua and Taupo show up as high-priority starting points because ECan publishes dairy-effluent, groundwater and surface-water consent layers [data.govt.nz, ECan dairy-effluent active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/effluent-dairy-discharge-active-consented-activities3), Waikato PC1 targets nitrogen, phosphorus, sediment and bacteria in the Waikato and Waipa rivers [Waikato Regional Council, PC1](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/healthy-rivers-plan-for-change/), Southland has farm-plan requirements under its Water and Land Plan [Environment Southland, Southland Farm Plans](https://www.es.govt.nz/community/farming/southland-farm-plans), Bay of Plenty Plan Change 10 limits nitrogen entering Lake Rotorua [BOPRC PC10 snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260316120315/https://www.boprc.govt.nz/your-council/plans-and-policies/plans/regional-plans/regional-natural-resources-plan/lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10/), and Lake Taupo has an implemented nitrogen cap and buy-back model [Waikato Regional Council, Variation 5 Lake Taupo](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/regional-plan/protecting-lake-taupo/).

## What is already being done

**National direction:** The NPS-FM 2020 is in force with December 2025 amendments, requires councils to improve degraded water bodies, maintain or improve others, set freshwater objectives and limits, monitor annually, and publish five-year synthesis reports [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/). The NES-FW regulates activities that pose risks to freshwater, including dairy conversion, stockholding areas, intensive winter grazing, synthetic nitrogen fertiliser, and activities that often require regional-council consent [MfE, NES-FW](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/regulations/national-environmental-standards-for-freshwater/). Stock-exclusion regulations require dairy cattle, dairy-support cattle and pigs to be excluded from specified wetlands, lakes and rivers and from land closer than three metres to river and lake beds [MfE, stock exclusion regulations](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/regulations/stock-exclusion-regulations/).

**Freshwater farm plans:** MfE says freshwater farm plans help farmers and growers identify, manage and reduce farming impacts on freshwater, and commercial dairy farms remain within the threshold for requiring a plan [MfE, freshwater farm plans](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/freshwater-implementation-guidance/freshwater-farm-plans/). MPI says the 2024 rollout pause is being followed by changes to reduce duplication, recognise existing plans and industry programmes such as Fonterra's Tiaki plans, and make certification more targeted [MPI, freshwater farm plans](https://www.mpi.govt.nz/agriculture/farm-management-the-environment-and-land-use/protecting-freshwater-health/freshwater-farm-plans). Environment Southland's current page, rendered on 2026-07-05, says Southland farmers must submit farm plans for certification by 27 November 2027 [Environment Southland, Southland Farm Plans](https://www.es.govt.nz/community/farming/southland-farm-plans).

**Consent-duration transition:** The 2025 Duration of Consents Act extends resource consents that would otherwise expire before 31 December 2027, but excludes water-related consents from extension beyond a total duration of 35 years and recommends councils proactively contact affected water-related consent holders [MfE, Duration of Consents Amendment Act 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/resource-management-duration-of-consents-amendment-act/). This means the renewal cliff is partly deferred for some consents and partly still binding for water-related consents, depending on the original commencement date and whether the 35-year cap is reached [MfE, Duration of Consents Amendment Act 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/resource-management-duration-of-consents-amendment-act/).

**Regional catchment rules:** Waikato PC1 is an 80-year pathway to improve Waikato and Waipa river water quality, and its page says PC1 manages point-source and agricultural non-point-source discharges of nitrogen, phosphorus, sediment and bacteria [Waikato Regional Council, PC1](https://www.waikatoregion.govt.nz/council/policy-and-plans/healthy-rivers-plan-for-change/). Bay of Plenty Plan Change 10 limits nitrogen entering Lake Rotorua from land use [BOPRC PC10 snapshot](https://web.archive.org/web/20260316120315/https://www.boprc.govt.nz/your-council/plans-and-policies/plans/regional-plans/regional-natural-resources-plan/lake-rotorua-nutrient-management-plan-change-10/). Canterbury's Land and Water Regional Plan provides the regional framework for land and water management [Environment Canterbury, Canterbury Land and Water Regional Plan](https://www.ecan.govt.nz/your-region/plans-strategies-and-bylaws/canterbury-land-and-water-regional-plan), and ECan's open-data consent layers provide a concrete starting point for quantifying dairy-relevant permits [data.govt.nz, ECan surface-water-take active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/surface-water-take-active-consented-activities3); [data.govt.nz, ECan dairy-effluent active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/effluent-dairy-discharge-active-consented-activities3).

**Farm practice and industry response:** DairyNZ advises farmers to map water systems, check troughs, upgrade weak reticulation, detect leaks and manage shed water efficiently [DairyNZ, water use overview](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/environment/water-use/water-use-overview/). DairyNZ also advises irrigation managers to work within regional-council or irrigation-scheme conditions, monitor volume and rate limits, prepare for restrictions, and apply for replacement consents at least six months before expiry if they need to keep operating while a replacement is processed [DairyNZ, irrigation management and operation](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/environment/irrigation/management-and-operation/).

## Proposed child research issues

1. **What dairy water-take, discharge and irrigation consent volume, and linked production, expires by 2031 and 2036?** This is the denominator issue: join regional consent layers, expiry dates, allocation volumes/rates, permit purpose, dairy land use and milk-production proxies, starting with ECan, Waikato, Southland, Bay of Plenty, Otago and Northland [data.govt.nz, ECan groundwater-take active layer](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/groundwater-take-active-consented-activities6); [data.govt.nz, Waikato allocated water take](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/dataset/allocated-water-take-seasonal-water-allocation).
2. **Which freshwater rules are most likely to reduce dairy headroom at consent renewal or plan transition?** This should map NPS-FM, NES-FW, water-measurement, stock-exclusion, freshwater farm-plan, PC1, PC10, Southland, Canterbury and Otago pathways into "can reduce water/nutrient/discharge headroom" versus "mainly reporting or practice management" [MfE, NPS-FM page](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/national-policy-statements/national-policy-statement-freshwater-management/); [MfE, NES-FW](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/regulations/national-environmental-standards-for-freshwater/).
3. **What production or land-use change has actually followed NZ freshwater caps and renewals?** This should test observed effects in Taupo, Rotorua, Selwyn-Waihora, Waikato-Waipa, Southland and Otago against DairyNZ/LIC production trends and council records [Protecting Lake Taupo project](https://www.protectinglaketaupo.nz/about/the-project/); [DairyNZ/LIC, 2024/25 Dairy Statistics](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/media/oglesqfm/nz-dairy-statistics-24-25.pdf).
4. **How are dairy farms adapting to tighter freshwater constraints, and what does it cost?** This should separate stocking-rate change, productivity gains, effluent storage, irrigation efficiency, water metering, farm plans, stock exclusion, land-use change and exit from confounders such as milk price, interest rates and emissions policy [DairyNZ, water use overview](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/environment/water-use/water-use-overview/); [DairyNZ, irrigation management and operation](https://www.dairynz.co.nz/environment/irrigation/management-and-operation/).
5. **How does NZ's consent-and-plan approach compare with Australia, California and the Netherlands?** This should compare allocation security, transition periods, trading/buy-back tools, compensation or adjustment support, environmental limits, and documented production effects [MDBA, sustainable diversion limits](https://www.mdba.gov.au/water-use/water-limits/sustainable-diversion-limits); [California State Water Resources Control Board, SGMA](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/about_sgma.html); [USDA FAS, Dutch Loss of Manure Derogation](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dutch+Loss+of+Manure+Derogation_The+Hague_Netherlands_NL2024-0007.pdf).

## What would change this framing

- A national official dataset joining consent expiry, water allocation, dairy land use and production would move the top conclusion from "not yet quantified" to a direct exposure estimate [data.govt.nz search results for resource consents](https://catalogue.data.govt.nz/api/3/action/package_search?q=resource+consents+regional+council&rows=10).
- Evidence that regional councils routinely renew dairy water-take and discharge consents with no material volume, nutrient, discharge or monitoring changes would weaken the production-at-risk premise [MfE, Duration of Consents Amendment Act 2025](https://environment.govt.nz/acts-and-regulations/acts/resource-management-duration-of-consents-amendment-act/).
- Evidence of material production loss in a major dairy catchment after a rule change or renewal cohort would strengthen the risk premise beyond the Lake Taupo nitrogen-reduction lead [Protecting Lake Taupo project](https://www.protectinglaketaupo.nz/about/the-project/).
- Final RMA replacement legislation or a replacement NPS-FM could change the legal trajectory, because the Government says the Planning Bill and Natural Environment Bill were introduced on 9 December 2025 and new processes will begin when the Bills become law in 2026 [MfE, RMA reform](https://environment.govt.nz/what-government-is-doing/areas-of-work/rma/rmreform/).
